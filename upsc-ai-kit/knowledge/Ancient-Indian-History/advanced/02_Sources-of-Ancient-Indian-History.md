## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Advanced — source criticism is the core skill.**

✅ Upinder Singh stresses that literary, archaeological, inscriptional, numismatic and visual sources must be **contextualized and analyzed** with attention to nature, potential and limitations. Where several sources exist, their evidence must be **co-related**.
- ✅ Texts and archaeology together produce a more comprehensive and inclusive history.
- ✅ Ancient texts often interweave religious and non-religious themes, so modern categories cannot be applied mechanically.
- ✅ Archaeological evidence is especially important where textual narratives dominate but material contexts are thin.

**Advanced — source does not equal fact.**

✅ Upinder's chapter treats the source-list itself as an analytical field: Vedas, epics, Puranas, Dharmashastra, Buddhist and Jaina texts, Sangam literature, biographies, foreign accounts, inscriptions, coins and visual material all speak differently.
- ✅ The Mahabharata/Ramayana and Puranas need attention to stratification, redaction and genre.
- ✅ Inscriptions and coins are closer to political-economic data but still selective and elite.
- ⚠️ The best answers use triangulation: text claim + material correlate + chronological caution.
- ✅ Archaeological interpretation depends on **provenance, stratigraphy, association and recovery method**. Vertical trenches clarify sequence; wide horizontal exposure better reveals settlement layout.
- ✅ Scientific techniques—radiocarbon dating, archaeobotany, archaeozoology, metallurgy and ancient DNA—answer different questions and carry different margins of uncertainty.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Source | Advanced value | Advanced limitation |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Vedas / epics / Puranas | social ideas, ritual, memory, genealogy | oral layers, redaction, normative claims |
| ✅ Buddhist-Jaina texts | urban life, merchants, heterodox ethics | sectarian perspective and didactic framing |
| ✅ Sangam literature | early Tamilakam, chiefs, trade, bardic culture | dating/layering problems |
| ✅ Archaeology | everyday life, technology, food, settlement | interpretation depends on context |
| ✅ Inscriptions | rulers, donations, administration, language | formulaic and elite-heavy |
| ✅ Coins / visual sources | economy, kingship, symbols, art history | circulation and dating must be established |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Upinder lists textual, archaeological, inscriptional, coin and visual sources as major categories.
- ✅ Texts and archaeology must be **co-related** for a fuller history.
- ✅ Ancient texts often mix religious and non-religious content.
- ✅ Major textual source-groups include epics, Puranas, Dharmashastra, Buddhist, Jaina and Sangam texts.
- ✅ Foreign accounts are a distinct source category.
- ⚠️ Absence of a source is not always absence of a practice; it may reflect preservation and discovery bias.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: "Source available" does not mean "source decisive".

- ❌ A literary reference proves an archaeological culture. → It needs material correlation.
- ❌ A royal inscription gives the common people's view. → It mostly gives elite/public claims.
- ❌ Coins always circulate only where they are minted. → Hoards and circulation patterns can differ.
- ❌ Sangam literature is identical to archaeology of Tamilakam. → It must be read with material evidence.

## 6. 📰 Current link

⚠️ **Current-link discipline:** A newly reported inscription should be cited only after publication or an official epigraphy notice. The safe linkage is that one securely read inscription can revise local political and cultural micro-history.
