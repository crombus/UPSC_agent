## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Advanced — the six-system list is itself historical.**

✅ Upinder Singh cautions that the idea of the **"six systems of orthodox philosophy"** emerged fairly late, although the traditions themselves are old. Their early texts are often in cryptic **sutra** form and generated many commentaries.
- ✅ The systems are often treated as pairs: **Purva Mimamsa/Uttara Mimamsa (Vedanta)**, **Nyaya/Vaisheshika**, and **Samkhya/Yoga**.
- ✅ Astika/nastika labels do not simply mean "theist/atheist"; they relate to acceptance or rejection of authoritative frameworks such as Veda/other-world/transmigration in different traditions.
- ✅ Materialist Lokayata/Charvaka is placed among nastika/heterodox traditions.
- ⚠️ Advanced answers should avoid making the six-system list appear timeless and fixed from the Vedic age.

**Advanced — purusharthas and social ethics.**

✅ Upinder describes the goals of human life as **dharma, artha, kama**, with **moksha** added at some point. Material gain and sensual pleasure were legitimate when pursued in moderation according to dharma.
- ✅ This framework creates a bridge between social order, political economy, pleasure and liberation.
- ✅ Philosophical schools debated knowledge, reality, ritual, liberation and moral order through dense textual traditions.
- ✅ Medieval doxographies could arrange all schools hierarchically, often privileging one tradition such as Advaita Vedanta.
- ⚠️ Use philosophy as a historical product of debate and commentary, not as a single homogeneous "Indian view".

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Upinder notes that the six orthodox systems as a grouped list crystallised late.
- ✅ Common pairings: **Mimamsa-Vedanta**, **Nyaya-Vaisheshika**, **Samkhya-Yoga**.
- ✅ Purusharthas: dharma, artha, kama, later moksha.
- ✅ Sutra style led to multiple commentaries and interpretations.
- ✅ Charvaka/Lokayata is materialist and classified as nastika/heterodox.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Advanced UPSC statements may test the historicity of categories.

- ❌ Astika always means theistic. → It is a historical/classificatory term, not a simple God/no-God label.
- ❌ The six systems were always a fixed Vedic list. → The grouping emerged later.
- ❌ Artha and kama were rejected in Indian thought. → They were legitimate when governed by dharma.
- ❌ Sutras are self-explanatory textbooks. → They are terse and require commentarial traditions.
