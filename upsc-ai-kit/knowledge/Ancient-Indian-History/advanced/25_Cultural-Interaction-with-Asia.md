## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Advanced — connected history, not cultural export alone.**

✅ Upinder Singh's approach encourages seeing India, China, Central Asia and Southeast Asia as part of **premodern Asian networks**. Movement involved monks, merchants, texts, relics, artisans and political ideas.
- ✅ Buddhist and Hindu influences were visible in Southeast Asia from early centuries CE; in Cambodia they had taken firm root by the 6th century.
- ✅ Sarvastivada and Mahayana Buddhism are noted in north Myanmar from the 3rd century CE.
- ✅ Similar processes occurred in the Malay peninsula, Java, Sumatra and Vietnam.
- ⚠️ The term "Indianisation" must be used carefully: local rulers adapted Indian ideas for their own contexts.

**Advanced — relics, pilgrimage and power.**

✅ Upinder notes that special importance was attached to **Buddha relics**, believed to be corporeal remains and coveted across the Asian Buddhist world.
- ✅ The `Mahavamsa` assigns a special role to Ashoka, Mahinda and Sanghamitta in spreading Buddhism to Sri Lanka.
- ✅ Relics, monasteries and texts created sacred geographies linking India to Asia.
- ✅ Cultural flows were multi-directional: pilgrims came to India, texts travelled out, and Asian patrons reinterpreted Indian forms.
- ✅ The Ashoka–Mahinda–Sanghamitta narrative is specifically associated with the Sri Lankan `Mahavamsa`; distinguish chronicle memory from Ashoka's own inscriptions.
- ⚠️ For mains, connect material objects (relics, manuscripts, images) with diplomacy and legitimacy.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Advanced questions may reject simplistic "Greater India" narratives.

- ❌ Southeast Asia merely copied India. → Local societies selected and reshaped Indian elements.
- ❌ Relics are only religious objects. → They also create legitimacy, pilgrimage and diplomacy.
- ❌ Buddhism moved only by kings. → Monks, merchants, texts and devotees mattered.
- ❌ Cultural contact means political conquest. → Most links were network-based, not direct rule.
