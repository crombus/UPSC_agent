## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Advanced — beyond decline: regional state formation.**

✅ Upinder Singh presents the early medieval period as marked by **proliferation and expansion of states** across the subcontinent. Land grants to Brahmanas helped legitimise political power and affected agrarian relations.
- ✅ Agrarian expansion occurred in various regions and rural societies became increasingly complex.
- ✅ Land grants could transfer fiscal, administrative and social rights, reshaping the relation between ruler, donee and cultivator.
- ✅ Brahmana beneficiaries and brahmadeya settlements are central to understanding agrarian and cultural change.
- ⚠️ Do not write the period only as "decay"; it also generated regional polities, temples, literatures and new identities.

**Advanced — historiography of the transition.**

✅ Upinder notes that scholars such as **B. D. Chattopadhyaya** and **Hermann Kulke** offered alternatives to simple political-breakdown models: early medieval India saw intensive **state formation at regional and sub-regional levels**.
- ✅ Land grants were one strategy among several for rulers to legitimise power.
- ✅ Chattopadhyaya's larger processes include expansion of state society, transformation of tribes into peasants, caste formation and cult appropriation.
- ✅ Urban processes did not disappear uniformly; contraction in some zones coexisted with agrarian expansion and later renewal.
- ⚠️ The best answer contrasts RS Sharma's feudal-tendency thesis with regional-process models.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Advanced answers must avoid one-school historiography.

- ❌ Early medieval = total dark age. → It saw regional states, temples, literature and agrarian expansion.
- ❌ RS Sharma's model is the only interpretation. → Later historians modified it with regional state-formation models.
- ❌ Land grants only affected religion. → They altered fiscal and agrarian relations.
- ❌ Urban decline was uniform and permanent. → It varied by region and time.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ A strong answer compares **feudalisation** and **regional state formation** rather than choosing one mechanically.
- ⚠️ Land grants were political technology: they created allies, opened agrarian frontiers and sacralised authority.
- ⚠️ India's legacy is not static inheritance; it survives through language policy, manuscript preservation, medicine, temples and contested social memory.
