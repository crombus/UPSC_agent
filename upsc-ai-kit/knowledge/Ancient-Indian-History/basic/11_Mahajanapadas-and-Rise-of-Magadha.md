## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — from janapadas to large territorial states.**

✅ RS Sharma says that in the age of the Buddha there were **sixteen large states called mahajanapadas**, mostly north of the Vindhyas, extending from the north-west frontier to Bihar.
- ✅ Of these, **Magadha, Koshala, Vatsa and Avanti** were especially powerful.
- ✅ The political history from the sixth century BC is one of struggles among these states, with Magadha eventually emerging strongest.
- ✅ The period belongs to the wider context of the **second urbanisation** in the Gangetic basin.

**Standard — why Magadha won.**

✅ RS Sharma highlights Magadha's advantageous geographical position in the age of iron: rich iron deposits were not far from Rajgir, enabling effective weapons.
- ✅ Rajgir/Girivraja, the earliest capital, was surrounded by five hills and made impregnable by stone walls.
- ✅ Pataliputra later occupied a strategic position near river junctions, improving defence and communication.
- ✅ Ambitious rulers such as **Bimbisara, Ajatashatru and Mahapadma Nanda** used conquest and diplomacy to expand Magadha.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Sixteen mahajanapadas existed in the age of the Buddha.
- ✅ Most were north of the Vindhyas, from north-west frontier to Bihar.
- ✅ Four powerful states: **Magadha, Koshala, Vatsa, Avanti**.
- ✅ Bimbisara ruled Magadha **544–492 BC** in RS Sharma's chronology.
- ✅ Rajgir/Girivraja was protected by five hills.
- ✅ Iron deposits near Rajgir and strategic river routes helped Magadha.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC tests **why Magadha**, not just who ruled Magadha.

- ❌ Magadha rose only because of one great ruler. → Geography, iron, rivers, economy and rulers all mattered.
- ❌ Rajgir and Pataliputra were the same capital. → Rajgir was earlier; Pataliputra rose later.
- ❌ All mahajanapadas were republics. → Some were monarchies; some were ganas/sanghas.
- ❌ Second urbanisation began with Harappa. → Harappa was first urbanisation; mahajanapadas mark the second urbanisation.
