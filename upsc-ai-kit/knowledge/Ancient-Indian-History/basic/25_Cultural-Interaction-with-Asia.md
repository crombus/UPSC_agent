## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — India did not live in isolation.**

✅ RS Sharma begins by rejecting the idea that sea-crossing restrictions meant India shunned the outside world. India had Asian contacts since Harappan times through traders, missionaries, conquerors and settlers.
- ✅ Indian traders went to Mesopotamian cities; Harappan seals have been found there.
- ✅ India maintained commercial contacts with **China, Southeast Asia, West Asia, Central Asia and the Roman world**.
- ✅ Indian land routes connected with the Chinese Silk Route.
- ✅ Cultural spread followed trade, pilgrimage, missions and settlement — not only conquest.

**Standard — Buddhism and Indian culture abroad.**

✅ Buddhism was a major carrier of Indian cultural contact with **Sri Lanka, Myanmar, China and Central Asia**.
- ✅ Buddhist missionaries were sent to Sri Lanka in Ashoka's reign in the 3rd century BC.
- ✅ Brahmi inscriptions of the 2nd–1st centuries BC have been found in Sri Lanka.
- ✅ Buddhism spread to Myanmar in the early Christian centuries and to China/Central Asia through missionaries and routes.
- ✅ Indian cultural influence in Southeast Asia is visible in places such as **Kamboja/Cambodia, Champa, Java, Sumatra, Angkor and Borobudur**.
- ⚠️ Write "cultural interaction" rather than one-way "Indian colonisation"; give-and-take matters.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Ashoka's reign is linked to Buddhist missions to **Sri Lanka**.
- ✅ India had contacts with China, Southeast Asia, West Asia, Central Asia and Rome.
- ✅ Early Christian centuries: Buddhism spread to Myanmar; missionaries active in China/Central Asia/Afghanistan.
- ✅ Srivijaya was an important power and centre of Indian culture from 5th–11th centuries.
- ✅ Cambodia/Kamboja appears in RS chronology from the 6th century.
- ✅ Cultural give-and-take is the correct frame; avoid one-way civilising claims.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not confuse cultural influence with direct Indian political rule everywhere.

- ❌ Sea-crossing rules ended India's external contacts. → RS Sharma says contacts continued.
- ❌ Buddhism spread only within India. → It travelled to Sri Lanka, Myanmar, China and Central Asia.
- ❌ Angkor and Borobudur are Indian sites. → They are Southeast Asian sites with Indian cultural links.
- ❌ Cultural contact was one-way. → RS Sharma includes "cultural give and take".

## 6. 📰 Current link

📰 **ASI conservation at Ta Prohm/Angkor, Cambodia (ongoing 2025–26):** official India-Cambodia heritage cooperation highlights ASI's long role at Angkor Wat earlier and continuing work at Ta Prohm. Use it as a live bridge between ancient Indian cultural diffusion and modern cultural diplomacy.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Buddhism functioned as ancient India's most durable trans-Asian soft power.
- ⚠️ Trade routes carried ideas, scripts, art forms and religious institutions along with goods.
- ⚠️ Modern Act East/cultural diplomacy can be historically grounded in ancient Asian networks.
