## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — megaliths before kingdoms.**

✅ RS Sharma begins the Deep South with **megalithic culture** rather than ready-made kingdoms. The Cholas, Pandyas and Keralaputras/Cheras mentioned in Ashokan inscriptions were probably still in the late megalithic phase.
- ✅ Megaliths are known mainly from **funerary structures**, not abundant settlements.
- ✅ Tamil Nadu shows urn burials and other burial forms; grave goods help reconstruct material culture.
- ✅ By about the 2nd century BC, megalithic people moved from uplands into fertile river basins, supporting stronger political centres.

**Standard — Sangam texts and early Tamil society.**

✅ Sangam literature gives the main picture of social, economic and political life in deltaic Tamil Nadu in the early centuries AD, though it was finally compiled later.
- ✅ The texts speak of rudimentary state formation: warriors, taxation, judiciary, merchants, craftsmen and farmers.
- ✅ Major towns include **Kanchi, Korkai, Madurai, Puhar/Kaveripattanam and Uraiyur**.
- ✅ Yavanas came in ships, bought **pepper with gold**, and supplied wine and women slaves; archaeology and Greco-Roman texts support the trade picture.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Sangam age is placed broadly at **c. 300 BC–AD 300** in the chronology spine.
- ✅ The deep south before large states is reconstructed through **megalithic burials**.
- ✅ Ashokan inscriptions mention Cholas, Pandyas and Keralaputras/Cheras.
- ✅ Sangam texts describe towns such as **Kanchi, Korkai, Madurai, Puhar and Uraiyur**.
- ✅ Yavana trade: gold for pepper; wine and luxury goods came by sea.
- ✅ **Arikamedu** is a key Indo-Roman trade site; **Muziris** is linked to the Malabar coast.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not treat Sangam poems as if they are a single dated inscription.

- ❌ Sangam society began directly as an empire. → RS Sharma roots it in **megalithic** material culture.
- ❌ The Chera-Chola-Pandya world was isolated. → It had north-Indian and Indo-Roman contacts.
- ❌ Sangam literature is useless because it was compiled later. → It is usable, but must be read with archaeology.
- ❌ All megaliths are settlements. → Many are **burial monuments**; settlements are rarer.

## 6. 📰 Current link

📰 **Keeladi, 2025:** The continuing debate over the Keeladi excavation report and Tamil-Brahmi/urban finds keeps the Sangam-age question alive: how early was literacy and urbanism in Tamilakam, and how should literature be cross-checked with archaeology?

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Use Sangam as a case of **regional state formation**: ecology, warriors, trade and poetic patronage together.
- ⚠️ Explain how archaeology supports and corrects literature: megaliths, Arikamedu, Keeladi and coins give material control.
- ⚠️ Write the Deep South as part of Indian Ocean history, not as a peripheral appendix to north India.
