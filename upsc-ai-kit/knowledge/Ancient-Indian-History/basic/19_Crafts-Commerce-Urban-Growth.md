## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — most flourishing craft-commerce age.**

✅ RS Sharma calls the age of the **Shakas, Kushans, Satavahanas and first Tamil states (200 BC–AD 250)** one of the most flourishing periods for crafts and commerce in ancient India.
- ✅ Cities concentrated non-producing groups, merchants and artisans.
- ✅ Texts and inscriptions refer to many kinds of craftsmen, more than in earlier texts.
- ✅ Trade expanded by land and sea: north-west, Ganga valley, Deccan, western coast and Tamil ports.

**Standard — money, guilds and towns.**

✅ The earlier NBPW phase created the urban base: iron implements, writing, punch-marked coins, fine pottery, burnt bricks, ring wells and towns in north-eastern India.
- ✅ In the post-Mauryan age, Roman and Satavahana coins, craft finds and cave donations show a monetized trading world.
- ✅ Guilds (*shrenis*) could receive deposits to provide robes and necessities for monks, showing trust in corporate bodies.
- ✅ Roman trade brought gold and luxury exchange, especially through western and southern ports.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ 200 BC–AD 250 = craft-commerce boom linked to Shakas, Kushans, Satavahanas and Tamil states.
- ✅ NBPW phase is tied to iron, writing, punch-marked coins, burnt bricks, ring wells and towns.
- ✅ Ujjain exported beads, agate and carnelian after c. 200 BC.
- ✅ Buddhist devotees in 2nd-century Maharashtra deposited money with guilds for monastic needs.
- ✅ Roman trade was strongest with western/southern India; Roman coins are a key archaeological marker.
- ✅ Arikamedu and Tiruchirapalli show dyeing vats in the 1st–3rd centuries AD.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Urban growth did not begin only under the Guptas; a major boom occurred earlier.

- ❌ Guilds were only religious bodies. → They were **economic corporate bodies**; some funded religious donations.
- ❌ NBPW is just ordinary black pottery. → It is a fine deluxe ware linked with wider urban material culture.
- ❌ Roman trade means India imported only. → India exported pepper, textiles, pearls, beads and other goods.
- ❌ Ports mattered only in medieval Chola times. → Early-historic ports were already active.
