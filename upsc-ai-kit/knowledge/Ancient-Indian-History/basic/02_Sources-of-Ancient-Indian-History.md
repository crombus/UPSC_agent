## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — source-types build history (RS Sharma).**

✅ RS Sharma groups evidence into **material remains, coins, inscriptions, literary sources, foreign accounts, village study, natural sciences, historical sense, constructing history and chronology**. Ancient history is not built from one source alone.
- ✅ For pre-Ashokan times, Vedic and post-Vedic texts are used critically, but archaeology remains very important.
- ✅ From Ashokan times, inscriptions provide **solid evidence** for historical reconstruction.
- ✅ Ancient Indians left **innumerable material remains**, including monuments and temples.

**Standard — literary sources must be used critically.**

✅ Texts are not neutral reports. Vedas, epics, Puranas, Buddhist-Jain texts and Sangam literature preserve social memory, norms and regional worlds, but need dating, layering and cross-checking.
- ✅ Coins and inscriptions are usually closer to administration, economy, rulers, religion and language.
- ✅ Foreign accounts are useful but must be checked for outsider bias and limited observation.
- ✅ Linguistic antiquity helps trace language histories, but language cannot be equated mechanically with race.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Source type | Examples | Use | Caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ Literary | Vedas, epics, Puranas, Buddhist-Jain texts, Sangam | Society, religion, norms, memory | Layers, exaggeration, prescriptive tone |
| ✅ Archaeological | settlements, pottery, tools, monuments | Material life, economy, technology | Needs context and dating |
| ✅ Inscriptions | Ashokan edicts, copper-plates, donative records | Direct political/social/economic evidence | Formulaic language, elite voice |
| ✅ Coins | punch-marked, Indo-Greek, Kushana, Gupta, Satavahana | Economy, trade, kingship, iconography | Findspot and chronology matter |
| ✅ Foreign accounts | Greek, Roman, Chinese, Arab notices | External observations | Outsider perspective and selective reporting |
| ✅ Linguistic evidence | Indo-Aryan, Dravidian, Munda traces | Migration/contact/culture clues | Do not reduce language to ethnicity |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ RS Sharma lists **material remains, coins, inscriptions, literary sources and foreign accounts** as core sources.
- ✅ Inscriptions become a strong source from the **Ashokan** period.
- ✅ Archaeology is crucial even where texts exist, especially for pre-Ashokan India.
- ✅ Material remains include monuments and temples, not only tools and pottery.
- ✅ Natural sciences and chronology are part of historical construction.
- ⚠️ Vedic texts are not archaeological evidence; pottery/site evidence must be separately established.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC often asks which source is best for which kind of fact.

- ❌ The Puranas are straightforward political history. → They are layered texts and need critical use.
- ❌ Inscriptions are always unbiased. → They are closer to events but can be royal or donor propaganda.
- ❌ Coins only show economy. → They also show script, symbols, rulers and religious imagery.
- ❌ Foreign accounts are automatically superior. → They carry outsider bias and limited geographic knowledge.
