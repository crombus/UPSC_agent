## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the first Indian urbanisation (RS Sharma).**

✅ The Harappan (Indus) civilization was India's **first urban culture** and a **Bronze Age** civilization, contemporaneous with Mesopotamia and Egypt but far larger in area. It is called *Harappan* after **Harappa**, the first site excavated (1921, in Montgomery district, now Pakistan). It flourished in the Indus and its tributaries and eastward to the Ganga-Yamuna divide; **Mohenjo-daro** (500 ha) is the largest site, followed by **Rakhigarhi** (250 ha), **Harappa** (150 ha) and **Dholavira** (50 ha).
- ✅ **Town planning** is its hallmark: a **grid layout** (streets crossing at right angles), a raised **citadel/acropolis** to the west (rulers) and a lower **residential town** to the east.
- ✅ **Burnt-brick** buildings (rare elsewhere in antiquity) and an **excellent covered drainage system** — no other contemporary civilization matched it.
- ✅ Signature structures: the **Great Bath** at Mohenjo-daro, the **granaries** (Harappa, Mohenjo-daro), and the **dockyard** at **Lothal** (Gujarat).
- ✅ **Agriculture + domestication:** wheat, barley, peas, sesame, mustard; **first people to produce cotton** (Greeks called it *sindon*). Cattle, sheep, goats, buffalo; the **humped bull** is prominent — the **horse is not a characteristic animal**.

**Standard — economy, society, religion, script & decline (RS Sharma).**

✅ Harappans were traders and craftsmen, not a military-imperial people; there is **no clear evidence of temples or a standing army**. Society appears relatively **egalitarian in material terms** but with a ruling elite in the citadel. Their **script is pictographic and undeciphered**, written **boustrophedon** (right-to-left then left-to-right).
- ✅ **Trade:** internal and long-distance; **Mesopotamian texts call it *Meluha***. Intermediate trading stations: **Dilmun (Bahrain)** and **Makan (Makran)**. Trade was by **barter**; standardised **weights in multiples of 16**.
- ✅ **Crafts:** bronze tools, bead-making, seal-cutting; distinctive **red ware painted with black designs**.
- ✅ **Religion:** worship of the **Mother Goddess**, a **male deity (Pashupati / "proto-Shiva" seal)**, **tree and animal worship** (pipal, humped bull), and possibly the **unicorn**; no monumental temples. Phallus (**lingam**) and yoni worship inferred.
- ✅ **Decline (c. 1900 BC):** urban traits disappear; RS Sharma lists multiple causes — **ecological/environmental change, floods, river shifts (Indus/Ghaggar-Hakra), decline of trade, possibly incursions** — no single agreed cause.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ First urbanisation; **Bronze Age**; contemporaneous with Mesopotamia/Egypt but **larger in extent**.
- ✅ Mature phase **c. 2600–1900 BC**; Mohenjo-daro largest, **Rakhigarhi largest Indian site**.
- ✅ Grid town-planning; **citadel (west) + lower town (east)**; burnt bricks; covered drains.
- ✅ Great Bath (Mohenjo-daro), Dockyard (Lothal), Ploughed field & fire altars (Kalibangan), Reservoirs & signboard (Dholavira).
- ✅ **No iron, no horse (characteristic), no temples/army**; first cotton growers; weights in **multiples of 16**.
- ✅ Mesopotamia called it **Meluha**; trading posts Dilmun (Bahrain) & Makan (Makran).
- ✅ Religion: Mother Goddess, Pashupati/proto-Shiva seal, pipal & humped-bull worship; **undeciphered pictographic script**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC repeatedly tests **what the Harappans did NOT have** and exact **site-find pairings**.

- ❌ Harappans used iron. → **No iron** — it is a **Bronze Age** culture; iron appears only in the **later Vedic** period.
- ❌ The horse was a characteristic Harappan animal. → It is **not**; horse remains are rare/debated (Surkotada). The **humped bull** dominates.
- ❌ Harappans built grand temples like Mesopotamia's ziggurats. → **No monumental temples** identified.
- ❌ The Great Bath is at Harappa. → It is at **Mohenjo-daro**; the **dockyard is at Lothal**; the **ploughed field at Kalibangan**.
- ❌ The Harappan script has been deciphered. → It remains **undeciphered**.

## 6. 📰 Current link

📰 **Rakhigarhi (Haryana), 2026:** ASI's 2025–26 dig at **Mound-7 (cemetery)** yielded fresh burials/skeletons sent to the **Anthropological Survey of India** and **BSIP** for **ancient-DNA, isotope and facial-reconstruction** studies — extending the landmark **2019 Rakhigarhi genome** finding that Harappans carried **Iranian-farmer + ancient South-Asian hunter-gatherer** ancestry **without Steppe ancestry**. Keeps the *Harappan–Aryan* debate live.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ "The Harappan civilization was urban and secular in character." — Examine using town-planning, standardisation, absence of temples/army, and craft-trade economy.
- ⚠️ Use Harappan **water management** (Dholavira reservoirs, Lothal dock, Mohenjo-daro drains) as a GS-I/GS-III bridge to contemporary urban-planning and water-security answers.
- ⚠️ Discuss why the **decline** has no single explanation — tie environmental/river-shift causes to lessons on climate-resilience of cities.
