## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — second major north Indian empire.**

✅ The Guptas, with their base in Uttar Pradesh and Bihar, ruled north and western India for about 160 years, until the mid-6th century AD. RS Sharma treats them as the next great imperial formation after the Mauryas, though less centralized.
- ✅ **Chandragupta I** laid the foundation and used the title **maharajadhiraja**.
- ✅ His marriage to a **Lichchhavi princess Kumaradevi** helped legitimize and expand power.
- ✅ The Gupta era begins in **AD 319–20**, associated with Chandragupta I.

**Standard — conquest, prestige and decline.**

✅ **Samudragupta** enlarged the kingdom enormously. Harishena's Allahabad pillar inscription praises his conquests and personality.
- ✅ RS Sharma contrasts Samudragupta with Ashoka: Ashoka adopted peace after Kalinga, while Samudragupta delighted in conquest.
- ✅ **Chandragupta II Vikramaditya** defeated the Shakas of western India, controlled Ujjain and patronized scholars including Kalidasa and Amarasimha.
- ✅ The Chinese pilgrim **Fa-hsien (AD 399–414)** visited India during Chandragupta II's time.
- ✅ Huna invasions from Central Asia and feudatory assertion weakened the empire.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Gupta Empire: **c. AD 319–550** in the master chronology.
- ✅ Chandragupta I issued/was linked to coins commemorating Kumaradevi and the Lichchhavis.
- ✅ Samudragupta's main source is the **Allahabad/Prayag pillar inscription** composed by **Harishena**.
- ✅ Chandragupta II adopted **Vikramaditya** and defeated western Shakas.
- ✅ Fa-hsien visited India in **AD 399–414**.
- ✅ Huna invasions and feudatory assertion contributed to decline; Yashodharman challenged Gupta authority in AD 532.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Gupta political facts are often tested through inscriptions and travellers.

- ❌ Harishena wrote about Chandragupta II's court. → He is linked with **Samudragupta's** prashasti.
- ❌ Fa-hsien visited during Ashoka. → He came during **Chandragupta II's** period.
- ❌ The Guptas had Mauryan-level bureaucracy. → RS Sharma sees their system as less elaborate, with feudal tendencies.
- ❌ Hunas immediately ended the empire in one event. → Decline was gradual, with invasions and regional challenges.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Compare Mauryan and Gupta empires: territorial ambition but different administrative depth and ideology.
- ⚠️ Use the Prayag Prashasti to discuss conquest, diplomacy and limits of eulogistic sources.
- ⚠️ Explain Gupta decline as a combination of Huna pressure, fiscal strain and feudatory assertion rather than one invasion.
