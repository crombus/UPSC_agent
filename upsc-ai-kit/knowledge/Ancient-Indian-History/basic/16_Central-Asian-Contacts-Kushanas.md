## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — contact and mutual impact after the Mauryas.**

✅ RS Sharma states that the period beginning about **200 BC** did not see a large empire like the Mauryas, but it was notable for intimate and widespread contacts between Central Asia and India.
- ✅ In eastern/central India and the Deccan, the Mauryas were followed by native dynasties such as the **Shungas, Kanvas and Satavahanas**.
- ✅ In the north-west, they were followed by dynasties from Central Asia: **Indo-Greeks, Shakas, Parthians and Kushanas**.
- ✅ The Indo-Greeks were the first to issue **gold coins in India**; such coins increased under the Kushanas.
- ✅ Greek contact contributed to Hellenistic features in the north-west, best seen in **Gandhara art**.

**Standard — Shakas, Kushanas and cultural consequences.**

✅ The Shakas controlled a larger part of India than the Greeks and had several branches in Afghanistan, Punjab/Taxila, Mathura, western India and the upper Deccan.
- ✅ The most famous Kushana ruler was **Kanishka**, associated with **Purushapura/Peshawar**, a monastery and a great stupa.
- ✅ Kanishka became a great patron of **Mahayana Buddhism**, in which Buddha images began to be worshipped.
- ✅ Central Asian contact affected cavalry, trade, agriculture, polity, social groups, religion, art and language.
- ✅ The **Gandhara** and **Mathura** schools of art developed under this wider contact zone.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Period begins about **200 BC** after Mauryan decline.
- ✅ **Indo-Greeks** were the first to issue **gold coins in India** (RS Sharma).
- ✅ **Menander/Milinda** is dated **165–145 BC** in RS chronology.
- ✅ **Saka era = AD 78**, started by **Kanishka** in RS Sharma's chronology.
- ✅ **Rudradaman I** belongs to **AD 130–150** in RS chronology.
- ✅ Kanishka patronised **Mahayana Buddhism**.
- ✅ Gandhara and Mathura schools flourished in this contact age.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC loves mixing **Indo-Greek, Shaka and Kushana** facts.

- ❌ Kanishka was an Indo-Greek ruler. → He was a **Kushana** ruler.
- ❌ Menander started the Saka era. → RS Sharma links Saka era **AD 78** with **Kanishka**.
- ❌ Gandhara and Mathura are identical styles. → Gandhara is strongly north-west/Hellenistic-Buddhist; Mathura is more indigenous Indian in idiom.
- ❌ Mahayana began under Ashoka. → It grew prominently in the post-Mauryan/Kushana context; Kanishka patronised it.
- ❌ Shakas ruled only one small area. → RS Sharma lists multiple Shaka branches across regions.

## 6. 📰 Current link

📰 **Saka Samvat, 2025:** Government/PIB material continues to note the Indian National Calendar based on the **Saka era**, with 2025 corresponding to Saka Samvat 1946–47/1947 depending on the date. This keeps a Kushana-linked chronology point alive in current governance and calendar use.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Central Asian contacts were not merely invasions; they reshaped coins, cavalry, trade, religion and art.
- ⚠️ Compare Gandhara and Mathura as two artistic responses to the same Kushana-age environment.
- ⚠️ Use Kanishka to link Silk Route wealth, Mahayana patronage and cosmopolitan coinage.
