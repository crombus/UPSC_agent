## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — why the north-west mattered.**

✅ RS Sharma treats the Iranian and Macedonian invasions as a **north-west frontier episode** that linked India with West and Central Asian politics before the Mauryas. The Achaemenid ruler **Darius** annexed **Punjab and Sindh** in **516 BC**, while Alexander came after conquering Iran.
- ✅ The north-west was politically fragmented into small monarchies and tribal republics, which made it easier for an external conqueror to advance.
- ✅ The **Khyber pass** route became important: Alexander moved from Kabul into India through it.
- ✅ **Ambhi of Taxila** submitted to Alexander; **Porus** offered the strongest resistance on the **Jhelum**.
- ✅ Alexander defeated Porus but restored his kingdom, impressed by his bravery.

**Standard — impact, not long occupation.**

✅ The invasions did not create a long-lived foreign empire in India, but they produced important contacts and consequences.
- ✅ Iranian contact encouraged the spread of **Aramaic-derived Kharoshthi** in the north-west.
- ✅ Alexander founded cities and placed conquered territories under Greek governors, but had little time to consolidate.
- ✅ RS Sharma says Alexander's invasion brought ancient Europe and ancient South Asia into close contact for the first time.
- ✅ By destroying petty states in the north-west, Alexander indirectly **paved the way for Mauryan expansion** in that region.
- ✅ Tradition links Chandragupta Maurya's success partly to knowledge of Alexander's military machine.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Aspect | Iranian/Achaemenid contact | Macedonian/Alexander contact |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Core area | Punjab and Sindh under Darius | North-west up to Jhelum/Hyphasis zone |
| ✅ Route / base | Persian imperial frontier | Kabul → Khyber → Indus |
| ✅ Key ruler | Darius | Alexander |
| ✅ Indian figures | NW satrapal zone | Ambhi of Taxila; Porus of Jhelum-Chenab region |
| ✅ Legacy | Kharoshthi/Aramaic link; administrative contact | Greek settlements, military exposure, route knowledge |
| ⚠️ Duration | Longer imperial contact | Brief campaign; no durable Greek empire in India |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Darius** annexed **Punjab and Sindh** in **516 BC**.
- ✅ Alexander entered India through the **Khyber pass** in **326 BC**.
- ✅ **Ambhi** of Taxila submitted; **Porus** resisted on the **Jhelum/Hydaspes**.
- ✅ Alexander remained in India for about **nineteen months (326–325 BC)**.
- ✅ Greek possessions in India were soon lost to the **Mauryas**.
- ✅ The north-west was fragmented into many monarchies and tribal republics.
- ✅ Iranian contact is linked with **Kharoshthi** in the north-west.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC tests **sequence + impact**, not romantic stories of conquest.

- ❌ Alexander conquered the whole of India. → He campaigned only in the **north-western rim**.
- ❌ Porus defeated Alexander decisively. → RS Sharma records Alexander's victory but also his respect for Porus.
- ❌ Greek rule in India lasted for centuries immediately after Alexander. → His Indian possessions were **soon lost to the Mauryas**.
- ❌ Kharoshthi is a south-Indian script. → It is linked to the **north-west** and Aramaic/Achaemenid influence.
- ❌ Darius and Alexander belong to the same invasion. → Darius was **Achaemenid Iranian**; Alexander was **Macedonian Greek**.
