## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — politics of the peninsula, AD 300–750.**

✅ RS Sharma's core frame is not just dynastic war; it is **new states + rural expansion + Brahmanization**. In the Deccan and far south, land grants, temples and agrarian settlements reshaped society.
- ✅ The main political conflict was between the **Pallavas of Kanchi** and the **Chalukyas of Badami**; the Pandyas were a weaker third participant.
- ✅ **Pulakeshin II (AD 609–642)** is known from Ravikirti's **Aihole inscription**, a Sanskrit eulogy of high poetic quality but also exaggeration.
- ✅ Pulakeshin II checked Harsha and pressed towards Kanchipuram; later **Narasimhavarman I Mahamalla** reversed Pallava fortunes.
- ✅ The Tungabhadra often formed a historic boundary between Deccan and deep-south powers.

**Standard — Brahmanization, grants and temples.**

✅ Land grants to Brahmanas stimulated agrarian expansion under the Pallavas in south Andhra and north Tamil Nadu, but RS Sharma also notes adverse effects on peasants.
- ✅ The **Kalabhra revolt** is linked to revocation of Brahmana land grants and therefore to social-political protest.
- ✅ Pallavas and Chalukyas both championed Brahmanism, performed Vedic sacrifices and made grants to Brahmanas.
- ✅ Pallavas built rock-cut and structural monuments at **Mahabalipuram/Mamallapuram** and Kanchi; Chalukyas built at **Aihole, Badami and Pattadakal**.
- ⚠️ The Bhakti beginnings of Alvars/Nayanars should be linked to temple society, vernacular devotion and changing religious culture.
- ✅ Pallava literary culture included the Sanskrit farce **`Mattavilasa-prahasana`** of Mahendravarman I; early medieval Tamil devotional songs of the **Alvars and Nayanmars** developed in the same broad south-Indian milieu.
- ✅ Pallava inscriptions often used Sanskrit for eulogistic *prashasti* and Tamil for documentary portions.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Pallavas of **Kanchi** and Chalukyas of **Badami** fought for supremacy from the 6th to 8th centuries.
- ✅ Pulakeshin II is known from **Ravikirti's Aihole inscription** and checked Harsha.
- ✅ Narasimhavarman I Mahamalla is linked to Pallava revival and Mamallapuram.
- ✅ Aihole, Badami and Pattadakal are Chalukya; Mahabalipuram and Kanchi are Pallava.
- ✅ Kalabhra revolt is linked to anti-existing-order protest and revocation of Brahmana grants.
- ✅ Land grants aided agrarian expansion but could increase burdens on peasants.
- ✅ Mahendravarman I is associated with `Mattavilasa-prahasana`; Pallava inscriptions used Sanskrit and Tamil for different textual functions.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Site-dynasty pairing and the social meaning of land grants are repeatedly confused.

- ❌ Aihole inscription belongs to the Pallavas. → It is linked to **Pulakeshin II/Chalukyas**.
- ❌ Mahabalipuram is a Chalukya centre. → It is a **Pallava** centre.
- ❌ Land grants were only harmless religious charity. → They also changed rural power and peasant obligations.
- ❌ Kalabhra revolt was only a dynastic episode. → RS Sharma links it to social-political protest around grants.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Pallava-Chalukya history is a bridge between political rivalry and temple-state formation.
- ⚠️ Land grants show how Brahmanization, agrarian expansion and rural hierarchy developed together.
- ⚠️ Peninsular temples were not only religious spaces; they became markers of power, settlement and cultural integration.
