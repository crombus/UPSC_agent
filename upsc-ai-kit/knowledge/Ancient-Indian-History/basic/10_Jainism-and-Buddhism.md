## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — protest against ritualism and social strain.**

✅ RS Sharma says numerous religious sects arose in the mid-Gangetic plains in the **sixth–fifth centuries BC**; as many as **sixty-two** are mentioned. Jainism and Buddhism were the most important and became powerful religious reform movements.
- ✅ Their rise is linked to the social and economic conditions of north-east India, including new towns, trade and questioning of ritualistic Vedic religion.
- ✅ Jainism made a serious attempt to mitigate the evils of the varna order and ritualistic religion.
- ✅ Both traditions used accessible languages and monastic organisation rather than relying on Sanskritic sacrifice.

**Standard — doctrines and institutions.**

✅ Jainism taught five doctrines: non-violence, truth, non-stealing, non-hoarding and celibacy; RS Sharma notes the fifth was added by Mahavira, while the earlier four were linked to previous teachers.
- ✅ Jain liberation can be obtained through **right knowledge, right faith and right action** — the **Triratna**.
- ✅ Buddhism explains suffering through desire and prescribes the **Eightfold Path** for ending misery and attaining nirvana.
- ✅ Buddhism spread across South Asia, South-East Asia and East Asia, but became practically extinct in India by the twelfth century AD.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Sixth–fifth centuries BC saw many sects in the mid-Gangetic plains; Jainism and Buddhism became the most important.
- ✅ Jain five vows: non-violence, truth, non-stealing, non-hoarding, celibacy.
- ✅ Triratna: right knowledge, right faith, right action.
- ✅ Jainism split later into **Shvetambara** and **Digambara** traditions.
- ✅ Buddhism: desire causes sorrow; Eightfold Path leads toward nirvana.
- ✅ Buddhism spread widely outside India but declined in India by the twelfth century AD.
- ✅ **Prelims identity distinction:** *Shakyamuni* and *Tathagata* are epithets of Gautama Buddha; **Nayaputta/Nataputta** refers to Mahavira in Pali tradition.
- ✅ Jain doctrine recognizes four main forms of existence: **deva** (gods), **manushya** (humans), **naraki** (hell beings) and **tiryancha** (animals and plants).
- ✅ Upanishads predate the Puranas and do contain parables/dialogues; do not confuse later Puranic narrative with all early philosophical storytelling.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC mixes founders, doctrines and sects.

- ❌ Mahavira founded ahimsa for the first time. → RS Sharma notes earlier teachers already taught four vows; Mahavira added celibacy.
- ❌ Triratna belongs to Buddhism. → It is a Jain formulation in this context.
- ❌ *Nayaputta* is an epithet of the Buddha. → It is associated with **Mahavira**; Buddha is *Shakyamuni/Tathagata*.
- ❌ Buddhism survived strongly in India everywhere after the 12th century. → RS Sharma says it virtually disappeared from most of India.
- ❌ Jainism and Buddhism were only spiritual movements. → They were also responses to social, economic and ritual conditions.
