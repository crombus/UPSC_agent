## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — pastoral, tribal, north-western.**

✅ RS Sharma presents Rig Vedic society as **primarily pastoral** and semi-nomadic. Cattle were the chief wealth; the word *gau* occurs very frequently in early Rig Vedic portions, and a wealthy man was called *gomat*.
- ✅ Wars were fought largely for cattle, and the *rajan*'s main duty was protection of cattle and people.
- ✅ The geographical focus was the land of the **Seven Rivers (Sapta-Sindhu)** in the north-west.
- ✅ The society was organised around **lineage, clan and tribe**, rather than territorial states.

**Standard — polity, society and religion before the state.**

✅ The Rig Vedic polity was tribal: *jana* was the people/tribe, *rajan* was chief, and assemblies such as **sabha** and **samiti** checked chiefly power.
- ✅ The economy was not yet a tax-based agrarian state economy; gifts, booty and cattle wealth mattered.
- ✅ Important gods included **Indra** (war/rain), **Agni** (sacrificial fire) and **Varuna** (cosmic order).
- ⚠️ The famous **Battle of Ten Kings (Dasharajna)** is treated as a key Rig Vedic conflict involving Sudas and a confederacy of tribes.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Rig Vedic phase: **c. 1500–1000 BC**.
- ✅ Economy was predominantly **pastoral**; cattle were the principal form of wealth.
- ✅ *Rajan* was a tribal chief, not an absolute monarch.
- ✅ **Sabha** and **samiti** were important assemblies.
- ✅ Major gods: **Indra, Agni, Varuna**.
- ⚠️ Battle of Ten Kings is linked to Sudas and the Bharatas in Book 7 of the Rig Veda.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC asks what had **not yet developed** in the Rig Vedic period.

- ❌ Rig Vedic society was fully urban. → It was predominantly pastoral and tribal.
- ❌ The *rajan* was a tax-collecting king with a bureaucracy. → He was a chief supported by gifts/tributes and tribal loyalty.
- ❌ Iron agriculture defines the Rig Vedic age. → Iron becomes important in the later Vedic phase.
- ❌ Varna was already as rigid as in later texts. → Social divisions existed but later hardening belongs to the later Vedic phase.
