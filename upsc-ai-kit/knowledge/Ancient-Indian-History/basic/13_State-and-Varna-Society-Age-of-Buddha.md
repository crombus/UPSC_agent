## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — material base of the second urbanisation.**

✅ RS Sharma reconstructs this age from **Pali texts, Sanskrit Sutra literature and archaeology**. The core change was the rise of a food-producing economy in eastern UP and Bihar, helped by **iron tools in alluvial areas**.
- ✅ Archaeologically, the **5th century BC** marks the beginning of the **Northern Black Polished Ware (NBPW)** phase in the Gangetic plains.
- ✅ Towns such as **Rajgir, Pataliputra, Champa, Vaishali and Chirand** belonged to this urbanising zone.
- ✅ Many artisans and traders used coins for the first time; earliest coins were generally **punch-marked**.
- ✅ Pali texts give early definite coin terms—**kahapana, nikkha, kamsa, pada, masaka and kakanika**—corroborated by archaeological finds of mostly silver punch-marked coins.
- ✅ Agriculture generated a surplus from which rulers could collect taxes.

**Standard — state, army, taxation and varna.**

✅ Larger states became possible because rulers could collect regular taxes and tributes from peasants and maintain professional armies.
- ✅ RS Sharma links the growth of the state with a more defined **varna order**, where duties of each varna were increasingly specified.
- ✅ The age saw **large states called mahajanapadas**, mostly north of the Vindhyas.
- ✅ **Magadha, Kosala, Vatsa and Avanti** were especially powerful.
- ✅ Republics/ganasanghas survived, but monarchies with taxation and armies became more effective in territorial expansion.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ The **NBPW phase** begins around the **5th century BC** in the Gangetic plains.
- ✅ The age is reconstructed from **Pali texts, Sanskrit Sutras and archaeology**.
- ✅ **Iron tools** in alluvial areas helped agricultural expansion.
- ✅ Earliest coins were generally **punch-marked** and linked to towns/trade.
- ✅ High-yield Pali coin terms: **kahapana, nikkha, kamsa, pada, masaka, kakanika**.
- ✅ **Magadha, Kosala, Vatsa and Avanti** were major mahajanapadas.
- ✅ Regular taxation made professional armies possible.
- ✅ Republics/ganasanghas existed alongside monarchies.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC often mixes **Vedic society**, **Buddha-age society** and **Mauryan empire** in one statement.

- ❌ The age of Buddha was purely pastoral. → It was marked by agriculture, towns, coins and surplus.
- ❌ NBPW belongs to the Harappan period. → It marks the early historic Gangetic urban phase.
- ❌ Republics meant modern democracy. → They were oligarchic/kin-based gana-sanghas, not universal adult democracy.
- ❌ Varna disappeared because Buddhism rose. → Varna norms hardened even as heterodox movements criticised ritual hierarchy.
- ❌ Standing armies were impossible before the Mauryas. → Large states before the Mauryas already maintained armies through taxes.
