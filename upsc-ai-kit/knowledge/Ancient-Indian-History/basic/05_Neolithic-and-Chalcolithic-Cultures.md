## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Neolithic means food production.**

✅ The Neolithic marks the move toward **farming, cattle-keeping, pottery and polished stone tools**, but it appeared at different times in different regions. Key UPSC sites include Mehrgarh, Burzahom, Chirand and Koldihwa.
- ✅ Mehrgarh is the earliest farming horizon in the north-west tradition.
- ✅ Burzahom is famous for Kashmir Neolithic features such as pit dwellings and bone/stone tools.
- ✅ Chirand and Koldihwa are important for eastern/northern Neolithic and rice-related questions.
- ✅ **Lahuradewa** in the middle Ganga valley is important in Upinder Singh's updated account of early rice-based agriculture; at Koldihwa/Mahagara the dates and the identification of wild versus domesticated rice require caution.
- ✅ South Indian Neolithic evidence includes cattle pastoralism, polished stone axes and distinctive **ash-mound** sites; this regional trajectory was not a copy of Mehrgarh.

**Standard — Chalcolithic means copper + stone, mostly rural.**

✅ RS Sharma defines Chalcolithic as cultures based on **stone and copper implements**. These settlements are found in south-eastern Rajasthan, western MP, western Maharashtra and eastern/southern India.
- ✅ Ahar and Gilund lie in the dry Banas valley of south-eastern Rajasthan.
- ✅ Kayatha and Eran are excavated sites in western MP/Malwa.
- ✅ Malwa culture is associated with Navdatoli, Eran and Nagda; Jorwe culture belongs to Maharashtra.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Chalcolithic = **copper + stone** tools.
- ✅ Chalcolithic settlements occur in SE Rajasthan, western MP, western Maharashtra and eastern/southern India.
- ✅ Ahar and Gilund are in the **Banas valley**.
- ✅ Kayatha culture: **2000–1500 BC**; OCP: **2000–1500 BC**.
- ✅ Malwa culture: **1700–1200 BC**, found at Navdatoli, Eran and Nagda.
- ✅ Jorwe culture: **1400–700 BC**, associated with Maharashtra.
- ⚠️ Rice evidence is site-specific: a rice remain or impression does not by itself settle whether the variety was wild or domesticated.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not confuse Neolithic, Chalcolithic and Harappan markers.

- ❌ Chalcolithic people used iron. → Chalcolithic means copper + stone, not iron.
- ❌ All Neolithic sites began at the same date. → Neolithic chronology is regionally varied.
- ❌ Ahar is a Harappan city. → It is a Chalcolithic culture/site in the Banas valley.
- ❌ Jorwe belongs to the Ganga plains. → It is associated with Maharashtra.

## 6. 📰 Current link

⚠️ **Current-link discipline:** Do not cite a “new Mehrgarh date” without the published study. The safe linkage is that radiocarbon calibration and archaeobotany can revise the chronology and character of early food production.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Explain Neolithic as a regional transition to food production, not a uniform revolution.
- ⚠️ Discuss Chalcolithic cultures as rural, regional and resource-linked rather than urban like Harappa.
- ⚠️ Use Ahar-Malwa-Jorwe to show cultural diversity before iron-age state formation.
