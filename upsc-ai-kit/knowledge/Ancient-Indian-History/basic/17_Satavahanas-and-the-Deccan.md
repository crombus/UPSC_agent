## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Deccan bridge between north and south.**

✅ The Satavahanas were the first major ruling family to turn the **Deccan** into a durable political zone after the Mauryas. RS Sharma treats them as crucial because they connected Maharashtra, Andhra, Karnataka and the western coast with northern trade, Buddhism and Brahmanical ideology.
- ✅ Their greatest competitors were the **Shakas** of western India and the upper Deccan.
- ✅ **Gautamiputra Satakarni** restored the dynasty after Shaka pressure and claimed to have destroyed the **Kshaharata** lineage of Nahapana.
- ✅ More than **8,000 silver coins of Nahapana** found near Nasik were restruck by the Satavahana king, supporting the claim of victory.

**Standard — society, language, grants and monuments.**

✅ The Satavahanas claimed Brahmana status even though RS Sharma sees them as originally a local Deccan tribe that became Brahmanized.
- ✅ They were among the **first rulers to make land grants to Brahmanas**, while more numerous grants went to Buddhist monks.
- ✅ Official inscriptions used **Prakrit** and **Brahmi**; the *Gathasaptasati/Gathasattasai* is attributed to King Hala, though later retouching is likely.
- ✅ Buddhism flourished at **Nasik, Junnar, Nagarjunakonda and Amaravati**, with trader and artisan support.
- ✅ Iron ploughshares, **paddy transplantation**, cotton production and trade strengthened the Deccan agrarian base; the Krishna-Godavari delta became a major rice-producing zone.
- ✅ Matronymic names such as **Gautamiputra** and **Vashishthiputra** indicate maternal/gotra affiliation, not matriarchy; dynastic succession remained patrilineal.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Satavahanas: c. 1st c. BC–mid-3rd c. AD; Deccan-based polity.
- ✅ Gautamiputra Satakarni defeated Shakas/Kshaharatas and restruck Nahapana's silver coins.
- ✅ District units were called **ahara**; officials included **amatyas** and **mahamatras**.
- ✅ Satavahanas used **Prakrit** in inscriptions written in **Brahmi**.
- ✅ They began early **land grants to Brahmanas**, but Buddhist monks also received grants.
- ✅ **Karle/Nasik/Junnar** represent western Deccan rock-cut Buddhist activity; **Amaravati** became a major Buddhist centre.
- ✅ Hala is associated with the Prakrit *Gathasaptasati*.
- ✅ Matronymics do not equal matrilineal succession; paddy transplantation is a high-yield Deccan agrarian marker.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: UPSC mixes Satavahanas with Shakas, Kushanas and Guptas because all are early-historic coin/trade powers.

- ❌ Satavahanas were a north-Indian Ganga dynasty. → They were a **Deccan** power.
- ❌ Gautamiputra Satakarni was defeated by Nahapana. → RS Sharma says Gautamiputra **restored Satavahana power** and overstruck Nahapana's coins.
- ❌ Satavahana official language was Sanskrit. → Their inscriptions were in **Prakrit**, written in Brahmi.
- ❌ Land grants begin only in the Gupta age. → Satavahanas show an **early beginning**, though grants expand later.
