## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — why "classical/golden age" is used.**

✅ RS Sharma notes that Gupta age is famous for Sanskrit literature, art, religion and science. But he is also critical: the period was not golden for everyone, especially subordinate groups and peasants under growing land grants.
- ✅ Sanskrit was the court language of the Guptas.
- ✅ Kalidasa's *Abhijnanashakuntalam* made the period famous; Bhasa also wrote plays.
- ✅ Aryabhata's *Aryabhatiya* belongs to the 5th century and shows advanced mathematics/astronomy.

**Standard — administration, economy and society.**

✅ The Guptas did not maintain Mauryan-style elaborate bureaucracy. Local administration involved leading scribes, merchants, artisans and landed classes.
- ✅ Land grants expanded agriculture but also created new rights in land and strengthened Brahmana supremacy.
- ✅ Peasants could be subjected to forced labour; virgin soil was brought under cultivation with Brahmana beneficiaries in tribal areas.
- ✅ Bhagavatism/Vaishnavism gained royal prominence, while Buddhism and Jainism continued.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Sanskrit was the Gupta court language; secular literature flourished.
- ✅ Kalidasa wrote *Abhijnanashakuntalam*; Bhasa wrote thirteen plays.
- ✅ Gupta architecture includes brick temples at **Bhitargaon** and **Bhitari**, and the **Deogarh** stone temple.
- ✅ Nalanda's earliest brick structure relates to the Gupta period.
- ✅ Aryabhata wrote *Aryabhatiya* in the 5th century; decimal system evidence appears by AD 448.
- ✅ The Gupta period was culturally brilliant but socially unequal; do not write an uncritical "golden age" answer.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: "Golden age" is a label, not a complete social description.

- ❌ Gupta age was golden for all classes. → RS Sharma stresses hierarchy, land grants and peasant burdens.
- ❌ Gupta architecture was massive like later temple complexes. → RS Sharma says the period was architecturally modest, with a few early temples.
- ❌ Sanskrit literature began under Guptas. → Sanskrit is older; the Gupta age is a major **courtly flowering**.
- ❌ Decimal system begins only in medieval India. → Gupta evidence suggests it was known by the early 5th century AD.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Critically evaluate the Gupta age as "classical": high culture and science flourished, but social inequalities deepened.
- ⚠️ Use land grants to connect economy, administration, Brahmanization and early feudal tendencies.
- ⚠️ Discuss Sanskritization and Bhagavatism as tools of cultural integration and royal legitimacy.
