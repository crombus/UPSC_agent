## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — tool stages are the backbone.**

✅ RS Sharma places the Indian Palaeolithic in Lower, Middle and Upper phases, followed by the Mesolithic. The shift is mainly visible through **tool technology**, from large core tools to flakes and blades, then tiny **microliths**.
- ✅ Lower Palaeolithic is dated **600,000–150,000 BC** in Sharma's chronology.
- ✅ Middle Palaeolithic is dated **150,000–35,000 BC**.
- ✅ Upper Palaeolithic is dated **35,000–10,000 BC**.

**Standard — Mesolithic is a transition, not farming yet.**

✅ The Mesolithic (9000–4000 BC) used microliths and shows hunting, gathering, rock art and early steps toward animal domestication. It bridges Palaeolithic foraging and later Neolithic food production.
- ✅ Adamgarh (MP) and Bagor (Rajasthan) provide early evidence for domestication of animals around **5000 BC**.
- ✅ Sambhar deposits suggest plant cultivation around **7000–6000 BC** in Rajasthan.
- ✅ Bhimbetka is a key rock-art and prehistoric occupation site.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Lower Palaeolithic: **600,000–150,000 BC**; Middle: **150,000–35,000 BC**; Upper: **35,000–10,000 BC**.
- ✅ Mesolithic culture in India: **9000–4000 BC**.
- ✅ Mesolithic tools are **microliths**.
- ✅ Adamgarh and Bagor provide early domestication evidence around **5000 BC**.
- ✅ Plant cultivation around **7000–6000 BC** is suggested in Rajasthan from Sambhar deposits.
- ✅ Bhimbetka is high-yield for rock art and prehistoric occupation.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Stone Age questions test chronology + tool association.

- ❌ Mesolithic = polished stone farming culture. → Polished tools and settled farming are mainly Neolithic markers.
- ❌ Microliths are Lower Palaeolithic tools. → They are Mesolithic markers.
- ❌ Domestication began only after 3000 BC in India. → RS Sharma notes evidence around 5000 BC at Adamgarh/Bagor.
- ❌ Bhimbetka is only a historical-period art site. → It is central to prehistoric rock art and occupation.

## 6. 📰 Current link
