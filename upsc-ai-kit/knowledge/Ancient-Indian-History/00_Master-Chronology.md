## 1. Prehistory & Protohistory (Stone → Bronze Age)

| Era | Approx date (RS Sharma) | Defining feature | Marker sites / evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ Lower Palaeolithic | c. 600,000–150,000 BC in R.S. Sharma's chronology | Hand-axes, cleavers; regional Acheulian sequences | Bori, Soan valley, Hunsgi-Isampur, Attirampakkam |
| ✅ Middle Palaeolithic | c. 150,000–35,000 BC in R.S. Sharma; much earlier transition at Attirampakkam in newer research | Flake tools; prepared-core/Levallois techniques | Narmada basin, Didwana, Attirampakkam |
| ✅ Upper Palaeolithic | c. 35,000–10,000 BC | Blades, burins; art and symbolic evidence | Bhimbetka, Kurnool caves |
| ✅ Mesolithic | c. 9000 – 4000 BC | Microliths; first domestication, rock paintings | Bagor, Bhimbetka, Adamgarh |
| ✅ Neolithic | c. 7000 – 1000 BC (regionally varied) | Farming, pottery, polished tools, cattle | Mehrgarh (earliest), Burzahom, Chirand, Koldihwa |
| ✅ Chalcolithic | c. 2000 – 700 BC | Copper + stone; painted pottery; rural | Ahar, Malwa, Jorwe, Kayatha |
| ✅ Harappan (Bronze Age) | **c. 2600 – 1900 BC** (Mature) | First urbanisation, script, town planning, bronze | Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Dholavira, Rakhigarhi, Lothal |

## 2. Early Historic — Vedic to Mahajanapadas

| Era | Approx date | Defining feature | Key marker |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ Rig Vedic (Early Vedic) | c. 1500 – 1000 BC | Pastoral, tribal (*jana*), PGW absent, no iron | Sapta-Sindhu; Rig Veda |
| ✅ Later Vedic | c. 1000 – 600 BC | Iron (PGW), agriculture, *janapada*, varna hardens | Ganga-Yamuna doab; Kuru-Panchala |
| ✅ Second Urbanisation / Mahajanapadas | c. 600 – 321 BC | 16 Mahajanapadas, NBPW, coins, cities, heterodoxy | Magadha rises; Jainism & Buddhism (c. 6th–5th c. BC) |
| ✅ Iranian invasion (Achaemenid) | c. 516 BC (Darius) | NW India into Persian empire | Aramaic/Kharoshthi influence |
| ✅ Macedonian invasion (Alexander) | 326 BC (Battle of Hydaspes vs Porus) | Opened land route; exposed small NW states | Left 326 BC |

## 3. Empires & the "Classical" Age

| Dynasty / phase | Dates (RS Sharma) | Founder / apex ruler | One-line significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Mauryas** | **c. 324–187 BC** | Chandragupta Maurya; Ashoka (reign c. 273–232 BC in R.S. Sharma; consecration chronology differs) | First virtually subcontinental empire; Kalinga in the 9th regnal year → Dhamma policy |
| ✅ Shungas | c. 187–75 BC | Pushyamitra Shunga | Post-Mauryan regional rule; Bharhut/Sanchi activity; “Brahmanical reaction” is debated |
| ✅ Indo-Greeks | c. 2nd c. BC | Menander (Milinda) | Attributable bilingual coinage; first gold coins in R.S. Sharma's formulation; Heliodorus pillar |
| ✅ Shakas / Western Kshatrapas | c. 1st c. BC–4th c. AD (regional branches) | Rudradaman I (Junagadh inscription) | Shaka era begins in AD 78; its founder is debated |
| ✅ **Kushanas** | c. 1st–3rd c. AD | **Kanishka I (c. AD 127–150 in Upinder Singh; older view AD 78)** | Silk Routes; plural coin iconography; Gandhara & Mathura art; Kashmir council tradition |
| ✅ **Satavahanas** | c. 1st c. BC–mid-3rd c. AD | Simuka; apex Gautamiputra Satakarni | Deccan state formation; early fiscal land-grant vocabulary; Prakrit |
| ✅ Sangam Age (Deep South) | c. 300 BC – 300 AD | Cheras, Cholas, Pandyas | Tamil Sangam literature; Indo-Roman trade (Muziris) |

## 4. Gupta & Post-Gupta (early medieval threshold)

| Dynasty / phase | Dates (RS Sharma) | Apex ruler | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Guptas** | **c. AD 319–550** | Samudragupta, Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) | Sanskrit court culture, early temples, land grants, major mathematical-astronomical work |
| ✅ Hunas / decline | c. AD 500 – 550 | Toramana, Mihirakula | Central-Asian raids weaken Guptas |
| ✅ **Harsha** | AD 606–647 | Harshavardhana (Pushyabhuti) | North-Indian regional hegemony centred on Kannauj; Bana and Xuanzang; checked in the Deccan |
| ✅ Deccan/South (post-Gupta) | c. AD 550 – 750 | Chalukyas of Badami vs Pallavas | Pallava-Chalukya conflict; temple architecture blooms |
| ⚠️ Transition to early medieval | c. AD 750 – | Palas, Pratiharas, Rashtrakutas (Tripartite) | Land grants, feudal tendencies, regional kingdoms |

## 5. Memory ladder (one line per era — revise in 60 seconds)

- ⚠️ **Stone → Bronze:** Palaeolithic hunt → Mesolithic tame → Neolithic farm → Chalcolithic copper-villages → **Harappan cities (2600–1900 BC)**.
- ⚠️ **Vedic:** Rig Vedic *pastoral tribes* (1500–1000 BC) → Later Vedic *iron + kingdoms* (1000–600 BC).
- ⚠️ **6th c. BC:** cities return (2nd urbanisation) + **Jainism & Buddhism** + **Magadha** rises.
- ⚠️ **Empire I:** **Mauryas (324–187 BC)** — Ashoka & Dhamma; then regional successor states including the Shungas.
- ⚠️ **Post-Maurya:** Indo-Greeks/Shakas/**Kushanas** (north) + **Satavahanas** (Deccan) + **Sangam** (south) — interaction, trade, coinage, art and regional state formation (c. 200 BC–AD 300).
- ⚠️ **Empire II:** **Guptas (319–550 AD)** — classical culture; then **Harsha (606–647)**.
- ⚠️ **Threshold:** post-Gupta land-grants & regional kingdoms → early medieval.

> 🔑 **Trap:** Harappan **Mature phase = 2600–1900 BC**, NOT contemporaneous with the Rig Veda (1500–1000 BC). The two only *overlap* in the *late/post-Harappan* window — RS Sharma treats the "Was the Harappan culture Vedic?" question as **unsettled**, and the notes must not assert identity.

> 🔑 **Source-conflict trap:** R.S. Sharma follows the older association of Kanishka with AD 78. Upinder Singh's 2nd edition states that most scholars now date Kanishka's accession to **c. AD 127** and treat the Kushana and Shaka eras as distinct.
