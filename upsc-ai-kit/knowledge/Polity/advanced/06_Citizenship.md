## 1. Citizen vs alien
✅ India has **citizens** (full members, all civil + political rights, owe allegiance) and **aliens**
(citizens of another state). Aliens = **friendly** (cordial-relation countries) or **enemy** (country
at war with India — get fewer rights, e.g. **no Art 22** protection against arrest/detention).

✅ **Rights available ONLY to citizens** (denied to aliens): Art **15** (non-discrimination), Art **16**
(equality in public employment), Art **19** (six freedoms), Art **29 & 30** (cultural/educational),
right to **vote** (LS + state assembly), right to **contest** Parliament/state legislature, eligibility
for **high offices** (President, VP, SC/HC judges, Governor, AG, Advocate General). ✅ In India **both
birth & naturalised** citizens can be President (in USA, only birth citizen).

## 2. Constitutional provisions (Art 5–11, Part II)
✅ Constitution has **neither permanent nor elaborate** citizenship provisions — it only identifies who
were citizens **at commencement (26 Jan 1950)**. It leaves acquisition/loss thereafter to **Parliament**
→ **Citizenship Act 1955**.

### Four categories who became citizens on 26 Jan 1950
✅ (a) **domiciled** persons (born in India / a parent born in India / ordinarily resident 5 yrs);
(b) persons who **migrated from Pakistan**; (c) persons who **migrated to Pakistan but returned** for
resettlement; (d) **persons of Indian origin residing outside India** (registered by Indian consulate).
✅ **Art 9:** voluntarily acquiring a **foreign citizenship** = **not** an Indian citizen (no dual
citizenship). ✅ Art 10 (continuance), Art 11 (Parliament's power over all citizenship matters).

## 3. Citizenship Act 1955 — Acquisition (5 ways) ⭐
| Mode | Key rule |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Birth** | Born in India **26 Jan 1950–1 Jul 1987**: citizen *irrespective of parents*. **On/after 1 Jul 1987**: if **either parent** is a citizen. **On/after 3 Dec 2004**: **both parents** citizens, OR one citizen & other **not an illegal migrant**. (Children of foreign diplomats & enemy aliens excluded.) |
| ✅ **Descent** | Born outside India, **before 10 Dec 1992**: if **father** was a citizen. **On/after 10 Dec 1992**: if **either parent**. **From 3 Dec 2004**: birth must be **registered at Indian consulate within 1 year**. |
| ✅ **Registration** | For PIOs / spouse of citizen (**7 yrs** ordinary residence), minor children, OCI-cardholder of 5 yrs, etc. Oath of allegiance required. |
| ✅ **Naturalisation** | For an alien (not illegal migrant): **12 months** continuous residence just before application + **11 of the preceding 14 years**; good character; knows an **8th-Schedule language**. Govt may **waive** all conditions for **distinguished service** to science/art/peace etc. |
| ✅ **Incorporation of territory** | Govt specifies citizens of newly-added territory (e.g. **Citizenship (Pondicherry) Order 1962**). |

✅ **Assam Accord special provision (Citizenship Amendment Act 1985):** persons who came from
Bangladesh to Assam **before 1 Jan 1966** → deemed citizens from that date; those **1 Jan 1966–24 Mar
1971** register & become citizens after **10 yrs** from detection (no vote in interim). ⭐ (Basis of the
**NRC** cut-off of **24 March 1971**.)

## 4. Loss of citizenship (3 ways) ⭐
✅ **Renunciation** (voluntary declaration; minor children also lose it, may resume at 18); **Termination**
(automatic on voluntarily acquiring foreign citizenship); **Deprivation** (compulsory by Centre — fraud,
disloyalty, trading with enemy in war, 2-yr imprisonment within 5 yrs of registration/naturalisation,
or 7 yrs continuous residence abroad).

## 5. Single citizenship ⭐
✅ Despite a federal dual polity, India (like **Canada**) has **single citizenship** — no separate state
citizenship; all citizens owe allegiance only to the Union. USA & Switzerland have **double citizenship**.
Aim: fraternity, unity, integrated nation. **Exceptions to uniform rights:** (1) Art 16 residence
qualification for certain state jobs (Public Employment Act 1957, now only AP & Telangana); (2) states
may give residents concessions outside constitutional rights (e.g. education fees); (3) Art 19
restricted to protect **STs / tribal areas**; (4) *till 2019* J&K permanent-resident privileges under
**Art 35A** (inserted by 1954 Presidential Order under Art 370; **abolished 2019**).

## 6. Overseas Citizenship of India (OCI) ⭐
✅ **L.M. Singhvi** High-Level Committee on Indian Diaspora (2000, report 2002) → **Citizenship (Amdt)
Act 2003** created **OCI** for PIOs of 16 countries (except Pakistan & Bangladesh) and repealed
Commonwealth citizenship. **2005 Amdt** widened it to PIOs of all countries (except Pak/Bangladesh).
**2015 Amdt** merged **PIO + OCI** into a single **"OCI Cardholder"** scheme.
✅ **OCI is NOT dual citizenship** (Art 9 forbids it). ✅ OCI cardholders **cannot**: vote, become
President/VP/SC-HC judge, become MP/MLA, get public employment, or equality of opportunity in public
jobs. Registration cancellable for fraud/disaffection/trading with enemy.

## 📰 CA anchor — Citizenship (Amendment) Act, 2019 ⭐⭐
📰 ✅ **CAA 2019** offers a **facilitated application route** for **six communities — Hindu, Sikh, Buddhist,
Jain, Parsi, Christian** — from **Pakistan, Bangladesh, Afghanistan** who entered India **on or before
31 December 2014** (naturalisation residence cut to **5 years**). Muslims and other countries excluded —
hence the equality/secularism (Art 14) debate.
📰 **Rules notified March 2024**; **first citizenship certificates granted May 2024**
(online portal). The Act does **not grant automatic citizenship** merely by community/origin;
the applicant must satisfy the statutory route and scrutiny.
⚠️ Constitutional challenge pending in Supreme Court (Art 14 discrimination + linkage with NRC fears).

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ Constitution has detailed permanent citizenship provisions → only **who were citizens in 1950**; rest is by **Parliament (Citizenship Act 1955)**.
- ❌ India allows dual citizenship via OCI → **Art 9 forbids dual citizenship**; OCI ≠ citizenship.
- ❌ Anyone born in India is automatically a citizen → true only **1950–1987**; tightened in 1987 & 2004.
- ❌ Naturalisation needs 5 years → **11 of preceding 14 years + 12 months** continuous.
- ❌ Citizenship is a Fundamental Right → it is **not**; governed by ordinary law (Art 11).
- ❌ CAA grants citizenship to all migrants → only **6 named communities** from **3 countries**, cut-off **31 Dec 2014**.
- ❌ NRC cut-off is 2014 → **Assam NRC** cut-off is **24 March 1971** (Assam Accord).
