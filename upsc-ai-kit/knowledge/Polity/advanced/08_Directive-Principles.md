## 0. Source, meaning & features
✅ DPSP are in **Part IV, Art 36–51**. Borrowed from the **Irish Constitution of 1937** (Ireland copied
from **Spain**). Ambedkar: "**novel features**." DPSP + FR = philosophy & **soul** of the Constitution;
Granville Austin called them the "**Conscience of the Constitution**."

**Features (5):**
1. ✅ Ideals the State keeps in mind while framing policy/law. **Art 36:** "State" = same meaning as
   Part III (legislative + executive organs of Centre & states, local & public authorities).
2. ✅ Resemble the "**Instrument of Instructions**" in the **Govt of India Act, 1935** (Ambedkar) —
   only difference: now addressed to legislature & executive.
3. ✅ Comprehensive economic-social-political programme → embody a **welfare state**, not a police state.
4. ✅ **Non-justiciable** — not enforceable by courts (**Art 37**), yet **"fundamental in governance"**
   and the State's **duty** to apply in making laws.
5. ✅ Help courts judge constitutional validity — a law giving effect to a DPSP may be deemed
   "reasonable" under **Art 14/19** and saved from unconstitutionality.

## 1. Classification (book: 3 categories — Constitution itself gives none)

### 🟥 Socialistic Principles (welfare/social-economic justice)
| Art | Directive |
|---|---|
| ✅ 38 | Welfare of people via a just social order; **minimise inequalities** in income, status, facilities, opportunities |
| ✅ 39 | (a) adequate livelihood; (b) equitable distribution of material resources; (c) prevent wealth concentration; (d) **equal pay for equal work** (men & women); (e) protect health of workers & children; (f) healthy development of children |
| ✅ 39A | Equal justice & **free legal aid** to the poor |
| ✅ 41 | Right to work, education, public assistance (unemployment, old age, sickness, disablement) |
| ✅ 42 | Just & humane work conditions + **maternity relief** |
| ✅ 43 | **Living wage**, decent standard of life, social/cultural opportunities for workers |
| ✅ 43A | **Workers' participation** in management of industries |
| ✅ 47 | Raise nutrition, standard of living & **public health** |

### 🟩 Gandhian Principles (reconstruction programme)
| Art | Directive |
|---|---|
| ✅ 40 | Organise **village panchayats** as units of self-government |
| ✅ 43 | Promote **cottage industries** (individual/co-operative) in rural areas |
| ✅ 43B | Promote **co-operative societies** (voluntary, autonomous, democratic, professionally managed) |
| ✅ 46 | Promote educational & economic interests of **SCs, STs & weaker sections** |
| ✅ 47 | **Prohibit intoxicating drinks & drugs** injurious to health |
| ✅ 48 | Prohibit slaughter of **cows, calves, milch & draught cattle**; improve breeds |

### 🟦 Liberal-Intellectual Principles
| Art | Directive |
|---|---|
| ✅ 44 | **Uniform Civil Code** throughout India |
| ✅ 45 | Early-childhood care & education for children **until age 6** (post-86th Amdt) |
| ✅ 48 | Organise agriculture & animal husbandry on modern scientific lines |
| ✅ 48A | **Protect environment**, forests & wildlife |
| ✅ 49 | Protect **monuments/places/objects** of national importance |
| ✅ 50 | **Separate judiciary from executive** |
| ✅ 51 | Promote **international peace & security**; respect international law & treaties; encourage **arbitration** of disputes |

> 🔑 Trap-buster: **Art 39A (legal aid), 43A (workers in management), 48A (environment)** are
> **socialistic/liberal but were ADDED by 42nd Amdt** — don't confuse "new" with a separate category.

## 3. Sanction behind DPSP
✅ **Sir B.N. Rau** recommended splitting rights into justiciable (→ Part III) & non-justiciable
(→ Part IV); Drafting Committee accepted. Real force = **political / public opinion** (Ambedkar:
electorate will hold govt to account). Made non-justiciable because: (1) insufficient financial
resources; (2) vast diversity & backwardness; (3) new State needed freedom to decide order/time/mode.

## 6. FR vs DPSP conflict — the case-law spine ⭐ (highest yield)
| Case / Amendment | Year | Ruling |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Champakam Dorairajan** | 1951 | **FR prevail**; DPSP subsidiary to FR; but FR amendable → led to 1st, 4th, 17th Amdts |
| ✅ **Golaknath** | 1967 | Parliament **cannot** abridge FR (FR "sacrosanct") → FR can't be amended even for DPSP |
| ✅ **24th & 25th Amdt** | 1971 | 24th: Parliament **can** amend FR. 25th: inserted **Art 31C** — (1) laws implementing **Art 39(b)&(c)** immune from Art 14/19/31 challenge; (2) such laws non-questionable in court |
| ✅ **Kesavananda Bharati** | 1973 | Struck down **2nd provision** of 31C (judicial review = basic feature); **1st provision valid** |
| ✅ **42nd Amdt** | 1976 | Extended 31C to **ALL** DPSP (not just 39 b,c) → primacy of DPSP over Art 14,19,31 |
| ✅ **Minerva Mills** | 1980 | Struck down that 42nd-Amdt extension → DPSP again subordinate to FR; **but Art 39(b)&(c) still override Art 14 & 19**. Famous "**two wheels of a chariot**," balance = **basic structure** |

✅ **Present position:** FR enjoy **supremacy** over DPSP, **but** Parliament can amend FR to implement
DPSP **so long as basic structure isn't damaged**. Art 31 (property) abolished by **44th Amdt 1978**.

### FR vs DPSP — distinction (Table 8.1)
| Fundamental Rights | Directive Principles |
|---|---|
| Negative (prohibit State) | Positive (require State action) |
| Justiciable | Non-justiciable |
| **Political** democracy | **Social & economic** democracy |
| Legal sanctions | Moral & political sanctions |
| Auto-enforced (no legislation) | Need legislation to implement |
| Court declares violating law void | Court can't void, but can **uphold** a law made to give effect to a directive |

## 7. Implementation (schemes/laws — Mains fodder)
✅ Planning Commission 1950 → **NITI Aayog 2015**; land reforms (abolition of intermediaries,
tenancy reform, ceilings, surplus redistribution, co-operative farming); labour laws (Minimum Wages
1948, Payment of Wages 1936, Bonus 1965, Contract Labour 1970, Child Labour 1986→renamed 2016,
Bonded Labour 1976, Factories 1948, ID Act 1947); Maternity Benefit 1961 & Equal Remuneration 1976;
nationalisation (LIC 1956, 14 banks 1969, general insurance 1971, Privy Purses abolished 1971);
**Legal Services Authorities Act 1987** (Lok Adalat — statutory, civil-court status, binding awards).

## 8. Directives outside Part IV (non-justiciable but equally weighted)
✅ **Art 335** (Part XVI) — claims of **SC/ST to services** (consistent with efficiency of admin);
✅ **Art 350-A** (Part XVII) — **mother-tongue instruction** at primary stage for linguistic minorities;
✅ **Art 351** (Part XVII) — Union's duty to **develop & spread Hindi**.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ DPSP borrowed from USA → **Ireland** (Ireland ← Spain).
- ❌ DPSP are justiciable → **non-justiciable** (Art 37).
- ❌ Champakam gave DPSP primacy → gave **FR** primacy.
- ❌ Minerva Mills struck down all of Art 31C → only the **42nd-Amdt extension**; 39(b)&(c) protection stands.
- ❌ Art 44 (UCC) is Gandhian → **liberal-intellectual**; Art 40 (panchayats) is Gandhian.
- ❌ Free legal aid (39A) & environment (48A) were original → **added by 42nd Amdt**.
- ❌ Prohibition of cow slaughter is a FR → it's a **DPSP** (Art 48).
