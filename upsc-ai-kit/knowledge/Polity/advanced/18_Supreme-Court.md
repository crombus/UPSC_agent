# PART A — ORGANISATION
✅ India has an **integrated (single) judiciary** — SC at top, HCs below, subordinate courts under them —
adopted from **GoI Act 1935**; enforces **both** Central & state laws (unlike the USA's double system). SC
**inaugurated 28 Jan 1950**, succeeded the **Federal Court (1937)** and replaced the **British Privy Council**
as the highest court of appeal. **Articles 124–147 (Part V).**

## Composition ⭐
✅ Present strength = **34 (1 CJI + 33 judges)** — raised from 31 by the **Supreme Court (Number of Judges)
Amendment Act, 2019**. Originally **8** (1949).

---

# PART B — APPOINTMENT & THE COLLEGIUM ⭐
✅ Judges appointed by the **President**. CJI appointed after "consultation"; other judges after consultation
**with the CJI** (obligatory).

| Case | Year | Held |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **First Judges** | 1981 | "consultation" ≠ concurrence; **executive primacy** |
| ✅ **Second Judges** | 1993 | consultation = **concurrence**; **collegium** born; CJI's advice **binding** |
| ✅ **Third Judges** | 1998 | collegium = **CJI + 4 seniormost** SC judges; plurality of judges |
| ✅ **Fourth Judges (NJAC)** | 2015 | struck down **99th Amdt + NJAC Act 2014** as unconstitutional (violates judicial independence) → collegium restored |

⚠️ **CJI appointment convention:** seniormost SC judge (broken in 1973 A.N. Ray & 1977 M.H. Beg by
supersession; **Second Judges Case (1993)** restored seniority rule).

## Qualifications, tenure, removal ⭐
✅ Citizen of India + **5 yrs HC judge / 10 yrs HC advocate / "distinguished jurist"**. **No minimum age.**
Holds office till **65 years**; resigns to President. **Removal (impeachment):** by President after **special
majority** of both Houses on grounds of **proved misbehaviour or incapacity**, per the **Judges Enquiry Act
1968** (motion by 100 LS / 50 RS members → 3-member inquiry committee). ✅ **No SC judge has ever been
removed** (V. Ramaswami 1991-93 survived — Congress abstained). ✅ Salary charged on **Consolidated Fund**;
can't be varied to disadvantage (except financial emergency).

# PART C — JURISDICTION ⭐
| Type | Scope |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Original (exclusive)** | Federal disputes: Centre↔state(s), state↔state. Excludes political questions, pre-Constitution treaties, **inter-state water disputes**, Finance Commission matters |
| ✅ **Writ** | Issues 5 writs (habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, certiorari, quo warranto) — **only for Fundamental Rights** (Art 32); **concurrent** with HCs; HC writ power (Art 226) is **wider** |
| ✅ **Appellate** | Constitutional (HC certifies substantial question of law) · Civil · Criminal · **Special Leave Petition (Art 136)** — discretionary, plenary, against any court/tribunal except military |
| ✅ **Advisory (Art 143)** | President may seek opinion; SC **may** refuse (public-importance) but **must** advise on pre-Constitution treaty disputes; opinion **not binding** |
| ✅ **Court of Record (Art 129)** | Records as precedents; **contempt of court** power |
| ✅ **Judicial Review** | Examine constitutionality of laws/executive orders; strike down **ultra vires** ones |

✅ **Other powers:** decides President/VP election disputes (original, exclusive, final); reviews its own
judgments (**self-correcting** — Kesavananda 1973 overruled Golak Nath 1967); law binding on all courts
(**Art 141**); transfers cases between HCs.

# PART D — JUDICIAL REVIEW ⭐
✅ Power to examine constitutionality of legislative & executive action; not explicitly named but flows from
**Art 13, 32, 131-136, 143, 226, 246, 251, 372**. Basis = **basic structure** (Kesavananda 1973). ⚠️ Three
grounds: violation of FRs, exceeding constitutional scope, procedural non-compliance. **Ninth Schedule** laws
(post-**24 April 1973**) are also reviewable (**I.R. Coelho, 2007**).

---

# PART E — JUDICIAL ACTIVISM vs RESTRAINT ⭐
✅ **Judicial activism** = judges going beyond review to *make policy/law* (term originated in USA mid-20th c.;
in India via **expanded locus standi & PIL**). ⚠️ Justifications (Wadehra/Kashyap): legislative-executive
failure, hung legislatures, protection of citizens' rights, legislative vacuum. **Judicial restraint** = deference
to the elected branches; separation of powers. ⚠️ Criticism: judicial over-reach, "government by judiciary."

## Public Interest Litigation (PIL) ⭐
✅ Originated in **USA (1960s)**; introduced in India **early 1980s** by **Justice V.R. Krishna Iyer &
Justice P.N. Bhagwati**. Key = **relaxation of locus standi** → *any* public-spirited person can move the court
for the disadvantaged. Also called SAL/SIL/CAL. Aim: rule of law + access to justice + realising FRs. ⚠️
Downside: frivolous/"publicity-interest" litigation, judicial overload.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ SC writ jurisdiction is wider than HC's → HC (**Art 226**) is **wider** (FRs *and* other purposes).
- ❌ SC's advisory opinion (Art 143) is binding → **not binding**; on pre-Constitution treaties SC **must** advise but opinion still non-binding.
- ❌ Original jurisdiction covers inter-state water disputes → **excluded** (Art 262 → tribunals).
- ❌ Collegium is in the Constitution → judicial creation (Second/Third Judges cases); NJAC (99th Amdt) was struck down in 2015.
- ❌ A "distinguished jurist" can't be an SC judge → **can** be (though never yet appointed).
- ❌ An SC judge has been impeached → **none ever removed** (Ramaswami survived).
- ❌ SLP (Art 136) lies against military courts → **excluded**.
- ❌ SC judge retires at 62 → **65** (HC judge = 62).
