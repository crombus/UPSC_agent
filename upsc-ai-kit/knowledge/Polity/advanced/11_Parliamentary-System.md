## 1. What it is
✅ India adopts a **parliamentary form** at both Centre (**Art 74 & 75**) and states (**Art 163 & 164**).
Classification is by **executive–legislature relations**: in a **parliamentary** system the executive is
**responsible to the legislature**; in a **presidential** system the executive is **not responsible** and
has a **fixed** term, constitutionally independent.

✅ Also called **cabinet government / responsible government / Westminster model** (Britain, Japan,
Canada, India). Presidential = **non-responsible / fixed-executive** (USA, Brazil, Russia, Sri Lanka).
✅ **Ivor Jennings** → "cabinet system" (cabinet = nucleus of power). ✅ PM once "**primus inter pares**"
(first among equals); now analysts (**Crossman, Mackintosh**) call it "**prime ministerial government**"
— true in India too.

## 2. Features of the parliamentary system (8) ⭐
| # | Feature | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ 1 | **Nominal & real executives** | President = **nominal/de jure/titular**; PM = **real/de facto**. Art 74 CoM aids & advises President — **advice is binding**. |
| ✅ 2 | **Majority party rule** | Majority party in **Lok Sabha** forms govt; leader = PM. Coalition if no single majority. |
| ✅ 3 | **Collective responsibility** ⭐ | **Bedrock** principle (**Art 75**). CoM collectively responsible to **Lok Sabha** — a **no-confidence motion** removes the whole ministry. |
| ✅ 4 | **Political homogeneity** | Ministers usually same party/ideology (coalition → bound by consensus). |
| ✅ 5 | **Double membership** | Minister must be an MP; if **not an MP for 6 consecutive months**, ceases to be minister. |
| ✅ 6 | **Leadership of the PM** | PM = leader of CoM, of Parliament, and of the ruling party. |
| ✅ 7 | **Dissolution of Lower House** | President can dissolve **Lok Sabha** on PM's advice → fresh elections. |
| ✅ 8 | **Secrecy** | Ministers take **oath of secrecy** (administered by President). |

## 3. Presidential system (USA) — contrast ⭐
✅ (a) President = head of State **and** government; (b) elected by **electoral college** for **fixed
4-year** term, removable only by **impeachment**; (c) governs via non-elected secretaries ("**Kitchen
Cabinet**"), responsible only to him; (d) executive **not** responsible to Congress, not members of it;
(e) **cannot dissolve** House of Representatives; (f) based on **separation of powers**.

| | Parliamentary | Presidential |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Executive | **Dual** (nominal + real) | **Single** |
| ✅ Term | Not fixed (removable) | **Fixed** |
| ✅ Responsibility | **Responsible** | Non-responsible |
| ✅ Membership | Double | Single |
| ✅ Dissolution of lower house | **Yes** | No |
| ✅ Powers | **Fusion** of powers | **Separation** of powers |

## 4. Merits & demerits ⭐
✅ **Merits:** (1) harmony between legislature & executive; (2) **responsible government** (question
hour, adjournment/no-confidence motions); (3) prevents despotism (authority in a group); (4) **ready
alternative government** — Jennings: "leader of opposition is the alternative PM"; (5) wide
representation.
✅ **Demerits:** (1) **unstable government** (Morarji Desai, Charan Singh, V.P. Singh, Chandra Shekhar,
Deve Gowda, I.K. Gujral); (2) no continuity of policies; (3) **dictatorship of the cabinet** (Laski;
Ramsay Muir — "dictatorship of the cabinet"; Indira/Rajiv era); (4) against separation of powers
(**Bagehot**: "cabinet is a hyphen that joins…the executive and legislative"); (5) government by
amateurs.

## 5. Why India adopted it ⭐
✅ (1) **Familiarity** with the system from British rule (**K.M. Munshi**: "why…buy a novel experience");
(2) **Ambedkar** — preferred **more responsibility to more stability** (US = more stability/less
responsibility; British = more responsibility/less stability); (3) **avoid legislature–executive
conflict** in an infant democracy; (4) **heterogeneous society** needs wide representation.
✅ **Swaran Singh Committee (1975)** examined presidential-vs-parliamentary → keep parliamentary.

## 6. Indian vs British model — 3 differences ⭐
✅ (1) India = **republican** (elected President) vs Britain = **monarchical** (hereditary King/Queen);
(2) Britain = **parliamentary sovereignty**; India = **Parliament NOT supreme** (written Constitution,
federalism, judicial review, FRs); (3) UK PM must be from **House of Commons**; Indian PM may be from
**either House**.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ CoM collectively responsible to Rajya Sabha → to the **Lok Sabha** (Art 75).
- ❌ A non-MP can never be a minister → allowed, but must become an MP within **6 months**.
- ❌ Parliamentary system = separation of powers → it is **fusion** of powers.
- ❌ Indian PM must be from Lok Sabha → may be from **either House** (unlike UK's Commons rule).
- ❌ President's advice from CoM is optional → **binding** (42nd/44th Amendments to Art 74).
- ❌ Swaran Singh Committee recommended presidential system → it recommended **keeping parliamentary**.
