## 1. Article 1 — Union of States
✅ Art 1 describes **"India, that is, Bharat"** as a **"Union of States"** (not "Federation"). Deals
with (i) name & (ii) type of polity. CA compromise on name (India + Bharat). ✅ **Ambedkar** — "Union"
preferred because: (i) Indian federation is **not the result of an agreement** among states (unlike
USA); (ii) states have **no right to secede** — it's **indestructible**.

✅ **Territory of India = 3 categories:** (1) territories of states; (2) union territories; (3)
territories that may be **acquired**. Names in the **First Schedule**. Present: **28 states, 9 UTs**
(per book, 2019). ✅ **"Territory of India" is WIDER than "Union of India"** — Union = only states;
Territory = states + UTs + acquired territories. Special provisions (Part XXI, Art 371–371J) apply to
**12 states**; **5th & 6th Schedules** cover scheduled/tribal areas.

✅ India (sovereign) can **acquire foreign territory** by cession (treaty/purchase/gift/lease/
plebiscite), occupation, conquest or subjugation.

## 2. Articles 2 vs 3 — the key distinction ⭐
| | Article 2 | Article 3 |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Deals with | **Admit / establish NEW states** (not part of Union) | **Reorganise EXISTING states** (internal readjustment) |
| ✅ Powers | (a) admit existing states; (b) establish new ones | form/increase/diminish/alter boundary/alter name |

## 3. Article 3 — Parliament's power to reorganise ⭐
✅ Parliament may: (a) form a new state (separation/uniting); (b) increase area; (c) diminish area;
(d) alter boundaries; (e) alter name. **Two conditions:** (1) bill only on **prior recommendation of
President**; (2) President **refers it to the state legislature** for its views within a specified time.

✅ **BUT** President/Parliament are **NOT bound** by the state's views (may accept/reject); no fresh
reference needed for each amendment; **for a UT, no reference at all**. → Parliament can **redraw the
political map without state consent**. Hence India = **"an indestructible union of destructible
states"** (contrast USA = "indestructible union of indestructible states").

✅ **Art 4:** laws under Art 2 & 3 are **NOT** deemed constitutional amendments under Art 368 → passed
by **simple majority**, ordinary legislative process.

## 4. Cession of territory — case law ⭐
✅ **Berubari Union (1960, Presidential reference):** power to *diminish* a state's area (Art 3) does
**NOT** include **ceding Indian territory to a foreign country** → cession needs a **constitutional
amendment (Art 368)**. → **9th Amendment Act (1960)** transferred Berubari to Pakistan.
✅ **1969 ruling:** settling a **boundary dispute** does **not** need an amendment — done by **executive
action** (no cession involved).

## 5. Exchange of territories with Bangladesh
✅ **100th Amendment Act (2015)** implemented the **Land Boundary Agreement (LBA)**: India transferred
**111 enclaves** to Bangladesh, received **51 enclaves**; modified First Schedule for **Assam, West
Bengal, Meghalaya, Tripura**. Background: Radcliffe Award 1947 → Bagge Award 1950 → Nehru-Noon
Agreement 1958 → LBA 1974 + 2011 Protocol.

## 6. Evolution — integration & reorganisation ⭐
✅ **Integration of princely states:** Independence Act 1947 gave 3 options (India/Pakistan/independent).
Of **552** princely states, **549 joined India**; **3 refused** — **Hyderabad** (police action),
**Junagarh** (referendum), **Kashmir** (Instrument of Accession).
✅ **1950 four-fold classification:** Part A (9 ex-governors' provinces), Part B (9 ex-princely states
w/ legislatures), Part C (10, centrally administered), Part D (Andaman & Nicobar).

### Reorganisation commissions
| Body | Year | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Dhar Commission** (S.K. Dhar) | 1948 | reorganise on **administrative convenience**, not language |
| ✅ **JVP Committee** (Nehru, Patel, Pattabhi Sitaramayya) | 1948–49 | **rejected** language as basis |
| ✅ **Fazl Ali Commission** (Fazl Ali, K.M. Panikkar, H.N. Kunzru) | 1953–55 | **accepted language** but rejected "one language–one state" |

✅ **First linguistic state = Andhra (Oct 1953)** — after **Potti Sriramulu's 56-day hunger strike &
death**. ✅ **States Reorganisation Act 1956 + 7th Amendment 1956:** abolished Part A/B/C/D distinction
→ **14 states + 6 UTs (1 Nov 1956)**. Fazl Ali's 4 factors: unity/security, linguistic-cultural
homogeneity, financial/economic/administrative, welfare.

## 8. Union Territories via constitutional amendments
✅ **Dadra & Nagar Haveli** (Portuguese; **10th Amdt 1961**); **Goa, Daman & Diu** (police action 1961;
**12th Amdt 1962**; Goa → statehood 1987); **Puducherry** (French; **14th Amdt 1962**).

## 9. J&K reorganisation (2019)
✅ Till 2019, J&K had **its own constitution** (Art 370). **2019:** Presidential Order extended the
full Constitution to J&K (Art 370 now inoperative but still in text); **J&K Reorganisation Act 2019**
split it into **two UTs — Jammu & Kashmir** and **Ladakh** (Ladakh = Kargil + Leh). → **28 states,
9 UTs (2019)**.

## 10. Change of names
✅ United Provinces → **Uttar Pradesh (1950, first renamed)**; Madras → **Tamil Nadu (1969)**; Mysore →
**Karnataka (1973)**; Laccadive… → **Lakshadweep (1973)**; Delhi → **NCT of Delhi (69th Amdt, 1991)**;
Uttaranchal → **Uttarakhand (2006)**; Pondicherry → **Puducherry (2006)**; Orissa → **Odisha (2011)**.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ Art 2 = reorganising existing states → Art 2 = **new** states; **Art 3** = existing states.
- ❌ State consent is required for reorganisation → **not required** (Parliament not bound by state views).
- ❌ Reorganisation needs a constitutional amendment → **simple majority** (Art 4, outside Art 368).
- ❌ Ceding territory to a foreign country can be done under Art 3 → needs **Art 368 amendment** (Berubari).
- ❌ First linguistic state was Maharashtra → **Andhra (1953)**.
- ❌ Fazl Ali endorsed "one language–one state" → **rejected** it.
- ❌ India currently has 9 UTs → **8 UTs** (after DNH + Daman & Diu merger, 2020).
- ❌ States have a right to secede → **no** ("indestructible union").
