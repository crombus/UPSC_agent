## 0. Overview & the 12 features

✅ FRs are in **Part III (Art 12–35)**, inspired by the **US Bill of Rights**; Part III = "Magna
Carta of India." Originally **7** rights; **Right to Property (Art 31) deleted by 44th Amendment,
1978** → now a legal right under **Art 300-A**. Present count = **6**.

The 12 characteristic features (exam hooks in brackets):
1. ✅ Some citizen-only, some for all persons · 2. ✅ Qualified, not absolute (reasonable
restrictions; courts decide) · 3. ✅ Mostly vs State, some vs private · 4. ✅ Some negative, some
positive · 5. ✅ Justiciable · 6. ✅ Directly enforceable by SC (Art 32) · 7. ✅ Amendable but subject
to **basic structure** · 8. ✅ Suspendable in National Emergency **except Art 20 & 21** · 9. ✅ Scope
limited by Art 31A/31B/31C · 10. ✅ Restrictable for armed forces (**Art 33**) · 11. ✅ Restrictable
under **martial law (Art 34)** · 12. ✅ Some need enabling law — **only Parliament** (**Art 35**).

## Article 12 — Definition of "State"
✅ Includes: Govt & Parliament of India; Govt & legislatures of states; **all local authorities**
(municipalities, panchayats, district boards) and **other authorities** (statutory/non-statutory
bodies like LIC, ONGC) within India or under Govt of India control.

## Article 13 — Laws inconsistent with FRs
✅ Laws violating FRs are **void**; establishes **judicial review**. "Law" includes ordinances,
orders, bye-laws, rules, regulations, notifications, custom or usage. ⚠️ Art 13 does **not** include
constitutional amendments (*Kesavananda*, 1973) — but they must not violate basic structure.

## 1. RIGHT TO EQUALITY (Art 14–18)

### Article 14 — Equality before Law & Equal Protection of Laws
✅ Applies to **all persons** (citizens, foreigners, legal persons). Two concepts:

| Concept | Origin | Nature | Core meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Equality **before** law | ✅ British | Negative | no special privileges; equal subjection to ordinary law; no one above law |
| Equal **protection** of laws | ✅ American | Positive | equal treatment in equal circumstances; like treated alike |

✅ Permits **reasonable classification** (intelligible differentia + ⚠️ rational nexus to object),
forbids **class legislation**. ✅ **Dicey's Rule of Law**: 3 elements — only the **first two** apply
to India (Constitution is the *source* of rights here). ✅ Rule of Law = **basic feature**.

**Exceptions to equality:** ✅ Art **361** (President/Governor immunities) · Art **361-A** (true
reports of legislative proceedings) · Art **105** (MPs) · Art **194** (state legislators) · Art
**31-C** ("where 31-C comes in, Art 14 goes out") · foreign sovereigns/diplomats · **UN & agencies**.

### Article 15 — Prohibition of Discrimination
✅ No discrimination against **citizens** on grounds **only** of religion, race, caste, sex, place of
birth. Keywords: **"discrimination"** and **"only."** 2nd provision (shops, wells, ghats…) binds
**State AND private persons**. **Four exceptions:** (a) women & children; (b) SEBC/SC/ST; (c) **93rd
Amdt 2005** → 27% OBC in educational institutions (aided/unaided, **except minority**), SC upheld
2008 w/ creamy-layer exclusion; (d) **103rd Amdt 2019** → up to 10% EWS.

**Creamy layer (OBC) — excluded:** constitutional-post holders; Group A/B officers (+PSU/bank
equivalents); Colonel & above; professionals; business/trade; large landholders; **income > ₹8 L**.
✅ Ceiling history: ₹1 L (1993) → ₹2.5 L (2004) → ₹4.5 L (2008) → ₹6 L (2013) → **₹8 L (2017)**.
**EWS:** family income < ₹8 L AND not owning ≥5 acres / ≥1000 sq ft flat / ≥100 sq yd (notified) / ≥200 sq yd (other).

### Article 16 — Equality of Opportunity in Public Employment
✅ Grounds barred: religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, **residence**. **Four
exceptions:** (a) Parliament may fix **residence** (now only **AP & Telangana**; 1957 Act expired
1974); (b) backward-class reservation; (c) religious-institution offices; (d) **EWS 10%**.

**Mandal:** ✅ B.P. Mandal Commission (appointed 1979, Art 340; reported 1980) — **3743 castes**,
**~52%** population, recommended **27% OBC**. Implemented 1990 (V.P. Singh). **Indra Sawhney /
Mandal case (1992):** (a) exclude creamy layer; (b) **no reservation in promotions** (5 yrs only);
(c) total **≤ 50%** except extraordinary; (d) carry-forward valid but within 50%; (e) permanent
statutory body. **Amendments overriding it:** ✅ **77th (1995)** promotions for SC/ST · **85th
(2001)** consequential seniority (retrospective Jun 1995) · **81st (2000)** backlog as separate
class · **102nd (2018)** NCBC constitutional status (**Art 338-B**). ✅ Tamil Nadu's **69%** put in
**9th Schedule (1994)**. Scientific/technical posts (Cabinet Secretariat Order 1961) exemptable from EWS.

### Article 17 — Abolition of Untouchability
✅ Practice punishable; **Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955** (renamed 1976). Term **undefined**
(Mysore HC: historical caste-based practice, not literal). ✅ Enforceable **against private individuals**.

### Article 18 — Abolition of Titles
✅ (a) State can't confer titles except **military/academic**; (b) citizens can't accept foreign
titles; (c)/(d) office-holders need President's consent for foreign titles/gifts. ✅ **National
Awards case (1996):** Bharat Ratna/Padma awards are **not "titles"**; valid but **no prefix/suffix**
(else forfeited). Instituted 1954, discontinued 1977 (Morarji Desai), revived 1980 (Indira Gandhi).

## 2. RIGHT TO FREEDOM (Art 19–22)

### Article 19 — Six Freedoms (citizens only; vs State only)
✅ (a) speech & expression · (b) assembly · (c) association/unions/**co-operative societies** ·
(d) movement · (e) residence · (f) profession. (Property right (g) deleted, 44th Amdt 1978.)

**Speech & expression** ✅ SC-read inclusions: propagate own/others' views, **freedom of press**,
commercial ads, against phone tapping, right to telecast (no govt media monopoly), against bandh,
**right to know**, freedom of silence, against pre-censorship, demonstration/picketing (**not
strike**). Restriction grounds: sovereignty & integrity, security of state, friendly foreign
relations, public order, decency/morality, contempt of court, defamation, incitement.

**Assembly** ✅ peaceful, unarmed, public land; not strike. Restrictions: sovereignty/integrity,
public order. CrPC **Sec 144** (magistrate restrains); IPC **Sec 141** (unlawful assembly ≥5).
**Association** ✅ includes negative right (not to join); recognition **not** a FR; **no** guaranteed
right to strike/lock-out. **Movement** ✅ internal only (external dimension under Art 21); restricted
for public interest & ST protection. **Residence** ✅ reside (temporary) + settle (permanent); ST
areas restricted. **Profession** ✅ excludes immoral/dangerous trades; State may set qualifications
& run monopolies without justification.

### Article 20 — Protection re Conviction (all persons)
✅ (a) **No ex-post-facto law** (criminal only; not civil/tax; bars conviction not trial; not for
preventive detention); (b) **No double jeopardy** (only before court/judicial tribunal, not
departmental); (c) **No self-incrimination** (oral + documentary; NOT thumb impression, specimen
signature, blood, body exhibition; criminal proceedings only). **Non-suspendable in Emergency.**

### Article 21 — Protection of Life & Personal Liberty (all persons)
✅ *Gopalan* (1950): narrow — only vs arbitrary **executive** action ("procedure established by
law"). ✅ **Menaka Gandhi (1978)**: wide — procedure must be **fair, just, reasonable** (imports
"due process"), vs executive **and** legislative action; life = live with **human dignity**.
✅ SC-declared Art 21 rights include: dignity, **clean environment**, livelihood, **privacy**,
shelter, health, free legal aid, speedy trial, against handcuffing/solitary confinement, travel
abroad, **against bonded labour**, information, reputation, sleep, against noise pollution,
sustainable development (35 listed in book). **Art 20 & 21 never suspended in Emergency.**

### Article 21A — Right to Education
✅ **86th Amendment (2002)** — free & compulsory education **6–14 years**. Shifted old Art 45 to
early-childhood (<6 yrs); added FD under Art 51A. **RTE Act, 2009** operationalises it. SC (1993)
had already read primary education into Art 21 (Unni Krishnan), overruling *Mohini Jain* (1992).

### Article 22 — Protection Against Arrest & Detention
✅ **Punitive** (post-trial) vs **Preventive** (without trial, precautionary).
- **Ordinary law (Part 1):** grounds of arrest; consult lawyer; produced before magistrate **within
  24 hrs**; release after 24 hrs unless extended. (Not for enemy aliens / preventive detention.)
- **Preventive detention (Part 2):** detention **≤ 3 months** unless **Advisory Board** (HC judges)
  approves; grounds communicated (except public-interest facts); representation opportunity.
- ✅ **44th Amdt (1978)** sought to cut 3→2 months **but was never brought into force** — 3 months
  stands. ⚠️ Parliament has exclusive power for preventive detention re defence/foreign affairs;
  concurrent with states for security/public order. Laws: MISA (repealed), NSA 1980, COFEPOSA, etc.

## 3. RIGHT AGAINST EXPLOITATION (Art 23–24)

### Article 23 — Traffic in humans & forced labour
✅ Bars trafficking, **begar**, and forced labour (incl. bonded labour, devadasis, slavery); against
State **and** private persons; all persons. "Force" includes **economic compulsion** (below minimum
wage). Laws: Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act 1956, Bonded Labour System (Abolition) Act 1976,
Minimum Wages Act 1948. **Exception:** State may impose **compulsory service** for public purposes
(e.g. military/social service) — no religion/race/caste/class discrimination.

### Article 24 — Child labour
✅ Bars employment of children **below 14** in factory, mine, or hazardous work (not harmless work).
Laws: Child Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Act 1986 (renamed **Child and Adolescent Labour…** by
2016 Amdt); Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act 2005. SC (1996): ₹20,000 fine per child
→ Child Labour Rehabilitation Welfare Fund.

## 4. RIGHT TO FREEDOM OF RELIGION (Art 25–28)

### Article 25 — Conscience & profession/practice/propagation (all persons)
✅ Freedom of **conscience** + right to **profess, practice, propagate**. Propagation ≠ right to
**convert** (forcible conversion violates others' conscience). Subject to public order, morality,
health, other FRs. State may regulate secular activity & throw open Hindu institutions. Explanations:
**kirpans** included for Sikhs; "Hindus" include **Sikhs, Jains, Buddhists**.

### Article 26 — Manage religious affairs (denominations)
✅ Rights: establish/maintain institutions; manage religious matters; own & administer property.
Subject to public order/morality/health (**not** other FRs). Denomination test (SC): common belief
system + common organisation + distinctive name. ✅ Ramakrishna Mission & Ananda Marga = denominations
(within Hindu religion); Aurobindo Society = **not** a religious denomination.

### Article 27 — No tax for promotion of religion
✅ Bars **taxes** (not **fees**) for promoting/maintaining a particular religion. Fees for regulating
secular administration or pilgrim services are allowed.

### Article 28 — Religious instruction in institutions
✅ Four types: (a) wholly State-maintained → instruction **prohibited**; (b) State-administered but
trust-established → **permitted**; (c) State-recognised & (d) State-aided → permitted **voluntarily**
(with consent).

## 5. CULTURAL & EDUCATIONAL RIGHTS (Art 29–30)

### Article 29 — Protection of minorities' interests
✅ Any **section of citizens** with distinct language/script/culture may conserve it; no denial of
admission to State/State-aided institutions on grounds only of religion/race/caste/language. ✅ SC:
scope not limited to minorities ("section of citizens" = minority **or** majority).

### Article 30 — Minorities' right to establish/administer institutions
✅ Only for **minorities** (religious/linguistic); "minority" **undefined**. Rights: establish &
administer; fair compensation on acquisition (44th Amdt); no discrimination in aid. Three types
(recognition+aid / recognition only / neither). ✅ *Malankara Syrian Catholic College* (2007): right
includes choosing governing body, staff, students, fees — but **no right to maladminister**;
regulatory measures for standards valid; aid doesn't change minority character.

## 6. RIGHT TO CONSTITUTIONAL REMEDIES (Art 32) + WRITS

✅ Art 32 = "**heart and soul**" of the Constitution (Ambedkar); a **basic feature** (not amendable
away). Four provisions: (a) right to move SC; (b) SC issues writs; (c) Parliament may empower other
courts; (d) suspendable only per Constitution (**Art 359** during Emergency). ✅ SC jurisdiction here
is **original but not exclusive** (concurrent with HC under **Art 226**). Only **FR violations**
enforceable under Art 32.

**SC (Art 32) vs HC (Art 226) writ jurisdiction:**
1. ✅ SC: FRs only; HC: FRs **+ any other purpose** (ordinary legal right) → HC wider here.
2. ✅ SC: throughout India; HC: within territorial jurisdiction / where cause of action arises.
3. ✅ Art 32 remedy is itself a FR → SC **cannot refuse**; Art 226 is **discretionary**.

**The five writs (borrowed from English "prerogative writs"):**

| Writ | Meaning | Key scope |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Habeas Corpus | "have the body" | vs public **and** private; not if lawful/contempt/competent-court/outside-jurisdiction |
| ✅ Mandamus | "we command" | vs public official/body; **not** vs private, discretionary duty, contract, President/Governor, HC CJ (judicial) |
| ✅ Prohibition | "to forbid" | higher→lower court, **preventive**; only judicial/quasi-judicial; not admin/legislative/private |
| ✅ Certiorari | "to be certified" | transfer/quash; **preventive + curative**; since 1991 also vs **administrative** authorities |
| ✅ Quo-Warranto | "by what authority" | legality of public-office claim; only substantive statutory/constitutional office; **any interested person** may seek |

## 7. Art 33 (Armed Forces), Art 34 (Martial Law), Art 35 (Enabling laws)

✅ **Art 33:** **Parliament** (not states) may restrict/abrogate FRs of armed/para-military/police/
intelligence forces (Army/Navy/Air Force Acts 1950, Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) Act 1966);
can exclude court martial from writ jurisdiction. Covers non-combatants (cooks, barbers…).
✅ **Art 34:** restricts FRs under **martial law**; Parliament may **indemnify** acts / validate
sentences. Martial law = "military rule" (undefined; from English common law); no express provision
(implicit in Art 34). ✅ SC: martial law does **not** ipso facto suspend habeas corpus.
✅ **Art 35:** only **Parliament** legislates on specified FRs → ensures pan-India uniformity.

### Martial Law vs National Emergency (Table 7.3)
| | Martial Law | National Emergency |
|---|---|---|
| Affects | ✅ Only FRs | FRs + Centre-state relations, revenue, legislative powers, Parliament tenure |
| Govt/courts | ✅ Suspends govt & ordinary courts | Continues them |
| Cause | Breakdown of law & order (any reason) | Only war, external aggression, armed rebellion |
| Area | Specific area | Whole country or part |
| Constitution | Implicit (no express provision) | Explicit, detailed |

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ Art 14/20/21/23/24/25 are citizen-only → they apply to **all persons**; **Art 15,16,19,29,30**
  are citizen-only.
- ❌ All three Dicey elements apply to India → only **first two**.
- ❌ Art 19 protects external movement/travel abroad → that's **Art 21**.
- ❌ Right to propagate includes conversion → **no**.
- ❌ Art 27 bars fees → bars only **taxes**, not fees.
- ❌ Certiorari only vs judicial bodies → since **1991** also vs administrative authorities.
- ❌ All FRs suspended in Emergency → **Art 20 & 21 never**; Art 19 only in external emergency.
- ❌ 44th Amdt cut preventive detention to 2 months → provision **never enforced**; 3 months stands.
- ❌ Reservation in promotion / Art 16(4) is a right → it's an **enabling** power.
