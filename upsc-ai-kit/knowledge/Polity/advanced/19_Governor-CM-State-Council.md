# PART A — THE GOVERNOR (Art 153–162)

## Appointment & conditions ⭐
✅ **Not elected** — **appointed by the President** by warrant (Canadian model, not the US elected-Governor
model). A "nominee of the Centre" but (SC 1979) an **independent constitutional office**, not a Central-govt
employee. **Qualifications (Art 157):** citizen of India + **35 years**. Two **conventions** (often breached):
be an **outsider** to the state, and appointed after **consulting the CM**. ✅ Not a member of Parliament/state
legislature; no other office of profit; oath by the **HC Chief Justice**.

## Term & removal ⭐
✅ **5-year term** but holds office during the **pleasure of the President** — **no security of tenure**,
removable anytime, no grounds specified. Can be **transferred** and **reappointed**; continues till successor
takes charge (no interregnum). ⚠️ Mass changes after regime change (V.P. Singh 1989; Narasimha Rao 1991).

## Powers ⭐
✅ Executive (all state actions in his name; appoints **CM, Advocate-General, State Election Commissioner,
SPSC chair/members, VCs as Chancellor**; recommends President's Rule) · Legislative (summons/prorogues,
dissolves Assembly; **nominates 1/6 of Legislative Council**; assents/returns/**reserves bills for President**;
**ordinances Art 213**) · Financial (money bills need his recommendation; **State Finance Commission**) ·
Judicial (**pardons Art 161** — but **cannot pardon death sentences / court-martial**, unlike President).

## Constitutional position & discretion ⭐
✅ **Nominal executive** (Art 154, 163, 164). Unlike the President, (a) the Governor **can act in discretion**,
and (b) **ministerial advice is NOT expressly binding** on him (no equivalent of the 42nd Amdt proviso).
| **Constitutional (express) discretion** | **Situational (implied) discretion** |
|---|---|
| ✅ Reserving a bill for the President (Art 200) | ✅ Appointing CM in a hung Assembly / on sudden death |
| ✅ Recommending President's Rule | ✅ Dismissing a CoM that has lost confidence |
| ✅ Functions as UT administrator (additional charge) | ✅ Dissolving the Assembly if CoM lost majority |
| ✅ Sixth Schedule tribal royalty (Assam etc.) | |

✅ **Dual role:** constitutional head of the state **and** the Centre's representative — the structural source
of Centre-state friction.

# PART B — CHIEF MINISTER (Art 164, 167) ⭐
✅ **Real executive (de facto)** — head of government; parallels the PM. **Appointed by the Governor**
(leader of majority; discretion in hung house / sudden death). Need not prove majority *before* appointment;
**non-MLA CM allowed 6 months**; may be from **either House**. Holds office during Governor's pleasure but
**can't be dismissed while he holds a majority**.

## Powers
✅ **CoM:** recommends ministers, allocates/reshuffles portfolios, presides over CoM; his resignation/death
**dissolves the whole CoM**. **Governor:** principal channel of communication (**Art 167**); advises on key
appointments. **Legislature:** advises summoning/dissolution; announces policies. **Other:** chairs State
Planning Board; vice-chairman of the **zonal council** (by rotation); member of **Inter-State Council** &
**NITI Aayog Governing Council**.

---

# PART C — STATE COUNCIL OF MINISTERS (Art 163, 164) ⭐
✅ **Art 163:** CoM (with CM as head) aids & advises the Governor, except where he acts in discretion.
✅ **Art 164:** CM appointed by Governor, other ministers on CM's advice; **91st Amdt (2003):** total ministers
**≤ 15% of the Assembly** and **not fewer than 12**; defector barred from ministership. **Collectively
responsible to the Legislative Assembly.** Three ranks (Cabinet / State / Deputy). ✅ Compulsory **Tribal
Welfare Minister** in Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, MP, Odisha (Bihar excluded by 94th Amdt).

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ Governor is elected → **appointed by the President** (min age **35**, not 25).
- ❌ Ministerial advice is binding on the Governor → **not** expressly binding (no 42nd-Amdt equivalent).
- ❌ Governor can pardon death sentences → **cannot** (only the President, Art 72).
- ❌ Governor nominates members to the Legislative **Assembly** → nominates **1/6 to the Legislative Council** (Anglo-Indian Assembly nomination now lapsed).
- ❌ Governor has a fixed 5-yr tenure → holds office during **President's pleasure**, no security of tenure.
- ❌ State CoM cap is 15% of both Houses → **≤ 15% of the Assembly, minimum 12** (91st Amdt).
- ❌ A non-MLA can never be CM → allowed for **6 months**.
- ❌ Reserving a bill for the President is on cabinet advice → a **discretionary** power of the Governor.

## 📰 CA hooks
- 📰 ✅ **Governor's assent to bills — landmark 2025 rulings:** in *State of TN v. Governor* (Apr 2025) the SC
  set a 3-month timeline & "deemed assent" (Art 142); the **Presidential Reference (Art 143) 5-judge opinion
  of 20 Nov 2025** clarified — **no fixed judicial timelines, no "deemed assent"**, but Governors/President
  **cannot sit indefinitely** ("prolonged inaction" is judicially reviewable). *(web-verified 2026)*
- 📰 ⚠️ Recurrent **Governor vs elected government** friction (Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Punjab, West Bengal) — bill
  delays, VC appointments, and use of discretion.
