## 0. Origin & source
✅ Original Constitution had **only rights, no duties**. Fundamental Duties (FDs) added in **1976**;
one more added in **2002**. Inspired by the **Constitution of the erstwhile USSR**. ✅ None of the
major democracies (USA, Canada, France, Germany, Australia) list citizens' duties; **Japan** is
perhaps the only democratic constitution that does. Socialist states gave rights & duties equal weight.

✅ Placed in **Part IV-A**, a **single Article — 51A**.

## 1. Swaran Singh Committee (1976)
✅ Set up by **Congress Party in 1976** — need felt during the **internal Emergency (1975–77)**.
Recommended a **separate chapter** on FDs. Govt accepted → enacted **42nd Amendment Act, 1976**,
adding **Part IV-A / Art 51A** with **ten** duties.

> 🔑 Key nuance: Committee suggested **eight** duties → 42nd Amdt actually included **ten**.

✅ **Committee recommendations NOT accepted** (high PYQ value):
1. Parliament may impose **penalty/punishment** for non-compliance.
2. Such penalty laws **not questionable** in courts (no FR/other-provision challenge).
3. **Duty to pay taxes** should be a Fundamental Duty.

## 2. List of Fundamental Duties (Art 51A) — all 11
✅ It shall be the duty of every citizen of India:
| | Duty | Type |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | Abide by Constitution; respect its ideals, institutions, **National Flag & National Anthem** | civic |
| (b) | Cherish & follow **noble ideals of the freedom struggle** | moral |
| (c) | Uphold & protect **sovereignty, unity & integrity** of India | civic |
| (d) | **Defend the country** & render national service when called | civic |
| (e) | Promote **harmony & common brotherhood** beyond religion/language/region; **renounce practices derogatory to dignity of women** | moral |
| (f) | Value & preserve rich heritage of **composite culture** | moral |
| (g) | Protect & improve **natural environment** (forests, lakes, rivers, wildlife); compassion for living creatures | civic |
| (h) | Develop **scientific temper**, humanism, spirit of inquiry & reform | moral |
| (i) | Safeguard **public property**; abjure violence | civic |
| (j) | Strive towards **excellence** in individual & collective activity | moral |
| (k) | Provide **education** to one's child/ward aged **6–14** — ✅ added by **86th Amendment, 2002** | civic |

> 🔑 Mnemonic (11th duty): "**86th → education 6–14**" pairs with **Art 21A** & **Art 45** — the
> education-trio all trace to the 86th Amendment.

## 3. Features (4)
1. ✅ Mix of **moral** (noble ideals) & **civic** (respect Flag/Anthem) duties.
2. ✅ Codify values integral to Indian tradition & way of life.
3. ✅ Confined to **citizens only** — do **not** extend to foreigners (unlike some FRs).
4. ✅ **Non-justiciable** — no direct court enforcement, no legal sanction; **but Parliament may
   enforce by suitable legislation**.

## 4. Criticism (5)
✅ (1) **Not exhaustive** — omits casting vote, paying taxes, family planning (tax duty was even
recommended by Swaran Singh). (2) **Vague/ambiguous** — "noble ideals," "composite culture,"
"scientific temper" hard for common man. (3) **Code of moral precepts** (non-justiciable). (4)
**Superfluous** — people would perform them anyway. (5) **Wrongly placed** — appended after Part IV
(DPSP); should have followed Part III to be on par with FRs.

## 5. Significance (5)
✅ (1) Reminder that rights carry duties. (2) Warning against anti-national/anti-social acts (flag
burning, destroying public property). (3) Source of inspiration & discipline; citizens = active
participants. (4) ✅ **Help courts judge constitutional validity** — SC (**1992**): a law giving
effect to an FD may be deemed "reasonable" under **Art 14/19** and saved. (5) **Enforceable by law**
— Parliament can provide penalties.

✅ **Political voices:** H.R. Gokhale (Law Min.) — FDs would have a "sobering effect on restless
spirits"; Indira Gandhi — inclusion "strengthens democracy" / "democratic balance." ✅ Morarji
Desai's Janata govt (which reversed much of the 42nd Amdt via **43rd & 44th Amdts**) did **not**
annul FDs → eventual **consensus**, reinforced by the 2002 addition.

## 6. Verma Committee (1999) — existing legal provisions ⭐
✅ Justice J.S. Verma Committee on **Operationalisation of FDs (1999)** identified laws already
enforcing some FDs:
| Law | Enforces which duty |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Prevention of Insults to National Honour Act, 1971** | respect to Constitution/Flag/Anthem |
| ✅ Various criminal laws | punish enmity between groups (language/race/religion) |
| ✅ **Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955** | caste/religion offences |
| ✅ **IPC** | imputations prejudicial to national integration |
| ✅ **Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967** | declare communal org unlawful |
| ✅ **Representation of People Act, 1951** | disqualify members for corrupt practice / soliciting votes on religion |
| ✅ **Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972** | ban trade in endangered species |
| ✅ **Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980** | check deforestation |

## 7. 📰 CURRENT AFFAIRS — Enforcement of FDs (2024–25)
📰 **Durga Dutt v. Union of India** (SC, Sept 2024, bench of Justices Sanjiv Khanna, Sanjay Kumar,
R. Mahadevan): petitioner sought to make FDs **legally enforceable** + a High-Powered Committee.
- 📰 SC held it **cannot direct the legislature** to enact enforcement laws.
- 📰 Enforcement must come through **"citizen consciousness and awareness,"** not coercion —
  echoing the **Verma Committee's** emphasis on education & civic values (SC had, in 2003, advised
  govt to implement Verma recommendations).
- 📰 **Art 51A(g)** (environment) increasingly linked to **Art 21** clean-environment jurisprudence
  (e.g., *Vanashakti v. UoI*, 2025).

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ FDs inspired by USA/Japan → **USSR** (Japan is only the notable *other* democracy that lists duties).
- ❌ Swaran Singh recommended 10 → recommended **8**, but 42nd Amdt added **10**.
- ❌ There are 10 FDs → **11** (86th Amdt added the 11th in 2002).
- ❌ Duty to pay taxes / vote is a FD → **not included** (tax duty only *recommended*).
- ❌ FDs apply to all persons → **citizens only**.
- ❌ FDs are justiciable → **non-justiciable** (but Parliament may legislate).
- ❌ Verma Committee added new duties → it only **identified existing laws** enforcing them.
