# PART A — ORGANISATION
✅ **No uniformity** — most states are **unicameral** (Governor + Legislative Assembly); only **6 are
bicameral** (Governor + Legislative Council + Assembly): **Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar,
Maharashtra, Karnataka**. **J&K Council abolished** (Reorganisation Act 2019); Tamil Nadu's 2010 Council Act
never enforced.

## Abolition/Creation of a Council (Art 169) ⭐
✅ Parliament can **create/abolish** a Legislative Council if the state **Assembly passes a special-majority
resolution**; such an Act is **NOT a constitutional amendment** (Art 368) and passes by **simple majority** in
Parliament. History: AP created 1957 → abolished 1985 → revived 2007; TN abolished 1986; Punjab & WB 1969.

# PART B — COMPOSITION

## Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha) ⭐
✅ **Directly elected**, universal adult franchise; strength **60–500** (special: Goa/Sikkim/Arunachal **30**,
Mizoram **40**, Nagaland **46**). Governor could nominate 1 **Anglo-Indian** (lapsed, 104th Amdt). Delimitation
frozen at 1971 census till **2026** (84th Amdt); SC/ST reservation on population ratio.

## Legislative Council (Vidhan Parishad) ⭐
✅ **Indirectly elected / partly nominated**; strength = **max 1/3 of Assembly, min 40**. Composition:
| Fraction | Electorate |
|---|---|
| ✅ **1/3** | members of **local bodies** |
| ✅ **1/12** | **graduates** (3 yrs standing) |
| ✅ **1/12** | **teachers** (3 yrs standing) |
| ✅ **1/3** | elected by **MLAs** (from non-members) |
| ✅ **1/6** | **nominated by Governor** (literature, science, art, cooperative movement, social service) |

So **5/6 indirectly elected + 1/6 nominated**, via **STV proportional representation**.

## Duration
✅ **Assembly** — 5-yr term, dissolvable (like LS). **Council** — **permanent**, 1/3 retire every 2 yrs,
6-yr term (like RS).

# PART C — COUNCIL vs ASSEMBLY (the key contrast) ⭐
✅ **Equal:** ordinary bills, ordinance approval, choosing ministers/CM, considering constitutional-body reports.
✅ **Unequal (Assembly dominates):**
- Money bill only in the **Assembly**; Council **only recommends**, returns in **14 days**; **Speaker** certifies.
- Ordinary bill: Council can only **delay 4 months** (3 + 1) → **not a revising body like RS, only a "dilatory
  / advisory chamber."**
- Council can't vote demands for grants; **can't pass a no-confidence motion** (CoM responsible only to Assembly).
- Council **doesn't** vote in President's election / RS elections; **no effective say** in ratifying
  constitutional amendments; its very **existence depends on the Assembly**.

⚠️ Council is **much weaker than the Rajya Sabha** (RS reflects the federal element; Council has no federal
role and a heterogeneous composition). Critics: "costly ornamental luxury", "white elephant". Utility: checks
hasty legislation + represents experts.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ All states have a Legislative Council → only **6 are bicameral**.
- ❌ Creating/abolishing a Council is a constitutional amendment → passed by Parliament by **simple majority** (Art 169), on the Assembly's special-majority resolution.
- ❌ The Council is a revising body like the Rajya Sabha → only a **dilatory chamber** (delays ordinary bills max **4 months**).
- ❌ Council can reject a money bill → only **recommends**, returns in **14 days**.
- ❌ Council members are directly elected → **5/6 indirectly elected + 1/6 nominated** by the Governor.
- ❌ Max Council strength = 1/3 of Assembly, min 60 → **min 40**.
- ❌ Council can remove the CoM by no-confidence → **no** (CoM responsible only to the **Assembly**).
