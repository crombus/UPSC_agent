# PART A — ORGANISATION (Art 79)
✅ Parliament = **President + Rajya Sabha (Council of States) + Lok Sabha (House of the People)**. The
President is not a member but is **integral** (summons/prorogues, assents to bills). British legacy of
**parliamentary sovereignty is diluted** in India by a written constitution, federalism, fundamental rights &
judicial review.

## Composition ⭐
| | **Rajya Sabha (RS)** | **Lok Sabha (LS)** |
|---|---|---|
| Max strength | ✅ **250** (238 states/UTs elected + **12 nominated**) | ✅ **550** (530 states + 20 UTs; Anglo-Indian nomination ceased) |
| Sanctioned/elected strength | ✅ **245 sanctioned** | ✅ **543 elected** |
| Election | Indirect — **STV proportional representation** by state MLAs; UTs (only Delhi, Puducherry, J&K) via electoral college | Direct — **universal adult franchise (18 yrs, 61st Amdt 1988)**; **First-Past-The-Post** |
| Nominated | 12 by President (art, literature, science, social service) | *Anglo-Indian 2 (95th Amdt extended to 2020; **104th Amdt 2019 abolished it**)* |
| Nature | ✅ **Permanent** — never dissolved; **1/3 retire every 2 yrs**; term **6 yrs** | ✅ Term **5 yrs**; dissolvable anytime; extendable **1 yr at a time** in national emergency (max 6 months post-emergency) |

⚠️ **Note:** the Anglo-Indian nomination provision was **abolished by the 104th Amendment (2020)** — so present LS has no nominated members.

## Delimitation & seat freeze ⭐
✅ **42nd Amdt (1976)** froze LS seat allocation at **1971 census** till 2000; **84th Amdt (2001)** deferred
readjustment until the relevant figures of the **first census taken after 2026 have been published**;
**87th Amdt (2003)** allowed re-drawing constituencies on **2001 census** *without changing state seat
totals*. ✅ SC/ST seats reserved on population ratio (no separate electorate).

# PART B — MEMBERSHIP & PRESIDING OFFICERS

## Speaker of the Lok Sabha ⭐
✅ Elected by LS from its members; date fixed by President. Vacates if: (1) ceases to be an LS member,
(2) resigns to **Deputy Speaker**, (3) removed by **effective majority** after **14 days' notice**. **Does NOT
vacate on LS dissolution** (continues till new LS meets). Key powers: maintains order; **final interpreter**
of the Constitution/Rules **within the House**; **decides if a bill is a Money Bill (final, Art 110)**;
casting vote in a tie; presides over **joint sittings**; decides anti-defection disqualifications (**Kihoto
Hollohan 1992 — subject to judicial review**); ex-officio chair of the Indian Parliamentary Group. Salary
charged on **Consolidated Fund** (independence).
⚠️ Deputy Speaker: elected by LS, resigns to Speaker; since 2018 the post has been left **vacant** — a
recurring constitutional-propriety debate.

## Rajya Sabha Chairman
✅ The **Vice-President** is ex-officio Chairman (not an RS member); Deputy Chairman is elected from RS
members.

# PART C — SESSIONS & DEVICES

## Sessions & terms
✅ President summons each House; **max gap between two sessions ≤ 6 months** → at least **twice a year**.
Three sessions: **Budget (Feb–May), Monsoon (Jul–Sep), Winter (Nov–Dec)**.
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Adjournment** | suspends a *sitting* (by presiding officer); no effect on pending business |
| ✅ **Adjournment sine die** | terminates a sitting indefinitely (presiding officer) |
| ✅ **Prorogation** | ends a *session* (by **President**); pending business survives, but pending notices lapse |
| ✅ **Dissolution** | ends the *life* of the LS only (RS never dissolves) |
| ✅ **Quorum** | **1/10** of total = **55 LS / 25 RS** |
| ✅ **Lame-duck session** | last session of an outgoing LS |

## Lapsing of bills on LS dissolution ⭐
✅ **Lapses:** bill pending in LS; bill passed by LS but pending in RS. **Does NOT lapse:** bill pending in
RS but not passed by LS; bill passed by both but awaiting President's assent; bill returned by President;
bill for which a **joint sitting** was already notified.

## Devices ⭐
✅ **Question Hour** (first hour) — starred (oral + supplementaries), unstarred (written), short-notice
(<10 days). ✅ **Zero Hour** — informal, **Indian innovation since 1962**, not in the Rules. Motions,
**Adjournment Motion** (censures govt, only in LS, needs 50 members), **No-Confidence Motion** (only LS,
50 members), **Censure/Calling-Attention/Cut Motions** (Policy = Re 1, Economy, Token = ₹100).

# PART D — LEGISLATIVE PROCEDURE ⭐

## Bill types
✅ **Ordinary (any non-financial matter), Money (Art 110), Financial (Art 117), Constitution Amendment
(Art 368).**

## Ordinary bill — 5 stages
✅ First Reading (introduction, no debate) → Second Reading (general discussion + **committee stage** +
consideration clause-by-clause) → Third Reading (accept/reject as whole) → **Second House** (4 options:
pass / amend & return / reject / sit on it 6 months) → **President's assent**. Deadlock → **joint sitting**
(Art 108, presided by **Speaker**; only **3 ever held** — Dowry 1961, Banking Service 1978, POTA 2002).
President has a **suspensive veto** on ordinary bills.

## Money Bill vs Ordinary Bill ⭐
| | **Money Bill (Art 110)** | **Ordinary Bill** |
|---|---|---|
| Introduce | ✅ **Only in LS**, on President's recommendation, by a minister | Either House |
| RS power | ✅ Cannot reject/amend; only recommends; must return in **14 days** | Equal powers |
| Speaker's certificate | ✅ Required, **final** | No |
| Joint sitting | ✅ **No provision** | Yes (Art 108) |
| President | ✅ Assent or withhold — **cannot return** | Can return once |

✅ **Financial bills:** Money bill (Art 110) ⊂ Financial bill (I) Art 117(1) ⊂ Financial bill (II) Art 117(3).
"All money bills are financial bills, but not vice-versa." ⚠️ **Money-bill route controversy** — Aadhaar Act
& Finance Act amendments certified as money bills; SC (Rojer Mathew, Puttaswamy) flagged concerns.

# PART E — PARLIAMENTARY COMMITTEES ⭐

## Financial committees
| Committee | Members | RS repn | Chairman |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Public Accounts (PAC)** | 22 (15 LS + 7 RS) | Yes | From **Opposition** (convention since 1967); examines **CAG** reports (post-mortem) |
| ✅ **Estimates** | 30 (**LS only**) | No | Ruling party; "continuous economy committee"; examines budget estimates |
| ✅ **Public Undertakings (CoPU)** | 22 (15 LS + 7 RS) | Yes | LS member; examines PSUs (Krishna Menon Cttee 1964) |

✅ **Departmentally-Related Standing Committees (DRSCs):** **24** committees (16 LS + 8 RS), **31 members
each** (21 LS + 10 RS); ensure executive accountability; examine demands for grants, bills, annual reports.
Ministers can't be members.
✅ **Ad hoc committees:** Select & Joint committees on bills; JPCs; consultative committees (not parliamentary
committees, chaired by minister).

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ RS elects members by list-PR → **Single Transferable Vote** proportional representation.
- ❌ Rajya Sabha can be dissolved → **permanent**, only 1/3 retire every 2 years.
- ❌ Zero Hour is in the Rules → informal, **not** in the Rules; **Question Hour** is.
- ❌ Money bill can start in either House → **only Lok Sabha**, on President's recommendation.
- ❌ RS can amend a money bill → only **recommend** (LS may reject the recommendations).
- ❌ Prorogation is done by the Speaker → by the **President**; adjournment by the presiding officer.
- ❌ Bill pending in RS lapses on LS dissolution → a bill **pending in RS (not passed by LS)** does **NOT** lapse.
- ❌ Estimates Committee has RS members → **Lok Sabha only** (30 members).
- ❌ PAC chairman is from ruling party → from the **Opposition** (since 1967).
- ❌ Joint sitting for a money bill / constitution amendment → only for **ordinary bills** (Art 108).

## 📰 CA hooks
- 📰 ✅ **Women's Reservation — Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam (106th Amdt, 2023)**: provides 33% seats for
  women in LS + State Assemblies (including within SC/ST-reserved seats). Section 1(2) requires a separate
  commencement notification; none was located as of **19 July 2026**. Operationalisation then depends on
  the constitutionally specified post-commencement census and delimitation sequence.

  is the live federal issue, not an automatic expiry in 2026.
- 📰 ⚠️ **Money-bill route** litigation (Aadhaar, Tribunal rules) pending before a larger SC bench.
- 📰 ⚠️ Vacant **Deputy Speaker** post since 2018 — constitutional-propriety debate.
