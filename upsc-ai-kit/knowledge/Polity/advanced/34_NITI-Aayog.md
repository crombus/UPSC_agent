# PART A — ESTABLISHMENT ⭐
✅ The 65-year-old **Planning Commission** was scrapped on **13 Aug 2014**; **NITI Aayog (National Institution for
Transforming India)** was established on **1 January 2015** as its successor — by an **executive resolution** (so
**non-constitutional & non-statutory / extra-constitutional**).
- ✅ India's **premier policy "Think Tank"** — provides directional & policy inputs; a **"Bharatiya approach to
  development."**
- ✅ Replaces the Planning Commission's **one-way, top-down, command-and-control** flow with a **bottom-up,
  collaborative** partnership of states — **cooperative federalism**.

---

# PART B — COMPOSITION ⭐
| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Chairperson** | The **Prime Minister** |
| ✅ **Governing Council** | **CMs of all states** + CMs of UTs with legislatures (Delhi, Puducherry, J&K) + **LGs of other UTs** |
| ✅ **Regional Councils** | For issues spanning multiple states/regions; formed for a fixed tenure; convened by the PM |
| ✅ **Vice-Chairperson** | Appointed by the PM; **rank of Cabinet Minister** |
| ✅ **Full-time Members** | Rank of **Minister of State** |
| ✅ **Part-time Members** | Max **2** (from universities/research bodies, rotational) |
| ✅ **Ex-officio Members** | Max **4** Union ministers (nominated by the PM) |
| ✅ **CEO** | Appointed by the **PM** for a fixed tenure; **rank of Secretary to the GoI** |
| ✅ **Special Invitees** | Domain experts nominated by the PM |

## Specialised wings ✅
**Research Wing** (in-house think tank) · **Consultancy Wing** (match-making with private/public expertise) ·
**Team India Wing** (representatives of every state & ministry — the liaison for cooperative federalism).

---

# PART C — OBJECTIVES & FUNCTIONS ⭐
✅ **Four core functions:** (1) **Design policy & programme framework**; (2) **Foster cooperative federalism**;
(3) **Monitoring & evaluation**; (4) **Think-tank / Knowledge & Innovation Hub**.
- ✅ Two hubs: **Team India Hub** (cooperative federalism + policy design) & **Knowledge & Innovation Hub**
  (resource centre / best practices).
- ⚠️ **Key limitation:** NITI Aayog **only recommends** — it has **NO power to allocate funds** (that power moved to
  the **Finance Ministry**). It cannot impose plans on states.

---

# PART D — PLANNING COMMISSION vs NITI AAYOG (compare) ⭐⭐
| Point | Planning Commission (1950–2014) | NITI Aayog (2015–) |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Basis | Executive resolution | Executive resolution |
| ✅ Role | Allocated **funds/grants** to states | **Advisory think tank** — no fund allocation |
| ✅ Federal approach | **Top-down** (command) | **Bottom-up** (cooperative) |
| ✅ Plans | Five-Year Plans (imposed) | Strategy/vision docs, no imposition |
| ⚠️ States' role | Passive recipients | **Equal partners** (Governing Council) |
| ⚠️ Members | Full-time economists | PM-led + CMs + experts |

⚠️ **Note:** Five-Year Plans ended with the **12th Plan (2012–17)**; NITI Aayog issued a **3-year Action Agenda**,
a **7-year Strategy** and a **15-year Vision** document instead.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ NITI Aayog is a constitutional/statutory body → it is **neither** (executive resolution).
- ❌ NITI Aayog allocates funds to states → it is **advisory only**; fund allocation is with the **Finance
  Ministry**.
- ❌ The President is the chairperson → the **Prime Minister**.
- ❌ Governing Council excludes UT chiefs → includes **CMs of UTs with legislatures + LGs of other UTs**.
- ❌ NITI Aayog still makes Five-Year Plans → those **ended** (12th Plan 2012–17); it makes vision/strategy docs.
- ❌ The Vice-Chairperson has Cabinet Minister rank but is elected → **appointed by the PM**.
