## 0. Overview
✅ **Part XVIII, Art 352–360.** Purpose: safeguard sovereignty, unity, integrity, security & the
Constitution. During an emergency the **federal structure becomes unitary without a formal amendment** —
Ambedkar: the Constitution can be "**both unitary and federal according to time and circumstances**."
✅ **Three types:** National (Art 352), President's Rule / State Emergency (Art 356), Financial (Art 360).

---

## 1. NATIONAL EMERGENCY (Art 352) ⭐⭐

### Constitutional triggers
✅ Declared when security of India (or part) is threatened by **war, external aggression, or armed
rebellion** — even on **imminent danger**. **"War/external aggression" = External Emergency; "armed
rebellion" = Internal Emergency.** ✅ **44th Amendment (1978)** replaced "**internal disturbance**"
(vague, used in 1975) with "**armed rebellion**." ✅ **42nd Amdt (1976)** allowed limiting it to **part**
of India.

### Safeguards (mostly 44th Amendment) ⭐
| Safeguard | Rule |
|---|---|
| ✅ Recommendation | Only on **written recommendation of the Cabinet** (not PM alone) — post-1975 fix |
| ✅ Parliamentary approval | Within **1 month** (was 2), by **special majority** (was simple) |
| ✅ Duration | **6 months**, renewable **indefinitely** every 6 months |
| ✅ Revocation | President anytime; **must revoke** if **Lok Sabha** (simple majority) disapproves; 1/10 of LS members can force a special sitting in 14 days |
| ✅ Judicial review | 38th Amdt made it immune → **deleted by 44th**; *Minerva Mills (1980)* — reviewable for mala fide/perverse |

### Effects ⭐
**(a) Centre-State:** Centre can direct a state on **'any' matter** (states not suspended); Parliament
legislates on **State List** (laws lapse 6 months after); President can issue **ordinances on state
subjects**; can **modify revenue distribution** (Centre↔states). 42nd Amdt extended executive/legislative
effects to **all states**, not just the emergency one.
**(b) Lok Sabha & assemblies:** LS term extendable **1 year at a time** (max 6 months after emergency
ends) — e.g. **5th Lok Sabha (1971-77)**; same for state assemblies.
**(c) Fundamental Rights:** ⭐
- ✅ **Art 358** — **automatically suspends Art 19** (six freedoms); post-44th Amdt **only during
  War/External Emergency** & only for laws *related* to the emergency.
- ✅ **Art 359** — President may **suspend enforcement** (not the rights themselves) of *specified* FRs;
  post-44th Amdt **Art 20 & 21 can NEVER be suspended**.

### Art 358 vs 359 (7 differences) ⭐
| | Art 358 | Art 359 |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Scope | Only **Art 19** | Any FR in Presidential Order (except 20, 21) |
| ✅ Operation | **Automatic** | Needs Presidential Order |
| ✅ Trigger | **External** Emergency only | External **and** Internal |
| ✅ Duration | Whole emergency | Period specified |
| ✅ Extent | Whole country | Whole or part |

### Declarations (3 times) ⭐
✅ **1962** (China/NEFA, till 1968), **1971** (Pakistan war), **1975** (internal disturbance — Indira
Gandhi, most controversial). ✅ **Shah Commission** (Janata govt) did not justify 1975 → led to the
**44th Amendment 1978**.

## 2. PRESIDENT'S RULE (Art 356) — "State/Constitutional Emergency" ⭐⭐

### Grounds
✅ **Art 355** = Centre's duty to ensure states run per the Constitution. **Art 356** — President
satisfied that state government "**cannot be carried on in accordance with the Constitution**" (on
Governor's report **or otherwise**). **Art 365** — state failing to comply with Centre's directions.

### Approval & Duration ⭐
✅ Approval within **2 months**, by **simple majority**. Continues **6 months**, extendable to **max 3
years** (every 6 months). ✅ **44th Amdt:** beyond **1 year**, extension needs BOTH — (a) a National
Emergency in force, and (b) **ECI certifies** elections can't be held.

### Consequences
✅ President assumes state government's functions + Governor's powers; **dismisses the CoM/CM**;
Parliament exercises state legislature's powers ("**President's Acts**"); assembly **suspended or
dissolved**. ✅ **President CANNOT** take over the **state High Court's** powers.

### Bommai case (1994) — 10 propositions ⭐⭐ (very high-yield)
✅ Proclamation is **subject to judicial review**; satisfaction must rest on **relevant material** (burden
on Centre); court can **restore** a dismissed government; **assembly dissolved only after Parliament
approves** (suspend till then); **secularism is a basic feature** (anti-secular govt → Art 356); majority
tested on the **floor of the House**; a new Centre can't dismiss opposition state governments; Art 356 is
an **exceptional, last-resort** power.
✅ **Ambedkar** hoped Art 356 would remain a "**dead letter**" / last resort — Kamath: "Ambedkar is dead
and the Articles are very much alive." Imposed **125+ times** (often misused).

### National Emergency vs President's Rule ⭐
| | National (352) | President's Rule (356) |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Approval majority | **Special** | **Simple** |
| ✅ Max duration | **Indefinite** | **3 years** |
| ✅ State organs | Continue (concurrent Centre power) | Executive **dismissed**, legislature suspended/dissolved |
| ✅ Effect on FRs | **Yes** | **No** |
| ✅ LS revocation resolution | **Yes** | No (President only) |

## 3. FINANCIAL EMERGENCY (Art 360) ⭐
✅ Declared if **financial stability/credit of India** is threatened. Approval within **2 months** (simple
majority); once approved, continues **indefinitely** (no renewal needed). ✅ **Effects:** Centre directs
states on **canons of financial propriety**; can **reduce salaries** of state employees AND **SC/HC
judges** & Union employees; reserve state money bills for President. ✅ **Never declared** (closest: 1991
crisis). ✅ Modelled on the US **National Recovery Act 1933** (Great Depression) — per Ambedkar.

## 4. Criticism (Constituent Assembly)
✅ **H.V. Kamath** — "foundation of a totalitarian/police state." **K.T. Shah** — "chapter of reaction and

states' financial autonomy. ✅ Defenders: **Alladi Krishnaswami Ayyar** — "**very life-breath of the
Constitution**"; **Mahabir Tyagi** — "**safety-valve**."

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ National Emergency can be declared on "internal disturbance" → replaced by "**armed rebellion**" (44th Amdt).
- ❌ Art 20 & 21 can be suspended in emergency → **never** (post-44th Amdt).
- ❌ Art 358 operates in armed-rebellion emergencies → **only War/External** Emergency.
- ❌ President's Rule needs special majority → **simple majority**; National Emergency needs **special**.
- ❌ President's Rule has no time limit → **max 3 years**; National Emergency **is** indefinite.
- ❌ Financial Emergency needs periodic renewal → **once approved, continues indefinitely**.
- ❌ During President's Rule, President takes over the High Court → **cannot** touch the HC.
- ❌ PM alone can advise National Emergency → needs **written Cabinet recommendation** (44th Amdt).
