## 1. Federal vs unitary
✅ **Unitary** = all powers in the national govt (regions derive authority from it) — Britain, France,
Japan, China. **Federal** = powers **divided by the Constitution itself**; both levels operate
independently — USA, Switzerland, Australia, Canada, Russia. ✅ "Federation" ← Latin **foedus** =
"treaty/agreement". Units called states (US), cantons (Switzerland), provinces (Canada), republics
(Russia).

✅ **Two ways to form a federation:** (i) **integration** — weak states unite into a strong union
(**USA**, oldest federation, 1787, "coming together"); (ii) **disintegration** — a big unitary state
grants autonomy to provinces (**Canada** 1867, "holding together"). ✅ **India followed the Canadian
route** (disintegration → strong centre).

✅ India adopted federalism due to (i) **large size** and (ii) **socio-cultural diversity** — reconciling
**national unity with regional autonomy**. ✅ **"Federation" is NEVER used** in the Constitution — Art 1
says **"Union of States"** (Ambedkar: not an agreement, no right to secede → indestructible).
✅ Indian model = **Canadian** (strong centre), not American: resemblance in (i) formation by
disintegration, (ii) term "Union", (iii) centralising tendency.

## 2. Federal features (7) ⭐
| # | Feature | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ 1 | **Dual polity** | Union at centre + states at periphery, each sovereign in its field |
| ✅ 2 | **Written Constitution** | Lengthiest in world; defines structure/powers/limits of both levels |
| ✅ 3 | **Division of powers** | 7th Schedule — **Union List (98), State List (59), Concurrent List (52)**; residuary → **Centre** |
| ✅ 4 | **Supremacy of Constitution** | Highest law; laws violating it struck down via judicial review |
| ✅ 5 | **Rigid Constitution** | Federal provisions need **special majority + ratification by half the states** |
| ✅ 6 | **Independent judiciary** | SC guards Constitution + settles Centre-state/inter-state disputes |
| ✅ 7 | **Bicameralism** | Rajya Sabha (states) + Lok Sabha (people); RS maintains federal equilibrium |

## 3. Unitary features (14) ⭐⭐ (very high-yield)
| # | Feature | Key point |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ 1 | **Strong Centre** | Union List longer + more important subjects; Centre overrides Concurrent; **residuary to Centre** (in US → states) |
| ✅ 2 | **States not indestructible** | Parliament can alter area/boundary/name by **simple majority** → "indestructible Union of **destructible states**" |
| ✅ 3 | **Single Constitution** | States can't frame own constitution (exception: pre-2019 J&K) |
| ✅ 4 | **Flexibility** | Bulk amendable unilaterally by Parliament; only Centre can initiate amendment (US → states can too) |
| ✅ 5 | **No equal state representation** | Rajya Sabha seats by **population (1 to 31)**; US Senate = 2 per state |
| ✅ 6 | **Emergency provisions** | National/state/financial — converts federal → unitary **without amendment** |
| ✅ 7 | **Single citizenship** | Like Canada; no separate state citizenship |
| ✅ 8 | **Integrated judiciary** | SC → HCs, one system enforcing both Central & state laws (US = double courts) |
| ✅ 9 | **All-India Services** | IAS/IPS/IFS common to Centre & states, controlled by Centre — "violate federalism" |
| ✅ 10 | **Integrated audit** | CAG audits states too; appointed/removed by President without consulting states |
| ✅ 11 | **Parliament's authority over State List** | Art 249 — RS resolution lets Parliament legislate on State List in national interest (no emergency needed) |
| ✅ 12 | **Appointment of Governor** | Appointed by President, holds office at his pleasure, acts as **Centre's agent** (US → elected) |
| ✅ 13 | **Integrated election machinery** | ECI runs Central & state elections; states have no say |
| ✅ 14 | **Veto over state bills** | Governor reserves bills for President, who has **absolute veto** (not suspensive) |

## 4. Critical evaluation — scholar tags ⭐⭐ (PYQ favourite)
| Scholar | Characterisation |
|---|---|
| ✅ **K.C. Wheare** | **"Quasi-federal"** — "unitary state with subsidiary federal features" |
| ✅ **K. Santhanam** | **Unitary bias** from (i) Centre's financial dominance, (ii) erstwhile Planning Commission |
| ✅ **Paul Appleby** | **"Extremely federal"** |
| ✅ **Morris Jones** | **"Bargaining federalism"** |
| ✅ **Ivor Jennings** | **"Federation with a strong centralising tendency"** |
| ✅ **Alexandrowicz** | **"Sui generis"** (unique) |
| ✅ **Granville Austin** | **"Cooperative federalism"** — "a new kind of federation for India's needs" |

✅ **Ambedkar:** Constitution is federal (dual polity; both derive authority from the Constitution) but
"can be **both unitary and federal** according to the requirements of time and circumstances."
✅ **S.R. Bommai v. UoI (1994):** Constitution is **federal** and federalism is a **basic feature**;
states are **not mere appendages** — "within the sphere allotted to them, the states are supreme."
✅ Indian federalism = compromise between (i) normal division of powers (state autonomy) & (ii) national
integrity + strong Union in exceptional circumstances.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ The word "Federation" appears in the Constitution → **never**; Art 1 uses **"Union of States"**.
- ❌ India follows the American federal model → **Canadian** model (strong centre, disintegration, "Union").
- ❌ Residuary powers rest with states → with the **Centre** (opposite of US).
- ❌ Wheare called India "federal" → **"quasi-federal"**.
- ❌ Cooperative federalism was coined by Ambedkar → by **Granville Austin**.
- ❌ Rajya Sabha gives equal seats to states → by **population (1–31)**; only the US Senate is equal.
- ❌ Bommai diluted federalism → it **affirmed** federalism as a **basic feature**.
- ❌ USA = "coming together", India = same → India = **"holding together"** (disintegration).
