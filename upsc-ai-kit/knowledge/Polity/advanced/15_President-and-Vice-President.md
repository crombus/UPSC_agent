# PART A — THE PRESIDENT (Art 52–62, 72, 74)

## 1. Election (Art 54–55) ⭐
✅ **Indirect** election by an **electoral college** = **elected** members of both Houses of Parliament +
**elected** members of state legislative assemblies + **elected** members of **Delhi & Puducherry**
assemblies (added by **70th Amdt 1992**). **Nominated members and MLCs do NOT vote.**
✅ System: **proportional representation by single transferable vote, secret ballot** → winner needs an
**absolute majority (quota)**. ✅ Value of an MLA's vote uses **1971 census population** (frozen by **84th
Amdt 2001** till after 2026). Disputes → **Supreme Court** (final). ✅ Indirect election chosen because
President is a **nominal executive** and direct election would be costly for a symbolic head.

## 2. Qualifications, oath, conditions
✅ Citizen of India · **35 years** · qualified for **Lok Sabha** · no office of profit. Nomination:
**50 proposers + 50 seconders**, deposit **₹15,000** (forfeited if <1/6 votes). ✅ Oath administered by
**CJI** (or senior-most SC judge). Immune from criminal proceedings during term; civil proceedings only
after **2 months' notice**.

## 3. Term, impeachment, vacancy ⭐
✅ **5-year** term; resigns to **Vice-President**; eligible for **any number of re-elections** (US = max
twice). ✅ **Impeachment (Art 61)** — only ground = **"violation of the Constitution"** (undefined).
Charge by **1/4 members** of either House, **14 days' notice**, passed by **2/3 of total membership** of
both Houses. ⭐ **Nominated members CAN participate in impeachment** (though not in election); **state
MLAs CANNOT** (though they vote in election). No President impeached so far.
✅ **Vacancy:** if term expires, outgoing President continues till successor takes charge (no interregnum,
VP does not act). If death/resignation/removal → VP acts, election within **6 months**.

## 4. Powers & functions ⭐
| Type | Highlights |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Executive** | All Union executive action in his name; appoints PM, ministers, AG, CAG, CEC & ECs, UPSC, Governors, Finance Commission |
| ✅ **Legislative** | Summon/prorogue Parliament, dissolve LS, joint sitting; **nominates 12 RS members** (art/science/literature/social service); assent to bills; ordinances |
| ✅ **Financial** | Money bills need his prior recommendation; lays **Union Budget**; Contingency Fund advances; constitutes **Finance Commission** |
| ✅ **Judicial** | Appoints CJI/SC/HC judges; seeks SC advice (Art 143, **not binding**); **pardoning power (Art 72)** |
| ✅ **Diplomatic** | Treaties/agreements in his name (subject to Parliament) |
| ✅ **Military** | **Supreme Commander** of defence forces; appoints service chiefs |
| ✅ **Emergency** | Art 352, 356/365, 360 |

> Note: **Anglo-Indian nomination (2 to LS)** lapsed via **104th Amdt (2020)**. ⚠️

## 5. Veto power (Art 111) ⭐⭐
✅ Three alternatives: assent · withhold · return (not money bill). India's President has **absolute,
suspensive, pocket** vetoes — **NO qualified veto** (that's the US President).
| Veto | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Absolute** | Withholds assent, bill dies | Rajendra Prasad — PEPSU Appropriation Bill (1954); Venkataraman — MP Salary Bill (1991) |
| ✅ **Suspensive** | Returns bill; overridden by **ordinary** majority re-passage (US needs higher) | — |
| ✅ **Pocket** | Sits on bill indefinitely (no time limit) | **Zail Singh — Indian Post Office (Amendment) Bill, 1986** |

✅ **No veto over a constitutional amendment bill** — **24th Amdt (1971)** made assent **obligatory**.
✅ Over **state bills reserved by Governor** (Art 201): assent / withhold / direct return — but **NOT bound**
to assent even if re-passed (state legislature cannot override); can also **pocket veto**.

## 6. Ordinance-making power (Art 123) ⭐
✅ Most important legislative power; ordinances = temporary laws with the force of an Act. **Limits:**
(1) only when a House is **not in session**; (2) only on subjects Parliament can legislate & **cannot
abridge FRs**; (3) must be laid before Parliament, **ceases 6 weeks** after reassembly (max life ≈ **6
months + 6 weeks**); (4) **cannot amend the Constitution**. ✅ *Cooper case (1970)* — satisfaction
justiciable on **mala fide**; ✅ *D.C. Wadhwa (1987)* — **re-promulgation** of ordinances is a "fraud on
the Constitution."

## 7. Pardoning power (Art 72) ⭐
✅ **Pardon, reprieve, respite, remission, commutation.** President can pardon **court-martial** sentences
and **death sentences** — Governor (Art 161) **cannot** do either (but has concurrent power to
suspend/remit/commute death). Exercised on **Cabinet's advice**; subject to judicial review only if
arbitrary/mala fide.

## 8. Constitutional position (Art 53, 74, 75) ⭐⭐
✅ **Nominal executive** — Ambedkar: "head of the State but not of the Executive… symbol of the nation."
✅ **42nd Amdt (1976):** advice of CoM made **binding**. ✅ **44th Amdt (1978):** President can **return
once** for reconsideration, but **reconsidered advice is binding**. (K.R. Narayanan used this over UP 1997
& Bihar 1998 President's Rule.)
✅ **Situational discretion** (no constitutional discretion): appointing a PM in a **hung Lok Sabha**;
dismissing a CoM that can't prove confidence; dissolving LS when CoM has lost majority.

# PART B — THE VICE-PRESIDENT (Art 63–71) ⭐

## Election & qualifications
✅ Electoral college = **members of both Houses of Parliament ONLY** — includes **nominated members**,
**excludes state MLAs** (Ambedkar: VP mainly presides over Rajya Sabha). Same PR-STV secret ballot.
✅ Qualifications: citizen · **35 years** · qualified for **Rajya Sabha** · no office of profit. Nomination:
**20 proposers + 20 seconders**, deposit **₹15,000**. Oath administered by the **President**.

## Term, removal, functions
✅ **5-year** term; resigns to **President**. **Removal:** resolution passed by **effective majority** of
**Rajya Sabha** + agreed by **Lok Sabha** (simple majority); **can only be initiated in Rajya Sabha**;
14 days' notice; **no ground required** (no formal impeachment). ✅ **Functions:** (1) **ex-officio Chairman
of Rajya Sabha**; (2) **acts as President** on a vacancy for **max 6 months**. ✅ Unlike the **US VP**,
the Indian VP does **NOT** become President for the unexpired term — only **acts** until a new President is
elected. Called "**His Superfluous Highness**" by some scholars.

---

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ State MLAs vote in the VP election → **only MPs** vote for VP; MLAs vote for President.
- ❌ Nominated MPs vote in the presidential election → **no** (only elected); but they **can** vote in impeachment.
- ❌ Indian President has a qualified veto → he has **absolute/suspensive/pocket**; **qualified = US President**.
- ❌ President can veto a constitutional amendment bill → **must assent** (24th Amdt).
- ❌ Governor can pardon a death sentence → **only the President** can (Art 72 vs 161).
- ❌ VP is removed by impeachment → by an **RS effective-majority resolution + LS agreement**, no ground stated.
- ❌ VP succeeds as President for the full unexpired term → only **acts for max 6 months** (unlike US VP).
- ❌ SC's advice under Art 143 binds the President → **not binding**.
- ❌ President's ordinance can amend the Constitution → **cannot**.
