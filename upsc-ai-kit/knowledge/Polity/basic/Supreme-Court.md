## Core idea
✅ The Supreme Court is the **guardian of the Constitution** and **guarantor of Fundamental Rights**. India
has **one integrated judiciary** enforcing both Central and state laws (unlike the US double system).

> 🔑 **Qualification:** Citizen + **5 yrs HC judge / 10 yrs HC advocate / distinguished jurist** — **no minimum age.**

## The Collegium (must remember) ⭐
✅ Judges appointed by the **President** but effectively by the **collegium** = **CJI + 4 seniormost judges**
(Second & Third Judges cases). The **NJAC (99th Amdt 2014)** was **struck down in 2015** (Fourth Judges Case)
→ collegium restored.

## Five jurisdictions
✅ **Original** (federal disputes, exclusive) · **Writ** (only for FRs, Art 32) · **Appellate** (incl. **SLP,
Art 136**) · **Advisory** (Art 143, *not binding*) · **Court of Record** (Art 129, contempt power).

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ **Judicial Review** — power to strike down laws violating the Constitution; part of the **basic structure**.
- ✅ HC's writ power (Art 226) is **wider** than SC's (Art 32).
- ✅ **No SC judge ever removed** (Ramaswami 1993 survived).
- ✅ **PIL** introduced early 1980s by **Justices Krishna Iyer & Bhagwati** (relaxed *locus standi*).
- ✅ SC law is **binding on all courts** (Art 141); it can review its own judgments.
