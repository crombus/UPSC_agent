## Core idea
**National integration** is the sense of unity above region/religion/caste/language; the **National Integration
Council (NIC, 1961)** is a **non-statutory advisory** forum (chaired by the PM) to fight communalism/casteism/
regionalism. **India's foreign policy** flows from **Art 51 (DPSP)** and rests on **non-alignment, Panchsheel,
anti-colonialism/racialism, disarmament and support to the UN**.

> 🔑 **Panchsheel:** territorial respect · non-aggression · non-interference · equality · peaceful co-existence.
> 🔑 India **did NOT sign NPT (1968) or CTBT (1996)** — "discriminatory."
> 🔑 NIC = **advisory, not constitutional/statutory**; often criticised as **ineffective**.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ Panchsheel = **1954**, with **China** (Tibet Treaty).
- ✅ India snapped ties with **South Africa (1954)** over apartheid.
- ✅ India joined the **UN in 1945**; **Vijaya Lakshmi Pandit** was UNGA President (1953).
- ✅ India helped form **SAARC (1985)**, G-77, BIMSTEC.

## 📰 Current link
📰 The **NIC is defunct** (last met **2013**, not reconstituted since 2014). India has shifted from **non-alignment
to "multi-alignment / strategic autonomy"** (QUAD, SCO, BRICS). **G20 Presidency 2023** (African Union added) and
the **"Voice of the Global South"**; India presses for a **permanent UNSC seat**.
