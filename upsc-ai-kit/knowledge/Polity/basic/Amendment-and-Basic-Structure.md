# Amendment of the Constitution & Basic Structure — BASIC

> **Subject:** Polity · **Tier:** Basic (foundation) · **GS Paper:** GS-II
> **Grounded in:** Indian Polity by M. Laxmikant (Part XX, Article 368)
> ✅ = from source book · ⚠️ = inference / standard knowledge · 📰 = current affairs

---

## 1. Snapshot

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Article | ✅ **368** (Part XX) |
| Nature | ✅ Neither too flexible (Britain) nor too rigid (USA) — a **synthesis** |
| Power type | ✅ **Constituent power** of Parliament |
| Limit | ✅ Cannot alter the **basic structure** (*Kesavananda*, 1973) |

## 2. Core idea

✅ Only **Parliament** can initiate an amendment (not states). Bill passed by **special majority**;
federal provisions also need **half the states** to ratify. President **must** give assent
(24th Amdt made this compulsory). Parliament can amend **any** part — including FR — **but not** the
basic structure.

## 3. Three ways to amend

| Way | Majority | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Simple majority** (outside Art 368) | Members present & voting | New states, citizenship, legislative councils, 5th/6th Schedule |
| ✅ **Special majority** | Total-membership + 2/3 present & voting | FR, DPSP, most provisions |
| ✅ **Special majority + half states** | Above + ratification by ½ states | President's election, SC/HC, Centre-state powers, **GST Council**, 7th Schedule |

> 🔑 Trap: state ratification is by **simple** majority, and only **half** the states (USA = 3/4).
> **No joint sitting** for amendment deadlocks.

## 4. Basic Structure — the case sequence ⭐

| Case | Year | What it said |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Shankari Prasad | 1951 | Parliament **can** amend FR |
| ✅ Golak Nath | 1967 | Parliament **cannot** amend FR |
| ✅ 24th Amendment | 1971 | Parliament **can** amend FR (reversed Golak Nath) |
| ✅ **Kesavananda Bharati** | 1973 | Can amend FR **but not basic structure** (13-judge bench) |
| ✅ Minerva Mills | 1980 | Judicial review & limited amending power = basic features |

## 5. Some basic-structure elements

✅ Supremacy of Constitution · secularism · federalism · separation of powers · **judicial review** ·
rule of law · free & fair elections · independence of judiciary · FR-DPSP balance.

## 6. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Amendment power = **Art 368**, Part XX; only **Parliament** initiates.
- ✅ **Special majority** = total membership + 2/3 present & voting.
- ✅ **Kesavananda Bharati (1973)** = basic-structure doctrine (13 judges).
- ✅ President **must** assent to amendment bills (24th Amdt, 1971).
- ✅ **No joint sitting** for constitutional amendment bills.

## 7. 📰 Current link

📰 2023 = **50 years of Kesavananda**. VP Dhankhar questioned the doctrine as diluting parliamentary
sovereignty; judiciary reaffirmed that Indian Parliament is **not supreme** — bound by judicial review.
