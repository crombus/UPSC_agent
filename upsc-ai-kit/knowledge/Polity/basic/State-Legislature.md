## Snapshot
| Item | Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha) | Legislative Council (Vidhan Parishad) |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | ✅ Lower/popular, **directly elected** | ✅ Upper, **indirect + nominated**, permanent |
| **Strength** | ✅ Normally **60–500**, with special constitutional/statutory exceptions for smaller states | ✅ max 1/3 of Assembly, min 40 |
| **Term** | 5 yrs, dissolvable (like LS) | Permanent, 1/3 retire every 2 yrs (like RS) |
| **Articles** | 168–212 | |

## Core idea
✅ Most states are **unicameral**; only **6 are bicameral** — **AP, Telangana, UP, Bihar, Maharashtra,
Karnataka**. A Council can be **created or abolished** (Art 169) by Parliament (simple majority) if the
**Assembly passes a special-majority resolution** — it is **not** a constitutional amendment.

> 🔑 **Council composition (1/3–1/12–1/12–1/3–1/6):** local bodies **1/3**, graduates **1/12**, teachers
> **1/12**, MLAs **1/3**, Governor nominates **1/6**. → 5/6 elected + 1/6 nominated.

## The Council is weak — remember
✅ Only a **dilatory chamber** (delays ordinary bills max **4 months**), NOT a revising body like the Rajya
Sabha. Money bill only in the **Assembly**; Council **can't** vote grants or pass a no-confidence motion. Its
existence depends on the Assembly.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ 6 bicameral states (mnemonic: **KATBUM** — Karnataka, AP, Telangana, Bihar, UP, Maharashtra).
- ✅ CoM is responsible **only to the Assembly**.
- ✅ Council members elected by **STV proportional representation**.
- ✅ Readjustment remains deferred until relevant figures of the **first census taken after 2026
  have been published** (84th Amdt); there is no automatic 2026 delimitation.
