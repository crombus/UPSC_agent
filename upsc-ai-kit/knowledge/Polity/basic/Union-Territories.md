## Core idea
A UT is centrally administered by the **President** through an **Administrator (LG/Administrator)** — his **agent**,
not a head of state. Some UTs have their own assembly + CoM, but **Parliament can still legislate on any subject
(incl. State List)** for them.

> 🔑 **8 UTs:** A&N · Chandigarh · **DNH-DD** · Delhi · J&K · Ladakh · Lakshadweep · Puducherry.
> 🔑 DNH + Daman & Diu **merged 26 Jan 2020**; **J&K + Ladakh** carved out **2019** (Ladakh = no legislature).

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ Administrator is an **agent of the President**; designation LG / Chief Commissioner / Administrator.
- ✅ Assemblies with legislature: **Puducherry, Delhi, J&K**.
- ✅ **Delhi (Art 239-AA, 69th Amdt 1991):** NCT, 70-member assembly; **CM appointed by President**; assembly
  excludes **public order, police, land**.
- ✅ Only **Delhi** has its own High Court.
- ✅ **President's Regulations** govern A&N, Lakshadweep, DNH-DD, Ladakh.
