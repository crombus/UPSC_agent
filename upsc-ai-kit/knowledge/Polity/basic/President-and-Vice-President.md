## President — core idea
✅ **Nominal executive** (Ambedkar: "head of the State, not of the Executive"). Elected by **PR-STV,
secret ballot**; MLA vote value uses **1971 census** (84th Amdt). ✅ **42nd Amdt** made CoM advice
**binding**; **44th Amdt** lets him **return once**, then it's binding.

## Impeachment (Art 61)
> 🔑 Only ground = **"violation of the Constitution."** Charge by **1/4 members**, passed by **2/3 of
> total membership of BOTH Houses**. **Nominated MPs can join impeachment** (but not the election).

## Powers — 7 heads
> 🔑 **Mnemonic:** **"E-L-F-J-D-M-E"** = **E**xecutive · **L**egislative · **F**inancial · **J**udicial ·
> **D**iplomatic · **M**ilitary · **E**mergency. Nominates **12 Rajya Sabha** members; **Supreme
> Commander** of armed forces.

## Veto (Art 111)
✅ President has **Absolute, Suspensive, Pocket** vetoes — **NO qualified veto** (that's the US President).
✅ **Pocket veto example: Zail Singh — Indian Post Office Bill (1986).** ✅ **Must assent** to a
constitutional amendment bill (24th Amdt).

## Ordinance (Art 123) & Pardon (Art 72)
✅ Ordinance only when a House is **not in session**; lapses **6 weeks** after reassembly; **can't amend
Constitution**. ✅ Only the **President** (not Governor) can pardon a **death sentence** or a
**court-martial** sentence.

## Vice-President
✅ **Ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha**; **acts as President for max 6 months** on a vacancy. ✅ Unlike
the **US VP**, does **not** take over for the full unexpired term. Removal resolution starts **only in
Rajya Sabha**; **no ground needed**.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ President's oath → by **CJI**; VP's oath → by **President**.
- ✅ Only **Dr. Rajendra Prasad** served two full presidential terms.
- ✅ *D.C. Wadhwa (1987):* re-promulgating ordinances = "fraud on the Constitution."

## 📰 Current link
📰 ✅ **Current Art 200/201 position (20 Nov 2025 Article 143 opinion):** courts
cannot create rigid timelines or automatic deemed assent, but prolonged, unexplained and
indefinite gubernatorial inaction remains open to limited judicial review and mandamus.
