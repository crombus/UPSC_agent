## Core idea
The Finance Commission is the **"balancing wheel of fiscal federalism"** — it recommends how central taxes are
shared between the **Centre and states (vertical)** and **among states (horizontal)**, plus grants-in-aid. Its
advice is advisory but rarely rejected.

> 🔑 **Composition = 1 + 4** (chairman + 4 members) — **reappointment allowed** (unlike UPSC/EC).
> 🔑 **Vertical** = Centre↔states split · **Horizontal** = state↔state split.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ Chairman = experience in **public affairs**; members from **HC judge / finance-accounts / administration /
  economics**.
- ✅ Recommends **tax devolution, grants-in-aid, and measures for panchayat/municipality funds** (via SFC).
- ✅ **"Net proceeds"** = tax minus cost of collection, certified by the **CAG**.
- ✅ Report → President → both Houses with **action-taken memo**.
- ✅ **Planning Commission** (which undermined the FC) replaced by **NITI Aayog in 2015**.
