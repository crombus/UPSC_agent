## Core idea
The 73rd Amendment gave **constitutional status** to **Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs)** — a **3-tier**
(village–intermediate–district) system of local self-government, with mandatory elections, reservations, and a
5-year term, so panchayats no longer depend on the state government's will.

> 🔑 **Mnemonic "GET-RD-FE"** — **G**ram Sabha · **E**lections (direct seats at all 3 tiers) · **T**hree-tier ·
> **R**eservation (SC/ST + 1/3 women) · **D**uration 5 yrs · **F**inance Commission (SFC) · **E**lection
> Commission (SEC).

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ **3-tier** system; a state **< 20 lakh** population may skip the **intermediate** level.
- ✅ **All territorial seats** at all three levels are directly elected; chairpersons at
  intermediate/district levels are elected indirectly, the village-chair method is left to
  state law, and legislatures may provide representation for MPs/MLAs and others under Art 243-C.
- ✅ Reservation: SC/ST by population + **not less than 1/3 seats for women** (many states now **50%**).
- ✅ **5-year term**; re-election within **6 months** of dissolution.
- ✅ Candidate minimum age = **21** (not 25).
- ✅ **State Election Commission** conducts polls; **State Finance Commission** every 5 years.
- ✅ **PESA Act 1996** extends Part IX to **Fifth Schedule (tribal) areas**.
- ✅ Not applicable to **Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram** & scheduled/tribal areas.
