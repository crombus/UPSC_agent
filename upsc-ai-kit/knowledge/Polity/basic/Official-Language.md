## Snapshot
| Item | Fact |
|---|---|
| Official language of Union | **Hindi (Devanagari)** — **Art 343** |

## Core idea
**Hindi in Devanagari** is the **official language of the Union** (Art 343) — but **not a "national language."**
English was to end in **1965**, yet the **Official Languages Act 1963** keeps it **indefinitely**. States may choose
their own official language (**not limited to the 8th Schedule**).

> 🔑 **No national language** — only an **official** one. Big UPSC trap.
> 🔑 8th Schedule = **22** langs. Additions: **Sindhi (1967)** · **Konkani/Manipuri/Nepali (1992)** ·
> **Bodo/Dogri/Maithili/Santhali (2003)**.
> 🔑 SC/HC and authoritative legislative texts ordinarily use **English**. Under Art
> 348(2), a Governor with the President's consent may authorise Hindi/another state official
> language in HC proceedings; **Official Languages Act, 1963 s.7** separately permits it in
> HC judgments/decrees/orders if accompanied by an authorised English translation.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ First Official Language Commission = **B.G. Kher (1955)**; examined by **G.B. Pant** committee.
- ✅ Committee of Parliament on Official Language = **30 members (20 LS + 10 RS)**, chaired by **Home Minister**.
- ✅ **Art 351:** Union duty to develop Hindi so it may serve as a **medium of expression
  for all elements of India's composite culture**.
