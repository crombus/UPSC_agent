## Snapshot
| Article | What it does |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Art 1** | India = **"Union of States"**; territory = states + UTs + acquired territory |
| ✅ **Art 2** | Admit / establish **NEW** states (outside the Union) |
| ✅ **Art 3** | Reorganise **EXISTING** states (form/rename/alter boundary/area) |
| ✅ **Art 4** | Such laws = **simple majority**, NOT an Art 368 amendment |

## Core idea
✅ **"Union of States"** (Ambedkar): the federation is **not from an agreement** and states have **no
right to secede** → **indestructible union of destructible states**. Parliament can redraw the map
under Art 3 **without state consent** (President only *refers* the bill to the state legislature; its
views are **not binding**).

✅ **Berubari case (1960):** giving Indian territory to a **foreign country** is **cession** → needs a
**constitutional amendment (Art 368)**, not Art 3 → led to the **9th Amendment**.

## Commissions (in order)
✅ **Dhar Commission (1948)** → admin convenience → **JVP Committee (1948-49)** → rejected language →
**Fazl Ali Commission (1953-55)** → accepted language, rejected "one language-one state".

> 🔑 **Mnemonic (commissions):** **"Dhar Just Fell"** = **D**har → **J**VP → **F**azl Ali.
> 🔑 **Art 2 vs 3:** "**2 = New (outside)**, **3 = old (inside)**."

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ **552** princely states; **3 holdouts** = **Hyderabad, Junagarh, Kashmir**.
- ✅ **100th Amendment (2015):** India-Bangladesh enclave exchange (111 out, 51 in).
- ✅ First state renamed = **United Provinces → Uttar Pradesh (1950)**.
- ✅ Book count (2019): **28 states, 9 UTs**.
- ⚠️📰 **Current (post-2020): 28 states, 8 UTs** — DNH and Daman & Diu **merged into one UT** in Jan 2020.
