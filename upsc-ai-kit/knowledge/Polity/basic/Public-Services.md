## Core idea
India has **three tiers** of services — **All-India (IAS/IPS/IFoS), Central, and State**. AIS officers serve **both**
Centre and states but are **recruited by the Centre**. Civil servants hold office at the **pleasure** of the
President/Governor (**Art 310**), but **Art 311** protects them from arbitrary dismissal.

> 🔑 **Art 312 = RAJYA SABHA** resolution (2/3 present & voting) to create a new AIS — protects **states**.
> 🔑 **Art 311's two shields:** (1) no dismissal by a **subordinate** authority; (2) **inquiry + hearing** required.
> 🔑 3 exceptions to the inquiry: **criminal conviction · not practicable · security of State**.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ **ICS→IAS, IP→IPS** in 1947; **IFoS** = third AIS (1966).
- ✅ **All-India Services Act, 1951** governs the AIS; up to **33⅓%** senior posts by state-service promotion.
- ✅ **All-India Judicial Service (AIJS)** — enabled by 42nd Amdt but **never created**; post ≥ District Judge.
- ✅ **42nd Amdt (1976)** removed the second-stage (punishment) hearing under Art 311.
