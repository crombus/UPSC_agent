## Core idea
✅ Executive (real = **PM + Council of Ministers**) is **drawn from and answerable to** the legislature,
and stays only while it holds the **Lok Sabha's confidence**. President = **nominal** head; PM = **real**
head. Art 74 advice is **binding** on the President.

## The 8 features
> 🔑 **Mnemonic:** **"N-M-C-P-D-L-D-S"** = **N**ominal/real executives · **M**ajority-party rule ·
> **C**ollective responsibility · **P**olitical homogeneity · **D**ouble membership · **L**eadership of PM ·
> **D**issolution of Lok Sabha · **S**ecrecy.

✅ **Collective responsibility (Art 75)** is the **bedrock** — the whole ministry falls on a
**no-confidence motion** in the Lok Sabha ("swim and sink together").
✅ **Double membership:** a minister not an MP for **6 months** ceases to hold office.

## Parliamentary vs Presidential
| | Parliamentary | Presidential (USA) |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Executive | Dual (nominal+real) | Single |
| ✅ Term | Not fixed | **Fixed** |
| ✅ Powers | **Fusion** | **Separation** |
| ✅ Dissolve lower house | Yes | No |

## Why India chose it
✅ **Ambedkar:** preferred **more responsibility** over more stability. Plus **familiarity** (K.M.
Munshi), avoiding legislature-executive conflict, and **wide representation** for a plural society.
✅ **Swaran Singh Committee (1975)** → keep the parliamentary system.

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ India = **republican** (elected President); Britain = **monarchical** (hereditary).
- ✅ Britain has **parliamentary sovereignty**; **India's Parliament is NOT supreme**.
- ✅ UK PM must be from **Commons**; **Indian PM may be from either House**.
- ✅ Jennings: "leader of the opposition is the **alternative PM**." Bagehot: cabinet is a **"hyphen"**.
