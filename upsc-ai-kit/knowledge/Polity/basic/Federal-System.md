## Core idea
✅ Powers are **divided by the Constitution itself** between Centre & states (federal), but the balance
is deliberately **tilted toward the Centre** (unitary bias). Hence India is **federal in form, unitary in
spirit** — a "**quasi-federal**" state.

## 7 Federal features
> 🔑 **Mnemonic:** **"Dual Writing Divides Supreme Rigid Independent Bicameral"** = **D**ual polity ·
> **W**ritten Constitution · **D**ivision of powers · **S**upremacy of Constitution · **R**igidity ·
> **I**ndependent judiciary · **B**icameralism.

## Unitary features (the 14) — high-yield
✅ **Strong Centre · destructible states · single Constitution · flexibility · unequal RS seats ·
emergency provisions · single citizenship · integrated judiciary · All-India Services · integrated
audit (CAG) · Parliament over State List (Art 249) · Governor as Centre's agent · integrated ECI ·
President's veto over state bills.**

## Must-Know Facts
- ✅ India = "indestructible Union of **destructible states**"; USA = "…of indestructible states".
- ✅ **Residuary powers → Centre** (opposite of USA).
- ✅ **S.R. Bommai (1994):** federalism is a **basic feature**; states are **not appendages**.
- ✅ Only exception to single Constitution was **pre-2019 J&K**.
