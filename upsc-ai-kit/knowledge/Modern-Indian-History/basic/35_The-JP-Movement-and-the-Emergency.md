## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — an economic and political crisis produced a mass movement.**

- ✅ By 1973 **inflation, unemployment, food scarcity, drought and the oil shock** had eroded Indira Gandhi's popularity.
- ✅ Student agitations in **Gujarat (Nav Nirman)** and **Bihar** grew into a nationwide protest under **Jayaprakash Narayan (JP)**, who called for "**Total Revolution (Sampoorna Kranti)**."
- ✅ The **1974 railway strike** and the movement's momentum challenged the government directly.
- ⚠️ The movement drew on **students, the middle class, traders and the intelligentsia** rather than the rural or urban poor.

**Foundation — the Emergency (1975–77) suspended democracy, then democracy reasserted itself.**

- ✅ The **Allahabad High Court judgment (12 June 1975)** unseating Indira triggered the declaration of a national **Emergency on 26 June 1975** under Article 352.
- ✅ Civil liberties were suspended; the press was censored; opposition leaders were jailed under **MISA**; the **42nd Amendment** expanded central power.
- ✅ **Sanjay Gandhi** became an extra-constitutional power centre; **compulsory sterilisation** and slum demolitions were the era's worst excesses.
- ✅ In the **1977 elections**, the electorate defeated Congress and ended the Emergency — a verdict for civil liberties.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ The **Nav Nirman** agitation began in **Gujarat (1974)**; the Bihar movement followed under **JP**.
- ✅ **JP's** slogan was "**Total Revolution / Sampoorna Kranti**."
- ✅ The **Allahabad High Court** unseated Indira Gandhi on **12 June 1975** (Raj Narain's petition, Justice Jagmohanlal Sinha).
- ✅ The **Emergency** was proclaimed on **26 June 1975** under **Article 352**.
- ✅ Detentions were made under **MISA** (Maintenance of Internal Security Act).
- ✅ The **42nd Amendment (1976)** greatly expanded central and executive power.
- ✅ The Emergency ended with Congress's defeat in the **1977 elections**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The **Allahabad High Court verdict (June 1975)**, not the JP movement alone, was the immediate trigger for the Emergency.

- ❌ The Emergency was declared under Article 356. → It was a **national emergency under Article 352**.
- ❌ JP led a movement of workers and peasants. → It was led mainly by **students, the middle class and traders**.
- ❌ The 44th Amendment was passed during the Emergency. → The **42nd Amendment (1976)** was; the **44th (1978)** later reversed many of its provisions.
- ❌ Indira was unseated by the Supreme Court in 1975. → It was the **Allahabad High Court**; the SC later granted a conditional stay.
- ❌ The Emergency was ended by the courts. → It was ended by the **1977 electoral verdict**.
