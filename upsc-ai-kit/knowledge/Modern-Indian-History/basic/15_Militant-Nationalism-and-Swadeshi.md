## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Extremism arose from disillusion and self-assertion.**

- ✅ Militant nationalism grew because many nationalists were dissatisfied with Moderate methods and slow constitutional concessions.
- ✅ Curzon's reactionary measures, including the Calcutta Corporation Act and Universities Act, deepened distrust of official reform.
- ✅ Economic distress, famines and plague conditions sharpened anger against colonial rule.
- ✅ World events such as Ethiopia's resistance to Italy and Japan's victory over Russia in 1905 encouraged Asian self-confidence.
- ✅ Leaders such as Tilak, Vivekananda and Bankim Chandra helped revive self-respect, cultural pride and the idea of active resistance.

**Foundation — Swadeshi turned anti-partition protest into a new political technique.**

- ✅ Curzon partitioned Bengal in 1905; nationalists saw it as a deliberate divide-and-rule measure.
- ✅ The anti-partition agitation became the Swadeshi and Boycott movement: boycott of foreign goods, promotion of indigenous enterprise, national education and public mobilisation.
- ✅ Cultural symbols such as **Vande Mataram**, festivals, songs and samitis helped carry politics beyond petitions.
- ✅ Lal-Bal-Pal — Lala Lajpat Rai, Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Bipin Chandra Pal — became the best-known Extremist leadership trio.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Partition of Bengal** was announced in **July 1905** and came into effect on **16 October 1905**.
- ✅ **Curzon** was associated with the Partition of Bengal and reactionary measures before it.
- ✅ **Swadeshi** means use of indigenous goods; **boycott** means rejection of foreign goods and colonial institutions where possible.
- ✅ **National Council of Education** was founded in **1906**; **Bengal National College** was associated with **Aurobindo Ghosh**.
- ✅ **Lal-Bal-Pal** refers to Lala Lajpat Rai, Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Bipin Chandra Pal.
- ✅ **Surat Split** occurred in **1907** between Moderates and Extremists.
- ✅ **Morley-Minto reforms / Indian Councils Act 1909** introduced separate electorates for Muslims.
- ✅ **Partition of Bengal was annulled in 1911**, and the capital shifted from Calcutta to Delhi.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Swadeshi began as an anti-partition movement, but its techniques outgrew Bengal.

- ❌ Swadeshi started as a general economic campaign without a political trigger. → ✅ It began directly from the anti-partition agitation of 1905.
- ❌ Separate electorates were introduced by the Government of India Act 1919. → ✅ Separate electorates for Muslims were introduced by Morley-Minto in 1909.
- ❌ The Partition of Bengal remained permanent. → ✅ It was annulled in 1911.
- ❌ Extremists rejected nationalism's earlier economic critique. → ⚠️ They rejected Moderate methods but used the nationalist critique already built by earlier leaders.
