## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — war created both hope and hardship.**

- ✅ India supported Britain in **World War I** with men, money and materials; Indian leaders initially expected that loyalty would be rewarded with constitutional reforms.
- ✅ Wartime conditions produced discontent: rising prices, heavier taxation, war loans, shortages and recruitment pressure made British rule appear more exploitative.
- ⚠️ The war internationalised Indian politics: slogans like democracy and self-determination sharpened the contradiction between British liberal claims and colonial rule in India.

**Foundation — Home Rule revived national agitation before Gandhi.**

- ✅ The **Home Rule Leagues** demanded self-government for India on the Irish Home Rule model, not immediate complete independence.
- ✅ Tilak's League and Besant's League used public meetings, pamphlets, newspapers, branches and local workers to carry nationalist politics beyond annual Congress sessions.
- ✅ The **Lucknow Pact** brought a double unity: reunion of Moderates and Extremists inside Congress, and cooperation between Congress and the Muslim League.
- ⚠️ The price of Congress-League unity was Congress acceptance of **separate electorates for Muslims**, a concession later remembered as a major communal trap.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **World War I (1914–18)** produced economic hardship and expectations of post-war reforms in India.
- ✅ **Tilak's Home Rule League** began in **April 1916**; **Annie Besant's Home Rule League** began in **September 1916**.
- ✅ Tilak's slogan, **"Swaraj is my birthright and I shall have it,"** became a popular nationalist cry.
- ✅ Besant's associates included **George Arundale** and **B.P. Wadia**.
- ✅ The **Lucknow Session of Congress (December 1916)** achieved Moderate-Extremist reunion and the Congress-League Pact.
- ✅ The **Lucknow Pact** accepted separate electorates for Muslims while presenting a joint reform demand.
- ✅ The **Montagu Declaration** was issued on **20 August 1917**.
- ✅ Annie Besant became **Congress president in 1917**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not merge the two Home Rule Leagues; UPSC likes the leader-date-place distinction.

- ❌ Tilak and Besant founded one common league. → ✅ They founded **separate leagues** in 1916, with different organisational zones.
- ❌ Home Rule meant Purna Swaraj. → ✅ It meant **self-government within the Empire**, inspired by the Irish model.
- ❌ Lucknow Pact only meant Hindu-Muslim unity. → ✅ It also meant **Moderate-Extremist reunion** inside Congress.
- ❌ Separate electorates began with the Lucknow Pact. → ✅ They were introduced by **Morley-Minto reforms, 1909**; Congress accepted them in the Pact.
- ❌ Montagu Declaration granted immediate responsible government. → ✅ It promised **gradual** development of responsible government.
