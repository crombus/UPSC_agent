## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Gandhi brought a new method of politics.**

- ✅ In South Africa, Gandhi organised Indians through the Natal Indian Congress and developed **satyagraha**, a method based on truth-force, non-violent resistance and willingness to suffer.
- ✅ Gandhi returned to India in 1915, treated **Gopal Krishna Gokhale** as his political mentor and spent time touring India before launching major campaigns.
- ✅ His early Indian struggles at Champaran, Ahmedabad and Kheda tested satyagraha in peasant and worker contexts.

**Foundation — Rowlatt and Jallianwala made Gandhi a national leader.**

- ✅ The **Rowlatt Act, 1919** was condemned as the "Black Act" because it allowed detention without trial and continuation of wartime emergency powers.
- ✅ Gandhi launched the **Rowlatt Satyagraha** and called a nationwide hartal on **6 April 1919**.
- ✅ At **Jallianwala Bagh**, General Dyer ordered firing on an unarmed gathering; the massacre shocked India and destroyed remaining faith in British justice.
- ✅ Rabindranath Tagore renounced his knighthood in protest; Gandhi returned his **Kaiser-i-Hind medal** later, in **1920**, over the Punjab wrongs and the Khilafat issue.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Gandhi was in **South Africa from 1893 to 1914** and developed satyagraha there.
- ✅ Gandhi returned to India in **1915**; **Gokhale** was his political mentor.
- ✅ **Champaran (1917)** was linked to indigo planters and the **tinkathia** system.
- ✅ **Ahmedabad (1918)** involved mill workers and Gandhi's first hunger strike in India.
- ✅ **Kheda (1918)** involved peasants, crop failure and revenue remission; Vallabhbhai Patel emerged.
- ✅ **Rowlatt Act (1919)** allowed detention without trial; the hartal was on **6 April 1919**.
- ✅ **Jallianwala Bagh massacre** occurred on **13 April 1919** at Amritsar under General Dyer.
- ✅ **Tagore renounced his knighthood** after Jallianwala Bagh.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Keep the sequence fixed: Champaran 1917 → Ahmedabad/Kheda 1918 → Rowlatt/Jallianwala 1919.

- ❌ Champaran was Gandhi's first hunger strike. → ✅ Champaran was his first civil-disobedience success; **Ahmedabad** saw the first hunger strike in India.
- ❌ Kheda was about indigo cultivation. → ✅ Kheda was about **revenue remission** after crop failure.
- ❌ Rowlatt Act introduced separate electorates. → ✅ Separate electorates came in **1909**; Rowlatt meant detention without trial.
- ❌ Jallianwala Bagh happened during Non-Cooperation. → ✅ It occurred in **April 1919**, before NCM was launched.
- ❌ General Dyer was cleared by Indian opinion. → ✅ He became a symbol of colonial brutality and Punjab repression.
