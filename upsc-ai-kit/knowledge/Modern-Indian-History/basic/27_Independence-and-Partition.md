## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the Cabinet Mission scheme collapsed under communal mistrust.**

- ✅ The Cabinet Mission Plan had tried to preserve unity through a weak centre and provincial grouping, but Congress and the League interpreted grouping differently.
- ✅ **Direct Action Day (16 August 1946)** led to severe communal violence in Calcutta and widened distrust between Congress, League and communities.
- ✅ Violence in **Noakhali, Bihar and Punjab** made the demand for quick settlement more urgent and more tragic.
- ⚠️ By early 1947, the question shifted from whether India would remain united to how power could be transferred while limiting civil war.

**Foundation — Partition was accepted as the price of transfer and a strong centre.**

- ✅ **Attlee's statement of 20 February 1947** declared that Britain would transfer power not later than June 1948.
- ✅ **Mountbatten's 3 June Plan** accepted partition, the division of Punjab and Bengal, referendums in NWFP and Sylhet, and boundary commissions.
- ✅ The **Indian Independence Act (July 1947)** legally created two dominions, India and Pakistan, from **15 August 1947** and ended British paramountcy over princely states.
- ⚠️ Partition produced massive migration and communal carnage; estimates commonly place displacement around **10–15 million**, but exact numbers should not be stated with false precision.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Attlee's statement** came on **20 February 1947**.
- ✅ **Lord Mountbatten** was the last Viceroy and arrived in March 1947.
- ✅ **3 June Plan** is the Mountbatten Plan that accepted Partition.
- ✅ **Indian Independence Act (July 1947)** legally created the dominions of India and Pakistan.
- ✅ **15 August 1947** is the date of Indian independence and transfer of power.
- ✅ **Cyril Radcliffe** headed the boundary commissions for Punjab and Bengal.
- ✅ The Act provided for the **lapse of British paramountcy** over princely states.
- ✅ Nehru delivered the **"Tryst with Destiny"** speech at independence; Gandhi worked for communal peace in Bengal.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: **3 June Plan = political plan for Partition**; **Indian Independence Act = legal enactment**; **Radcliffe = boundary line**.

- ❌ Cabinet Mission itself partitioned India. → It tried to preserve a united India through grouping.
- ❌ Mountbatten Plan was passed by Parliament as the Act. → The plan preceded the **Indian Independence Act**.
- ❌ Radcliffe was an Indian nationalist leader. → Cyril Radcliffe was a British lawyer appointed to chair boundary commissions.
- ❌ British paramountcy passed automatically to India. → Paramountcy **lapsed**, creating the princely-state problem.
- ❌ Gandhi celebrated independence in Delhi. → Gandhi was in Bengal working for communal peace.
