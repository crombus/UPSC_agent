## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the Nehru years institutionalised democracy, planning and non-alignment.**

- ✅ The **First General Election (1951–52)** — with ~**173 million voters** — was "the biggest experiment in democracy anywhere in the world."
- ✅ **Planning** through the Planning Commission (1950) and successive Five Year Plans aimed at a **self-reliant, mixed economy** with a large public sector.
- ✅ The **Avadi resolution (1955)** committed Congress to a "**socialistic pattern of society**" — flexible, not doctrinaire.
- ✅ **Non-alignment** meant an *active* independent judgement on each issue — not neutrality — anchored in anti-colonialism and disarmament.

**Foundation — achievements were real, but 1962 exposed the limits.**

- ✅ Institutions of science and education expanded: the **Atomic Energy Commission (1948)**, CSIR laboratories, IITs, universities and technical education supported the planned-development strategy, though mass literacy remained a major failure.
- ✅ **Panchsheel (1954)** and the **Bandung Conference (1955)** made India a leader of the newly decolonising world.
- ✅ The **1962 India–China war** was a shock that damaged Nehru's prestige and non-alignment's optimism.
- ⚠️ Nehru is remembered as the "**architect of modern India**" — democracy, secularism, planning and a scientific temper — despite organisational weaknesses.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ The **Planning Commission** was set up in **1950** (by executive resolution, not the Constitution).
- ✅ The **First General Election (1951–52)** used universal adult franchise with ~173 million voters.
- ✅ **Panchsheel (Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence)** was articulated in **1954**.
- ✅ The **Avadi session (1955)** adopted the "**socialistic pattern of society**."
- ✅ The **Second Five Year Plan (1956–61)** followed the **Mahalanobis** heavy-industry strategy.
- ✅ **Kerala (1957)** elected the first Communist state government under **E.M.S. Namboodiripad**.
- ✅ The **India–China war** was in **1962**; **Nehru died in May 1964**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: **Non-alignment ≠ neutrality.** It was an *active* policy of independent judgement, anti-colonialism and disarmament.

- ❌ The Planning Commission was a constitutional body. → It was created by an **executive resolution in 1950**.
- ❌ The socialistic pattern meant full state ownership. → It meant a **mixed economy** with public-sector leadership.
- ❌ Panchsheel dates from 1962. → It was enunciated in **1954** (with China, over Tibet).
- ❌ Kerala's communist government came in the 1960s. → It was elected in **1957**.
- ❌ Non-alignment meant staying neutral in every dispute. → It meant judging each issue **on its merits**.
