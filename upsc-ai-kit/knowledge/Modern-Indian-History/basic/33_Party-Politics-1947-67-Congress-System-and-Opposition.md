## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the "Congress system": one-party dominance with internal pluralism.**

- ✅ For its first two decades Congress was **dominant** but not dictatorial — it was **broad, plural and consensual**, absorbing many social groups and tolerating open dissent.
- ✅ Congress won large **seat** majorities while polling **under 50% of votes** — non-Congress candidates together often polled more, but were divided.
- ✅ **Patel's rule (1948)** barring dual party membership pushed the Socialists out and defined Congress's organisational boundary.
- ⚠️ Congress functioned as a **"party of consensus"** with a left-of-centre orientation, co-opting the agendas of its rivals.

**Foundation — a fragmented opposition could not yet displace Congress.**

- ✅ The **Socialists / PSP** (JP, Narendra Dev, Lohia, Asoka Mehta) split repeatedly over whether to cooperate with or oppose Congress.
- ✅ The **Communists (CPI)** moved from insurrection (1948–51) to elections, won **Kerala (1957)**, then **split in 1964** (CPI vs CPM).
- ✅ On the right, the **Jana Sangh** (1951, RSS-linked) and the **Swatantra Party** (1959, pro-free-market, anti-planning) offered conservative alternatives.
- ⚠️ Because the opposition was **ideologically scattered and often ex-Congress**, it could challenge but not yet defeat the ruling party nationally.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ The **"Congress system"** = one-party dominance with internal factional pluralism (Rajni Kothari's phrase).
- ✅ Congress won large seat majorities while polling **less than half the popular vote**.
- ✅ The **PSP (Praja Socialist Party)** formed in **1952** from the KMPP and the Socialist Party.
- ✅ The **CPI won Kerala in 1957** and **split into CPI and CPM in 1964**.
- ✅ The **Jana Sangh** was founded in **1951** by **Shyama Prasad Mookerjee**.
- ✅ The **Swatantra Party** was founded in **1959** by **C. Rajagopalachari** (with Masani and Ranga).
- ✅ **Lohia's socialists** stressed backward-caste reservation and Hindi as the link language.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: One-party *dominance* is not one-party *rule* — the "Congress system" contained a functioning opposition and internal dissent.

- ❌ Congress won majorities of the popular vote. → It won **seat** majorities on **under 50%** of votes.
- ❌ The CPM was founded in 1947. → The CPI **split** into CPI and CPM in **1964**.
- ❌ The Swatantra Party was left-wing. → It was **pro-free-market and anti-planning**.
- ❌ The Jana Sangh had no organisational parent. → It was closely linked to the **RSS**.
- ❌ The opposition was united against Congress. → It was **ideologically fragmented** and often composed of ex-Congressmen.
