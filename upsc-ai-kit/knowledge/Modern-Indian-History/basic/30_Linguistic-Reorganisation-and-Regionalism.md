## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — India chose to redraw its internal map on the basis of language.**

- ✅ Provincial boundaries drawn during a century of British conquest ignored **linguistic and cultural cohesion**; most provinces were multilingual.
- ✅ The national leadership initially **delayed** reorganisation — Nehru (27 Nov 1947): "First things must come first and the first thing is the security and stability of India."
- ✅ Both the **Dar Commission (1948)** and the **JVP Committee (Dec 1948)** advised against immediate linguistic states, fearing they would threaten unity.
- ✅ Popular pressure — climaxing in **Potti Sriramulu's death (1952)** — forced the creation of **Andhra (1953)**, opening the floodgates.

**Foundation — the SRC institutionalised the linguistic principle.**

- ✅ The **States Reorganisation Commission** (chaired by **Fazl Ali**) recommended broad reorganisation, and the **States Reorganisation Act, 1956** created **14 states and 6 Union Territories**.
- ✅ **Bombay** was split into **Maharashtra and Gujarat only in 1960** after violent agitation (Bombay city went to Maharashtra).
- ✅ **Punjab** was reorganised in **1966** into Punjab and **Haryana**, with **Chandigarh** a shared Union Territory capital.
- ⚠️ Contrary to fears, reorganisation **strengthened** national unity by making administration and democracy work in the people's own languages.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ The **Dar Commission (1948)** and **JVP Committee (1948)** initially opposed linguistic states.
- ✅ **Potti Sriramulu's** fast-unto-death led to the creation of **Andhra in 1953** — the first linguistic state.
- ✅ The **SRC** was chaired by **Justice Fazl Ali** (members K.M. Panikkar and H.N. Kunzru).
- ✅ The **States Reorganisation Act, 1956** created **14 states and 6 Union Territories**.
- ✅ **Bombay** was bifurcated in **1960**; **Punjab** in **1966** (Haryana created; Chandigarh a UT).
- ✅ The **anti-Hindi agitation of 1965** reinforced the political assurance that **English would continue for Union official purposes**; "associate official language" is common shorthand, not the Constitution's formal term.
- ✅ The **Shiv Sena** was founded in **1966** on a "sons of the soil" platform.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The **first linguistic state was Andhra (1953)** — created *before* the SRC report; the SRC (1955) then generalised the principle, and the Act came in **1956**.

- ❌ The SRC created the first linguistic state. → **Andhra (1953)** came first; the SRC reported in **1955**.
- ❌ Bombay was split in 1956. → It was split into Maharashtra and Gujarat in **1960**.
- ❌ Haryana was created in 1956. → Punjab was reorganised and **Haryana created in 1966**.
- ❌ Hindi replaced English as the sole official language in 1965. → **English continued for Union official purposes** under statute and political assurance.
- ❌ Linguistic reorganisation weakened India. → The author argues it **strengthened** national unity.
