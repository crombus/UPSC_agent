## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — colonial education created a new public sphere.**

- ✅ Early British policy included Orientalist institutions such as **Calcutta Madrasa (1781)** and **Sanskrit College, Banaras (1791)**.
- ✅ The **Charter Act, 1813** provided **₹1 lakh** for education and allowed Christian missionaries to work in India.
- ✅ The **Orientalist–Anglicist controversy** debated whether state support should go to traditional learning in Indian languages/classical languages or Western education through English.
- ✅ **Macaulay's Minute (1835)** supported English education and the aim of creating a class "Indian in blood and colour, but English in taste".
- ✅ Bentinck's **1835 resolution** accepted English as the medium for higher education and reflected the **downward filtration** theory.

**Foundation — the press became both regulated and nationalist.**

- ✅ **Wood's Education Despatch, 1854** is called the "Magna Carta of English education" in India.
- ✅ Wood's Despatch recommended grants-in-aid, Departments of Public Instruction, universities, vernacular primary education and teacher training.
- ✅ Universities of **Calcutta, Bombay and Madras** were established in **1857**.
- ✅ **Hicky's Bengal Gazette (1780)** was the first newspaper in India; the press later became a major tool of nationalism.
- ✅ **Metcalfe** is called the "liberator of the press" for the press reforms of **1835**.
- ✅ **Vernacular Press Act, 1878** under Lytton targeted Indian-language newspapers and was repealed by Ripon in **1882**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: **Macaulay's Minute (1835)** and **Wood's Despatch (1854)** are not the same policy document.

- ❌ Wood's Despatch introduced English education in 1835. → ✅ Macaulay's Minute and Bentinck's resolution did that in **1835**.
- ❌ Wood's Despatch ignored vernacular education. → ✅ It supported vernacular primary education along with higher education.
- ❌ Vernacular Press Act applied equally to English newspapers. → ✅ It targeted **Indian-language** press.
- ❌ Metcalfe restricted the press. → ✅ He is called the **liberator of the press**.
- ❌ Sati abolition was only a British humanitarian act. → ✅ Indian reformers, especially **Rammohan Roy**, were central to the campaign.
