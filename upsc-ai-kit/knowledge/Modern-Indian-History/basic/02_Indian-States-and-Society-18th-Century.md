## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — 18th-century India was politically fragmented, not socially dead.**

- ✅ After Mughal decline, power shifted to **regional states**: Bengal, Awadh, Hyderabad, Marathas, Sikhs, Jats, Mysore, Rajput states and Kerala/Travancore.
- ✅ Bipin Chandra groups these states into **successor states**, **new/rebel states**, and **independent kingdoms**.
- ⚠️ The key exam idea is **decentralisation**: Mughal central authority declined, but many regions retained revenue systems, trade networks and cultural vitality.
- ✅ Most states continued older Mughal administrative forms, used Persianate court culture, and sought legitimacy through local elites and revenue rights.

**Foundation — society mixed continuity with limits.**

- ✅ The economy remained primarily **agrarian**, with self-sufficient villages forming the base of production.
- ✅ Towns, crafts and trade continued in many regions; Bengal and Gujarat remained commercially important.
- ✅ Common people faced heavy taxation and insecurity during warfare, but the picture was not one of uniform decline.
- ✅ Religious tolerance and composite culture survived strongly in everyday life, despite political conflict.
- ⚠️ A major weakness was the absence of sustained **scientific and technological advance**, which later widened the gap with Europe.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Type of 18th-century state | Examples | Core feature |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Successor states | Bengal, Awadh, Hyderabad | Mughal provinces became autonomous under former governors/nobles |
| ✅ New/rebel states | Marathas, Sikhs, Jats, Rohillas/Afghans | Rose from resistance, military mobilisation or local chieftaincy |
| ✅ Independent kingdoms | Mysore, Rajput states, Kerala/Travancore | Less directly born from Mughal provinces; strengthened local sovereignty |
| ✅ Bengal | Murshid Quli Khan, Alivardi Khan, Siraj-ud-Daula | Wealthy province; strong revenue administration; later Company target |
| ✅ Awadh | Saadat Khan Burhan-ul-Mulk, Safdar Jang | Fertile middle-Ganga state; important buffer between Bengal and northwest |
| ✅ Hyderabad | Nizam-ul-Mulk Asaf Jah, 1724 | Deccan successor state; formally loyal to Mughal emperor, autonomous in practice |
| ✅ Marathas | Peshwas at Poona; Gaekwad, Holkar, Scindia, Bhonsle | Confederacy model; strong cavalry and revenue claims (*chauth*, *sardeshmukhi*) |
| ✅ Mysore/Travancore | Haidar Ali, Tipu Sultan; Martanda Varma | Stronger centralising rulers in the south; military and administrative reforms |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Bengal**: Murshid Quli Khan founded autonomy; Alivardi Khan ruled before Siraj-ud-Daula.
- ✅ **Awadh**: Saadat Khan Burhan-ul-Mulk founded the autonomous line; Safdar Jang strengthened it.
- ✅ **Hyderabad**: Nizam-ul-Mulk Asaf Jah established autonomy in **1724**.
- ✅ **Maratha confederacy**: Peshwa at Poona; Gaekwad of Baroda, Holkar of Indore, Scindia of Gwalior, Bhonsle of Nagpur.
- ✅ **Sikhs**: Banda Bahadur's revolt came first; later the Sikhs organised into **12 misls** before Ranjit Singh unified Punjab.
- ✅ **Jats**: Churaman and Suraj Mal of Bharatpur were important Jat leaders.
- ✅ **Rohillas/Afghans**: Rohilkhand and Farrukhabad were important centres.
- ✅ **Travancore**: Martanda Varma consolidated power in Kerala.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not treat 18th-century regional states as either "modern nation-states" or as proof of total anarchy; they were autonomous early-modern polities with mixed continuity and innovation.

- ❌ Successor states were fully independent nation-states. → They were **autonomous in practice** but often retained Mughal symbols of legitimacy.
- ❌ 18th-century India was uniformly declining. → Many regions had active trade, administration and cultural life.
- ❌ Marathas were a single centralised state under the Peshwa. → They evolved into a **confederacy** with powerful chiefs.
- ❌ Sikh power began directly with Ranjit Singh. → Banda Bahadur and the **12 misls** came before Ranjit Singh's kingdom.
- ❌ Mysore was a Mughal successor state. → It is better treated as an **independent southern kingdom**.

## 6. 📰 Current link

- ⚠️ **Heritage and regional-state memory.** Sites such as Hyderabad, Bharatpur, Mysore/Srirangapatna, Amritsar and Travancore's temple-city landscape keep 18th-century regional polities in public memory through conservation, tourism and textbook debates. *(Escalate to 📰 when pegged to a verified news item.)*
