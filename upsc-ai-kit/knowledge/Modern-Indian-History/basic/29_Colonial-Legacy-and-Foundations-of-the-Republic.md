## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — India inherited a crippled economy and a fractured society, but a strong political legacy.**

- ✅ Colonialism left India with a **dependent, stagnant economy** — deindustrialisation, low per-capita income, mass poverty and recurring famine — what the author calls "the development of underdevelopment."
- ✅ In 1951 only about **25% of males and 7.9% of females were literate** (roughly **84% illiterate**); life expectancy at birth was only about **32 years**.
- ✅ The franchise had been kept tiny — only ~**3%** of Indians could vote after 1919 and ~**15%** after 1935; the Republic leapt straight to **universal adult franchise**.
- ⚠️ The single greatest asset was the **legacy of the national movement**: a tested leadership, a broad social base, and the values of democracy, secularism and social reform.

**Foundation — the immediate task was to hold the nation together and give it a democratic form.**

- ✅ Partition produced **refugees, communal riots, a divided army and treasury**, and an unresolved Kashmir war with Pakistan.
- ✅ The republic faced three internal challenges at once: **communal violence**, a **Communist (Telangana) insurgency**, and the **integration of princely states**.
- ✅ Leaders such as **Nehru, Patel, Rajendra Prasad, Maulana Azad and Rajagopalachari** anchored the state in **secular, democratic** principles.
- ⚠️ Nation-building rested on four pillars: **democracy, civil liberties, secularism and planned development** within a strong but federal state.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ India became independent on **15 August 1947**; Nehru's "**Tryst with Destiny**" address was delivered on the night of 14 August.
- ✅ **Gandhiji was assassinated on 30 January 1948** by Nathuram Godse; the RSS was banned and the ban lifted in **July 1949**.
- ✅ The **Nehru–Liaquat Pact (8 April 1950)** dealt with the security of minorities in both dominions.
- ✅ The **Telangana armed struggle** (led by the CPI) was withdrawn by **mid-1951**.
- ✅ **Pondicherry** (French) was transferred in 1954; **Goa** (Portuguese) was integrated in **December 1961**.
- ✅ In **1951** only about a quarter of men and under 8% of women were literate.
- ✅ The colonial legacy is summed up as the "**development of underdevelopment**" — a dependent, stagnant economy.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Independence solved the *legal* question of sovereignty; **nation-building** (integration, secularism, democracy, development) was a separate, longer struggle.

- ❌ India inherited a healthy economy in 1947. → It inherited **stagnation, deindustrialisation and mass poverty**.
- ❌ Universal adult franchise existed under the British. → The colonial franchise was tiny; **universal suffrage** came with the Republic.
- ❌ Goa joined India in 1947. → Goa was liberated only in **December 1961**; Pondicherry was transferred in **1954**.
- ❌ Gandhi was killed by a Muslim assailant. → He was assassinated by **Nathuram Godse**, a Hindu communal extremist.
- ❌ Partition ended communal violence. → It **triggered** the largest refugee crisis and communal killings of the era.
