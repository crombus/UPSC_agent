## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — colonial economy meant extraction, not balanced development.**

- ✅ The British conquest tied India to the needs of British capitalism: raw materials, markets for manufactured goods, revenue for administration and profits for investors.
- ✅ **Drain of wealth** meant a unilateral transfer of Indian resources to Britain without an equivalent economic return.
- ✅ **Dadabhai Naoroji** explained the drain in *Poverty and Un-British Rule in India*; **R.C. Dutt** analysed it in *Economic History of India*.
- ✅ Drain operated through home charges, salaries and pensions of officials, profits of British capital, military expenditure and payments made in Britain from Indian revenues.
- ⚠️ Nationalist economic critique converted poverty from a "natural" condition into a political indictment of colonial rule.

**Foundation — deindustrialisation and land pressure reshaped society.**

- ✅ British machine-made goods and one-way free trade damaged Indian handicrafts, especially textiles, causing **deindustrialisation**.
- ✅ As urban crafts declined, many artisans moved back to villages, producing **de-urbanisation**, ruralisation and greater pressure on land.
- ✅ **Commercialisation of agriculture** pushed peasants toward cash crops such as indigo, cotton and other market-oriented produce.
- ✅ Land-revenue systems — Permanent Settlement, Ryotwari and Mahalwari — reorganised agrarian relations to secure revenue for the colonial state.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Dadabhai Naoroji** is linked with the **Drain Theory** and *Poverty and Un-British Rule in India*.
- ✅ **R.C. Dutt** wrote *Economic History of India* and criticised colonial economic policy.
- ✅ **Permanent Settlement (1793)** was introduced by **Cornwallis** in Bengal-Bihar-Orissa.
- ✅ Permanent Settlement made **zamindars** proprietors and fixed revenue demand permanently.
- ✅ **Ryotwari** is associated with **Thomas Munro** and **Alexander Read** in Madras and Bombay.
- ✅ **Mahalwari** is associated with **Holt Mackenzie (1822)** and **R.M. Bird** in North-Western Provinces, Punjab and parts of Central India.
- ✅ The **Strachey Famine Commission** followed the famine of 1876–78 and reported in **1880**.
- ✅ Railways, telegraph and canals were introduced, but mainly to serve imperial-commercial needs.
- ⚠️ "Colonial modernisation" means modern infrastructure without autonomous industrial development.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Match every land system with its **region + architect + unit of settlement**.

- ❌ Permanent Settlement was a ryot-based settlement. → ✅ It was a **zamindari** settlement.
- ❌ Ryotwari was mainly Bengal-Bihar-Orissa. → ✅ It was mainly **Madras and Bombay**.
- ❌ Mahalwari settled directly with individual peasants only. → ✅ It dealt with the **village/mahal community**.
- ❌ Railways prove colonial rule was economically benevolent. → ✅ Railways served imperial control, exports and British capital, though they had unintended integrating effects.
- ❌ Drain of wealth was only private corruption. → ✅ It included systematic public payments such as home charges and official salaries.
