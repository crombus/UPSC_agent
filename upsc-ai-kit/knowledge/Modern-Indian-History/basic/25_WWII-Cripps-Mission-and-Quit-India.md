## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the war crisis exposed the limits of colonial constitutionalism.**

- ✅ In **1939**, India was declared a belligerent in the Second World War without the consent of elected Indian representatives, proving that provincial autonomy under the 1935 Act was subordinate to imperial authority.
- ✅ The **Congress ministries resigned** because they would not support a war for democracy abroad while India remained denied democracy at home.
- ✅ The **August Offer (1940)** promised dominion status as an eventual goal, expansion of the Viceroy's Executive Council and protection of minority interests, but it did not concede immediate responsible government.
- ⚠️ The Congress rejection reflected a strategic shift: Indians were no longer satisfied with future promises; the demand was for real power in the present.

**Foundation — Quit India was the final great mass movement before freedom.**

- ✅ The **Cripps Mission (1942)** offered dominion status after the war and a constitution-making body; a British Indian province could refuse the new constitution and retain its existing position, while princely states would join through their rulers' decisions. Congress rejected the lack of immediate responsible government and the danger to unity.
- ✅ Gandhi criticised the offer as a **"post-dated cheque"** because it promised freedom after the war but retained British control during the crisis.
- ✅ On **8 August 1942**, the AICC at Bombay adopted the Quit India resolution; Gandhi gave the slogan **"Do or Die"**.
- ✅ With top leaders arrested on **9 August 1942**, the movement became a leaderless upsurge marked by strikes, protests, attacks on symbols of authority, underground activity and parallel governments.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **India was declared at war in 1939** without consulting Indian representatives.
- ✅ **Congress ministries resigned in 1939** over the war issue.
- ✅ **August Offer (1940)** was made by Viceroy Linlithgow and was rejected by Congress.
- ✅ **Individual Satyagraha** began in October 1940; **Vinoba Bhave** was first satyagrahi and **Jawaharlal Nehru** second.
- ✅ **Cripps Mission (1942)** was led by Sir Stafford Cripps and offered post-war dominion status.
- ✅ **Quit India** was adopted at **Gowalia Tank, Bombay, on 8 August 1942**.
- ✅ **Aruna Asaf Ali** is associated with hoisting the flag at Gowalia Tank; **Usha Mehta** is associated with the secret Congress Radio.
- ✅ **Parallel governments** emerged at Ballia, Tamluk and Satara.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: **Cripps = 1942 offer before Quit India**; **Quit India = 8 August 1942 Bombay**; do not merge them into one event.

- ❌ Cripps offered immediate full independence. → It offered **post-war dominion status**, not immediate transfer of power.
- ❌ Gandhi's "post-dated cheque" remark refers to the August Offer. → It is associated with the **Cripps proposals**.
- ❌ Quit India was centrally directed throughout. → Leaders were arrested early; much of the movement became **spontaneous and local**.
- ❌ Only peaceful protest occurred in Quit India. → The movement included strikes, sabotage and attacks on state symbols, though Congress ideology remained non-violent.
- ❌ Muslim League and Communists led Quit India jointly with Congress. → The League stayed aloof and the Communists, after the Nazi attack on the USSR, adopted the **People's War** line.
