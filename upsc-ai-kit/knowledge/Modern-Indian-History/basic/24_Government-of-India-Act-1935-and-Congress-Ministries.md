## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the 1935 Act was the largest colonial constitutional framework but not freedom.**

- ✅ The **Government of India Act 1935** drew on the Simon Report, Round Table Conferences, the 1933 White Paper and Joint Select Committee work.
- ✅ It proposed an **All-India Federation** of British Indian provinces and princely states, but the federation never came into being because the princes did not accede.
- ✅ It introduced **provincial autonomy** by abolishing dyarchy in the provinces, while **dyarchy was to be introduced at the Centre** — but only under the federal scheme, which never came into force.
- ✅ It enlarged the franchise to roughly **14%** of the population while keeping power under major safeguards and governor/viceroy controls.
- ✅ Separate electorates were extended, keeping communal representation at the heart of colonial constitutional politics.
- ✅ It provided for a **Federal Court**, which began functioning in **1937**. *(The Reserve Bank of India was a separate creation of the RBI Act 1934, not of this Act.)*

**Foundation — Congress used office as a field of struggle, then resigned over war.**

- ✅ Congress criticised the Act as inadequate; Jawaharlal Nehru described it as **"a machine with strong brakes but no engine"**.
- ✅ Congress still contested the **1937 elections** and formed ministries in **eight of eleven provinces**: Bombay, Madras, United Provinces, Bihar, Central Provinces, Orissa, and later NWFP and Assam.
- ✅ Congress ministries released political prisoners, expanded civil liberties, promoted education and attempted agrarian and tenancy reforms within limited powers.
- ✅ Muslim League propaganda about alleged **"Congress atrocities"** deepened Congress–League estrangement.
- ✅ Congress ministries resigned in **October–November 1939** after Viceroy Linlithgow declared India at war without consulting Indian leaders.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Government of India Act 1935** proposed an **All-India Federation**, but it was **never implemented**.
- ✅ The Act introduced **provincial autonomy** and abolished **dyarchy in provinces**.
- ✅ The Act introduced **dyarchy at the Centre** in the proposed federal scheme.
- ✅ **Federal Court** began in **1937**.
- ✅ The Act extended **separate electorates**.
- ✅ **1937 elections** led Congress to form ministries in **8 of 11 provinces**.
- ✅ Congress ministries resigned in **1939** because India was declared at war without Indian consultation.
- ✅ Muslim League observed **Deliverance Day** in **December 1939**.
- ✅ **Lahore Resolution (March 1940)** demanded separate Muslim states.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The 1935 Act is famous for both **provincial autonomy** and an **unrealised federation**; do not say the federation actually functioned.

- ❌ The Act introduced dyarchy in provinces. → ✅ It **abolished provincial dyarchy** and proposed dyarchy at the Centre.
- ❌ The All-India Federation came into being in 1937. → ✅ It never came into being because princely states did not accede.
- ❌ Congress boycotted the 1937 elections. → ✅ Congress contested and formed ministries in eight provinces.
- ❌ Congress ministries resigned because they lost elections. → ✅ They resigned over the unilateral war declaration in 1939.
- ❌ Lahore Resolution came before Deliverance Day. → ✅ Deliverance Day was **Dec 1939**; Lahore Resolution was **Mar 1940**.
