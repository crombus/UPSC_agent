## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — communalism is modern politics, not ancient religiosity.**

- ✅ Bipin Chandra treats communalism as a modern ideology that uses religious identity for political interests.
- ✅ Communalism is not the same as religion, religious reform or personal religiosity.
- ✅ Its core claim is that followers of one religion share common political, economic and social interests that are separate from other religious communities.
- ⚠️ This makes communalism a political ideology of identity-based representation, not a timeless Hindu-Muslim hatred.

**Foundation — colonial institutions and middle-class competition deepened communal politics.**

- ✅ Roots included uneven modern development, colonial divide-and-rule, separate electorates, use of religion in politics and competition among educated middle classes for jobs and representation.
- ✅ The All-India Muslim League was founded in **1906** at **Dhaka** by Muslim elites including Aga Khan and Nawab Salimullah.
- ✅ Morley-Minto reforms of **1909** gave separate electorates to Muslims, strengthening communal representation in constitutional politics.
- ✅ The Lucknow Pact of **1916** temporarily united Congress and League, but it accepted separate electorates as the basis of agreement.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **All-India Muslim League** was founded in **1906** at **Dhaka**.
- ✅ Key League-associated names include **Aga Khan** and **Nawab Salimullah**.
- ✅ **Separate electorates for Muslims** were introduced by the **Morley-Minto reforms / Indian Councils Act 1909**.
- ✅ **Hindu Mahasabha** emerged in **1915**; **RSS** was founded later in **1925**.
- ✅ **Lucknow Pact 1916** represented temporary Congress-League cooperation and accepted separate electorates.
- ✅ **Savarkar's Hindutva** appeared in **1923** as part of later Hindu communal ideology.
- ✅ **Lahore Resolution 1940** belongs to the later Pakistan-demand trajectory, not to the 1906 foundation itself.
- ⚠️ Communalism should be defined as political use of religious identity, not religion itself.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Communalism is not religion; it is politics organised through religious identity.

- ❌ Communalism is ancient Hindu-Muslim hatred. → ✅ Bipin Chandra treats it as a modern ideology shaped by colonial politics and socio-economic competition.
- ❌ Muslim League was founded after World War I. → ✅ It was founded at Dhaka in 1906.
- ❌ Separate electorates began with the Government of India Act 1919. → ✅ They began for Muslims with Morley-Minto in 1909.
- ❌ Lucknow Pact ended communal politics. → ✅ It created temporary unity but accepted separate electorates.
