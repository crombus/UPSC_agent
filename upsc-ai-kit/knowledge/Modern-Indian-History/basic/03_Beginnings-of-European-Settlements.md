## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Europeans came as traders but became political powers.**

- ✅ The Portuguese arrived first by sea when **Vasco da Gama reached Calicut in 1498**.
- ✅ Portuguese power rested on naval control, fortified ports, the **cartaz** pass system and the **Estado da India** centred on Goa.
- ✅ The Dutch VOC focused on Asian trade and later concentrated more on Indonesia than India.
- ✅ The English East India Company began as a chartered trading company in **1600**, but gradually acquired fortified settlements at Surat, Madras, Bombay and Calcutta.
- ✅ The French entered later through the Compagnie des Indes and became the main European rival of the English in south India.

**Foundation — the Carnatic Wars made the English supreme among Europeans.**

- ✅ The **First Carnatic War (1746–48)** ended with the Treaty of Aix-la-Chapelle; Madras was returned to the English.
- ✅ The **Second Carnatic War (1749–54)** involved Dupleix's political interventions in Indian succession disputes.
- ✅ The **Third Carnatic War (1756–63)** ended French hopes after **Wandiwash (1760)** and the Treaty of Paris (1763).
- ⚠️ English success came from naval strength, private enterprise, better finances and later access to Bengal's resources.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Vasco da Gama** reached **Calicut in 1498**.
- ✅ **Almeida** is linked with the Portuguese **Blue Water policy**.
- ✅ **Albuquerque** captured **Goa in 1510**.
- ✅ **English East India Company** was chartered in **1600** by Queen Elizabeth I.
- ✅ **Surat factory** was established in **1613** after Mughal permission; Hawkins and Sir Thomas Roe are linked to early Mughal contacts.
- ✅ **Madras/Fort St George** dates to **1639**; **Bombay** came to the English in **1668**; **Calcutta/Fort William** began in **1690**.
- ✅ **French Compagnie des Indes** was founded in **1664** by Colbert.
- ✅ **Wandiwash (1760)**: Eyre Coote defeated Lally.
- ✅ **Treaty of Paris (1763)** ended the Seven Years' War phase and curtailed French political ambitions in India.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not jump from 1498 to British rule; the Company first built coastal commercial bases before political conquest.

- ❌ Portuguese were defeated by the English at Wandiwash. → Wandiwash was an **Anglo-French** battle.
- ❌ The Dutch were the main political rivals of the English in 18th-century India. → The **French** were the main rival in the Carnatic Wars.
- ❌ The 1717 farman founded the English Company. → The Company was chartered in **1600**; the farman gave important trade privileges.
- ❌ Bombay was founded by Job Charnock. → Job Charnock is linked to **Calcutta**; Bombay came through the Portuguese dowry/English crown transfer.
- ❌ The Treaty of Aix-la-Chapelle ended the Third Carnatic War. → It ended the **First** Carnatic War.
