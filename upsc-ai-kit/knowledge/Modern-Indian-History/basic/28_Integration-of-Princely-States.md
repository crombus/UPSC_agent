## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — political unity had to be built after legal independence.**

- ✅ At independence there were about **565 princely states**, and British paramountcy over them **lapsed** rather than passing automatically to India.
- ✅ **Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel**, as Home and States Minister, and **V.P. Menon**, through the States Department, led the integration process.
- ✅ The **Instrument of Accession** allowed states to accede on three subjects: **Defence, External Affairs and Communications**.
- ⚠️ Patel's method is often described as an appeal to patriotism backed by firm pressure, or an **"iron fist in a velvet glove"**.

**Foundation — Junagadh, Hyderabad and Kashmir were the key problem states.**

- ✅ **Junagadh** acceded to Pakistan despite being Hindu-majority and geographically non-contiguous; a plebiscite in February 1948 favoured India.
- ✅ **Hyderabad** under the Nizam sought independence; Razakar violence and stalemate led to **Operation Polo** in September 1948.
- ✅ **Kashmir** under Maharaja Hari Singh hesitated; after Pakistani tribal invasion in October 1947, the Instrument of Accession was signed and Indian troops were sent.
- ✅ The **Constituent Assembly**, presided over by Dr Rajendra Prasad with B.R. Ambedkar chairing the Drafting Committee, adopted the Constitution on 26 November 1949.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Around **565 princely states** existed at the time of independence.
- ✅ **British paramountcy lapsed**; it was not transferred to India or Pakistan.
- ✅ **Patel** and **V.P. Menon** are the key names for integration.
- ✅ The **Instrument of Accession** covered **Defence, External Affairs and Communications**.
- ✅ The three famous hold-outs/problem states were **Junagadh, Hyderabad and Kashmir**.
- ✅ **Operation Polo** refers to Hyderabad's integration in September 1948.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: **Paramountcy lapsed**; it did not pass automatically to the Indian Union.

- ❌ All princely states joined India automatically on 15 August 1947. → They had to sign instruments of accession and later merge/integrate.
- ❌ Instrument of Accession transferred all subjects immediately. → It initially covered **Defence, External Affairs and Communications**.
- ❌ Junagadh, Hyderabad and Kashmir had identical problems. → Each involved a different mix of ruler's choice, people's composition, geography and security.
- ❌ Operation Polo relates to Kashmir. → It relates to **Hyderabad**.
- ❌ India became a republic on 15 August 1947. → India became independent then; it became a **Republic on 26 January 1950**.
