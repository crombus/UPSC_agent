## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the Congress grew out of earlier all-India political awakening.**

- ✅ Before 1885, educated Indians formed provincial and imperial associations such as the Landholders' Society, British Indian Association, East India Association, Poona Sarvajanik Sabha, Indian Association, Madras Mahajan Sabha and Bombay Presidency Association.
- ✅ These bodies trained early leaders in petitions, public meetings, memorials, press campaigns and constitutional criticism of colonial rule.
- ✅ The Indian National Congress was founded in **1885** with A.O. Hume's help; its first session met in **Bombay** in December 1885 with **72 delegates** and **W.C. Bonnerjee** as president.
- ⚠️ The early Congress was not yet a mass party; it was an all-India political platform of the educated middle class that slowly created a national vocabulary.

**Foundation — Moderates built the first systematic critique of colonial rule.**

- ✅ Moderate leaders included Dadabhai Naoroji, Gopal Krishna Gokhale, Pherozeshah Mehta, Surendranath Banerjea, M.G. Ranade, Badruddin Tyabji, W.C. Bonnerjee and R.C. Dutt.
- ✅ Their methods were constitutional agitation: petitions, resolutions, speeches, press work and deputations to British public opinion.
- ⚠️ The popular shorthand **"3 P's" — petition, prayer and protest** captures their style, while "political mendicancy" was a later criticism of their limited methods.
- ✅ Their main work was the economic critique of colonialism: drain of wealth, poverty, deindustrialisation, high military expenditure and exclusion of Indians from higher services.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Area | Moderate position / work |
|---|---|
| ✅ Political method | Constitutional agitation through petitions, meetings, press and legislative criticism |
| ✅ Economic critique | Drain theory, poverty, land-revenue pressure, high military expenditure and unfair trade policy |
| ✅ Administrative demand | Indianisation of higher services and simultaneous ICS examination in India and Britain |
| ✅ Constitutional demand | Expansion of legislative councils, elections and greater Indian representation |
| ✅ Civil-liberty demand | Freedom of speech, press and association; criticism of repressive official practice |
| ✅ Achievement | Indian Councils Act 1892, Welby Commission debate and creation of an all-India political framework |
| ⚠️ Limitation | Narrow social base, faith in British justice and limited immediate concessions |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Indian National Congress** was founded in **1885**; first session: **Bombay**, December 1885.
- ✅ **A.O. Hume** helped organise the INC; **W.C. Bonnerjee** was the first president.
- ✅ The first INC session had **72 delegates**.
- ✅ **East India Association** was founded by **Dadabhai Naoroji** in London in **1866**.
- ✅ **Indian Association** was founded in **1876** by **Surendranath Banerjea** and **Ananda Mohan Bose**.
- ✅ Moderate demands included council expansion, Indianisation of services, simultaneous ICS examination, reduced military expenditure and civil liberties.
- ✅ **Indian Councils Act 1892** was an early constitutional concession linked with Moderate pressure.
- ⚠️ Do not treat the Moderates as anti-national; they laid the intellectual and organisational foundation of nationalism.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The Moderates were limited in method, not irrelevant in outcome.

- ❌ The INC was created only by the British as a safety valve. → ✅ Bipin Chandra stresses it emerged from Indian national forces; Hume's role did not make it a British creation.
- ❌ Moderates achieved nothing. → ✅ They built the drain critique, public opinion, council politics and an all-India nationalist platform.
- ❌ Moderates demanded complete independence from the beginning. → ✅ Their early demands were constitutional reform, representation and administrative Indianisation.
- ❌ The Indian Councils Act 1892 introduced responsible government. → ✅ It only gave limited representation and indirect election-like procedures.
