## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — the colonial state rested on bureaucracy, army, police and courts.**

- ✅ The British built a highly centralised administrative machine to maintain order, collect revenue and protect imperial interests.
- ✅ **Cornwallis** is associated with founding the covenanted civil service, high official salaries and efforts to curb corruption among Company servants.
- ✅ The higher civil service was effectively European; Indians were excluded from top posts despite later talk of open competition.
- ✅ **Haileybury College** trained Company civil servants before the competitive-exam era.
- ✅ The **Charter Act, 1853** introduced open competitive examination, but the exam in London and restrictive age limits made Indian entry difficult.

**Foundation — rule of law existed in theory, racial hierarchy in practice.**

- ✅ The **Army** was a sepoy army led by European officers and served as an instrument of conquest and internal control.
- ✅ After **1857**, the army was reorganised with a higher European ratio and recruitment ideas that later came to be described as **martial races**.
- ✅ **Cornwallis** created the thana and daroga police system; the **Police Act, 1861** organised police under provincial Inspector-General and district Superintendent of Police.
- ✅ The judiciary developed through civil and criminal courts, Sadar courts and codified laws such as the Indian Penal Code.
- ⚠️ "Rule of law" and "equality before law" were proclaimed ideals, but racial discrimination and repressive laws limited them in practice.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Cornwallis** is linked with the covenanted civil service, high salaries and anti-corruption administrative reform.
- ✅ **Haileybury College** trained Company civil servants.
- ✅ **Charter Act, 1853** introduced open competition for civil service recruitment.
- ✅ **Statutory Civil Service (1878–79)** is associated with **Lytton**.
- ✅ **Aitchison Commission (1886)** examined public services and Indianisation.
- ✅ **Cornwallis** created the **thana/daroga** police system.
- ✅ **Police Act, 1861** created the colonial police structure with provincial IG and district SP.
- ✅ **Cornwallis Code, 1793** separated revenue and judicial functions in principle.
- ✅ **Indian Penal Code, 1860** is linked to codification and the Law Commission tradition.
- ⚠️ The **Ilbert Bill controversy** is the classic example showing racial limits of equality before law.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Colonial "rule of law" was formally universal but practically racial and repressive.

- ❌ Indians freely entered the civil service after 1853. → ✅ The exam was in London and conditions restricted Indian entry.
- ❌ Cornwallis democratised administration. → ✅ He professionalised it but kept higher posts European.
- ❌ Police Act 1861 created a people-friendly police. → ✅ It created an order-and-control police for the colonial state.
- ❌ The army after 1857 became less communalised. → ✅ Recruitment was divided by caste, region and religion under martial-race logic.
- ❌ Rule of law meant social equality. → ✅ Formal legal equality coexisted with racial privilege and laws such as sedition.
