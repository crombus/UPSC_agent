## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — thesis: the "Congress system" broke down and was replaced by plebiscitary, personalised leadership.**

- ✅ Under **Shastri** the **Prime Minister's Secretariat (PMO)** gained weight — an early sign of executive centralisation that would deepen under Indira.
- ✅ The **1967 elections** shattered one-party dominance: united anti-Congressism, coalition (SVD) governments and mass **defections** (nearly 800 legislators crossed floors, 1967–70) destabilised the states.
- ⚠️ Indira's response was to **go over the party bosses' heads to the electorate** — converting Congress from a federated machine into a **leader-centred, plebiscitary** party.
- ✅ The **1969 split** (Congress-R vs Congress-O) institutionalised a **left-of-centre vs right-of-centre** cleavage, with most MPs and the AICC siding with Indira.

**Deeper — the leftward turn was both ideological and tactical.**

- ✅ **Bank nationalisation (1969)** and the **abolition of privy purses (1971)** were populist-radical measures that isolated the Syndicate and rallied the poor.
- ✅ The **1971 "Garibi Hatao" landslide** (Congress-R, 352/518) rested on a direct appeal to a coalition of the poor and disadvantaged, delinking national from state voting.
- ✅ The **Bangladesh war (1971)** victory made Indira dominant, and **Pokhran-I (1974)** projected strategic strength.
- ⚠️ But personalisation weakened the party organisation and institutional checks — the very fragility that produced the crisis of 1973–75 (topic 35).

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ The **1967 elections** produced coalition (Samyukta Vidhayak Dal) governments and mass **floor-crossing** in the states.
- ✅ The **1969 Congress split** followed the **V.V. Giri** presidential contest and bank nationalisation.
- ✅ The **Supreme Court struck down** the first bank-nationalisation order (1970) and the privy-purse derecognition (1970); the government responded with legislation/amendment.
- ✅ **Privy purses were abolished in 1971** (26th Amendment).
- ✅ The **1971 election** (Congress-R, 352/518) rested on "**Garibi Hatao**."
- ✅ **Pokhran-I (1974)** was India's first nuclear test.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Indira's radicalism was **as much a strategy of power** (isolating the Syndicate) as an ideological programme — the two are inseparable.

- ❌ The Congress system ended in 1969. → It began cracking in **1967**; the **1969 split** completed the rupture.
- ❌ Bank nationalisation went unchallenged. → The **Supreme Court struck down** the first order (1970) before it was re-enacted.
- ❌ The 1971 mandate was a state-level verdict. → It was a **national plebiscite** delinked from state politics.
- ❌ Personalised leadership strengthened institutions. → It **de-institutionalised** the party, seeding the 1975 crisis.
- ❌ Pokhran-I was declared a weapons programme. → It was styled a **"peaceful nuclear explosion."**

## ➕ Historiography note (PYQ-gap)

⚠️ Chandra reads Indira's leftward turn sympathetically — as a genuine, if flawed, attempt to give the poor a stake — while conceding the cost of de-institutionalisation. Rival readings (e.g., institutionalist accounts) foreground the **erosion of party democracy** and the personalisation that made the 1975 Emergency possible. A high-scoring answer treats 1966–73 as a hinge: the moment India's dominant-party democracy became leader-centred, delivering electoral radicalism at the price of institutional decay. PYQ application: use this as the causal bridge in any "why did the Emergency happen?" answer.
