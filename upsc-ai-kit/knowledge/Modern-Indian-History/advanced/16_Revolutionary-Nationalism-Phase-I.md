## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — revolutionary nationalism was a politics of exemplary action.**

- ✅ *India's Struggle for Independence* treats early revolutionary activity as a response to Swadeshi's decline, colonial repression and the impatience of young nationalists seeking immediate action.
- ✅ The revolutionaries believed that assassination, bomb-making, arms procurement and planned mutiny could break fear, inspire patriotic sacrifice and expose the vulnerability of colonial authority.
- ✅ Secret organisations such as Anushilan Samiti, Jugantar and Abhinav Bharat combined physical training, ideological reading, oath-bound discipline and selective violence.
- ⚠️ The strategy's central weakness was substituting heroic conspiracy for sustained mass mobilisation.

**Deeper — the movement became international before it became mass-based.**

- ✅ India House in London, Madame Cama in Europe and the Ghadar Party in North America made anti-colonial politics transnational.
- ✅ Ghadar linked racial discrimination abroad, peasant-soldier migration, anti-imperial propaganda and First World War opportunity into a plan for revolt in India.
- ✅ The 1915 mutiny plan failed because British intelligence penetrated networks and because revolutionary groups lacked coordinated mass support inside India.
- ⚠️ Even when unsuccessful militarily, revolutionary nationalism created martyrs, symbols and networks that later nationalists remembered as proof of sacrifice.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Anushilan Samiti** and **Jugantar** were Bengal revolutionary organisations.
- ✅ **Muzaffarpur 1908** involved **Khudiram Bose** and **Prafulla Chaki** targeting Kingsford.
- ✅ **Alipore Bomb Case** is linked with the Bengal revolutionary circle of Aurobindo and Barindra Ghosh.
- ✅ **Delhi Conspiracy 1912** involved the bomb attack on Viceroy Hardinge and is linked with Rash Behari Bose and Sachin Sanyal.
- ✅ **Abhinav Bharat** was associated with **V.D. Savarkar**.
- ✅ **Ghadar Party** was founded in **1913** in **San Francisco**; **Lala Har Dayal**, **Sohan Singh Bhakna** and **Kartar Singh Sarabha** are key names.
- ✅ **Komagata Maru** occurred in **1914** and fed anti-colonial anger among overseas Indians.
- ⚠️ Revolutionary nationalism of Phase I should be distinguished from later socialist revolutionary politics of the 1920s.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Early revolutionary nationalism was geographically dispersed but organisationally fragmented.

- ❌ Delhi Conspiracy was a Ghadar event in North America. → ✅ It was the 1912 Hardinge bomb case in India, linked with Rash Behari Bose and Sachin Sanyal.
- ❌ Ghadar was only a newspaper. → ✅ *Ghadr* was the paper, while Ghadar Party was the organisation.
- ❌ Revolutionary nationalism had no impact because it failed militarily. → ⚠️ It had psychological, symbolic and international significance despite strategic limits.
- ❌ All revolutionary activity was communal or sectarian. → ✅ Its declared idiom was nationalist and anti-colonial, though social reach varied by region.

## ➕ Historiography note (PYQ-gap)

⚠️ The historiographical challenge is terminology. Colonial accounts labelled these activists as terrorists, while nationalist histories emphasised sacrifice and patriotism. The Bipin Chandra framework recognises their courage and anti-colonial role but also critiques the strategic limits of individual heroic action. For PYQs, use a balanced formulation: revolutionary nationalism was not a substitute for mass politics, but it broadened the emotional, geographical and sacrificial register of the freedom struggle.
