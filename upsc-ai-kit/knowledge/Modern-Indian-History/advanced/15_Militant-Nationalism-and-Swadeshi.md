## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — Extremism was a new ideology of active self-reliance.**

- ✅ *India's Struggle for Independence* presents militant nationalism as rooted in dissatisfaction with Moderate politics, official repression, economic hardship and a changing international psychological climate.
- ✅ Extremists emphasised **swaraj**, self-confidence, sacrifice, boycott, passive resistance and national education rather than reliance on British liberal conscience.
- ✅ Tilak's public festivals, Bankim's **Vande Mataram**, Vivekananda's message of strength and Aurobindo's political writings fed a language of assertive nationalism.
- ⚠️ Extremism did not simply replace Moderate nationalism; it radicalised existing economic critique and expanded methods of political action.

**Deeper — Swadeshi's strength and weakness came from the same social base.**

- ✅ Swadeshi mobilised students, women, urban middle classes, zamindars in parts of Bengal, workers in select strikes and sections of rural society through samitis and cultural networks.
- ✅ Its constructive programme included indigenous industry, arbitration courts, national schools and physical-culture organisations.
- ✅ Government repression, internal disagreements, limited peasant mobilisation and the Moderates-Extremists split contributed to decline after the initial high tide.
- ⚠️ Swadeshi's methods became a repertoire for later politics: boycott, self-reliance, national education and passive resistance reappeared in wider Gandhian movements.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Curzon** partitioned Bengal; announcement came in **July 1905** and implementation on **16 October 1905**.
- ✅ **Lal-Bal-Pal** stands for Lala Lajpat Rai, Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Bipin Chandra Pal.
- ✅ **Aurobindo Ghosh** was associated with Bengal National College and militant nationalist politics.
- ✅ **National Council of Education** was founded in **1906** during Swadeshi.
- ✅ **Surat Split** happened in **1907**; it was a Congress split between Moderates and Extremists.
- ✅ **Indian Councils Act 1909** introduced separate electorates for Muslims.
- ✅ **1911** saw annulment of Bengal partition and transfer of capital from Calcutta to Delhi.
- ⚠️ Tilak's slogan "Swaraj is my birthright and I shall have it" is linked with assertive nationalist mobilisation.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Extremist nationalism was not merely emotional; it had a coherent programme of self-reliance and passive resistance.

- ❌ Swadeshi was confined to buying Indian cloth. → ✅ It included boycott, national education, indigenous enterprise, samitis and cultural mobilisation.
- ❌ Surat Split caused Swadeshi. → ✅ Swadeshi preceded Surat; Surat reflected internal tensions sharpened by the movement.
- ❌ Morley-Minto satisfied nationalist demands. → ✅ It enlarged councils but introduced separate electorates, deepening communal representation.
- ❌ Swadeshi failed because Bengal partition was not annulled. → ✅ Partition was annulled in 1911, but the movement had already declined due to repression and internal limits.

## ➕ Historiography note (PYQ-gap)

⚠️ The historiographical issue is the **moderate-extremist continuum**. A simplistic view treats Extremists as a sharp break from Moderates, while the Bipin Chandra framework stresses continuity in anti-colonial economic critique and discontinuity in methods, idiom and mobilisation. For PYQs, argue that Swadeshi was not a failed episode but a transitional repertoire: it pushed nationalism from annual resolutions toward boycott, constructive work, passive resistance and emotionally resonant mass politics.
