## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — the Swarajist debate was tactical, not anti-national.**

- ✅ *India's Struggle for Independence* presents the No-changer versus Pro-changer debate as a disagreement over method after the suspension of mass struggle.
- ✅ No-changers defended Gandhian constructive work as preparation for future struggle and as a way to keep Congress rooted among the people.
- ✅ Swarajists argued that entering legislatures would prevent political passivity, expose colonial reforms and wreck dyarchy from within.
- ✅ Their 1923 successes gave them a platform, but obstruction had limits because executive power remained colonial and C.R. Das's death in 1925 weakened leadership.

**Deeper — revolutionary nationalism moved toward socialism.**

- ✅ The HRA aimed at an armed overthrow of colonial rule and a republican India; Kakori was intended to obtain funds and publicise revolutionary courage.
- ✅ Bhagat Singh's generation reorganised the movement as the HSRA, adding a clearer socialist and anti-imperialist vocabulary.
- ✅ The Assembly Bomb was deliberately non-lethal and symbolic, meant "to make the deaf hear" and use the courtroom as a platform.
- ⚠️ Bhagat Singh's writings, including "Why I am an Atheist," show a shift from heroic individual action toward ideological, rationalist and socialist politics.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Swaraj Party** was founded on **1 January 1923**; C.R. Das was president and Motilal Nehru secretary.
- ✅ Swarajists used **council-entry** to obstruct colonial government from within.
- ✅ **HRA** was founded in **1924** by Sachindranath Sanyal, Ram Prasad Bismil, Jogesh Chatterjee and others.
- ✅ **Kakori conspiracy** occurred in **1925**; key executions followed in 1927.
- ✅ **HSRA** emerged in **1928** with Bhagat Singh, Chandrashekhar Azad, Sukhdev and Bhagwati Charan Vohra.
- ✅ **Saunders** was killed in December 1928 after Lala Lajpat Rai's death from lathi-charge injuries.
- ✅ **Central Assembly Bomb** was thrown on **8 April 1929** by Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt.
- ✅ **Azad** died in Alfred Park in February 1931; Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev and Rajguru were executed on **23 March 1931**.
- ✅ **Chittagong Armoury Raid** was led by Surya Sen in 1930; women revolutionaries included Kalpana Datta and Pritilata Waddedar.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not place Bhagat Singh in Kakori; his major cases are Saunders, Assembly Bomb and Lahore Conspiracy.

- ❌ Swarajists were constitutional loyalists. → ✅ They entered councils to **obstruct** and expose colonial rule, not to legitimise it.
- ❌ No-changers and Pro-changers represented a permanent split. → ✅ They were tactical streams within the wider national movement.
- ❌ HSRA was merely HRA renamed. → ✅ The new name reflected a **socialist ideological turn**.
- ❌ Assembly Bomb aimed at mass killing. → ✅ It was symbolic and designed to avoid indiscriminate killing.
- ❌ Revolutionary nationalism was confined to Punjab. → ✅ It had major centres in Punjab, United Provinces and Bengal.

## ➕ Historiography note (PYQ-gap)

⚠️ Historiography has moved beyond treating revolutionaries as romantic terrorists. Bipin Chandra stresses their anti-imperialist patriotism and ideological growth, while later scholarship highlights Bhagat Singh's rationalism, socialism and use of the courtroom as political theatre. Subaltern and regional studies of Chittagong also show that revolutionary activity included women, students and local networks, not only famous male martyrs.
