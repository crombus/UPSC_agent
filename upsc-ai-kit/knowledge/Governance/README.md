## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the must-do concepts and Indian
  applications; `advanced/NN` adds institutional design, failure modes, trade-offs,
  reform debates and Mains depth. The same number denotes the same topic.
- **Tagging:** ✅ = directly grounded in an official source or verified PYQ;
  ⚠️ = analytical inference or standard conceptual linkage; 📰 = a dated current
  policy, institutional or legal anchor.
- **Source discipline:** ARC reports are dated reform documents. Their recommendations
  are not presented as current law unless separately verified.
- **Dynamic facts:** rankings, platform-user counts, DBT savings and grievance-disposal
  figures change frequently. Always retain the reporting date and prefer the latest
  official dashboard or report.

## Boundaries with other subjects

- **Polity owns constitutional and institutional structure:** local-body provisions,
  CIC, CVC/CBI, Lokpal, CAG, Finance Commission, GST Council, NITI Aayog, public
  services and pressure groups. Governance studies how institutions design, deliver,
  regulate, coordinate and evaluate public action.
- **Ethics owns the probity and personal-conduct angle:** integrity, corruption,
  detailed RTI ethics, codes and the ethical design of Citizens' Charters. Governance
  studies administrative systems, statutory service guarantees and accountability
  mechanisms.
- **Social Justice owns vulnerable-group schemes, health, education, poverty and
  hunger.** Governance covers the cross-sector policy and implementation tools used
  to deliver and evaluate such interventions.

## Study sequence

1. **Foundation:** `01-03` — define governance, understand policy implementation
   and locate non-government development actors.
2. **Citizen-state interface:** `04-08` — civil society, e-governance, DPI,
   citizen-centric administration and accountability.
3. **Administrative capacity:** `09-11` — civil services, administrative reform and
   regulatory governance.
4. **Delivery and feedback:** `12-15` — local delivery, financial flows,
   participation and outcome evaluation.
