**Core proposition:** ✅ The poverty-malnutrition-human-capital vicious cycle (2024
PYQ Q7) traps households across generations; breaking it requires multi-sectoral
intervention — food entitlements (NFSA), nutrition services (Poshan 2.0, PM POSHAN),
portability (ONORC), women's anaemia focus and WASH convergence — not PDS reform
alone.

## 2. Essential definitions

| Concept | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Food security** | Reliable access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food to meet dietary needs — the NFSA 2013 primarily addresses calorie/grain access through statutory foodgrain entitlements (currently distributed free of cost). |
| ✅ **Nutrition security** | Goes beyond calorie access to include dietary diversity, micronutrients, care practices and the health/sanitation environment — multi-sectoral, not just PDS. |
| ✅ **Stunting (chronic undernutrition)** | Low height-for-age in children under 5; reflects cumulative nutritional and health deprivation (especially first 1000 days) — a chronic indicator. |
| ✅ **Wasting (acute undernutrition)** | Low weight-for-height in children under 5; reflects recent/acute food shortage or illness — a crisis indicator. |
| ✅ **Anaemia** | Haemoglobin deficiency (iron, folate, B12) affecting oxygen-carrying capacity; prevalent among children, adolescent girls and pregnant women — linked to productivity and maternal/child health outcomes. |
| ✅ **NFSA, 2013** | The National Food Security Act, 2013, providing statutory entitlement to foodgrains: 5 kg/person/month for priority households, 35 kg/household/month for Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households. The Act itself fixes Central Issue Prices as a statutory ceiling of ₹3/kg rice, ₹2/kg wheat and ₹1/kg coarse grains — but since 1 January 2023 the PM Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana (PMGKAY) has been merged with the NFSA and all NFSA foodgrains are distributed **free of cost** to AAY/priority-household beneficiaries; the Union Cabinet extended this free-foodgrain policy for five years, through December 2028. Current policy is free distribution, not the ₹1-3/kg statutory ceiling. |
| ✅ **ONORC** | One Nation One Ration Card — enables portability of NFSA entitlements across states using Aadhaar-seeded ration cards and ePoS (electronic Point of Sale) machines; critical for migrant/mobile beneficiaries. |
| ✅ **Poshan 2.0 / Saksham Anganwadi** | The umbrella nutrition programme (MWCD) merging ICDS and related schemes; delivers supplementary nutrition, growth monitoring, pre-school education and referral through anganwadi centres; emphasises first-1000-days (conception to age 2) intervention. |
| ✅ **PM POSHAN (erstwhile Mid-Day Meal)** | The school-nutrition programme (Ministry of Education) providing cooked meals to children in government/aided schools (Classes I-VIII); objectives include attendance, retention and nutritional status improvement. |

## 3. How the food-and-nutrition-security mechanism works

1. **NFSA entitlement architecture:** Coverage via priority households (~75% rural,
   ~50% urban) and AAY (poorest of poor); foodgrain entitlement per person per
   month; the Act's statutory Central Issue Price ceiling (₹3/kg rice, ₹2/kg wheat,
   ₹1/kg coarse grains) has been superseded in practice by free distribution since
   1 January 2023 (PMGKAY-NFSA merger, extended to December 2028) — but NFSA
   addresses calorie access, not dietary diversity.
2. **Poshan 2.0 / ICDS delivery:** Anganwadi centres provide supplementary nutrition
   (take-home ration, hot cooked meals), growth monitoring (weight/height tracking),
   pre-school education, immunisation referral and health/nutrition counselling —
   primary focus is children under 6, pregnant and lactating women.
3. **PM POSHAN (school nutrition):** Cooked mid-day meal in schools addresses
   immediate hunger, improves attendance and provides a platform for nutrition
   education — complements ICDS for school-age children.
4. **ONORC portability:** Aadhaar-seeded ration cards allow beneficiaries to draw
   entitlements from any ePoS-enabled fair-price shop nationwide — addresses
   migrant exclusion from state-specific PDS lists.
5. **WASH convergence:** Sanitation (SBM), safe water (JJM) and hygiene behaviour
   affect nutrient absorption; stunting reduction requires convergence beyond food
   alone.

## 4. Institutions and tools

- ✅ **NFSA, 2013:** Statutory entitlement; administered by Department of Food and
  Public Distribution (MoCAFPD) at Union level, state food/civil supplies
  departments at state level.
- ✅ **Poshan 2.0 / Saksham Anganwadi:** Administered by Ministry of Women and Child
  Development (MWCD); implemented through ICDS infrastructure (anganwadi centres,
  AWWs/helpers).
- ✅ **PM POSHAN:** Administered by Ministry of Education; implemented through
  state education departments and school management committees.
- ✅ **ONORC:** Technical backbone provided by NIC/FCI; requires Aadhaar seeding,
  ePoS rollout and inter-state data sharing.
- ✅ **NFHS (National Family Health Survey):** Primary survey source for stunting,
  wasting, anaemia and related household outcomes; NFHS-6 (2023-24) was officially
  released on 29 May 2026.

## 5. Indian applications and examples

- ✅ **2024 GS-II PYQ Q7 (10 marks, 150 words):** "Poverty and malnutrition create a
  vicious cycle, adversely affecting human capital formation. What steps can be
  taken to break the cycle?" — the expected answer traces the intergenerational
  trap and lists multi-sectoral levers (Poshan 2.0/first-1000-days, PM POSHAN,
  ONORC, women's anaemia, WASH convergence).
- ⚠️ ONORC enabled migrant workers during COVID-19 lockdowns to access rations
  outside their home states — illustrates the portability rationale.
- ⚠️ NFHS-5 (2019-21) found stunting at ~35% and anaemia among children under 5 at
  ~67% nationally (exact figures vary by state) — illustrates the scale of the
  nutrition challenge.

## 7. UPSC traps

- ❌ NFSA addresses nutrition security comprehensively. -> NFSA primarily addresses
  calorie/grain access (food security); nutrition security requires dietary
  diversity, micronutrients, care practices and WASH — a multi-sectoral effort
  beyond PDS.
- ❌ Stunting and wasting are interchangeable terms. -> Stunting (height-for-age)
  reflects chronic deprivation; wasting (weight-for-height) reflects acute
  deprivation — different indicators, different policy responses.
- ❌ PM POSHAN is administered by MWCD. -> PM POSHAN (erstwhile Mid-Day Meal) is
  administered by the Ministry of Education; Poshan 2.0/ICDS is administered by
  MWCD.
- ❌ ONORC is a separate ration card. -> ONORC is a portability mechanism enabling
  existing NFSA beneficiaries to draw entitlements anywhere; it is not a separate
  card but a system interoperability feature.
- ❌ NFSA beneficiaries currently pay ₹1-3/kg for their foodgrain entitlement. ->
  ₹3/kg rice, ₹2/kg wheat and ₹1/kg coarse grains is the Act's statutory Central
  Issue Price ceiling, not current practice; foodgrains have been provided free of
  cost to AAY/priority-household beneficiaries since 1 January 2023, a policy the
  Union Cabinet extended through December 2028.

## 8. 📰 Current anchor

- 📰 **NFHS-6 (2023-24):** The latest completed national round was officially
  released on 29 May 2026. The NFHS-5 (2019-21) figures cited above remain dated
  baseline values; do not relabel them as NFHS-6 outcomes, and use the official
  NFHS-6 factsheets for any newer nutrition indicator.
- 📰 **Free-foodgrain policy:** NFSA foodgrains have been distributed free of cost
  to AAY/priority-household beneficiaries since 1 January 2023 (PMGKAY merged
  into NFSA), and the Union Cabinet extended this free-distribution policy for
  five years, through December 2028, covering roughly 81 crore beneficiaries.
- 📰 ONORC portability transactions can be tracked on the IMPDS portal; the
  cumulative transaction count rises continuously and is not fixed here — the
  durable fact is the system's operational inter-state portability.

## 10. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Use the vicious-cycle diagram as the default framing for any poverty-nutrition
  question — it directly maps to 2024 Q7.
- ⚠️ Distinguish food security (calorie access via NFSA) from nutrition security
  (dietary diversity, micronutrients, WASH) — a recurring exam distinction.
- ⚠️ Emphasise the first-1000-days window: interventions during pregnancy and the
  first two years yield the highest returns in preventing stunting.
- ⚠️ Link ONORC to migrant-worker inclusion — a current-affairs-friendly angle.

> **Answer thesis:** The poverty-malnutrition-human-capital vicious cycle is
> intergenerational and multi-causal; breaking it requires not only food
> entitlements (NFSA) but also nutrition services focused on the first 1000 days
> (Poshan 2.0), school nutrition (PM POSHAN), portability for migrants (ONORC),
> women's anaemia reduction and WASH convergence — a single-scheme approach is
> structurally insufficient.
