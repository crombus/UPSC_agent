# Social Justice - Master Conceptual Framework

> **Purpose:** Connect vulnerable-group protection, social-sector services,
> entitlements and outcome measurement before studying the detailed files.
> **Core question:** How does a constitutional welfare state convert formal equality
> into substantive capability, dignity and participation for people facing unequal
> starting conditions?

---

## 1. The social-justice results chain

```text
IDENTIFY DISADVANTAGE
income, caste, tribe, gender, disability,
age, occupation, location or migration
             |
             v
RECOGNISE RIGHT OR PROTECTION

             |
             v

food, health, education, shelter,

             |
             v

Union ministry -> State -> local/frontline body
             |
             v

affordability + availability + accessibility

             |
             v
OUTCOME AND CAPABILITY
health, learning, livelihood, dignity,
security, voice and social mobility
             |
             v
DATA + ACCOUNTABILITY + CORRECTION
survey, administrative record, census,
crime data, social audit and grievance
```

**Core proposition:** ⚠️ Social justice is not achieved by formal legal equality or
scheme announcement alone. It requires protection against discrimination, accessible
services, enforceable entitlements and measurable improvement in capabilities.

## 2. Three dimensions of justice

| Dimension | Central concern | Typical policy response |
|---|---|---|
| Distributive justice | Who receives resources, services and opportunities? | Transfers, reservation, public services and social security |
| Corrective justice | How is a specific wrong remedied? | Compensation, rehabilitation, penalty and restoration |
| Recognitive justice | Whose identity, dignity and voice are denied? | Anti-discrimination law, representation and identity recognition |

Most UPSC questions require a combination. Manual scavenging, for example, requires
distributive support, corrective rehabilitation and recognitive dignity.

## 3. Equality is not sameness

```text
FORMAL EQUALITY
same rule for everyone
        |
        v
may preserve unequal starting conditions
        |
        v
SUBSTANTIVE EQUALITY
differentiated support removes structural barriers
        |
        v
CAPABILITY EQUALITY
people gain real freedom to be healthy, educated,
secure, mobile and politically/socially included
```

- **Universalism** reduces stigma and exclusion errors but may spend resources on
  people who need less support.
- **Targeting** concentrates resources but creates identification, documentation and
  exclusion risks.
- **Universal basic services** can coexist with targeted income, rehabilitation or
  affirmative-action measures.

## 4. Five recurring welfare gaps

| Gap | Failure | Typical correction |
|---|---|---|
| Identification gap | Eligible people are absent from lists or lack documents | Dynamic registries, portability and assisted enrolment |
| Legal-enforcement gap | Protection exists on paper but violations persist | Special procedures, enforcement capacity and remedies |
| Delivery gap | Funds or services do not reach the last mile | Convergence, frontline capacity and local accountability |
| Access-quality gap | A service exists but is unaffordable, unsafe or poor quality | Standards, reasonable accommodation and public provision |
| Outcome-learning gap | Outputs are counted without measuring capability change | Survey triangulation, independent evaluation and redesign |

## 6. Entitlement, scheme and institution

| Instrument | Meaning | Example type |
|---|---|---|
| Protective law | Prohibits harm or discrimination and creates remedies | Atrocities, disability, child or gender law |
| Statutory entitlement | Legally specifies access to a benefit or service | Food or education entitlement |
| Welfare scheme | Administrative programme implementing a policy objective | Scholarship, insurance or livelihood support |
| Protective institution | Investigates, advises, monitors or receives complaints | NCW, NCM or NCPCR |
| Social-sector system | Continuing public capacity rather than a single scheme | Public health or education system |

**Exam trap:** A scheme cannot substitute for enforcement of a protective law, and a
commission cannot substitute for the department responsible for service delivery.

### Status ladder for volatile claims

| Status | What it proves — and what it does **not** prove |
|---|---|
| Announced / approved | Political intent or budget decision; not a legal entitlement by itself |
| Enacted | Parliament or a legislature has made a law; commencement may still await notification |
| Rules notified | Subordinate framework exists; check its territorial and provision-specific commencement |
| Commenced | The notified provision is legally in force; this does not prove funds, capacity or uptake |
| Funded / operational | Delivery has begun; report the source date and distinguish a dashboard output from an outcome |

**Use in answers:** Name the precise rung. For example, an enacted labour code, a
notified rule and a benefit actually received by a worker are three different claims.

## 7. Measuring outcomes responsibly

| Source | Strength | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Census | Broad population coverage | Infrequent and slow |
| Household survey such as NFHS/PLFS | Comparable outcome indicators | Sampling and reporting lag |
| Administrative dashboard | High frequency and programme-specific | Excludes non-enrolled people and may incentivise gaming |
| NCRB data | Recorded crimes and justice-process information | Under-reporting and registration practices |
| Independent evaluation | Deeper causal/implementation insight | Costly and periodic |

Use multiple sources. Administrative coverage can rise while survey outcomes or
recorded discrimination remain poor.

## 9. Mains answer spine

1. **Identify the affected group and the structural barrier.**
2. **Name the legal or entitlement foundation.**
3. **Map the responsible institution and delivery route.**
4. **Distinguish coverage from effective access and quality.**
5. **State the legal and delivery status, then use dated outcome evidence rather than an
   undated dashboard total.**
6. **Diagnose the specific gap:** identification, enforcement, delivery, quality or
   learning.
7. **Recommend convergence, remedy and a measurable capability outcome.**
