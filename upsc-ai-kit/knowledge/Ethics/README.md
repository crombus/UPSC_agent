## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the must-do concept, definitions and usable
  examples; `advanced/NN` adds philosophical/administrative debates, legal nuance, institutional
  constraints, case analysis and answer-writing depth. **Same number = same topic** in both tiers.
- **Tagging discipline:** ✅ = directly grounded in a cited source (ARC Report, statute, official
  body, verified PYQ); ⚠️ = analytical inference, standard linkage or illustrative framing;
  📰 = a dated, verifiable current-affairs or status anchor.
- **Dated-source discipline:** The ARC's 4th Report is a **2007 document**. Its data, case
  vintage (e.g., conviction-rate tables) and *recommendations* (e.g., Lokpal, whistleblower law,
  collusive-bribery offence) are presented as **dated source material**, clearly separated from
  **current enacted-law status** verified as of July 2026 (e.g., Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013;
  Whistle Blowers Protection Act, 2014 — enacted but still not brought into force; Benami
  Transactions (Prohibition) Amendment Act, 2016, whose retrospective effect was struck down in
  *Union of India v. Ganpati Dealcom* (2022) and then recalled by the Supreme Court in 2024,
  leaving the question open).
- **No duplication of constitutional-body detail:** composition, appointment and removal
  procedure of CVC, CBI, Lokpal/Lokayukta, CIC/SIC and the Public Service Commissions are taught
  in `Polity/advanced/37_CVC-and-CBI.md`, `Polity/advanced/38_Lokpal-and-Lokayuktas.md`,
  `Polity/advanced/36_CIC-and-SIC.md` and `Polity/advanced/41_Public-Services.md`. This folder links to
  them and teaches only the **ethics-specific** angle: jurisdiction debates, ethical dilemmas,
  probity and dilemma analysis.
- **No duplication of optional-depth philosophy:** classical positions of Aristotle, Kant, Mill,
  the Buddha and Indian darshanas are taught in depth in `Philosophy/paper-1/western/` and
  `Philosophy/paper-1/indian/`. This folder links to them and teaches only what GS-IV actually
  tests — usable definitions, thinker-to-administration translation and PYQ-quotation analysis.

## Study sequence

1. **Foundations (01-05):** what ethics is, how values/attitude/aptitude/EQ shape conduct.
2. **Thought traditions (06-08):** Indian and Western thinkers, then applied moral theories.
3. **Public-service core (09-12):** values, sources of guidance, accountability, corporate/global ethics.
4. **Contemporary and administrative machinery (13-18):** emerging ethics, probity, transparency,
   codes, service delivery, public-money integrity.
5. **Anti-corruption architecture (19-21):** law, institutions, protection of the honest.
6. **Application (22-23):** case-study architecture, then named/comparative cases for the Mains
   answer bank.
