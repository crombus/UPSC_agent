## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the must-do definitional,
  institutional and legal map for each threat; `advanced/NN` adds the
  causal chain, federal/rights trade-offs, implementation gaps and
  Mains-ready answer architecture. Advanced does not repeat basic — it
  analyses what basic establishes.
- **Tagging:** ✅ = directly grounded in a retrieved book passage, an
  official document or a verified PYQ; ⚠️ = analytical inference, a
  contested assertion, an OCR defect carried from source, or a mandatory
  refresh warning; 📰 = a dated official current anchor; ❌ = a boundary/trap
  statement marking what this folder does **not** cover or a common
  misconception.
- **Date discipline — book-period vs current:** Ashok Kumar Singh's book
  reflects roughly the 2013-2016 period (pre-dating the August 2019
  reorganisation of Jammu & Kashmir, the 8 February 2024 decision to scrap
  the Myanmar
  Free Movement Regime, the 2024 FATF Mutual Evaluation, and the 2026
  PRAHAAR policy). Every book-period fact — group strength, district
  counts, agency operational status, leader names, law text — is labelled
  **historical/book-period evidence** and is never presented as the
  current state of affairs. Current facts are cited only to a dated
  official source.
- **No stale-danger claims:** this folder does not say an objective (e.g.
  LWE elimination by 31 March 2026) has been achieved merely because its
  deadline has arrived; it reports only what a dated official release
  confirms.

## Boundaries with other subjects

- **Polity owns constitutional doctrine:** Article 370's constitutional
  text, the litigation history and the Supreme Court's December 2023
  judgment upholding its abrogation, and detailed fundamental-rights
  jurisprudence. Internal Security owns the operational, federal and
  rights-versus-security trade-offs of counter-terror and counter-
  insurgency practice — see `05_Jammu-Kashmir-and-Cross-Border-Terrorism`
  and `12_Security-Forces-Intelligence-Coordination-and-Rights`.
- **International Relations owns external diplomatic/security
  cooperation:** bilateral defence pacts, diaspora diplomacy, multilateral
  counter-terror platforms. Internal Security owns the domestic
  operational effect of cross-border threats — see
  `International-Relations/basic/02_India-and-the-Neighbourhood.md`.
- **Economy owns macro black-money, tax and fiscal mechanics.** Internal
  Security owns anti-money-laundering/counter-financing-of-terrorism
  (AML/CFT) architecture and the criminal-security nexus — see
  `10_Terror-Financing-Money-Laundering-and-FATF`.
- **Science & Technology owns technical cyber internals** (encryption
  mathematics, network protocols as engineering). Internal Security owns
  cyber **threats, institutions and resilience** — see
  `08_Cyber-Security-CII-and-Cybercrime`.
- **Geography owns physical border and coastal maps** (terrain, river
  courses, coastline length as physical geography). Internal Security owns
  border and coastal **management architecture** — see
  `06_Border-Management-and-Border-Area-Development` and
  `07_Maritime-and-Coastal-Security`.
- **Disaster Management remains a separate, forthcoming module.** This
  folder mentions emergency-security interfaces (e.g. CAPF disaster-relief
  roles) only where the source material ties them directly to an internal
  security mandate; it does not cover the DM Act, NDMA or the hazard-
  vulnerability-resilience framework.

## Study sequence

1. **Foundation:** `01` — threat classification and the doctrinal elements
   every later topic reuses.
2. **Named threats in ascending complexity:** `02-05` — terrorism as the
   general category, then LWE, North-East insurgency and J&K as the three
   named threats the syllabus and PYQs repeatedly test.
3. **Territorial and domain architecture:** `06-07` — land border and
   maritime/coastal management as the spatial response layer.
4. **Digital-domain threats:** `08-09` — cybersecurity/CII first, then the
   social-media/information-warfare layer that sits on top of it.
5. **Financial-criminal nexus:** `10-11` — terror financing/FATF first
   (financing a political objective), then organised crime/narco-terrorism
   (a wider criminal-financial nexus that also funds terrorism).
6. **Institutional capstone:** `12` — security forces, intelligence
   coordination and rights, which every earlier topic's "measures taken"
   section ultimately routes through.

## Primary source routes

- Ashok Kumar Singh, *Challenges to Internal Security of India* — spine
  for definitions, institutional detail and case material across all
  twelve topics; treat all personnel numbers, leader names, group
  strengths and "yet to become operational" statements as book-period.
- VisionIAS Value Added Material, *Challenges to Internal Security through
  Communication Network* (2024 update) — cyber threat taxonomy, hybrid
  warfare, 5G risk and fake-news countermeasures.
- VisionIAS Value Added Material, *Security Challenges and Their
  Management in Border Areas* — China/Pakistan border tension chronology,
  the announced 2024 decision to scrap the Myanmar FMR, BADP/BIM/CIBMS,
  and the coastal security architecture. Current operational status
  requires the latest formal MHA/MEA notification.
- MHA (PRAHAAR policy, Annual Report, Rajya Sabha/Lok Sabha replies,
  Border Management divisions), MeitY (DPDP Rules), CERT-In, NCIIPC, NIA,
  NCB, FIU-IND, ED, FATF, Indian Navy and Indian Coast Guard official
  releases for all current-anchor material.
