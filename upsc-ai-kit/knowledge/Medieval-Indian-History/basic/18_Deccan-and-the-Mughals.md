## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — why the Mughals entered the Deccan.**

✅ After consolidating north India, Mughal involvement in the Deccan was logical: the Vindhyas were not an absolute barrier, and north-south trade, pilgrim traffic, migration and politics were already connected.
- ✅ Deccani politics was unstable due to wars among Ahmadnagar, Bijapur and Golconda, factional fights among nobles, and Deccani–afaqi/Habshi/Afghan rivalries.
- ✅ Akbar also worried that sectarian conflicts in the Deccan and Portuguese pressure on western sea-routes could affect Mughal interests.
- ✅ In 1591 Akbar began with diplomacy; only Khandesh accepted suzerainty clearly.

**Foundation — from Akbar's foothold to Shah Jahan's settlement.**

✅ The first Mughal breakthrough came in Ahmadnagar: **Chand Bibi** defended the fort but accepted a treaty in **1596**, ceding Berar and accepting Mughal suzerainty.
- ✅ In **1600**, after Chand Bibi's murder by a hostile faction, the Mughals captured Ahmadnagar fort and added Balaghat; in **1601**, Akbar captured Asirgarh and incorporated Khandesh.
- ✅ But the Mughals could not consolidate because **Malik Ambar**, an Abyssinian leader and Peshwa of Ahmadnagar, mobilised Maratha light cavalry and guerrilla methods.
- ✅ Shah Jahan changed course: he decided Ahmadnagar could not remain independent, captured Daulatabad in **1633**, and used the **1636 treaties** to secure Bijapur and Golconda's recognition of Mughal suzerainty.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Chand Bibi** defended Ahmadnagar; Berar was ceded to Mughals in **1596**.
- ✅ Ahmadnagar fort captured in **1600**; **Asirgarh/Khandesh** incorporated in **1601**.
- ✅ **Malik Ambar** was Abyssinian/Habshi and used Maratha **bargi** cavalry against Mughals.
- ✅ Jahangir deliberately avoided over-extension in the Deccan after victories.
- ✅ **Daulatabad (1633)** = end of Nizam Shahi dynasty.
- ✅ **Treaties of 1636** made Bijapur and Golconda accept Mughal suzerainty/protection.
- ✅ The **1597 battle of Sonepat** in the assigned source belongs to the Berar-Deccan campaign; it should not be confused with present-day Sonipat in Haryana.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The Mughals did not immediately annex all Deccan sultanates under Akbar or Jahangir; their policy often sought suzerainty, buffers and limited control.

- ❌ Akbar conquered the whole Deccan. → He secured Khandesh, Berar/Balaghat and Ahmadnagar fort, not all Deccani states.
- ❌ Malik Ambar was a Maratha ruler. → He was an **Abyssinian/Habshi** leader who used Maratha cavalry effectively.
- ❌ Jahangir failed because he was militarily weak. → Satish Chandra stresses his limited policy was deliberate.
- ❌ 1636 ended Deccan politics permanently. → It stabilised the region temporarily; 1656–57 reopened the problem.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Mughal Deccan policy evolved from **diplomatic suzerainty** to **selective annexation** and finally toward deeper intervention.
- ⚠️ Malik Ambar's importance lies less in permanent territorial success and more in training Maratha power and frustrating imperial consolidation.
- ⚠️ The Deccan shows the limits of Mughal expansion in a region of mobile warfare, competing sultanates and emerging Maratha elites.
