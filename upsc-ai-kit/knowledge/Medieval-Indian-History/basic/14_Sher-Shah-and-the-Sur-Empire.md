## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — why Sher Shah mattered.**

- ✅ Sher Shah rose from Farid, son of a small jagirdar, to the ruler of the mightiest north Indian empire since Muhammad bin Tughlaq.
- ✅ His empire extended from Bengal to the Indus, excluding Kashmir; in the west he conquered Malwa and almost all Rajasthan.
- ✅ Sher Shah defeated Humayun at **Chausa (1539)** and **Kanauj (1540)**, interrupting early Mughal rule.
- ⚠️ The Sur phase is not a mere gap between Humayun and Akbar; it created administrative precedents Akbar adopted and improved.

**Foundation — roads, revenue and order.**

- ✅ Sher Shah restored law and order, dealt sternly with robbers/dacoits and recalcitrant zamindars, and made roads safe for merchants.
- ✅ He restored the old imperial road later called the **Grand Trunk Road**, from the Indus to Sonargaon, and built roads from Agra to Jodhpur/Chittor and Lahore to Multan.
- ✅ He built **sarais** at regular intervals, with separate lodging arrangements for Hindus and Muslims, and used them as stages for the news service.
- ✅ His revenue system measured sown land, drew up crop rates (*rai*), generally fixed the state share at one-third, and issued *patta* to peasants.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Sher Shah's original name was **Farid**.
- ✅ His reign: **1540-1545**; Sur empire continued till 1555/56 phase.
- ✅ **Grand Trunk Road** restoration is associated with Sher Shah.
- ✅ State share under his measured revenue system was **one-third** of average produce.
- ✅ **Patta** recorded the area, crop and amount due from each peasant.
- ✅ He used **chehra** and **dagh** for army control.
- ✅ He died in **1545** during the siege of **Kalinjar**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Sher Shah's reforms were administrative and commercial, but they were also instruments of state control.

- ❌ Sher Shah invented the GT Road from nothing. → He restored and improved the old imperial road.
- ❌ Sarais were only welfare shelters. → They also supported trade, news and military movement.
- ❌ Sher Shah abolished zamindars. → He controlled them and made them responsible; he did not eliminate the local rural elite.
- ❌ Akbar's revenue system had no precedent. → Sher Shah's measurement and crop-rate system was an important predecessor.
