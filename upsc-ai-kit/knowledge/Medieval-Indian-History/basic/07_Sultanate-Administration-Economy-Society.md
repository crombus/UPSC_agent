## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Sultanate administration: centralised but negotiated.**

✅ The Delhi Sultanate became a powerful, highly centralised state, at times controlling territory as far south as Madurai; even after disintegration, its administration influenced later regional states and the Mughals.
- ✅ The **sultan** held supreme political, military and legal authority, acted as commander-in-chief, and could hear appeals against officials.
- ✅ Succession had no settled rule: all sons had claims, nomination needed noble acceptance, and military strength often decided outcomes.
- ✅ Key departments included **wazir** (revenue/income-expenditure), **diwan-i-arz** (military recruitment/equipment/pay), **diwan-i-risalat** (religious grants), and **diwan-i-insha** (state correspondence).
- ✅ **Barids** acted as intelligence agents; **karkhanas** supplied and sometimes manufactured royal goods; Firuz also created public works and slave departments.

**Foundation — economy and society: towns, trade, inequality.**

✅ With Sultanate consolidation, communications and a sound currency based on the silver **tanka** and copper **dirham** helped trade grow; towns and town life expanded.
- ✅ Ibn Battuta called Delhi the largest city in the eastern Islamic world and said Daulatabad equalled Delhi in size.
- ✅ Important towns included Lahore, Multan, Kara, Lakhnauti, Anhilwara/Patan and Cambay.
- ✅ India produced rice, sugarcane, wheat, oilseeds, sesame, indigo and cotton; village industries included oil-pressing, jaggery, weaving and dyeing.
- ✅ Wealthy merchants included Gujaratis, Marwaris, Jains, Bohras, Multanis and Khurasanis; Indian textiles reached the Red Sea, Persian Gulf and China.
- ✅ Society was unequal: nobles and wealthy merchants lived ostentatiously, while peasants, artisans and labourers often faced hardship.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Wazir** = revenue/income-expenditure; **diwan-i-arz** = military department.
- ✅ **Ariz-i-mamalik** recruited, equipped and paid the army; the sultan remained commander-in-chief.
- ✅ **Diwan-i-insha** handled state correspondence.
- ✅ **Barids** were intelligence agents.
- ✅ **Iqtas** were tracts assigned to nobles; holders were called **muqtis** or **walis**.
- ✅ Below provinces were **shiqs** and **parganas**; the pargana was headed by the **amil**.
- ✅ Cambay and Sonargaon were famous for textiles; Bengal for raw silk/fine cotton; Gujarat for textiles and gold/silver work.
- ✅ The Turks popularised/improved paper-making, spinning wheel, cotton-carder's bow, improved loom, iron stirrup and lime mortar use.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The Sultanate state was centralised at the top but relied heavily on local elites and older village structures.

- ❌ Iqta was identical to later Mughal jagir in every respect. → It was an earlier Sultanate assignment with its own controls and evolution.
- ❌ The sultan delegated supreme military command to the ariz. → The **sultan** remained commander-in-chief.
- ❌ All Hindus were excluded from administration. → Local machinery remained largely in Hindu hands, and Hindus served as managers/soldiers/officials.
- ❌ Sultanate towns were only military camps. → Many began around garrisons but grew into commercial and cultural centres.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ The Sultanate administration combined central authority with dependence on local intermediaries.
- ⚠️ Urbanisation under the Sultanate was driven by garrisons, ruling-class consumption, trade, crafts and currency.
- ⚠️ Sultanate society should be studied through inequality: elite consumption coexisted with peasant and artisan hardship.
