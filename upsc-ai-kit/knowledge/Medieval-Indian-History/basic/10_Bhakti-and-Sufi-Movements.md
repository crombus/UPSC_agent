## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Sufi orders and social contact.**

✅ Sufis were mystics who stressed devotion, love and nearness to God. They organised into **silsilahs** led by a pir in a **khanqah**, with disciples (murids) and successors (khalifas/walis).
- ✅ In north India the two major ba-shara orders were **Chishti** and **Suhrawardi**.
- ✅ Chishtis such as Muinuddin Chishti, Bakhtiyar Kaki, Baba Farid, Nizamuddin Auliya and Nasiruddin Chiragh-i-Delhi stressed simplicity, poverty, service, sama and distance from politics.
- ✅ Suhrawardis were stronger in Punjab and Multan and accepted state service/grants more readily than Chishtis.

**Foundation — Bhakti as devotion and social critique.**

✅ Bhakti stressed personal devotion and mystical union with God. It grew strongly in south India among the Nayanars and Alvars, then moved slowly north through saints such as Namadeva and Ramananda.
- ✅ Ramananda taught Bhakti to all four varnas and accepted disciples from low castes, including Ravidas, Kabir, Sena and Sadhana.
- ✅ Kabir emphasised one God, denounced idol worship, pilgrimages, ritual formalism and caste discrimination.
- ✅ Nanak emphasised one God, purity of character, guru's guidance, household life and rejection of empty ritual.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Silsilah** = Sufi order; **khanqah** = hospice; **pir-murid** link central.
- ✅ **Ba-shara** orders followed Islamic law; **be-shara** wandering saints were not bound by it.
- ✅ Chishtis used **sama** and often Hindawi/Hindi verses for devotional impact.
- ✅ Nizamuddin Auliya adopted yogic breathing; yogis called him a **sidh**.
- ✅ Ramananda's disciples included **Ravidas, Kabir, Sena, Sadhana**.
- ✅ Nanak did not intend to found a new religion; his ideas later gave birth to Sikhism.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Bhakti and Sufi movements were not identical, but they created shared ethical vocabularies: love, devotion, guru/pir and social tolerance.

- ❌ Chishtis mainly aimed at conversions. → Source says they were hardly interested in effecting conversions.
- ❌ Suhrawardis rejected all state contact. → They accepted grants and some held official posts.
- ❌ Kabir was a state-backed social reformer. → He focused on individual reform under a true guru and stayed outside royal courts.
- ❌ Nanak preached renunciation of household life. → He advocated a middle path combining spirituality and household duties.
