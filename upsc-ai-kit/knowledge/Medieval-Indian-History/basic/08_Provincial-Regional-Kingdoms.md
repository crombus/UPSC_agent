## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — why regional kingdoms mattered.**

✅ Timur's invasion and the weakening of Delhi encouraged provincial governors and local powers to assert independence. Bengal, Gujarat, Malwa, Jaunpur and Kashmir were not minor footnotes: they carried forward administration, trade, literature and regional architecture when Delhi was weak.
- ✅ A balance of power emerged: Gujarat, Malwa and Mewar checked one another in the west; Bengal was checked by Orissa and Jaunpur; the Lodis later fought Jaunpur for the Ganga-Yamuna valley.
- ✅ Malwa's plateau position made it a strategic "barrier" between Gujarat, Mewar, the Bahmanis and Delhi.
- ⚠️ UPSC uses this phase to test whether you see medieval India as **regional state formation**, not just Delhi Sultanate chronology.

**Foundation — regional culture, trade and tolerance.**

✅ Bengal flourished because Delhi found it hard to control: distance, climate and water-based communications favoured autonomy. Its sultans patronised Bengali language, and Alauddin Hussain Shah offered high offices to Hindus, including Rupa and Sanatan.
- ✅ Gujarat was rich due to fertile soil, handicrafts and seaports; Ahmad Shah I built Ahmedabad and developed a regional style using Jain architectural traditions.
- ✅ Jaunpur became the **"Shiraz of the East"** because Sharqi rulers patronised learning, saints and architecture.
- ✅ Kashmir under Zainul Abidin cancelled anti-Hindu orders, abolished jizyah and cow slaughter, restored temples and welcomed back non-Muslims.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Bengal's autonomy rested partly on **distance, climate and waterways**.
- ✅ **Ghiyasuddin Azam Shah** is remembered for justice and diplomatic-literary contacts.
- ✅ **Ahmad Shah I** founded Ahmedabad; **Mahmud Begarha** captured Girnar and Champaner.
- ✅ **Mandu** architecture = massive, high plinths, coloured/glazed tiles.
- ✅ **Jaunpur** = Sharqi capital, "Shiraz of the East", lofty gates and huge arches.
- ✅ **Zainul Abidin** of Kashmir = abolition of jizyah/cow slaughter and restoration of temples.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not reduce regional kingdoms to "decline of Delhi"; many were centres of prosperity and cultural synthesis.

- ❌ Bengal remained subordinate to Delhi after Firuz Tughlaq's campaigns. → It exchanged gifts but was **not subordinate**.
- ❌ Gujarat architecture simply copied Delhi. → It drew on **Jain/Gujarati traditions** and developed a distinct style.
- ❌ Malwa was important only culturally. → Its **geography** made it central to north Indian power politics.
- ❌ Zainul Abidin continued Sikandar's persecution. → He **cancelled** those orders and adopted broad toleration.
