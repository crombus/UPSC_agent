## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — foreign policy was Indo-centric and defensive.**

✅ Satish Chandra argues that Mughal foreign policy was basically linked to India's security, not a romantic attempt to reconquer Central Asian homelands.
- ✅ The main Asian powers were the **Uzbeks** in Central Asia, the **Safavids** in Iran/Persia, and the **Ottoman Turks** in West Asia.
- ✅ The Uzbeks were natural rivals because they had pushed the Timurids out of Samarqand and threatened Kabul/Balkh zones.
- ✅ The Mughals generally preferred friendship with Persia because a strong Iran checked Uzbek pressure.
- ✅ The Mughals refused a narrow Sunni alliance with Uzbeks and Ottomans against Shia Persia because that would upset the Asian balance of power.
- ✅ The source's apparently repeated Balkh dates reflect two stages: Abdullah's seizure in 1572–73, a Timurid recovery under Shahrukh Mirza, and Uzbek reconquest in 1583.

**Foundation — Qandhar and Balkh.**

✅ **Qandhar** was important because it was a strong fort, a gateway between Kabul and Herat, and a hub of trade between India and Central Asia.
- ✅ Akbar acquired Qandhar in **1595** to establish a defensible northwest line after Uzbek pressure.
- ✅ Jahangir lost Qandhar to Shah Abbas in **1622**, partly due to diplomatic overconfidence and military unpreparedness.
- ✅ Shah Jahan recovered it in **1638**, lost it again in **1649**, and failed in repeated campaigns to recover it.
- ✅ The **Balkh campaign (1646–47)** aimed to keep the Uzbeks divided and secure Kabul, not permanently annex Central Asia.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Power / region | Mughal concern | Policy |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Uzbeks | Threat to Kabul, Badakhshan, Balkh and Afghan frontier | Avoid direct entanglement unless Kabul/India threatened |
| ✅ Safavids / Persia | Rival over Qandhar, but useful check on Uzbeks | Friendship as keynote, despite Qandhar setbacks |
| ✅ Ottomans | Claimed Islamic prestige and fought Persia/Portuguese | No close alliance due to equality concerns and pro-Iran balance |
| ✅ Qandhar | Fort + trade hub + outer bastion | Acquire/defend when strategically necessary |
| ✅ Kabul-Ghazni-Qandhar line | Defensive frontier | Main Mughal security line |
| ✅ Trade routes | India-Central Asia commerce | Diplomacy used to promote trade |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Three major Asian powers: **Uzbek, Safavid, Ottoman**.
- ✅ **Qandhar** was the key Mughal-Persian dispute.
- ✅ Akbar acquired Qandhar in **1595**; Jahangir lost it in **1622**.
- ✅ Shah Jahan recovered Qandhar in **1638** and lost it again in **1649**.
- ✅ Shah Jahan's **Balkh campaign** took place in **1646–47**.
- ✅ Mughal foreign policy promoted both security and trade.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not reduce Mughal foreign policy to nostalgia for Samarqand; Satish Chandra calls it primarily defensive and India-centred.

- ❌ Mughals always sought to conquer Central Asia. → The "homeland" slogan was often diplomatic, not a serious continuous programme.
- ❌ Qandhar mattered only for prestige. → It mattered for **defence and trade** too.
- ❌ Mughals joined Sunni Uzbeks against Shia Persia. → They resisted sectarian diplomacy to preserve balance.
- ❌ Balkh was a total military failure. → Militarily impressive but politically difficult to sustain.
