## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Humayun inherited an unfinished empire.**

- ✅ Babur left Humayun with weak finances, unconsolidated administration, unsubdued Afghans and the Timurid problem of brothers claiming shares.
- ✅ Kamran's occupation of Lahore and Multan weakened central unity, though Humayun accepted it to avoid civil war and focus east.
- ✅ Humayun initially treated the Afghan threat seriously: at **Dadrah (1532)** he defeated Afghans and then besieged **Chunar**, the gateway to eastern India.
- ⚠️ Part II notes that Nizamuddin Ahmad places Dadrah/Daurah in 1531 and does not mention the siege in the same sequence; the strategic interpretation is firmer than the exact dating.
- ⚠️ His problem was not laziness alone; the Mughal ruling class had not yet rooted itself in India.

**Foundation — Sher Khan outmanoeuvred Humayun.**

- ✅ Sher Khan strengthened Bihar during Humayun's Gujarat/Malwa campaign and later took Bengal's capital **Gaur**.
- ✅ Humayun's Bengal march allowed Sher Khan to cut communications with Agra and isolate the Mughals.
- ✅ At **Chausa (1539)** Humayun was surprised and barely escaped; at **Kanauj (1540)** the Afghan victory decided the issue.
- ✅ Humayun wandered in Sindh and neighbouring regions before taking Iranian help to recover Qandhar and Kabul.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Challenge | Details |
|---|---|
| ✅ Afghan challenge | Afghan chiefs in east U.P./Bihar could unite under a capable leader |
| ✅ Rajput-Gujarat front | Bahadur Shah of Gujarat challenged the Mughals in Malwa/Rajasthan/Gujarat |
| ✅ Brother problem | Kamran, Askari and Hindal complicated central command |
| ✅ Strategic fort | Chunar controlled land and river route between Agra and the east |
| ✅ Bengal trap | Humayun entered Bengal while Sher Khan cut communications |
| ✅ Decisive defeats | Chausa (1539) and Kanauj (1540) displaced the Mughals |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Humayun succeeded Babur in **December 1530**.
- ✅ **Chunar** was held by Sher Khan and was called a gateway of eastern India.
- ✅ Bahadur Shah of Gujarat was killed in a scuffle with the Portuguese after the Gujarat episode.
- ✅ **Chausa = 1539**, Sher Khan defeats Humayun.
- ✅ **Kanauj = 1540**, Sher Khan decisively defeats Humayun.
- ✅ Humayun recovered Delhi in **1555** but died in **1556**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Humayun's defeat was not only because of personal weakness; Sher Khan's Afghan base and strategy mattered.

- ❌ Humayun ignored Sher Khan from the beginning. → He besieged Chunar and moved east, but misread Sher Khan's strength.
- ❌ The Gujarat campaign achieved nothing. → It removed the major threat from Bahadur Shah, even though Gujarat/Malwa were lost.
- ❌ Chausa alone ended Mughal power. → Chausa weakened Humayun; **Kanauj** decided the struggle.
- ❌ Humayun never recovered. → He regained Delhi in 1555 after Sur disintegration.
