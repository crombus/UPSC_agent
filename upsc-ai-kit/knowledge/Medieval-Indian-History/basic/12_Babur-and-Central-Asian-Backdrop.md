## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — why Babur turned from Central Asia to India.**

- ✅ The Timurid world fragmented after Timur and Shahrukh; Timurid princes repeatedly fought each other, allowing the **Uzbeks** in Trans-Oxiana and the **Safavids** in Iran to rise.
- ✅ Babur twice won Samarqand but could not hold it; after Uzbek pressure, he shifted to **Kabul (1504)**.
- ✅ Babur saw India as attractive because Kabul's income was too small for his army and nobles, Punjab had earlier Timurid claims, and India could become a refuge/base against the Uzbeks.
- ⚠️ The Central Asian backdrop is therefore not decorative: it explains why a Timurid prince became an Indian empire-builder.

**Foundation — why Panipat did not instantly create a stable empire.**

- ✅ Babur defeated Ibrahim Lodi at **Panipat (20 April 1526)** using a defended front of carts, guns, and flanking attacks, but he still had to face **Rana Sanga** and the eastern **Afghans**.
- ✅ At **Khanwa (1527)**, Babur again used wagons, trenches, musketeers and flanking parties to defeat the Rajput-Afghan coalition led by Rana Sanga.
- ✅ Babur's decision to stay in India, not return to Kabul, changed the political equation and provoked resistance.
- ⚠️ Panipat opened a phase; Khanwa and Ghagra consolidated it.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Babur was a Timurid; he claimed prestige from **Timur** and **Chingiz** lineages.
- ✅ Babur conquered **Kabul in 1504**.
- ✅ **First Battle of Panipat = 1526**, Babur vs Ibrahim Lodi.
- ✅ Babur used Ottoman-style carts/field defence and Ottoman master-gunners **Ustad Ali** and **Mustafa**.
- ✅ **Khanwa = 1527**, Babur vs Rana Sanga; Sanga was supported by Afghans including Mahmud Lodi and Hasan Khan Mewati.
- ✅ Babur's advent linked Kabul-Qandhar with north India and improved India's access to trans-Asian trade.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Panipat was decisive militarily, but Babur still had to defeat Rana Sanga and manage the Afghans.

- ❌ Babur came only because Indian nobles invited him. → He already had strategic, financial and Timurid motives; invitations only made the moment favourable.
- ❌ Artillery alone won Panipat. → The source stresses the combined effect of defensive carts, guns, cavalry, bowmen and flanking tactics.
- ❌ Khanwa was only a Hindu-Muslim war. → It was a political struggle; Afghans also joined Rana Sanga.
- ❌ Babur planned a raid like Timur. → After Panipat he deliberately chose to stay in India.
