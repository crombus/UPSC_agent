## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — religious policy was orthodox, but politics still mattered.**

✅ Satish Chandra presents Aurangzeb as orthodox in outlook: he emphasised the **sharia**, reimposed **jizyah**, and destroyed or converted a number of temples. Motives and contexts varied—rebellion, imperial punishment, local complaint and religious policy—and Chandra also notes the contradiction between formal protection of old temples and actual destruction.
- ✅ Yet the source does **not** reduce every conflict to religion: Guru Tegh Bahadur's execution is framed by the Mughal side as a law-and-order issue, though Chandra calls the action unjustified and notes the wider religious repercussions.
- ✅ The Rajput breach is also treated as a political-administrative blunder, not proof of a fixed plan to subvert Hinduism; many Rajputs and Marathas continued to enter Mughal service.
- ⚠️ UPSC framing: avoid one-line communal explanations; connect **orthodoxy + imperial authority + succession disputes + fiscal/military pressure**.

**Foundation — Rajput rupture weakened a key Mughal alliance.**

✅ Akbar-Jahangir-Shah Jahan had treated leading Rajputs as high-ranking partners; Aurangzeb too initially valued the alliance, restored Jaswant Singh's mansab, favoured Jai Singh, and raised Mewar's mansab.
- ✅ After Jaswant Singh's death (1678), Marwar was taken into **khalisa** pending succession; Mughal officials behaved as conquerors, searched property, and demolished/bricked up many temples.
- ✅ The Rathor sardars under **Durgadas** rejected Aurangzeb's compromise, escaped with Ajit Singh, and crowned him at Jodhpur.
- ✅ Mewar joined because Rana Raj Singh opposed Mughal interference in Rajput succession and feared Mughal occupation of Marwar.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Jizyah was reimposed in 1679** by Aurangzeb.
- ✅ **Jaswant Singh of Marwar** died in 1678; succession dispute triggered the Marwar crisis.
- ✅ **Durgadas Rathor** helped Ajit Singh escape and became central to Rathor resistance.
- ✅ **Mewar** joined the war because of Mughal interference in Rajput succession and occupation of Marwar.
- ✅ **Guru Tegh Bahadur** was executed in 1675; **Khalsa** was founded in 1699 by Guru Gobind Singh.
- ✅ Chandra judges the Marwar-Mewar policy as **clumsy and blundering**, weakening the Rajput alliance.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Aurangzeb's policy was orthodox and often alienating, but Satish Chandra does **not** explain every conflict as a simple Hindu-Muslim conflict.

- ❌ Rajput war began only because of jizyah. → Correct: the immediate trigger was the **Marwar succession dispute** after Jaswant Singh's death.
- ❌ Marwar was permanently annexed for strategic reasons. → Correct: Chandra rejects this as an overstatement; succession handling was central.
- ❌ All Rajputs stopped serving the Mughals. → Correct: Hada, Kachhwaha and other contingents continued to serve.
- ❌ Sikh conflict was purely religious from the start. → Correct: Chandra stresses law-and-order, local politics and religious repercussions together.
