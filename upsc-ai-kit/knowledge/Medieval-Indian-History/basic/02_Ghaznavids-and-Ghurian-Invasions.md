## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — Mahmud: raids, Punjab base, no Gangetic empire.**

- ✅ The conventional textbook count assigns Mahmud seventeen Indian expeditions, but the counting of separate operations is not uniform; the secure point is his repeated raiding between 1000 and 1027.
- ✅ His early wars were against the Hindu Shahis of Peshawar/Punjab; Jayapala and Anandapala offered prolonged resistance.
- ✅ Mahmud's raids into Mathura, Kanauj and Somnath aimed at wealth, prestige and Central Asian campaigns, not permanent annexation of the Gangetic states.
- ✅ Yet Mahmud's conquest of Punjab and Multan changed the political situation: Turks had crossed the north-western mountain shield.

**Foundation — Muizzuddin/Ghuri: from raids to political conquest.**

- ✅ Muizzuddin Muhammad first moved through Multan and Uchch, failed in Gujarat in 1178, and then built a Punjab base by taking Peshawar, Lahore and Sialkot.
- ✅ Tarain I (1191) was a Ghurid defeat; Tarain II (1192) was a turning point because Delhi and eastern Rajasthan passed under Turkish rule.
- ✅ Chandawar (1194) broke Gahadavala power near Kanauj and opened the upper Ganga valley.
- ✅ Qutbuddin Aibak, as Muizzuddin's trusted slave, managed Indian affairs after Tarain and expanded into the doab.
- ✅ Conquest remained staged: Aibak occupied Delhi and Ajmer after resistance, while Bakhtiyar Khalji's campaigns in Bihar and Bengal extended Turkish power eastward without producing immediate uniform control.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Mahmud ruled Ghazni from 998 to 1030 and died at Ghazni.
- ✅ Somnath raid occurred in 1025; Mahmud returned via Sind and later punished Jats who harried his return.
- ✅ Mahmud did not annex the Gangetic states; his durable Indian gain was Punjab/Multan.
- ✅ Muizzuddin was defeated near Mount Abu in 1178, then shifted to a Punjab-first strategy.
- ✅ First Tarain (1191) = Prithviraj wins; Second Tarain (1192) = Prithviraj loses.
- ✅ Chandawar (1194) was fought against Jaichandra near Kanauj.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Mahmud and Muizzuddin are not interchangeable: one raided deeply but ruled mainly Punjab; the other created the political opening for Turkish rule in north India.

- ❌ Tarain I was the Turkish victory. → Tarain I was Prithviraj's victory; Tarain II was the Ghurid victory.
- ❌ Mahmud created the Delhi Sultanate. → He created the Punjab base; the Sultanate followed the Ghurid phase.
- ❌ Somnath was followed by direct Ghaznavid rule over Gujarat. → Mahmud returned to Ghazni; no lasting annexation followed.
- ❌ Jaichandra's absence at Tarain is proved only by the Sanyogita legend. → Satish Chandra treats the romance cautiously and points to older political rivalry.
