## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Foundation — revenue reform: from Sher Shah to Akbar.**

- ✅ Akbar adopted Sher Shah's measured revenue approach but found the central price schedule delayed assessment and hurt peasants when court prices were higher than countryside prices.
- ✅ In **1580**, under the **dahsala** system, average produce and average prices of the last ten years were calculated; one-third of average produce was the state share, converted into cash.
- ✅ Measurement with bamboos linked by iron rings made dues knowable to state and peasant; remission was allowed if crops failed due to drought/flood.
- ✅ This measurement-based assessment was called the **zabti** system and is associated with **Raja Todar Mal**, though it was the work of a team.

**Foundation — mansabdari: rank, salary and army.**

- ✅ Every officer received a **mansab** (rank). Lowest rank was 10; nobles could reach 5000, later 7000 for premier nobles.
- ✅ Later the rank split into **zat** (personal status/salary) and **sawar** (cavalry to be maintained).
- ✅ The **dagh** system branded horses, and **chehra** recorded soldiers' descriptive rolls.
- ✅ Mansabdars were usually paid through **jagirs**, which gave revenue rights but no hereditary ownership.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Institution | Function |

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Dahsala was introduced in **1580**.
- ✅ State share under dahsala = **one-third** of average produce, stated in cash.
- ✅ **Todar Mal's bandobast** is a common name, but Satish Chandra stresses team effort.
- ✅ Land categories: **polaj**, **parati**, **chachar**, **banjar**.
- ✅ Mansab ranks split later into **zat** and **sawar**.
- ✅ Akbar divided the empire into **twelve subas in 1580**.
- ✅ Main central officers: *diwan*, *mir bakhshi*, *mir saman* / household, and *sadr/qazi*.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Dahsala is not a permanent ten-year settlement; it used ten-year averages.

- ❌ Dahsala meant revenue was fixed permanently for ten years. → It was based on ten-year average produce/prices; the state could modify it.
- ❌ Todar Mal alone created the system. → He was crucial, but it was a team's work under Akbar.
- ❌ Jagir meant ownership of land. → It meant assignment of state revenue due from land.
- ❌ Zat and sawar existed in the same form from the start. → The system developed gradually.
