## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — Sher Shah was part of a historical process, not an isolated genius.**

- ✅ Part II cautions against uncritical adulation: Sher Shah's north Indian empire emerged from processes begun under Sikandar Lodi, Babur and Humayun.
- ✅ The old balance of power had already been broken by Babur's victories, Humayun's Malwa-Gujarat campaigns, and Sher Khan's Bengal/Bihar successes.
- ✅ Sher Shah's rise from a small noble shows a fluid political society where bold, sometimes unscrupulous, men could advance rapidly.
- ⚠️ His achievement lay in turning opportunity into a functioning empire.

**Deeper — Sur administration had both strength and limits.**

- ✅ Sher Shah emphasised safe roads, sarais, customs simplification, uniform coinage, measurement-based revenue and justice.
- ✅ His *zabt* measured sown land, classified land into good/middling/bad, computed average yield, and set state share generally at one-third.
- ✅ Local headmen and zamindars remained involved in assessment and collection; the medieval state did not create a modern direct ryotwari system.
- ✅ His personal over-centralisation was a weakness; after a masterful sovereign disappeared, the system strained.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Farid gained practical administrative training in his father's parganas before rising in Bihar.
- ✅ Sher Shah's Rohtas fort in the northwest was intended to control Gakhhars and check possible Mughal incursion; Todar Mal Khatri was entrusted with completing it.
- ✅ Sher Shah built/maintained roads including Indus-Sonargaon, Agra-Jodhpur-Chittor, Lahore-Multan and Agra-Burhanpur.
- ✅ Sarais were placed at intervals of two *karohs* (about four miles in Part II wording).
- ✅ The revenue system used annual measurement; Multan was treated as an exception in the source.
- ✅ Islam Shah tried to assert monarchy over Afghan nobles, contributing to resentment after his death.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Sher Shah's state was effective but not modern; it worked through existing zamindars, muqaddams and jagir/iqta structures.

- ❌ Sher Shah created provinces like Akbar. → Part II says provincial governments evolved under the Mughals; under Sher Shah, sarkars/shiqs remained key.
- ❌ He ended jagirs/iqtas. → Nobles and soldiers continued to be paid through assignments.
- ❌ Patta means direct modern peasant-state settlement. → Local headmen and zamindars remained central to assessment and collection.
- ❌ Puran Mal proves uniform religious persecution. → The source treats it as a political act given a religious gloss; temple destruction is not a general pattern.
