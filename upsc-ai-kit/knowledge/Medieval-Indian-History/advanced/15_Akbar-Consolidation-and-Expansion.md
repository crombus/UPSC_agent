## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — consolidation meant defeating Afghan and noble alternatives.**

- ✅ Part II opens with the warning that the Afghan danger after Humayun's death was still severe: Hemu, Adil Shah's general, captured Agra and Delhi and marched with cavalry, elephants and artillery.
- ✅ Hemu was not simply a symbol of Hindu resurgence; Part II stresses his army was largely Afghan, and his rise reflected a relatively open Afghan political society.
- ✅ Bairam Khan's regency stabilised the empire, but his dominance also provoked noble jealousy and Akbar's desire to rule directly.
- ✅ The Uzbek rebellions represented noble resistance to Akbar's centralising authority; their defeat in 1567 opened the road to systematic expansion.

**Deeper — Rajput policy evolved in phases.**

- ✅ Part II divides Akbar's Rajput policy into phases: early loyal allies; then Rajputs as the sword-arm of the empire during Gujarat; finally partners in the kingdom after 1578.
- ✅ The alliance was not only matrimonial; Surjan Hada of Ranthambhor, for instance, served without such a marriage link.
- ✅ Rajput service gave the Mughals military strength and gave rajas prestigious offices, outside jagirs and secure *watan jagirs*.
- ✅ The Mewar conflict turned on personal submission, Chittor and local independence; Haldighati did not break the stalemate.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Bairam Khan's regency lasted about four years, **1556-1560**.
- ✅ Hemu had won many battles for Adil Shah before Panipat II, but his artillery was captured before the decisive battle.
- ✅ Akbar executed Adham Khan after the murder of Atka Khan.
- ✅ Baz Bahadur was later enrolled as a Mughal mansabdar and rose to 2000 in the basic source.
- ✅ Rana Pratap's forces at Haldighati included Afghans under Hakim Khan Sur and Bhils.
- ✅ Man Singh led the Mughal force at Haldighati.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Akbar's Rajput policy was a graded political alliance, not a simple story of forced marriages.

- ❌ Hemu's rise proves a purely Hindu anti-Mughal project. → Part II says his force was mainly Afghan and his rise reflected Afghan openness.
- ❌ Bairam Khan fell because he was Shia. → Part II gives factional, personal and authority-based reasons; sect alone is inadequate.
- ❌ Akbar's conquest of Rajasthan was only for territory. → Security of the Ganga basin and routes to Gujarat/Malwa mattered.
- ❌ Haldighati was a decisive Mughal conquest of Mewar. → It failed to end the stalemate; Rana Pratap continued resistance.
