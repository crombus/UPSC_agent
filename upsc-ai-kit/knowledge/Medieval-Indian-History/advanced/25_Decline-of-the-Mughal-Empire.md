## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — the historiography of decline.**

✅ Satish Chandra's own contribution is the **"jagirdari crisis"** thesis: as the empire stopped expanding, the pool of revenue-yielding **jagirs (paibaqi)** could not keep pace with the **growing number of mansabdars**, producing a chronic **be-jagiri** (jagirlessness), fierce factionalism and predatory revenue extraction that alienated the **zamindars and peasantry**.
- ✅ He weighs this against the older **"Aurangzeb's responsibility"** view (Deccan wars, religious policy) and the **agrarian-crisis** thesis (Irfan Habib) — presenting decline as a **convergence** of administrative, fiscal, agrarian and political strains rather than one cause.
- ✅ The **Deccan campaigns (1687–1707)** are pivotal: prolonged war swelled the nobility with defeated **Deccani/Maratha** entrants (widening the jagir shortage) while the emperor's absence from the north weakened administration there.

**Deeper — the eighteenth-century transition.**

- ⚠️ **Zat–sawar inflation & jagir transfers:** frequent reshuffling of jagirs discouraged nobles from investing in the land's productivity, worsening revenue realisation.
- ⚠️ **The Sayyid Brothers (Abdullah Khan & Husain Ali)** institutionalised noble supremacy — deposing emperors and, notably, **abolishing jizyah again** and partnering with the **Marathas**, who won the right to collect **chauth/sardeshmukhi** in the Deccan (1719).
- ⚠️ **Successor states** were not "anarchy" but a **regional reorganisation** of power — Bengal, Awadh and Hyderabad built stable revenue regimes; historians (Bayly, Alam) stress **regional buoyancy** amid imperial contraction.
- ⚠️ **Nadir Shah (1739)** and **Abdali** invasions were symptoms, not causes — they exposed a centre already hollowed by the jagirdari and financial crises.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Jagirdari crisis** = Satish Chandra's central explanatory frame; **be-jagiri** = jagirlessness.
- ✅ Decline is **multi-causal** (jagirdari + Deccan wars + agrarian strain), debated among historians.
- ⚠️ **Sayyid Brothers** deposed Farrukhsiyar (**1719**); re-abolished **jizyah**; allied with Marathas.
- ⚠️ Marathas secured **chauth/sardeshmukhi** rights (1719) — fiscal penetration of Mughal territory.
- ⚠️ **Nadir Shah 1739**; **Panipat III 1761** (Marathas vs Abdali).

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Know the **named theses and their authors** — jagirdari crisis (Satish Chandra) vs agrarian crisis (Irfan Habib) vs regional buoyancy (Bayly/Alam).

- ❌ Satish Chandra blames decline solely on Aurangzeb's religious policy. → His distinctive thesis is the **jagirdari crisis** (structural/fiscal).
- ❌ The 18th century was pure "chaos and darkness." → Recent scholarship stresses **regional economic and political vitality**.
- ❌ Chauth was a religious tax. → It was a **Maratha fiscal levy** (≈¼ of revenue), with **sardeshmukhi** an additional 10%.
- ❌ The Sayyid Brothers were provincial rebels. → They were **central king-makers** who controlled the throne itself.

## 6. 📰 Current link
