## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — the state, the nobility and the sources.**

✅ Our knowledge of Alauddin's reforms rests heavily on **Ziauddin Barani**, whose testimony is rich but **normative and opinionated** — Satish Chandra repeatedly cautions that "there is no means of checking the veracity of Barani's account." Barani (like Balban before) was preoccupied with keeping the "**low and ignoble**" out of high office; his lens colours what he reports about state policy.
- ✅ Balban's precedent mattered: he built an **exclusive, high-born Turkish nobility**, dealt ruthlessly with rivals, and used **barids (confidential spies)** — a template of centralised control Alauddin extended and hardened.
- ✅ Alauddin curbed the nobility more radically — banning **social wine-drinking, festive gatherings and marriage alliances** among nobles without permission, and using an intelligence network (**munhiyan/barids**) so that "nothing… remained hidden from the Sultan."

**Deeper — agrarian reform as the base of price control.**

✅ Alauddin's **market control could not work without agrarian reform**. In the **doab**, he abolished intermediary claims and had the state deal directly with cultivators: revenue was assessed by **measurement (masahat)** at **half (50%) of the produce**, plus grazing and house taxes, and the **privileges of the khuts, muqaddams and chaudhris** (rural intermediaries) were sharply curtailed so they could **no longer pass their burden onto the weak**.
- ✅ Part I records internally different regulations: assessment was calculated in kind and generally demanded in cash, while tracts near Delhi supplied part of the demand in kind to royal stores. Chandra therefore concludes that *kharaj* was taken in both cash and kind in practice.
- ✅ A specialised bureaucracy — **Diwan-i-Riyasat** (market ministry) under a **shahna-i-mandi**, with registers, spies and price-lists — enforced the schedule; **banjaras** were organised and made collectively responsible for haulage.

## 2. Key classification / data

| Instrument | Mechanism (Part I) | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Measurement (masahat) | Revenue = **½ of produce** in the doab | Maximise & standardise state grain/revenue |
| ✅ Curb on intermediaries | Khuts/muqaddams stripped of perquisites | Prevent burden-shifting; direct state–peasant link |
| ✅ Diwan-i-Riyasat / shahna | Market ministry, registers, price schedule | Enforce fixed prices at Delhi |
| ✅ Intelligence (munhiyan/barids) | Spies report violations to Sultan | Deter hoarding, short-weighing, black-market |
| ✅ Banjaras organised | Collective responsibility for grain haulage | Guarantee steady cheap supply to capital |
| ⚠️ Market segmentation | Grain mandi · cloth (Sarai-i-Adl) · horse/cattle/slave | Category-specific control |

> **Source caution:** Barani's regulations do not always describe a single perfectly implemented system; use them to reconstruct policy while retaining Chandra's warning about contradiction, exaggeration and uneven enforcement.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Chief source = **Barani**; use with caution (normative bias).
- ✅ Agrarian base: **measurement**, revenue **½ of produce**, intermediaries (khut/muqaddam/chaudhri) curbed.
- ✅ Market ministry = **Diwan-i-Riyasat**; enforced by **shahna** + **spies (munhiyan/barids)**.
- ✅ **Banjaras** organised for cheap grain haulage to Delhi.
- ✅ Motive chain: **Mongol threat → large standing army → low salaries → low fixed prices → agrarian + market control**.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Distinguish the **agrarian** measures (doab, measurement, ½ produce, curbing intermediaries) from the **urban market** control — both interlock but are examined separately.

- ❌ The reforms rest on neutral records. → They rest largely on **Barani**, whose account is **opinionated** and must be cross-checked.
- ❌ Alauddin abolished the intermediaries entirely. → He **curtailed their privileges** and burden-shifting, not the whole rural elite.
- ❌ Market control survived him as a lasting institution. → It **collapsed soon after his death (1316)** — it depended on his coercive apparatus.
- ❌ "Sarai-i-Adl" was a court of justice. → Here it is the **cloth market** in the price-control system.
