## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — anatomy of the mansabdari system.**

✅ The **dual rank (zat + sawar)** introduced by Akbar in his **40th regnal year (1595–96)** matured under Shah Jahan. **Zat** fixed personal status and pay; **sawar** fixed the number of **cavalry** to be maintained. Abul Fazl grouped mansabdars into **three categories** by how fully they met their sawar quota (full / half-or-more / less-than-half).
- ✅ Enforcement rested on the **dagh (branding of horses)** and **chehra (descriptive muster roll)** — yet Badayuni records chronic evasion: nobles brought **borrowed men and horses** to the muster, then kept the jagirs.
- ✅ The **do-aspa sih-aspa** device (troopers with two/three horses) let the state raise a noble's **military obligation without inflating his zat** — a flexible response to shortage.

**Deeper — the month-scale and the roots of crisis.**

✅ The central fiscal problem: between **1595 and 1656–57 the jama (assessed revenue) rose only modestly** (index 100 → ~176), while the **number and rank of mansabdars grew far faster**. To reconcile paper salaries with actual yields the state applied the **month-scale (monthly ratio)** to both zat and sawar pay.
- ✅ A jagir nominally worth 12 months' revenue might in practice yield only **8, 6, 5 or 4 months'** income; salary was scaled down accordingly. A sawar on a 12-month jagir was paid **Rs 40 p.m.**, but on an 8-month jagir **Rs 30**, on a 6-month **Rs 25** — the reduction tied to fewer remounts.
- ✅ In the **Deccan**, where the jama was **highly inflated**, mansabdars often got jagirs worth only **3–4 months** — a structural grievance feeding disaffection.
- ⚠️ Satish Chandra reads this as the **incubation of the jagirdari crisis**: the machinery still worked under Shah Jahan but the **be-jagiri** pressure was already building.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ **Zat + sawar** dual rank introduced by Akbar **1595–96**; matured under Shah Jahan.
- ✅ Fraud controls = **dagh (branding)** + **chehra (muster roll)**.
- ✅ **Do-aspa sih-aspa** = extra horses to raise military duty without raising zat.
- ✅ **Month-scale** ties pay to a jagir's actual yield; Deccan jagirs ~**3–4 months**.
- ✅ **Jama grew far slower than mansab numbers** (index to ~176 by 1656–57) — the fiscal squeeze.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Know the numbers and the **direction** of the squeeze — mansab claims outran jama, so pay was **reduced** via the month-scale.

- ❌ Do-aspa sih-aspa raised a noble's zat rank. → It raised the **sawar/military obligation only**, not zat.
- ❌ The month-scale was a bonus system. → It was a **salary-reduction** mechanism reflecting jagir shortfalls.
- ❌ Dagh and chehra eliminated fraud. → Evasion persisted (Badayuni's testimony) — borrowed men/horses at muster.
- ❌ Deccan mansabdars were the best paid. → Deccan jagirs were often worth only **3–4 months** — a grievance, not a privilege.
