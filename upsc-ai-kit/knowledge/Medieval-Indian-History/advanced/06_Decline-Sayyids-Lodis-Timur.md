## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — decline as regionalisation and balance of power.**

✅ Satish Chandra presents the fifteenth century not as simple chaos but as a **system of regional kingdoms** and balance of power after Delhi's collapse. Bengal, Gujarat, Malwa, Jaunpur, Mewar, Kashmir and the Deccan polities all filled the vacuum.
- ✅ Timur's 1398 invasion hastened the fall of the Tughlaqs; before it, two rival kings had already appeared in Delhi/Firuzabad and provincial kingdoms had broken away.
- ✅ Timur assigned Delhi, Multan and Dipalpur to Khizr Khan, but Khizr first retained Punjab/Multan and later entered Delhi after Tughlaq weakness.
- ✅ The Sayyids did not become secure rulers: Khokhars in Punjab, Mewatis and the Sharqis of Jaunpur constantly pressured Delhi.
- ✅ Jaunpur under the Sharqis became a major cultural centre, the "Shiraz of the East," and maintained order in eastern U.P. after Delhi's collapse.

**Deeper — Afghan Lodi consolidation and its limits.**

✅ The Lodi rise marked an Afghan phase: Bahlul Lodi used Afghan mobilisation from Roh and Punjab to overcome the Sharqis and revive Delhi's power over the upper Ganga valley.
- ✅ Afghan nobles had a strong sense of tribal equality; Sikandar Lodi therefore tried to make them stand before him, honour farmans and submit jagir accounts.
- ✅ Sikandar's administration combined discipline, justice, cheap prices, safe highways, grain-octroi abolition, agricultural interest and the **gazz-i-Sikandari** measurement.
- ✅ Yet Afghan ideas of partition and noble autonomy persisted; Bahlul had divided the kingdom among sons/relations, and Sikandar could only partially undo this.
- ✅ Lodi expansion towards eastern Rajasthan and Malwa brought them into conflict with Rana Sanga; Babur's arrival intervened before this struggle was resolved.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Timur gave Delhi/Multan/Dipalpur to Khizr Khan, but Khizr's secure base was Multan-Punjab before Delhi.
- ✅ Sayyids were pressured by Khokhars, Mewatis and Sharqis.
- ✅ Malik Sarwar's title **Malik-us-Sharq** gave the Sharqis their name.
- ✅ Jaunpur was called **"Shiraz of the East"** and was known for its own architectural style with lofty gates and huge arches.
- ✅ Bahlul Lodi invited Afghans of **Roh** to India to strengthen his position.
- ✅ Sikandar Lodi made nobles stand before him, honour farmans and submit jagir accounts.
- ✅ Agra was founded/selected by Sikandar Lodi in **1506** for eastern Rajasthan, Malwa/Gujarat route and doab control.
- ✅ Ibrahim Lodi's conflict zone included Mewar/Rana Sanga before Babur arrived.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: The fifteenth century is not a blank "decline" between Sultanate and Mughals; it is a regional-state century.

- ❌ Sayyids were Timurid governors in a simple sense. → They included Timurid names in khutba for some time but were not direct subordinates.
- ❌ Jaunpur was politically insignificant. → It was a major Sharqi state and cultural centre.
- ❌ Lodi centralisation was smooth. → Afghan noble equality and partition ideas limited monarchical authority.
- ❌ Babur entered a political vacuum. → He entered a competitive field shaped by Lodis, Mewar, Malwa and other regional powers.

## 7. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Assess the fifteenth century as a **balance-of-power system** rather than merely the "dark age" of Delhi's decline.
- ⚠️ The Lodi state revived Delhi but could not overcome Afghan noble autonomy fully.
- ⚠️ Timur's raid, Sayyid weakness and Lodi revival together show how external shock, regional assertion and elite politics shaped the end of the Sultanate.

## ➕ Concept: Afghan kingship vs Turkish kingship

⚠️ Earlier Turkish rulers stressed awe and hierarchy; Lodi Afghan politics contained a stronger "first among equals" idea, making Sikandar's court discipline especially significant for UPSC comparison.
