## 1. Snapshot & core idea

**Deeper — Akbar's state used checks, balances and expertise.**

- ✅ Part II argues that Akbar inherited Sultanate structures but reorganised departments so their functions did not encroach on each other and yet balanced one another.
- ✅ The all-powerful *vakil* was reduced: Bairam Khan's dominance convinced Akbar not to allow a second over-mighty minister.
- ✅ The *diwan* became a specialist in income/expenditure and revenue; the *mir bakhshi* handled mansabs, military presentation, intelligence and high appointments.
- ✅ Provincial government repeated the centre's checks: subah governor, diwan, bakhshi, sadr-qazi and news-writer were appointed from the centre.

**Deeper — dahsala and mansabdari were evolving systems.**

- ✅ The dahsala was implemented by a team including Muzaffar Khan, Todar Mal and Shah Mansur; popular memory over-associates it with Todar Mal alone.
- ✅ Mansabdari was unique because it made a graded single service combining civil and military responsibilities; rank fixed status, salary and retainers.
- ✅ The dagh system was introduced to reduce the gap between paper cavalry and actual mustered troops, but it caused noble resistance and administrative harassment.
- ✅ The dual **zat-sawar** rank was introduced late to distinguish personal status/pay from actual cavalry obligation.
- ✅ Chandra, citing later research, cautions that *zabt* probably did not cover the whole land of any province; crop-sharing, appraisement and estimate remained part of the working system.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Muzaffar Khan Turbati, Todar Mal and Shah Mansur were central to revenue reform.
- ✅ Mansabs formed a hierarchy from **10 to 5000**; later Man Singh and Aziz Koka reached **7000** under Akbar.
- ✅ The **dagh** system was introduced in the **18th regnal year (1573-74)**.
- ✅ **Zat-sawar** dual rank emerged in the **40th regnal year (1595-96)**.
- ✅ A 5000 zat mansabdar was required to maintain specified horses, elephants, camels, mules and carts in Part II's description.
- ✅ The jharoka darshan was Akbar's innovation for a daily personal bond with subjects.
- ✅ District units: **sarkar** and **pargana**; officers included faujdar, amalguzar/amil, qanungo and clerks.

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Akbar's administration was not a static blueprint; it evolved through experiments, resistance and corrections.

- ❌ Vakil remained the all-powerful wazir. → Akbar stripped the office of real power after Bairam.
- ❌ Mansabdars always maintained the full number of troops on paper. → Dagh was introduced because paper numbers and real musters diverged.
- ❌ Jagirs should have been replaced by cash salaries. → Part II stresses jagirs matched social realities and revenue collection difficulties.
- ❌ Akbar's state was modern egalitarian. → Abul Fazl's ideology was liberal for its age but hierarchical.

## ➕ Concept note (PYQ-gap)

⚠️ **Dahsala ≠ zabt ≠ batai.** Dahsala is the ten-year average method within revenue assessment; zabt is measurement-based assessment; batai is crop-sharing. UPSC often tests the distinction indirectly.
