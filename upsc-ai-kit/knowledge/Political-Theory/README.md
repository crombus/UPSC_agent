## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the must-do concept map, definitions, thinker
  positions and a revision-ready answer spine; `advanced/NN` adds direct-book argument
  reconstruction, thinker-by-thinker depth, internal debates, comparative matrices and an
  UPSC answer-engine. **Same number = same topic** in both tiers.
- **Tagging discipline:** ✅ = a claim or attribution taken directly from Gauba's book (or another
  named canonical/official source) and accurately cited by PDF page; ⚠️ = an analytical
  comparison, inference, contested interpretation or pedagogical synthesis built to make the
  concept exam-usable; 📰 = a genuinely dated, independently verifiable post-2009 anchor (used
  sparingly, always dated); ❌ = a common misconception, exam trap or misattribution, followed by
  the correction.
- **Single-edition, dated-source discipline:** the entire module is grounded in the **Fifth
  Edition (2009)** of Gauba's book. Every institutional description, "contemporary" example,
  Cold War/Third World reference and numerical claim in the source text is **book-period or
  historical** unless separately verified against an official, dated source. No file silently
  updates the book's examples into present-day claims — see `Political-Theory/00_Master-Framework.md` §6 and the
  per-file "Factual-risk checks" / "Sources, metadata and tags" sections.
- **Quotation discipline:** famous lines (Rousseau's "forced to be free," Marx on religion as
  "opium," Proudhon's "property is theft," Rawls's two principles, Gandhi on rights and duties)
  are **paraphrased and attributed** rather than quoted verbatim, unless the exact wording is
  independently verifiable. This avoids reproducing long copyrighted passages and avoids
  misquotation risk.
- **No duplication of constitutional Polity:** Articles, institutions, election law, judicial
  holdings and procedural detail belong to `Polity/`. This module teaches the underlying
  *concepts* (sovereignty, rights, representation, constitutionalism, rule of law) that Polity
  then anchors in Indian constitutional text — see `Polity/basic/` and `Polity/advanced/` for the
  applied Indian material, linked from each relevant file here.
- **No duplication of Governance, Ethics or IR:** `Governance/` owns administration, delivery and
  accountability machinery; `Ethics/` owns applied moral dilemmas and public-service values (this
  module supplies the normative vocabulary — liberty, equality, justice, common good — that
  Ethics applies); `International-Relations/` owns current global institutions, foreign policy
  and the present international order (this module only analyses the *conceptual* effect of
  globalisation/internationalism on sovereignty and the state, as Gauba frames it in 2009).
- **No duplication of Philosophy Optional:** `Philosophy/paper-2/socio-political/` already holds
  syllabus-specific, PYQ-targeted thinker depth in files such as
  `Philosophy/paper-2/socio-political/Social-Political-Ideals.md`,
  `Philosophy/paper-2/socio-political/Political-Ideologies.md`,
  `Philosophy/paper-2/socio-political/Sovereignty.md` and
  `Philosophy/paper-2/socio-political/Individual-and-State.md`. This module is the
  broader GS-II/PSIR-foundation conceptual scaffold; where relevant it **links** to those files as
  a separate application source for Philosophy-optional depth, and does not treat them as direct
  Political Theory paper evidence or reproduce their content.

## Source and edition policy

- ✅ Primary anchor: O. P. Gauba, *An Introduction to Political Theory*, Fifth Edition (2009),
  read directly and completely from the supplied page-preserving extraction
  `An Introduction to Political Theory by O P Gauba.txt` (cited by `PDF p.N` using the file's own
  `===== PDF PAGE n =====` anchors).
- ⚠️ Where the book's own examples are used (Cold War blocs, USSR/China illustrations, "Third
  World" framing, 1980s-90s numerical claims), they are explicitly labelled as **book-period
  illustrations**, never as present-day facts.
- 📰 Any post-2009 anchor used anywhere in this module is independently dated and sourced from an
  official body — it is never inferred from the book itself, since the book cannot describe
  events after its own edition date.
- ❌ This module does not use any automated retrieval pipeline or indexed-search tooling. All
  content is authored directly by reading the extracted book text and the research blueprint.

## Usage path

1. Read `Political-Theory/00_Master-Framework.md` first — it gives the five master questions (unit of analysis,
   legitimacy, freedom, justice, state-role) and the comparison matrices used across every file.
2. Work topic-by-topic in numeric order: `basic/NN` for the concept map and revision spine, then
   `advanced/NN` for thinker depth, debates and the answer-engine. Same-number files are
   companions and cross-link to each other explicitly.
3. Use the "Cross-links and boundaries" / "Cross-links and exclusions" sections in every file to
   move outward to `Polity`, `Governance`, `Ethics`, `International-Relations` or
   `Philosophy/paper-2/socio-political` only when the applied/institutional/syllabus-specific
   layer is needed — this module itself stays at the conceptual layer.

## Tag policy

- ✅ **Direct-book/canonical** — a claim, definition or attribution accurately drawn from Gauba's
  text (cited by page) or another named canonical/official source (e.g., the UDHR text itself).
- ⚠️ **Synthesis/inference** — a comparison table, cross-ideology matrix, pedagogical distinction
  (e.g., "rights generations") or contested interpretation built for exam use; always flagged as
  such rather than presented as the book's own terminology.
- 📰 **Dated current anchor** — a genuinely post-2009 fact, always carrying its own verification
  date; used only where independently confirmed, never inferred from the 2009 text.
- ❌ **Trap/misattribution** — a common misconception or boundary error, immediately followed by
  the correction.

## Risk protocol

1. **No fabricated PYQs.** No question in this module is invented as an actual UPSC paper
   question. Where existing verified PYQ files exist elsewhere (e.g., in `Philosophy/_application`
   or `Governance`), they are linked as a **separate application source**, never presented as
   direct Political Theory paper evidence. "Probable questions" sections here are explicitly
   labelled ⚠️ practice prompts, not attributed PYQs.
2. **No silent modernisation.** The book's examples, institutional descriptions and "contemporary"
   claims are Fifth-Edition-2009-era. They are never updated, softened or re-dated without an
   independent, cited, official source.
3. **No thinker caricature.** Hobbes, Rousseau, Marx, Gandhi, liberalism, feminism,
   communitarianism, Rawls and Nozick are each presented with their internal qualifications and
   diversity of interpretation, not reduced to a single slogan.
4. **No quotation risk.** Famous lines are paraphrased and attributed rather than placed in
   quotation marks unless independently verified against the original text.
5. **No boundary creep.** Constitutional Articles, case law, institutional mandates and current
   international-order detail are not authored here; they are cross-linked to `Polity`,
   `Governance` or `International-Relations` using full root-relative paths.
6. **No automated retrieval tooling.** This module is authored directly from the extracted book
   text and the accompanying research blueprint, without any indexed-search or retrieval pipeline.
