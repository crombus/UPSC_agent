## 1. One-screen conceptual map

```text
capitalist exploitation
        ->
scientific socialism
        ->
dialectical materialism + historical materialism
        ->
mode of production = forces of production + relations of production
        ->
base <-> superstructure
        ->
class conflict
        ->
surplus value as economic exploitation
        ->
socialist transition -> classless society
        ->
neo-Marxist extension: ideology, culture, alienation, technological domination
```

- ✅ Gauba presents classical Marxism through four main tenets: dialectical materialism, historical materialism, class conflict and surplus value. (PDF p.48, p.52)
- ⚠️ For UPSC purposes, the topic moves from economy-centred critique to a wider analysis of ideology, culture and domination.

## 2. Why this topic matters

- ✅ Marxism explains why formal legal equality can coexist with deep economic inequality under capitalism. (PDF pp.46-47)
- ✅ Neo-Marxism shows why domination cannot be reduced only to factory ownership; culture, communication and ideology also matter. (PDF pp.53-54)
- ⚠️ This file gives the conceptual base for later debates on hegemony, justice, the state and revolutionary social change.

## 3. Essential definitions

- ✅ **Marxism:** a set of political and economic principles founded by Marx and Engels to give socialism a scientific basis through historical analysis of conflict between antagonistic classes. (PDF p.47)
- ✅ **Classical Marxism:** the original scientific system centred on property, class division, contradiction and revolutionary transformation. (PDF p.47)
- ✅ **Neo-Marxism:** later Marxist thought that studies subtler forms of dominance, alienation and paths to human emancipation. (PDF p.47, p.53)
- ✅ **Dialectical materialism:** the view that matter, not idea, is fundamental, and that contradictions in material conditions drive development. (PDF p.48)
- ✅ **Historical materialism:** the claim that economic relations of production, distribution and exchange play the decisive role in shaping wider social and political life. (PDF p.48)
- ✅ **Surplus value:** the unpaid part of labour appropriated by the capitalist as profit, rent and interest. (PDF pp.52-53)

## 4. Core argument / chapter spine

- ✅ Gauba begins with capitalism's oppressive conditions and presents Marxism as a response to the failure of laissez-faire liberalism to deliver real human freedom. (PDF pp.46-47)
- ✅ Marxism argues that private property in the means of production divides society into dominant and dependent classes with antagonistic interests. (PDF p.47)
- ✅ The mode of production shapes the social structure; when productive forces clash with existing relations of production, revolution replaces one social formation with another. (PDF pp.48-50)
- ✅ Class struggle continues from slave society to feudalism to capitalism, and Marxism expects a socialist revolution to end this cycle in a classless society. (PDF pp.49-52)
- ✅ Neo-Marxism reopens Marxism by focusing on alienation, ideology, technological domination and the relative autonomy of superstructural factors. (PDF pp.53-54)

## 5. Essential distinctions

- ✅ **Dialectical materialism vs historical materialism:** the first is the philosophical basis; the second is the scientific-historical application to society. (PDF p.48)
- ✅ **Base vs superstructure:** base refers to the mode of production; superstructure includes law, politics, religion, morals, art and culture. (PDF p.48)
- ✅ **Forces of production vs relations of production:** forces include means of production plus labour power; relations concern ownership patterns and class relations. (PDF pp.48-49)
- ✅ **Classical Marxism vs neo-Marxism:** classical Marxism emphasizes property, class contradiction and revolution; neo-Marxism widens the lens to ideology, culture and new forms of domination. (PDF pp.47, 53-54)
- ❌ **Base determines superstructure mechanically:** Gauba explicitly notes that later Marxist debate recognizes interdependence between base and superstructure. (PDF p.53)

## 6. Thinkers and positions

- ✅ **Marx and Engels:** founders of Marxism and scientific socialism; they treat history as struggle rooted in the mode of production. (PDF p.47)
- ✅ **Lenin, Rosa Luxemburg and Mao Zedong:** Gauba lists them among chief exponents of classical Marxism. (PDF p.47)
- ✅ **Young Marx:** Gauba highlights the early humanist Marx on alienation, freedom and capitalism's dehumanizing effects. (PDF p.47)
- ✅ **Frankfurt School:** Adorno, Horkheimer, Marcuse and Habermas extend Marxism through critical theory and ideology critique. (PDF p.53)
- ✅ **Herbert Marcuse:** argues that advanced bourgeois society reduces human beings to one-dimensionality by anaesthetizing discontent through manipulated desires. (PDF p.54)
- ✅ **Louis Althusser:** represents the scientific strain of neo-Marxism focused on structures and modes of production. (PDF p.54)

## 7. Illustrative examples

- ✅ Gauba's stage-chart example shows how different modes of production generate different social formations: slave-owning, feudal, capitalist, socialist and communist. (PDF pp.49-50)
- ✅ The wage relation illustrates surplus value: the worker produces more value than is returned as subsistence wages, and the unpaid residue becomes capitalist profit. (PDF pp.52-53)
- ✅ Neo-Marxist cultural critique treats communication and consumer desire as sites of domination, not merely reflections of factory ownership. (PDF pp.53-54)
- ⚠️ For answer-writing, a safe illustration is to compare direct workplace exploitation with indirect ideological consent produced through culture and media.

## 9. UPSC traps and corrections

- ❌ **Marx simply said thesis-antithesis-synthesis.** -> ⚠️ That triadic language is a teaching shorthand drawn from Gauba's discussion of Hegel's dialectic; do not present it as Marx's own settled formula. (PDF p.48)
- ❌ **Historical materialism means economics alone explains everything.** -> ✅ Gauba's neo-Marxist section explicitly notes growing recognition of interdependence between base and superstructure. (PDF p.53)
- ❌ **Neo-Marxism abandons Marxism.** -> ✅ Gauba presents it as a development within Marxism, especially around alienation, ideology and emancipation. (PDF pp.47, 53-54)
- ❌ **Surplus value means any profit whatsoever is automatically identical with market price movement.** -> ✅ Gauba defines it as the unpaid part of labour appropriated by the capitalist, not simply any change in sale price. (PDF pp.52-53)
