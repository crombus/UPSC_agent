# Indian Economy - Master Conceptual Framework

> **Purpose:** Connect every Economy topic before studying the detailed files.
> **Core question:** How does India convert resources, labour, capital and state
> capacity into stable, inclusive and sustainable development?

## 2. Six pillars

| Pillar | Central question | Main policy tools |
|---|---|---|
| Output and development | What is produced, and does it improve welfare? | GDP/GVA, productivity, human capital |
| Monetary-financial system | Is money, credit and risk managed safely? | RBI rates, liquidity, regulation |
| Fiscal state | How does government raise and spend resources? | Tax, expenditure, borrowing, transfers |
| Productive sectors | Can agriculture, industry and services create value and jobs? | Markets, infrastructure, technology |
| External sector | Can India trade and finance growth without external instability? | Trade policy, exchange rate, reserves |
| Inclusion and sustainability | Who benefits, and can growth endure? | Social policy, labour, climate finance |

## 3. Core macro identities

| Identity | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `GDP = C + I + G + (X - M)` | Expenditure on domestic final output |
| `NDP = GDP - depreciation` | Net domestic production |
| `GNP = GDP + NFIA` | Income of normal residents |
| `GDP at market prices = GVA at basic prices + product taxes - product subsidies` | Producer value added to purchaser-price output |
| `Fiscal deficit = total expenditure - non-borrowing receipts` | Government borrowing requirement |
| `Current account = trade in goods/services + primary income + transfers` | External flow balance |

## 4. Measurement and vintage discipline

```text
Question asks for a number
        |
        v
What is the concept? -> stock / flow / share / growth / estimate
        |
        v
What is the coverage? -> Centre / states / general government; GDP / GVA
        |
        v
What is the vintage? -> BE / RE / PA / final; FAE / SAE / revised; series base
        |
        v
Quote the period, source and caveat before drawing the inference
```

- **Never splice series casually:** a new base year can change weights, sources,
  coverage and methods. Compare only through an official back-series or linking
  methodology.
- **Never turn a plan into an outcome:** an announcement, allocation, approval,
  sanction, Budget Estimate and completed expenditure are different stages.
- **Never turn a projection into an actual:** retain the issuing institution,
  forecast period and stated assumptions.

## 5. Policy transmission map

```text
RBI repo/liquidity
      |
      v
bank funding -> lending rates -> credit/demand/investment -> output and inflation

Government tax/spending/borrowing
      |
      +-> demand and welfare
      +-> public investment and productivity
      +-> debt, interest burden and possible crowding out

Global prices/trade/capital flows
      |
      +-> exchange rate -> imports/exports -> inflation/current account/growth
```

## 6. Recurring UPSC analytical tensions

| Tension | Balanced framing |
|---|---|
| Growth vs inflation | Stabilise prices without destroying productive demand |
| Fiscal support vs debt | Judge deficit composition, not only its size |
| Efficiency vs equity | Markets need competition, regulation and social protection |
| Farm support vs distortion | Protect income while improving productivity and markets |
| Openness vs resilience | Integrate globally while managing strategic dependencies |
| Technology vs employment | Raise productivity while enabling skills and transitions |
| Climate action vs development | Combine energy security, adaptation and green growth |
