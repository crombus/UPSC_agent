**Analytical claim:** Trade policy should lower economy-wide costs and build export
capability while using time-bound protection only where learning and strategic spillovers
are demonstrable.

## 2. Concepts and distinctions

| Concept | Precise meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Tariff** | Tax imposed on imported goods. |
| ✅ **Non-tariff measure** | Regulatory measure affecting trade, including standards, quotas or procedures. |
| ✅ **FTA** | Agreement reducing trade barriers among members under agreed coverage and rules. |
| ✅ **Rules of origin** | Criteria determining where a product originates for preferential treatment. |
| ✅ **Protectionism** | Use of barriers or preferences to shelter domestic producers from external competition. |

## 3. Detailed transmission

1. Tariffs, standards and trade agreements change landed prices and market access.
2. Firms respond by altering suppliers, scale, technology, product quality and investment
   location.
3. Rules of origin determine whether a product qualifies for an FTA preference.
4. Trade exposure redistributes gains and losses among consumers, exporters, import-
   competing firms and workers.
5. Adjustment support, logistics and domestic capability decide whether openness produces
   upgrading or persistent vulnerability.

### Deeper analytical layers

- ⚠️ Comparative advantage explains gains from trade, while strategic trade arguments focus
  on scale, learning and externalities.
- ⚠️ Effective protection can exceed the nominal tariff when output protection combines with
  low input duties, or reverse when inputs are taxed.
- ⚠️ GVC participation depends on predictable customs, logistics, standards, imported inputs
  and investment rules.
- ⚠️ Trade remedies require evidence of injury and causal linkage under applicable rules.
- ⚠️ Digital trade adds data, localisation, source-code, taxation and platform-regulation
  issues.
- ⚠️ Resilience policy should diversify suppliers and build capability rather than pursue
  indiscriminate autarky.

## 4. Institutional architecture

- ✅ **WTO:** provides multilateral trade rules, negotiation forums and dispute-settlement
  architecture.
- ✅ **Department of Commerce:** negotiates trade agreements and administers trade-policy
  instruments.
- ✅ **DGTR:** investigates specified trade-remedy cases under applicable law.
- ✅ **Customs and standards or testing bodies:** implement border treatment, origin
  verification and technical compliance.

## 5. Indian applications and boundary cases

- ⚠️ A high tariff on an imported component can protect one upstream producer while making
  downstream exports less competitive.
- ⚠️ Rules of origin prevent goods from a non-member merely passing through an FTA partner
  to obtain preference.
- ⚠️ A sanitary standard may protect health legitimately or become a disguised barrier if
  discriminatory and unsupported by evidence.

## 6. Limitations and trade-offs

- ⚠️ Openness lowers cost and expands markets but exposes firms and workers to adjustment
  shocks.
- ⚠️ Protection supports learning yet can create permanent lobbying and weak productivity.
- ⚠️ Deep FTAs improve access while narrowing domestic regulatory autonomy in covered areas.
- ⚠️ Export restrictions protect domestic supply briefly but weaken producer incentives and
  buyer trust.
- ⚠️ Standards protect health and quality but can function as disguised barriers if
  discriminatory.

⚠️ **Boundary condition:** Import substitution measured at the final-product level can
conceal deeper dependence on imported inputs, technology and machinery.

## 7. Must-Know Facts for Advanced Prelims

- ✅ WTO principles include non-discrimination through most-favoured-nation and national-
  treatment obligations, subject to exceptions.
- ✅ FTAs create preferences among members and require rules of origin to prevent simple
  trans-shipment.
- ✅ Tariffs protect producers but raise input or consumer costs when imports are used
  domestically.
- ✅ Anti-dumping action addresses specified unfair pricing injury; it is not a general
  safeguard against all imports.
- ✅ Services trade can be supplied across borders, through consumption abroad, commercial
  presence or movement of persons.
- ✅ Trade balance concerns goods; the current account is broader.

## 8. Advanced Prelims traps

- ❌ FTA means zero duty on every product immediately. -> Coverage, schedules, exclusions and
  phase-downs vary.
- ❌ MFN means the most favourable tariff for one chosen ally. -> It generally extends equal
  treatment across WTO members.
- ❌ Protection always creates self-reliance. -> It can preserve inefficient dependence on
  protected inputs or technology.
- ❌ Rules of origin are tariffs. -> They are eligibility criteria for preferences.
- ❌ A merchandise deficit equals a BoP deficit. -> Services, income, transfers and financial
  flows also matter.

## 11. Mains-ready framework

**Central thesis:** Trade policy should lower economy-wide costs and build export capability while using time-bound protection only where learning and strategic spillovers are demonstrable.

1. Define **Tariff** and distinguish it from **Non-tariff measure**.
2. Firms respond by altering suppliers, scale, technology, product quality and investment
   location.

   architecture.

   shocks.
