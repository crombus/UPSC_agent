# Science & Technology - Master Conceptual Framework

> **Purpose:** Connect every Science & Technology topic before studying the detailed files.
> **Core question:** How does India build strategic, economic and human-development capability
> through science, technology and innovation — and where do capability, regulation and ethics
> create tension?

**Core proposition:** Judge every S&T topic on four axes — **capability** (what has actually been
developed/tested/deployed), **institution** (who governs/funds/regulates it), **application**
(what real Indian problem it solves), and **constraint** (technical, financial, ethical or
regulatory limit) — never on capability claims alone.

## 2. Six pillars

| Pillar | Central question | Key institutions/instruments |
|---|---|---|
| Foundational science | What are the underlying physical, chemical and biological principles? | NCERT-level physics/chemistry/biology; DST/CSIR research base |
| Research governance | Who funds, coordinates and performs research? | DST, ANRF (absorbing SERB), CSIR labs, ICMR, DBT |
| Strategic frontier missions | Can India achieve autonomous space, nuclear and defence capability? | ISRO/IN-SPACe/NSIL, DAE/BARC/NPCIL, DRDO/DAP |
| Digital & data governance | Can India build safe, inclusive digital public infrastructure? | MeitY, UIDAI, NPCI, RBI, IndiaAI, DPDP framework, CERT-In/NCIIPC |
| Biotechnology & health science | Can biotechnology serve agriculture, health and industry safely? | DBT, BIRAC, GEAC/MoEFCC, CDSCO, ICMR |
| Materials, mobility & resources | Can India secure the materials and technologies of the next industrial transition? | DST Nano Mission, CGPDTM/DPIIT/WIPO, MNRE/MoRTH/BEE, DGCA, Ministry of Mines/GSI/KABIL |

## 3. Recurring mandatory distinctions (the exam's favourite traps)

| Distinction | Do not conflate |
|---|---|
| Launch vehicle vs missile | PSLV/GSLV/LVM3 (ISRO, space access) vs Agni/Prithvi/BrahMos (DRDO, weapons) |
| NavIC vs GAGAN | NavIC = India's own regional navigation constellation; GAGAN = SBAS augmenting GPS for aviation |
| Nuclear fission vs fusion | Fission (Stage 1-3 power programme, operational) vs fusion (ITER/tokamaks, experimental) |
| Aadhaar vs UPI | Aadhaar (UIDAI, identity) vs UPI (NPCI, payment rail) — different law, regulator and purpose |
| Data protection vs cybersecurity | DPDP Act (lawful use of personal data, Data Protection Board) vs CERT-In/NCIIPC (system/network defence) |
| GM crops vs gene editing | Transgenic GM (foreign DNA, GEAC-regulated as GMO) vs native-genome gene editing (distinct lighter track under 2022 guidelines) |
| Vaccine vs monoclonal antibody | Vaccine = active immunisation (trains the immune system); mAb = passive, lab-made, direct therapeutic |
| Patent vs GI vs trademark | Time-bound invention right vs place-based collective right vs brand identifier — three distinct instruments |
| Rare earths vs critical minerals | Rare earths = a specific 17-element group; critical minerals = a broader supply-risk policy category |
| CCUS vs nuclear/renewables | CCUS (Environment topic 21) captures/stores emissions; nuclear (fission/fusion) and renewables generate low-carbon power by different means |
| ISRO vs NSIL vs IN-SPACe | Government space agency vs commercial PSU vs private-sector regulator/promoter — three distinct entities |

## 4. Capability-status ladder (apply to every mission/technology claim)

```text
proposed -> approved -> under development -> tested (ground/uncrewed) -> operational -> deployed at scale
```

⚠️ Never assume a later stage than the last verified official statement. A "cabinet approval"
is not a "launch," and a "successful test" is not "operational deployment."

## 5. Recurring UPSC analytical tensions

| Tension | Balanced framing |
|---|---|
| Strategic autonomy vs cost/time | Indigenisation builds resilience but often costs more and takes longer than imports |
| Innovation speed vs regulation | AI, gene editing and data governance need rules that do not choke experimentation |
| Privacy vs security/welfare use of data | Digital public infrastructure needs both individual rights and state/market utility |
| Access vs IP incentive | Patents reward innovation but can raise access costs (health, agriculture) |
| Import dependence vs domestic scale-up | Semiconductors, critical minerals and batteries need ecosystem depth, not just fab/assembly announcements |
| Big-mission prestige vs broad-base research | Flagship missions (Gaganyaan, quantum, semiconductor) vs sustained curiosity-driven research funding |
| Technology adoption vs safety/ethics | Nanotechnology, gene editing, AI and drones all pair real benefit with an unresolved safety/ethics debate |

## 6. Study sequence

1. **Anchor the science:** Topics 21-23 (physics/chemistry/biology fundamentals).
2. **Understand who funds and governs research:** Topic 24 (DST/CSIR/ANRF).
3. **Study strategic frontier missions:** Topics 01-07 (space, nuclear, defence).
4. **Study digital and data governance:** Topics 08-12 (Digital India, AI, quantum, semiconductors, data/cyber).
5. **Study biotechnology and health science:** Topics 13-15.
6. **Study materials, mobility and resource security:** Topics 16-20.
7. **Revise distinctions and current anchors:** re-check every 📰-tagged date in each file before
   an exam attempt, since S&T current affairs move faster than any other GS-III sub-area.
