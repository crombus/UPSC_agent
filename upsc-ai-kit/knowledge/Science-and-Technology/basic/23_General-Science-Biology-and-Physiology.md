## 2. Essential definitions

| Term | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Cell** | Basic structural and functional unit of life. |
| ✅ **Organelle** | Specialized subcellular structure performing a defined function inside a cell. |
| ✅ **Mitochondrion** | Organelle associated with aerobic respiration and ATP generation. |
| ✅ **Gene** | Functional unit of heredity located on DNA. |
| ✅ **DNA** | Double-stranded nucleic acid storing hereditary information in most organisms. |
| ✅ **RNA** | Usually single-stranded nucleic acid involved in expression and regulation of genetic information. |
| ✅ **Hormone** | Chemical messenger secreted in small amounts and carried to target tissues. |
| ✅ **Antigen** | Foreign substance recognized by the immune system as capable of triggering a response. |
| ✅ **Antibody** | Specific protein produced by B-lymphocyte lineage that binds an antigen. |
| ✅ **Innate immunity** | Inborn, immediate and non-specific defense. |
| ✅ **Adaptive immunity** | Specific acquired defense involving memory, B cells and T cells. |
| ✅ **Photosynthesis** | Process by which green plants use light energy to synthesize food from carbon dioxide and water. |

## 3. Mechanism / how it works

### How adaptive immune response develops (without entering vaccine-platform details)

1. ✅ An antigen enters the body and is recognized as non-self.
2. ✅ Antigen-presenting cells help activate lymphocytes.
3. ✅ B cells differentiate into plasma cells that produce antibodies; T cells coordinate or directly attack depending on subtype.
4. ✅ Antibodies bind specific antigens and help neutralize or mark them for destruction.
5. ✅ Some lymphocytes become memory cells, enabling a faster response on future exposure.
6. ⚠️ This is the conceptual immune base; vaccine platforms, adjuvants and regulatory approval belong in `15_Vaccines-Monoclonal-Antibodies-and-Biopharma.md`, not here.

## 6. Must-Know Facts for Prelims

- ✅ Prokaryotic cells lack a true membrane-bound nucleus; eukaryotic cells possess one.
- ✅ Mitochondria are linked with cellular respiration; chloroplasts with photosynthesis.
- ✅ The small intestine is the principal site of digestion completion and nutrient absorption.
- ✅ Hemoglobin carries oxygen; blood plasma carries dissolved substances and proteins.
- ✅ The nervous system acts rapidly through impulses; the endocrine system acts more slowly through hormones.
- ✅ DNA contains deoxyribose sugar and thymine; RNA contains ribose sugar and uracil.
- ✅ Bacteria are living cellular organisms; viruses are acellular particles that require host cells for replication.
- ✅ Fungi are eukaryotic organisms and are neither plants nor bacteria.
- ✅ Innate immunity is non-specific; adaptive immunity is specific and can develop memory.
- ✅ Photosynthesis and respiration are related but not identical processes; plants respire all the time.

## 7. UPSC traps

- ❌ **All cells in the body perform the same function because they contain the same DNA.** -> Differential gene expression creates functional specialization.
- ❌ **DNA and RNA differ only in name.** -> They differ in sugar, one nitrogen base, typical structure and primary role.
- ❌ **Antibodies and antigens are the same thing.** -> Antigen triggers the response; antibody is part of the response.
- ❌ **Virus and bacteria can be treated identically in biology questions.** -> One is acellular and host-dependent; the other is cellular and independently living.
- ❌ **Hormones and enzymes are interchangeable terms.** -> Hormones are signaling molecules; enzymes are biological catalysts.
- ❌ **Plants only photosynthesize and do not respire.** -> Plants respire continuously; photosynthesis is light-dependent.
