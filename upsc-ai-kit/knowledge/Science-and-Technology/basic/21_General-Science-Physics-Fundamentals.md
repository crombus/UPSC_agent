## 2. Essential definitions

| Term | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Force** | A push or pull that can change the state of rest, motion or shape of a body. |
| ✅ **Newton’s first law** | A body remains at rest or in uniform straight-line motion unless acted upon by an external unbalanced force. |
| ✅ **Work** | Product of force and displacement in the direction of force. |
| ✅ **Energy** | Capacity to do work; appears in forms such as kinetic, potential, heat, electrical and nuclear. |
| ✅ **Power** | Rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. |
| ✅ **Temperature** | Measure of degree of hotness; related to average kinetic energy, not the same as heat. |
| ✅ **Wave** | Disturbance that transfers energy without net transfer of matter. |
| ✅ **Refraction** | Bending of light when it passes obliquely from one medium to another because speed changes. |
| ✅ **Current** | Rate of flow of electric charge. |
| ✅ **Resistance** | Opposition offered by a conductor to current flow. |
| ✅ **Electromagnetic induction** | Production of emf/current when magnetic flux linked with a conductor changes. |
| ✅ **Semiconductor** | Material whose conductivity lies between conductor and insulator and can be controlled by doping or external conditions. |
| ✅ **Laser** | Intense, coherent, nearly monochromatic beam produced by stimulated emission of radiation. |

## 3. Mechanism / how it works

### How electromagnetic induction lights homes

1. ✅ A conductor moving in a magnetic field, or a changing magnetic field around a conductor, changes magnetic flux.
2. ✅ This changing flux induces an emf; if the circuit is closed, current flows.
3. ✅ Generators use mechanical rotation to keep changing magnetic flux continuously.
4. ✅ Transformers then use mutual induction to step voltage up or down for transmission and distribution.
5. ✅ The same physics underlies bicycle dynamos, large power stations and many sensing devices.
6. ⚠️ UPSC often shifts from the core principle to application: if magnetic flux does not change, induction does not occur.

## 5. Indian applications, examples and limitations

- ✅ Electricity generation and grid transmission rely on electromagnetic induction, transformers and circuit design.
- ✅ Spectacles, cameras, microscopes and fibre-optic communication depend on reflection, refraction and lens behavior.
- ✅ Semiconductors and transistors form the basis of phones, computers, sensors, telecom switching and power electronics.
- ✅ Lasers are used in surgery, industrial cutting, communication, surveying and range-finding.
- ✅ Controlled radioisotopes are used in medicine, industry and agriculture; this does **not** mean all radiation use is nuclear-power related.
- ⚠️ **Limitation / pitfall 1:** heat and temperature are often confused; a hot object and a high-heat-content object are not automatically the same thing.
- ⚠️ **Limitation / pitfall 2:** current, voltage and power are linked but not identical; UPSC often exploits this confusion.
- ⚠️ **Limitation / pitfall 3:** modern devices hide the physics inside black boxes, so candidates forget the underlying principle and fail application-based questions.

## 6. Must-Know Facts for Prelims

- ✅ Newton’s third law explains action-reaction pairs; the two forces act on different bodies.
- ✅ Work is zero if there is no displacement in the direction of force, even if force is applied.
- ✅ Heat flows because of temperature difference; temperature itself is not a form of energy transfer.
- ✅ Sound needs a material medium; light does not.
- ✅ A convex lens can converge light; a concave lens diverges it.
- ✅ Resistance in a metallic conductor generally increases with temperature, while semiconductor behavior differs.
- ✅ A changing magnetic flux is essential for electromagnetic induction.
- ✅ Alpha particles are heavy and strongly ionizing but weakly penetrating; gamma rays are highly penetrating electromagnetic radiation.
- ✅ In a transistor, a small input signal/current can control a larger output, which is why transistors became basic switching/amplifying elements.
- ✅ Lasers are characterized by coherence and directionality, not merely by “bright light.”

## 7. UPSC traps

- ❌ **Mass and weight are the same.** -> Mass is amount of matter; weight is gravitational force on that mass.
- ❌ **Heat and temperature are interchangeable.** -> Heat is energy in transfer; temperature is a measure of thermal state.
- ❌ **Louder sound must have higher pitch.** -> Loudness depends mainly on amplitude; pitch depends on frequency.
- ❌ **A mirror and a lens work by the same optical principle.** -> Mirrors mainly reflect; lenses mainly refract.
- ❌ **Current is “used up” inside a circuit.** -> Charge flow is sustained through the circuit; electrical energy is transformed, not the current disappearing as a substance.
- ❌ **Beta radiation means electromagnetic radiation.** -> Beta radiation commonly involves fast electrons/positrons; gamma is electromagnetic.
