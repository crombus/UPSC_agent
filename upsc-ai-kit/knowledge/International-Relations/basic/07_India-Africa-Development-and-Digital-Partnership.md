## 3. How the India-Africa partnership mechanism works

1. **Summit-level political framework:** IAFS periodically convenes India and
   African leaders/the African Union to set the overall partnership direction.
2. **Capacity-building instruments:** ✅ the $500 million HRD/capacity-building
   grant (first IAFS) and the 1600 technical-cooperation training positions
   operationalise the human-capital dimension of the partnership.
3. **Joint institutions embed capacity on African soil:** ✅ the nineteen
   jointly established institutions (IT, foreign trade, educational planning,
   diamond processing, vocational training, human settlements) create durable,
   in-country capacity rather than one-off training abroad.
4. **Student mobility deepens the human link:** ✅ Tharoor notes "at any time
   there are at least 10,000 to 15,000 African students studying in various
   parts of India" — a sustained education-diplomacy channel.
5. **Digital connectivity extends the reach:** ✅ the historical Pan African
   e-Network demonstrated the tele-education model; after it concluded in 2017,
   e-VBAB carried forward tele-education and tele-medicine cooperation,
   supporting the "digital partnership" framing of the 2025 PYQ.
6. **Co-development framing:** ✅ Tharoor frames the political-cooperation
   dimension as "non-intrusive support to the development of democratic
   institutions" — a demand-driven, non-prescriptive posture distinguishing
   India's approach from more directive donor models.

## 4. Institutions and agreements

- ⚠️ **India-Africa Forum Summit (IAFS):** the umbrella political mechanism.
- ✅ **Pan African e-Network:** historical digital-partnership precursor,
  concluded in 2017.
- ✅ **e-VBAB:** the successor tele-education and tele-medicine network,
  launched in 2019.
- ✅ **Nineteen jointly established institutions** (IT, foreign trade,
  educational planning and administration, diamond processing, ten vocational
  training centres, five human settlement institutes) with the African Union
  Commission and member states.
- 📰 **India-SADC economic-cooperation MoU (2024):** explicitly includes
  ICT/DPI cooperation — the anchor current instrument for this topic's digital-
  partnership dimension.
- ✅ **Indian Technical and Economic Cooperation (ITEC) Programme:** per the
  Economic Survey 2025-26, ITEC has "trained more than two lakh persons from
  over 160 countries in both the civilian and the defence sector," and is
  described as "an important tool in strengthening India's cultural diplomacy
  and influence, especially in the South Asian region" — cite this as the
  broader technical-cooperation architecture within which Africa-specific
  training positions (the 1600 figure) sit, dated to the Economic Survey
  2025-26.

## 7. UPSC traps

- ❌ India's Africa partnership is primarily a trade/investment relationship. ->
  The 2024-2025 syllabus emphasis and the retrieved sources foreground capacity
  building, education, digital connectivity and institutional partnership;
  Economy owns bilateral trade-volume mechanics separately.
- ❌ The Pan African e-Network remains India's ongoing flagship Africa digital
  platform. -> MEA records it as concluded in 2017; e-VBAB is the successor
  tele-education and tele-medicine network launched in 2019.
- ❌ ITEC's 2-lakh-trainee figure (Economic Survey 2025-26) is Africa-specific.
  -> It is a global figure across 160+ countries with an explicit South Asian
  emphasis; the Africa-specific figure is the 1600 training positions cited
  separately by Tharoor.
- ❌ India's Africa engagement is donor-driven, mirroring a traditional aid
  model. -> Tharoor frames it as "non-intrusive" and demand-driven,
  distinguishing it from a conditionality-based aid relationship.
- ❌ MoUs with African regional bodies (e.g., SADC) equal delivered outcomes. ->
  An MoU states intent; delivery/implementation requires separate, dated
  verification (announcement-delivery gap).
