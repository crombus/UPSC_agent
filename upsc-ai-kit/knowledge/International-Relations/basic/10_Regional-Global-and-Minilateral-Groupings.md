**Core proposition:** ⚠️ India participates in a deliberately overlapping set of
regional, trans-regional, continental, global-governance and minilateral
groupings, each serving a distinct purpose, rather than anchoring its foreign
policy in membership of any single bloc.

## 2. Essential definitions

| Concept | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ⚠️ **SAARC** | The South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation, India's oldest South Asia-specific regional grouping, whose momentum has been constrained by India-Pakistan tensions in recent years. |
| ✅ **BIMSTEC** | ✅ Originally the Bangladesh-India-Myanmar-Sri Lanka-Thailand Economic Cooperation (BIMST-EC), "since renamed" the Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation (BIMSTEC) — a Bay-of-Bengal-focused grouping that has gained salience as SAARC's momentum has slowed; its Charter entered into force 20 May 2024. |
| ✅ **IORA / IOR-ARC** | ✅ The Indian Ocean Rim Association (earlier IOR-ARC), a trans-regional forum for Indian Ocean littoral-state economic and security cooperation, explicitly cited by Tharoor as a platform India engages alongside larger bodies under its multi-alignment approach. |
| ✅ **IBSA** | ✅ The India-Brazil-South Africa Initiative, a "potentially significant" trilateral grouping (Sikri) linking three major Southern democracies. |
| ✅ **RIC** | ✅ The Russia-India-China trilateral grouping, cited by Sikri as one of the trilateral platforms India participates in alongside IBSA. |
| ✅ **SCO** | The Shanghai Cooperation Organisation, a Eurasian security-and-economic body including China, Russia, Pakistan, India and Central Asian states (full connectivity/energy treatment in topic 05). |
| ✅ **BRICS** | Originally BRIC (Brazil-Russia-India-China), later expanded with South Africa and additional members; its Rio Declaration was issued 6 July 2025. |
| ⚠️ **Quad** | A non-treaty minilateral coalition (US, Japan, India, Australia) with a maritime-security and Indo-Pacific cooperation focus, marked by asymmetric treaty relationships among its members (full maritime-security treatment in topic 04). |
| ✅ **G20** | The premier global economic-governance forum; ✅ Tharoor's multi-alignment framing explicitly pairs India's G20 role alongside its UN role as parallel, non-exclusive engagements. |

**Current membership and institutional nature (dated July 2026):**

| Grouping | Current membership | Institutional nature |
|---|---|---|
| SAARC | 8 members | Charter-based South Asian regional organisation |
| BIMSTEC | 7 members | Charter-based Bay of Bengal regional organisation |
| ASEAN | 11 members, including Timor-Leste | Treaty/charter-based Southeast Asian regional organisation |
| IORA | 23 member states | Indian Ocean intergovernmental regional organisation |
| SCO | 10 members, including Iran and Belarus | Charter-based Eurasian intergovernmental organisation |
| BRICS | 11 full members | Intergovernmental political-economic grouping, not a treaty alliance |
| G20 | 19 states plus the European Union and African Union | Informal premier forum for international economic cooperation |
| Quad | India, Australia, Japan and the United States | Non-treaty consultative minilateral |

## 3. How the grouping-participation mechanism works

1. **Regional layer (SAARC, BIMSTEC):** ✅ SAARC represents the historical South
   Asia-wide framework, while BIMSTEC (renamed from BIMST-EC) has gained
   relative prominence as SAARC's functioning has been constrained; India
   invests in both, but increasingly channels South Asia-plus-Southeast Asia
   connectivity ambitions through BIMSTEC.
2. **Trans-regional layer (IORA, IBSA):** these platforms link India with
   littoral states across the Indian Ocean (IORA) or with major Southern
   democracies on other continents (IBSA), reflecting a "beyond the immediate
   neighbourhood" engagement logic.
3. **Continental/Eurasian layer (SCO):** provides India a seat in a body
   otherwise dominated by China, Russia and Pakistan, useful for continental
   dialogue despite mixed membership (topic 05 for connectivity detail).
4. **Global-governance layer (G20, BRICS):** gives India a voice in shaping
   international economic-governance norms, pursued in parallel with, not
   instead of, UN engagement.
5. **Minilateral/issue-based layer (Quad, RIC, I2U2):** addresses specific
   functional interests (maritime security, major-power trilateral dialogue,
   food/water/energy/technology cooperation) without binding treaty commitments.
6. **Overarching multi-alignment logic:** ✅ Tharoor's own listing —
   "both the United Nations and the G20; both the... NAM... and the Community of
   Democracies; both the G77... and... IOR-ARC; both SAARC and [ASEAN-linked
   engagement]" — is the explicit organising principle tying all these layers
   together.

## 4. Institutions and agreements

- 📰 **BIMSTEC Charter (entered into force 20 May 2024):** the anchor current
  instrument formalising BIMSTEC's institutional/legal status.
- 📰 **BRICS Rio Declaration (6 July 2025):** the anchor current BRICS summit
  outcome document.
- ✅ **IBSA and RIC:** trilateral groupings explicitly named by Sikri as part of
  India's overlapping-membership strategy.
- ⚠️ **Mekong-Ganga Cooperation:** a regional grouping linking India with
  mainland Southeast Asian states along the Mekong-Ganga corridor, under the
  broader Act East policy.
- ✅ **Book-period membership gap:** Sikri noted that India had not secured APEC
  or ASEM membership in the period he discussed. India joined ASEM in 2007;
  therefore only APEC remains a current non-membership example, and its status
  should be dated whenever used.

## 5. Indian applications and examples

- ⚠️ **PYQ mapping:** no GS-II Mains question in the audited 2024-2025 papers
  directly names SAARC, BIMSTEC, ASEAN, IORA, SCO, BRICS, G20 or Quad as the
  sole framing device. State this honestly; use the layered-grouping framework
  here as the structuring device for any regionalism/minilateralism-themed
  question.
- 📰 The BIMSTEC Charter's entry into force (20 May 2024) is a concrete,
  dateable institutional milestone worth citing for any "regionalism in South
  Asia" question, given SAARC's relatively constrained momentum.
- 📰 The BRICS Rio Declaration (6 July 2025) is the relevant dated reference for
  any current BRICS-agenda question.

## 6. Must-Know Facts for Prelims

- ✅ BIMSTEC was originally BIMST-EC (Bangladesh-India-Myanmar-Sri Lanka-
  Thailand Economic Cooperation) before being renamed.
- 📰 The BIMSTEC Charter entered into force on 20 May 2024.
- 📰 The BRICS Rio Declaration was issued 6 July 2025.
- ✅ IBSA (India-Brazil-South Africa) and RIC (Russia-India-China) are
  trilateral groupings India participates in.
- ✅ Sikri's APEC/ASEM statement is period-specific: India joined ASEM in 2007,
  while it is not an APEC member as of July 2026.
- ⚠️ Quad membership comprises the US, Japan, India and Australia, with a
  maritime-security/Indo-Pacific cooperation focus.

## 7. UPSC traps

- ❌ SAARC has been formally dissolved. -> SAARC continues to exist as an
  institution even though summit-level momentum has been constrained by
  India-Pakistan tensions; do not assert dissolution without a specific,
  verified source.
- ❌ BIMSTEC and BIMST-EC are two different organisations. -> BIMSTEC is the
  renamed continuation of BIMST-EC; they are the same institutional lineage.
- ❌ Quad is a treaty alliance comparable to NATO. -> It is a non-treaty
  minilateral coalition with acknowledged asymmetry among members (full
  treatment in topic 04).
- ❌ BRICS and the Global South are synonymous. -> BRICS has a specific,
  distinct (though expanding) membership; the Global South is a broader
  category (cross-link to topic 08).
- ❌ India holds membership in every major regional/global forum it has sought.
  -> APEC remains a current non-membership example; ASEM does not, because
  India joined it in 2007.

## 8. 📰 Current anchor

- 📰 The **BIMSTEC Charter (in force 20 May 2024)** and the **BRICS Rio
  Declaration (6 July 2025)** remain key instruments. India launched its
  **BRICS 2026 chairship** logo, theme and website on **13 January 2026**;
  "Building for Resilience, Innovation, Cooperation and Sustainability" is
  the current chairship anchor, not a new treaty or completed summit outcome.

## 9. PYQ application

- ✅ **2020 GS-II:** "'Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (QUAD)' is
  transforming itself into a trade bloc from a military alliance, in present
  times — Discuss." Correct the premise: Quad is neither a treaty military
  alliance nor a trade bloc; evaluate its widening functional agenda.
- ✅ **2021 GS-II:** "Critically examine the aims and objectives of SCO. What
  importance does it hold for India?" — mandate, interests, constraints.
- ✅ **2022 GS-II:** "How will I2U2 (India, Israel, UAE and USA) grouping
  transform India's position in global politics?" — use West Asia, investment,
  technology and strategic-autonomy dimensions.
- ⚠️ No 2024-2025 question names one grouping as the sole subject; use the
  layered-grouping framework and dated BIMSTEC/BRICS anchors.

## 10. Mains angles

- ⚠️ Always specify which layer (regional, trans-regional, continental,
  global-governance, minilateral) a grouping belongs to, rather than listing
  groupings without categorisation.
- ⚠️ Cite the dated BIMSTEC Charter/BRICS Rio Declaration rather than an
  undated "regional cooperation" claim.
- ⚠️ Distinguish treaty-bound institutions (BIMSTEC, SCO, SAARC) from non-treaty
  coalitions (Quad, RIC, IBSA, I2U2).
- ⚠️ Use Tharoor's explicit multi-alignment pairing list as the organising
  citation for any "why does India join so many groupings" question.

> **Answer thesis:** India's participation across regional (SAARC, BIMSTEC),
> trans-regional (IORA, IBSA), continental (SCO), global-governance (G20,
> BRICS) and minilateral (Quad, RIC) groupings reflects a deliberate,
> multi-alignment strategy of engaging every relevant platform on its own
> terms, rather than anchoring foreign policy in a single exclusive bloc.
