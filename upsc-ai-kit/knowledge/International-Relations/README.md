## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the must-do country/region, grouping,
  institution and instrument map; `advanced/NN` adds the causal architecture,
  precise conceptual distinctions, trade-offs, boundary cases and Mains depth.
- **Tagging:** ✅ = directly grounded in an official source, a retrieved book
  passage or a verified PYQ; ⚠️ = analytical inference or standard linkage;
  📰 = a dated current diplomatic anchor (summit, charter, vision document,
  declaration or bilateral brief).
- **Date discipline:** every summit outcome, charter, vision document, MoU,
  declaration and bilateral brief keeps its event date. A press statement, an
  announced MoU, a signed charter and an implemented outcome are treated as
  different evidentiary levels — none is upgraded to the next without a dated
  source.
- **No forecasting:** this folder does not predict UNSC permanent-seat outcomes,
  FTA conclusion, corridor completion/capacity or the result of an ongoing
  negotiation. It states what is proposed, signed, ratified or implemented, with
  the date attached.

## Boundaries with other subjects

- **Polity owns constitutional and procedural doctrine:** Article 51 (DPSP),
  treaty-making and ratification procedure, parliamentary treatment of
  international agreements, and citizenship/OCI legal doctrine. IR studies the
  diplomatic objective and external consequence of an engagement, not its
  domestic constitutional or legal mechanics — see
  `Polity/basic/National-Integration-and-Foreign-Policy.md`.
- **Economy owns macro and trade mechanics:** tariff schedules, balance of
  payments, forex reserves, import bills, commodity/energy pricing and trade
  modelling. IR owns the strategic and diplomatic meaning of trade, sanctions,
  energy and supply-chain relationships — see `Economy/basic/19-21`.
- **Geography owns physical and resource geography:** maps, chokepoints,
  sea lanes as physical features, resource distribution and route geography.
  IR owns the geopolitical implication of a route, chokepoint or region — see
  `Geography/basic/34-35`.
- **World History owns chronology:** the Cold War, decolonisation, and the
  institutional creation-history of bodies such as the UN. IR studies the
  present-day mandate, functioning and reform of the same institutions — see
  `World-History/basic/15-16, 18, 21`.
- **Internal Security owns operations:** border and maritime military
  operations, counter-terrorism operations and insurgency management. IR owns
  the diplomatic and security-cooperation framework around these issues (a
  dedicated Internal Security folder does not yet exist in this knowledge base;
  route conceptually rather than to an invented file).
- **Disaster Management owns the operational HADR cycle:** evacuation,
  command systems and relief logistics. IR owns HADR diplomacy — the
  agreements, frameworks and partnerships that enable cooperation (a dedicated
  Disaster Management folder does not yet exist in this knowledge base; route
  conceptually).

## Study sequence

1. **Foundation:** `01` — national interest, strategic autonomy and the
   instrument toolkit that every later topic reuses.
2. **Immediate and extended neighbourhood:** `02-06` — South Asia, major
   powers and supply chains, the Indo-Pacific/Indian Ocean, Central Asia and
   West Asia, moving outward by geography and stake.
3. **Partnership geographies beyond the neighbourhood:** `07-08` — Africa and
   the wider Global South as development-partnership arenas.
4. **People and platforms:** `09-10` — diaspora diplomacy and the architecture
   of regional/global/minilateral groupings India participates in.
5. **Systemic effects and institutions:** `11-12` — how globalisation and
   trade politics transmit into India's interests, and how the UN/Bretton
   Woods system structures global governance and reform debates.

## Primary source routes

- MEA bilateral documents, country briefs and press releases; PMO and PIB
  statements on summits and visits.
- UN (General Assembly, Security Council and IGN reform portal), WTO
  notifications portal, IMF and World Bank official pages.
- ASEAN, SCO, BRICS (including NDB-hosted texts), G20 and Quad official
  statements and joint declarations.
- Rajiv Sikri, *Challenges and Strategy: Rethinking India's Foreign Policy*,
  for national-interest and instrument-level analysis.
- Shashi Tharoor, *Pax Indica*, for multi-alignment, soft power and
  diaspora-diplomacy framing.
- Economic Survey 2025-26, for the ITEC training programme as an education
  and cultural-diplomacy data point (dated and scoped only to that context).
