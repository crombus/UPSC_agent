# PART A — THE UNIVERSE ✅
- ✅ **Galaxies** = vast clusters of stars, gas & dust. Our galaxy = the **Milky Way** (a spiral galaxy), containing
  ~**100 billion+ stars**. The nearest major galaxy = **Andromeda**.
- ⚠️ **Big Bang theory** (Georges Lemaître) — the universe began ~**13.8 billion years ago** from a singularity and is
  still **expanding** (Hubble). *(Modern addition; GC Leong predates emphasis on it.)*
- ✅ **Light-year** = distance light travels in one year (light speed = **~3 × 10⁵ km/s**, i.e., 1,86,000 mi/s).
  - Sunlight reaches Earth in **~8 minutes 20 sec**; moonlight in **~1.3 sec**; nearest star **Proxima Centauri** ~**4.2 light-years**.

# PART B — THE SOLAR SYSTEM ⭐⭐
✅ **Sun + 8 planets** + dwarf planets, satellites, asteroids, comets, meteoroids.
- ✅ The **Sun** holds **~99.8%** of the Solar System's mass; composed mainly of **hydrogen & helium**; surface
  ~**5,500–6,000°C**, core millions of °C.

## The 8 planets ⭐
| Group | Planets | Traits |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Inner / Terrestrial** (rocky) | **Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars** | Small, dense, rocky, few/no moons |
| ✅ **Outer / Jovian** (gas giants) | **Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune** | Huge, gaseous, ringed, many moons |

- ✅ **Mercury** — smallest & closest to the Sun; shortest year (~88 days).
- ✅ **Venus** — "**Earth's twin**" (size/mass); **hottest planet** (CO₂ greenhouse); rotates **clockwise/retrograde** (E→W).
- ✅ **Earth** — the "**Blue Planet**"; only known life; densest planet.
- ✅ **Mars** — "**Red Planet**" (iron oxide); has Olympus Mons (largest volcano).
- ✅ **Jupiter** — **largest** planet; **Great Red Spot**.
- ✅ **Saturn** — famous **rings**; least dense (would float on water).
- ✅ **Uranus** — rotates **on its side** (~98° tilt), retrograde.
- ✅ **Neptune** — farthest, coldest, strongest winds.
- ✅ **Asteroid belt** lies **between Mars and Jupiter**.
- 📰 **Pluto** was **reclassified as a "dwarf planet"** by the **IAU (24 Aug 2006)** — hence 8 planets, not 9.

> ⭐ **Mnemonic (8 planets):** **My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos** → Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars,
> Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.

# PART C — SHAPE & DIMENSIONS OF THE EARTH ⭐
- ✅ The Earth is an **oblate spheroid** — a **geoid** ("earth-shaped"): **bulged at the Equator, flattened at the
  poles** (due to rotation).
- ✅ **Equatorial diameter (~12,756 km) > polar diameter (~12,714 km)** — a difference of ~43 km.
- ✅ Equatorial circumference ~**40,077 km**.

## Proofs of sphericity (GC Leong) ✅
Circumnavigation · circular horizon at sea · a ship's hull disappears before its mast · Earth's **circular shadow in a
lunar eclipse** · **aerial/satellite photographs** · sunrise/sunset time differences with longitude.

# PART D — LATITUDES & LONGITUDES ⭐⭐
| Feature | Detail ✅ |
|---|---|
| **Latitudes** | Parallels, 0°–90° N/S; **Equator = 0°** (a **great circle**); all others are small circles |
| **Longitudes / Meridians** | 0°–180° E/W; **Prime Meridian = 0°** (Greenwich); all meridians are **half of a great circle** & equal length |
| **Key parallels** | Tropic of Cancer **23½°N** · Tropic of Capricorn **23½°S** · Arctic Circle **66½°N** · Antarctic Circle **66½°S** |

- ✅ **Time:** Earth rotates **15° per hour** (360°/24 h) → **4 minutes per 1° of longitude**.
- ✅ **IST = 82½°E**, **GMT + 5:30**. The **International Date Line (~180°)** — cross **W→E = subtract a day**, **E→W = add a day**.

# PART E — MOTIONS OF THE EARTH ⭐⭐

## Rotation ✅
- Spins **west → east** on its axis (tilted **23½°** to the orbital plane) in **~23 h 56 m** (a sidereal day).
- Speed **~1,670 km/h at the Equator**. **Effects:** day & night, apparent Sun/star motion, tides, the **Coriolis
  effect** (deflection — right in N hemisphere, left in S).

## Revolution ✅
- Orbits the Sun in **365¼ days** (→ a **leap year** every 4th year).
- Combined with the **axial tilt**, revolution causes the **seasons**.

| Event | Date | Feature ✅ |
|---|---|---|
| **Summer Solstice** | **21 June** | Sun overhead at Tropic of Cancer; longest day (N hemisphere) |
| **Winter Solstice** | **22 Dec** | Sun overhead at Tropic of Capricorn; shortest day (N hemisphere) |
| **Autumnal Equinox** | **23 Sept** | Equal day & night everywhere |
| **Spring (Vernal) Equinox** | **21 Mar** | Equal day & night everywhere |
| **Perihelion** | **~3 Jan** | Earth **closest** to the Sun |
| **Aphelion** | **~4 July** | Earth **farthest** from the Sun |

## Eclipses ✅
- **Solar eclipse** — Moon between Sun & Earth (only on a **new moon**).
- **Lunar eclipse** — Earth between Sun & Moon (only on a **full moon**).

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ The Solar System has 9 planets → **8** (Pluto = dwarf planet since 2006). *GC Leong's "9" is outdated.*
- ❌ Earth is a perfect sphere → an **oblate spheroid / geoid** (equatorial bulge).
- ❌ Earth is closest to the Sun in (N-hemisphere) summer → **Perihelion is ~3 Jan (winter)**; distance doesn't cause seasons — **axial tilt** does.
- ❌ All longitudes are great circles → each **meridian is only half** a great circle; **only the Equator** among latitudes is a great circle.
- ❌ A day = exactly 24 h of rotation → a **sidereal day is ~23 h 56 m**; the 24 h **solar day** accounts for orbital motion.
- ❌ Venus/Uranus rotate west-to-east like others → they rotate **clockwise (retrograde)**; Uranus is tilted ~98°.
- ❌ Crossing the IDL westward adds a day → **W→E subtract**, **E→W add**.
