## 1. Snapshot & core idea


**Advanced — Himalayan Glaciers & the Snowline (Khullar / Majid).**

✅ Khullar records ~15,000 Himalayan glaciers between the eastern and western syntaxial bends. The snowline (lowest limit of perpetual snow) is not fixed — it depends on latitude, precipitation and topography. Most large Indian glaciers lie in the Greater Himalaya and Trans-Himalaya (Karakoram, Ladakh, Zaskar); ancient moraines below present snouts prove a Pleistocene glacial age once reached far lower.
- ✅ Khullar: about 15,000 glaciers in the Himalaya, between the two syntaxial bends (east & west).
- ✅ Snowline = lowest level of perpetual snow; it varies with latitude, precipitation and local topography.
- ✅ Direct moisture rule (Majid): more atmospheric moisture -> lower snowline (and vice versa) — so the drier Tibetan side has a snowline ~900 m higher.
- ✅ Main glaciers lie in the Greater and Trans-Himalaya (Karakoram, Ladakh, Zaskar); the Lesser Himalaya has only small glaciers (traces in Pir-Panjal, Dhauladhar).
- ✅ Siachen, Biafo, Baltoro, Hispar (Karakoram) are among India's/the region's largest glaciers; Pleistocene moraines prove past glaciation to far lower levels.

**Ca Anchor — South Lhonak Lake & the Teesta GLOF (Sikkim).**

✅ GLOFs are the disaster face of glacial deposition: a moraine-dammed glacial lake that swells with meltwater can breach suddenly, releasing a catastrophic flood.
- ✅ South Lhonak is a moraine-dammed glacial lake that grew as its feeder glacier retreated — area rose from ~0.42 sq km (2009) to ~1.48 sq km (2023).
- ✅ Trigger: heavy rain/cloudburst breached the moraine dam -> sudden outburst down the Teesta, damaging the Teesta-III hydropower project.
- ✅ Mitigation: glacial-lake monitoring, early-warning systems, and restricting development in the flood path (NDMA GLOF guidelines).

## 5. UPSC Traps

> 🔑 Trap: Do not confuse similar-looking landforms, processes, scales or Indian locations; UPSC often tests the exact mechanism and setting.


**Advanced — Himalayan Glaciers & the Snowline (Khullar / Majid).**
- ❌ The snowline is the same altitude across the Himalaya. → It varies widely — lower in wet Kashmir (~2,500 m snout), higher on the dry Tibetan side (~900 m higher).
- ❌ Snowline and glacier snout are the same thing. → Snowline = limit of perpetual snow; snout = the glacier's lower terminus (they differ).
- ❌ Most Indian glaciers are in the Lesser Himalaya. → Most large glaciers are in the GREATER and TRANS-Himalaya (Karakoram etc.).

**Ca Anchor — South Lhonak Lake & the Teesta GLOF (Sikkim).**
- ❌ GLOFs are caused only by earthquakes. → They result from a moraine-dam breach — often triggered by rain/cloudburst or lake overtopping, not necessarily quakes.
