## 1. Thar: India’s Hot Desert

✅ The Thar / Great Indian Desert lies mainly in **western Rajasthan**, extending into adjacent Gujarat, Punjab, Haryana and Pakistan. ✅ Returned Majid Husain passage identifies the desert region as **Rajasthan west of the Aravalli mountains and northern Gujarat**. ✅ The mean July and January temperatures vary between **45°C and 10°C** respectively, and mean annual rainfall is **less than 25 cm**.

|---|---|
| Core region | Rajasthan west of Aravallis; northern Gujarat |
| Climate | Hot desert / BWhw in existing notes |

> 🔑 Trap: The Thar is arid even though monsoon winds reach the region; rainfall remains low.

## 2. Why the Thar is Arid

✅ Existing grounded notes identify the key Indian cause: the **Aravallis run parallel to the Arabian Sea branch** of the SW monsoon, so they do not force air to rise and rain. ✅ Dry, stable, descending air and continental influence reinforce aridity. ✅ Some salt flats are linked to former sea-bed / inland drainage conditions in the source notes.

| Cause | ✅ Effect |
|---|---|
| Aravallis parallel to monsoon current | No effective orographic rain barrier |
| Dry subsiding air | Suppresses clouds/rain |
| Continental position | Moisture weakens inland |
| Inland drainage / playas | Salt lakes and dhands form |

## 3. Landforms, Drainage & Ecology

✅ The Thar shows aeolian landforms such as **barchans**, **seif dunes** and sand sheets. ✅ It has ephemeral salt lakes/playas locally called **dhands**; **Sambhar** is a major salt lake in the source notes. ✅ The **Luni** is the main inland-draining river.

✅ Vegetation is xerophytic: **khejri (Prosopis cineraria)**, babul, ber, cacti and thorn scrub. ✅ Fauna includes arid-adapted species such as the **Great Indian Bustard**, chinkara, desert fox and Indian wild ass in the nearby Rann.

| Feature | ✅ Examples |
|---|---|
| Dunes | Barchan, seif, sand sheets |
| Playas / dhands | Sambhar salt lake |
| River | Luni, inland drainage |
| Vegetation | Khejri, babul, ber, cacti, thorn scrub |
| Fauna | GIB, chinkara, desert fox; Indian wild ass nearby |

## 4. Human Adaptation & Resource Frontier

✅ The source notes describe the Thar as the world’s most densely populated desert. ✅ Traditional adaptation includes pastoralism and water harvesting such as **tanka, khadin and johad**. ✅ Modern transformation includes the **Indira Gandhi Nahar / Rajasthan Canal**, irrigation-based agriculture, tourism and renewable energy parks.

✅ Returned book passage notes **bajra, pulses and fodder** as main crops, with rain-water harvesting, horticulture of **date-palm, water-melon and plums** needing more attention.

## 6. UPSC Traps

- ❌ Aravallis block the monsoon and should make Thar wet → **They run parallel to the Arabian Sea branch and fail to force uplift.**
- ❌ Thar is uninhabited like a core desert → **It is densely populated, with pastoralism, towns and canal irrigation.**
- ❌ Thar rainfall is moderate because it lies in monsoon India → **Western Thar gets <25 cm annual rainfall.**
- ❌ Desert crops are mainly rice/wheat → **Returned passage notes bajra, pulses and fodder.**

## 7. 📰 Current link

📰 **Great Indian Bustard vs solar power lines:** In **March 2024**, the Supreme Court modified its **2021** order that had required all overhead power lines in GIB habitat to go underground. The Court accepted blanket undergrounding was impractical for clean-energy goals and set up an expert committee to balance GIB conservation with solar/wind expansion in the Thar. GIB (*Ardeotis nigriceps*) is **Critically Endangered**, with **<150** left, mostly in Rajasthan; the main modern threat is collision with overhead power lines due to poor frontal vision. Conservation tools include Project GIB, captive breeding, Desert National Park and bird diverters.
