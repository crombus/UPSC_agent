## 1. India’s Steppe Analogue

✅ India has no true temperate-steppe wheat climate like Eurasia’s chernozem grasslands, but the **Indo-Gangetic alluvial plain**, especially **Punjab-Haryana-western Uttar Pradesh**, plays the equivalent economic role: India’s wheat granary. ✅ Punjab is identified in the returned passage as the **“Food Basket and granary of India.”**

| Element | ✅ India-specific fact |
|---|---|
| Core belt | Punjab, Haryana, western Uttar Pradesh |
| Other producers | Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Bihar in existing notes |
| Soil | Alluvial, not chernozem |
| Crop season | Rabi / winter wheat |
| Enablers | HYV seeds, irrigation, fertilisers, MSP/procurement |

> 🔑 Trap: India’s wheat belt is **alluvial**, not black chernozem like the steppe.

## 2. Punjab-Haryana Granary

✅ Returned passage notes Punjab has **84%** of its total geographical area under cultivation and cropping intensity around **189%**. ✅ With **1.5%** of India’s total area, Punjab produces **20% of wheat**, **11% of rice** and **13% of cotton** in the returned passage, and is called the **Food Basket and granary of India**.

✅ Haryana’s wheat-producing districts in the returned D.R. Khullar passage include **Karnal, Kurukshetra, Ambala, Kaithal, Panipat, Sonipat, Rohtak, Jind, Hisar, Sirsa, Fatehabad and Gurgaon**.

| State / region | ✅ Book-grounded details |
|---|---|
| Punjab | 84% area under cultivation; cropping intensity ~189%; 20% of wheat in returned passage |
| Haryana | Karnal, Kurukshetra, Ambala, Kaithal, Panipat, Sonipat, Rohtak, Jind, Hisar, Sirsa, Fatehabad, Gurgaon |
| Trade surplus | Punjab, Haryana, UP, Rajasthan, MP supply deficit regions |

## 3. Green Revolution & Wheat Diffusion

✅ Existing grounded notes identify the Green Revolution package — **HYV semi-dwarf wheat**, fertiliser, assured canal/tube-well irrigation and MSP/procurement — as transforming the NW plains into a surplus granary. ✅ Returned Khullar passage notes large-scale extension of irrigation, HYV seeds and chemical fertilisers altered Haryana’s cropping pattern.

| Green Revolution input | ✅ Effect |
|---|---|
| HYV wheat seeds | Raised yield potential |
| Irrigation | Reduced rainfall dependence |
| Chemical fertilisers | Supported intensive cultivation |
| MSP/procurement | Assured market and central pool stocks |
| Farmer adoption | Rapid diffusion in Punjab-Haryana belt |

## 4. Trade, Food Security & Sustainability

✅ Returned Khullar passage states that about **one-third** of total wheat production enters trade. ✅ Surplus states include **Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh**, supplying deficit states like **Maharashtra, West Bengal, Bihar and Delhi**. ✅ India may import or export wheat depending on domestic production and requirement.

⚠️ Intensive wheat-rice systems have sustainability costs: groundwater depletion, soil-nutrient imbalance and stubble-burning.

## 6. UPSC Traps

- ❌ India’s wheat belt sits on chernozem like the steppe → **It sits on Indo-Gangetic alluvial soil.**
- ❌ Wheat is a Kharif monsoon crop → **Wheat is a Rabi winter crop.**
- ❌ Punjab dominates because it has huge area → **Returned passage says Punjab has only 1.5% of India’s area but high cultivated area/intensity.**
- ❌ MSP is the open-market price → **MSP is a government-assured floor/procurement price.**
- ❌ Green Revolution had no ecological cost → **Groundwater depletion, nutrient imbalance and stubble-burning are major strains.**

## 7. 📰 Current link

📰 **India’s record 2025–26 wheat procurement despite heat stress:** Despite pre-harvest heat, unseasonal rain and hail, India’s wheat procurement reached a multi-year high of **~35 million tonnes**, up **~17% YoY**, exceeding the **34.5 mt** target. Wheat MSP was **₹2,425/quintal**; about **~67%** was bought under relaxed quality norms due to weather damage. Buffer stock on **1 July** was about **~358 lakh mt** against a norm of **~275.8 lakh mt**, remaining well above norm. Punjab, Haryana, MP, Rajasthan and UP were main procuring states.
