# PART A — STRUCTURE OF THE EARTH'S INTERIOR ⭐⭐
✅ The Earth (~**4,500 million years** old) has **concentric layers** — known chiefly from **earthquake (seismic)
waves**, volcanic material, and deep borings.

| Layer | GC Leong term | Modern detail |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ **Crust (Lithosphere)** | **SIAL** (continental) + **SIMA** (oceanic) | Continental = **granitic** (Silica+Alumina, density ~2.7); oceanic = **basaltic** (Silica+Magnesium, density ~3.0) |
| ✅ Crust thickness | — | **Oceanic ~5–10 km** (thin), **continental up to ~70 km** (thick) |
| ✅ **Mantle** | ~1,800 mi thick, **olivine-rich** | Contains the **asthenosphere** — the plastic source of **magma** |
| ✅ **Core** | **NiFe** (nickel + iron) | **Outer core = liquid**, **inner core = solid**; source of Earth's **magnetism** |

## Discontinuities ⚠️ (modern, high-yield)
- **Mohorovičić (Moho)** — crust ↔ mantle boundary.
- **Gutenberg** — mantle ↔ core boundary.
- ✅ Temperature & pressure **rise with depth**.

> ⚠️ **Plate tectonics** (covered in Ch 3): the lithosphere is broken into **plates** that drift over the
> asthenosphere — driving earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountain-building.

# PART B — CLASSIFICATION OF ROCKS ⭐⭐
✅ Three groups by **origin**: **Igneous · Sedimentary · Metamorphic.**

## 1. Igneous rocks ("primary/parent" rocks) ✅
- Formed by **solidification of magma/lava**; **crystalline**; **no strata, no fossils.**
- **By place of cooling:**
  - **Intrusive / Plutonic** — cools **slowly** at depth → **large crystals** (**granite, gabbro, diorite**).
  - **Extrusive / Volcanic** — lava cools **fast** on the surface → **small crystals** (**basalt** → the **Deccan Traps**).
- **By silica content:**
  - **Acidic** (>65% silica) — light-coloured, less dense (**granite**).
  - **Basic** (<55% silica) — dark, denser, iron/magnesium-rich (**basalt**).
- ✅ **Dykes** (vertical) & **sills** (horizontal) = minor intrusions.

## 2. Sedimentary rocks (stratified) ✅
- Formed by **accumulation of sediments** (usually under water); **layered (strata)**, **non-crystalline**, often
  contain **fossils**.
- **Mechanically formed (clastic):** **sandstone, conglomerate** (rounded pebbles), **breccia** (angular), **shale, clay**.
- **Organically formed:** **Calcareous** (**limestone, chalk** — from shells/coral); **Carbonaceous** (**peat → lignite →
  coal** — from plant matter).
- **Chemically formed:** **rock salt, gypsum, potash, nitrates** (precipitated/evaporite — e.g., from the Dead Sea).

## 3. Metamorphic rocks ("changed" rocks) ✅
Any rock altered by **heat & pressure**:
| Original | → Metamorphic |
|---|---|
| Limestone | **Marble** |
| Sandstone | **Quartzite** |
| Shale / Clay | **Slate → Schist** |
| Granite | **Gneiss** |
| Coal | **Graphite / Diamond** |

> ⚠️ **Rock cycle:** igneous ⇄ sedimentary ⇄ metamorphic — rocks continuously transform.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ Igneous rocks contain fossils → **no fossils** (fossils are in **sedimentary** rocks).
- ❌ Granite is extrusive/volcanic → granite is **intrusive/plutonic**; **basalt** is the volcanic one.
- ❌ Acidic rocks are denser and darker → **basic** rocks (basalt) are denser/darker; **acidic** (granite) are lighter.
- ❌ The whole core is liquid → **outer core liquid, inner core solid**.
- ❌ SIAL forms the ocean floor → **SIMA** (basaltic) forms ocean floors; **SIAL** (granitic) forms continents.
- ❌ Marble comes from sandstone → **marble from limestone**; **quartzite from sandstone**.
- ❌ Moho separates mantle and core → **Moho = crust–mantle**; **Gutenberg = mantle–core**.
