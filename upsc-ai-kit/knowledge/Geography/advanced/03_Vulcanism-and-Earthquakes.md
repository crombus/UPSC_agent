# PART A — VULCANISM ⭐⭐
✅ **Magma** (semi-molten rock + gases at depth) forcing into/onto the crust. On the surface it is called **lava**.
Temperature rises ~**1°F per 65 ft** of descent (GC Leong).

## Types of lava ✅
| | **Basic lava** | **Acid lava** |
|---|---|---|
| Temp | Hottest (~1,000°C) | Lower melting point, viscous |
| Silica | **Poor** in silica (Fe/Mg rich) | **High** silica |
| Flow | **Highly fluid**, flows far, quiet | **Viscous**, slow, **explosive** |
| Landform | Gentle **shield/lava dome** (Hawaii) | Steep **acid cone**; plugs/spines (Mt. Pelée) |

## Intrusive landforms (magma solidifies **within** crust) ✅
- **Sill** — horizontal sheet along bedding planes · **Dyke** — vertical wall.
- The "**-liths**": **Laccolith** (dome/blister), **Lopolith** (saucer), **Phacolith** (lens at fold crest/trough),
  **Batholith** (huge granite mass — cores of mountains).

## Extrusive landforms (reach the **surface**) ✅
- **Lava plains / basalt plateaux** — e.g., the **Deccan Plateau (NW)**, Iceland, Snake Basin (USA).
- **Cones:** shield (fluid), cinder/ash (explosive), **composite/strato-volcanoes** (alternating lava+ash — Vesuvius,
  Fuji, Etna, Stromboli = "Lighthouse of the Mediterranean").
- **Caldera** — giant collapse depression (e.g., **Lake Toba**, Sumatra); may hold a **caldera lake**.
- **Pyroclasts** = ejected solids (volcanic dust/ash, cinders/lapilli, pumice, bombs).

## Volcano status ✅
**Active · Dormant · Extinct** — but "extinct" can revive (Vesuvius, Krakatau were thought extinct).

> ⚠️ **Modern link — Plate tectonics:** volcanoes cluster at **plate boundaries** — the **Pacific Ring of Fire**
> (subduction), **mid-ocean ridges** (divergent), and **hotspots** (e.g., Hawaii).

# PART B — EARTHQUAKES ⭐⭐ ⚠️ (GC Leong + modern seismology)
- **Focus (hypocentre)** = origin at depth; **Epicentre** = point on surface directly above.
- **Seismic waves:** **P (primary)** — fastest, through solid+liquid; **S (secondary)** — through **solids only**
  (proves the **outer core is liquid**); **L (surface)** — most destructive.
- **Scales:** **Richter** (magnitude/energy, logarithmic) · **Moment Magnitude (Mw)** — modern standard for large quakes ·
  **Mercalli** (intensity/damage). Measured by a **seismograph**.
- ✅ Causes: **tectonic** (faulting — most quakes), volcanic, collapse, anthropogenic (reservoir-induced).
- **Seismic zones of India:** **Zone V** (highest — NE India, Kashmir, Kutch, Andaman) → **Zone II** (lowest).

## Secondary hazards
**Tsunami** (undersea quake — e.g., 2004 Indian Ocean), landslides, liquefaction, fires.

## UPSC Traps
- ❌ Acid lava flows far and quietly → that's **basic lava**; **acid lava is viscous & explosive**.
- ❌ Shield volcanoes are steep & explosive → shield = **gentle, fluid basic lava**; **composite/acid = steep**.
- ❌ Batholith is an extrusive landform → **intrusive** (solidifies at depth).
- ❌ S-waves travel through the liquid core → **S-waves cannot pass liquids** (that's how we know the outer core is liquid).
- ❌ Richter measures damage → Richter = **magnitude/energy**; **Mercalli** = intensity/damage.
- ❌ Epicentre is the underground origin → that's the **focus/hypocentre**; **epicentre is on the surface**.
- ❌ India's only active volcano is Narcondam → **Barren Island** (Narcondam is dormant).

## 📰 CA hooks
- 📰 **Myanmar earthquake (28 Mar 2025, Mw 7.7, Sagaing Fault)** — >3,600 dead; the **Sagaing Fault** marks the
  **India–Eurasia** boundary → tremors felt in **NE India**; classic GS-III disaster-management case.
- 📰 **Barren Island volcano** (Andaman Sea, **India's only active volcano**) **erupted Sept 2025** after a nearby
  earthquake — shows **quake–volcano coupling** on the Indian–Burmese plate arc. (**Narcondam** = dormant.)
- 📰 **Pacific Ring of Fire** eruptions/quakes recur; **NDMA** seismic microzonation & early-warning for Himalayan cities.
