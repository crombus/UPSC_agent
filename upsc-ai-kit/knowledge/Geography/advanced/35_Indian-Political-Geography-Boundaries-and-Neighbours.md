## 1. India's boundary architecture at a glance

✅ Majid Husain's source text treats India as sharing boundaries with **Afghanistan, Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Myanmar and Bangladesh**, while **Sri Lanka** lies across the Palk Strait / Gulf of Mannar. ⚠️ For UPSC today, add **Maldives** as the other high-salience maritime neighbour.

| Neighbour | Nature of adjacency | Static-geography cue |
|---|---|---|
| Afghanistan | ⚠️ Claimed land adjacency in India's official map position via the far north-west | Wakhan / Gilgit-Baltistan context |
| Pakistan | Land + maritime | Indus basin, Thar, Rann, Arabian Sea |
| China | High-mountain frontier | Western, middle and eastern sectors |
| Nepal | Land | Open-border Himalayan foothill interface |
| Bhutan | Land | Himalayan buffer-state geography |
| Bangladesh | Land + riverine / deltaic | Enclaves, chars, long alluvial border |
| Myanmar | Land | Forested hill frontier and Bay of Bengal link |
| Sri Lanka | Maritime | Palk Strait, Gulf of Mannar, Katchatheevu context |
| Maldives | Maritime | Atoll neighbour in central Indian Ocean |

## 2. High-yield boundary lines and what they mean

| Line / arrangement | What it separates | Exam-safe note |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Radcliffe Line (1947) | India and Pakistan in the partition settlement | Applies to the India-Pakistan international boundary framework inherited from partition |
| ✅ McMahon Line (1914 framework) | India-Tibet boundary in the eastern sector | India treats it as the boundary in the eastern India-China sector |
| ✅ Durand Line (1893) | Afghanistan-Pakistan | Not an India-Pakistan line, but matters to India's claimed far north-west context |
| ✅ Ceasefire Line (1949) -> LoC (1972) | Indian- and Pakistani-controlled parts of the former princely state of J&K | Military control line, not international boundary |
| ✅ LAC | India-China zones of actual control | Not mutually demarcated as a formal international boundary |
| ✅ Land Boundary Agreement chain | India-Bangladesh | 1974 agreement, 2011 protocol, 2015 implementation resolved enclaves / undemarcated stretches |

> 🔑 Trap: **International Boundary, LoC and LAC are not interchangeable categories**.

## 3. India-China boundary sectors

✅ D.R. Khullar's extracted discussion divides the India-China boundary into **western, middle and eastern sectors**.

| Sector | Geographic frame | High-yield recall |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Western sector | Ladakh / Karakoram / Aksai Chin space | Watershed logic and dispute-heavy sector |
| ✅ Middle sector | Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand frontier | Relatively narrower and less dispute-heavy than west/east |
| ✅ Eastern sector | Sikkim and Arunachal frontier | McMahon Line chiefly concerns Arunachal/Tibet; Sikkim has a distinct treaty/boundary history |

⚠️ The LAC is best understood as a **military-control reality over an undemarcated high-mountain frontier**, not as a settled legal boundary.

## 5. India-Bangladesh boundary settlement: why geographers care

✅ MEA's 2015 implementation documents note that the Land Boundary Agreement framework resolved **enclaves, adverse possessions and undemarcated stretches**, simplifying an exceptionally complex alluvial-political border.

| Milestone | Geographic significance |
|---|---|
| ✅ 1974 LBA | Basic settlement architecture |
| ✅ 2011 Protocol | Clarified enclaves, adverse possessions, undemarcated parts |
| ✅ 06 June 2015 exchange of letters / 31 July 2015 appointed day | Operational implementation; enclave exchange and boundary simplification |

⚠️ This is a classic UPSC example showing that political geography is not only about conflict; it is also about **cartographic cleaning-up of inherited anomalies**.

## 7. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) and UPSC traps

- ✅ Nepal and Bhutan are Himalayan neighbours, but their border character differs: Nepal has an open-border plains foothill interface, while Bhutan is a steeper mountain-buffer interface.
- ✅ Bangladesh is India's most intricate alluvial / enclave history case.
- ✅ Myanmar is the only land neighbour that physically opens India into mainland South-East Asia.
- ✅ Sri Lanka is separated by a shallow maritime gap, not by a land border.
- ✅ LAC = India-China control line; LoC = India-Pakistan military control line in J&K.

- ❌ Durand Line is India's boundary with Afghanistan -> it is the Afghanistan-Pakistan line, relevant to India's claimed north-west context, not an India-Pakistan line.
- ❌ McMahon Line and LAC are the same everywhere -> the McMahon Line relates to the eastern sector's boundary claim; the LAC is the broader present control reality.
- ❌ Border guarding forces and legal boundaries are the same subject -> the former manage terrain; the latter define sovereignty.
