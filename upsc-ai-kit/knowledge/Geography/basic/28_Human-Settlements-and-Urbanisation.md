## 1. What settlement geography studies

✅ Settlement geography studies the location, form, function and hierarchy of human habitations. ✅ Majid Husain links settlement form to site, situation, relief, water, transport, security and history.

| Core idea | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ Site | Exact ground on which the settlement stands |
| ✅ Situation | Relative location in relation to routes, markets, water and region |
| ✅ Rural settlement | Small, agriculture-linked habitation |
| ✅ Urban settlement | Larger, functionally diversified and non-primary activities dominant |
| ✅ Hierarchy | Village -> town -> city -> metropolis -> conurbation -> megalopolis |

## 2. Rural settlement forms

✅ Indian settlement geography commonly distinguishes compact, semi-compact, hamleted and dispersed settlements in India, and the same logic is widely useful for world human geography.

| Form | Main feature | Typical controls |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Compact / nucleated | Houses close together | Fertile plains, security, water concentration |
| ✅ Semi-compact | Main cluster plus small hamlets | Moderate relief, partial dispersion |
| ✅ Hamleted | Several small clusters | Social segmentation, land pressure |
| ✅ Dispersed | Isolated farmsteads or scattered dwellings | Hills, forests, rugged terrain, large holdings |

> 🔑 Trap: compact does not automatically mean urban; villages can also be highly nucleated.

## 3. Settlement patterns and morphology

✅ Settlement pattern refers to the geometrical shape or arrangement of dwellings. ✅ Linear pattern is especially common along roads, canals, rivers and coasts.

| Pattern | Shape logic | Typical location |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Linear | Houses stretched along one route | Road, canal, embankment, coast |
| ⚠️ Rectangular / grid | Streets intersect roughly at right angles | Planned towns, colonial layouts |
| ⚠️ Radial | Routes converge on a centre | Market or nodal towns |
| ⚠️ Circular | Houses arranged around a central open space | Defensive or cattle-centred layouts |
| ⚠️ Irregular | No obvious geometry | Organic growth over time |

## 4. Urbanisation as a process

✅ Urbanisation means rising share of people living in urban places and expansion of urban functions. ⚠️ It includes not only city growth but also occupational shift away from primary activities, service concentration and transport integration.

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ Urban agglomeration | Built-up continuity involving one town and adjoining outgrowths, or contiguous towns |
| ✅ Metropolis | Large city with regional command functions |
| ✅ Conurbation | Continuous built-up spread formed by merging towns/cities |
| ✅ Megalopolis | Very large urban region with multiple metropolitan nodes |

## 5. Classical models you should know

| Model | Core logic | UPSC use |
|---|---|---|
| ⚠️ Christaller's central place theory | Settlements form a hierarchy of market centres serving surrounding areas | Explains service range and urban hierarchy |
| ⚠️ Rank-size rule | City sizes tend to decline in regular order in a mature urban system | Helps compare balanced and unbalanced urban systems |
| ⚠️ Primate city idea | One city dominates national urban life disproportionately | Useful in regional-development answers |

> 🔑 Trap: central place theory is a simplified model; real relief, politics, coastlines and colonial legacies distort neat hexagonal patterns.

## 6. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) and UPSC traps

- ✅ Settlement pattern and settlement function are not the same thing.
- ✅ Dispersed settlements often reflect terrain, farm size or resource spread.
- ✅ Linear settlements commonly follow transport or water lines.
- ✅ Urbanisation is both a population process and a functional-economic process.
- ✅ Conurbation is a later stage of contiguous urban spread.

- ❌ Every compact settlement is a town -> Many villages are compact.
- ❌ Urbanisation only means more buildings -> It also means occupational and functional change.
- ❌ A metropolis and a conurbation are identical -> A metropolis may be one city; a conurbation is merged built-up spread.
