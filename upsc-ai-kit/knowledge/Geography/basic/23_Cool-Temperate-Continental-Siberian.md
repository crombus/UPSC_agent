## 1. What & Where (G.C. Leong)

✅ The Cool Temperate Continental (Siberian) climate is experienced **only in the Northern Hemisphere**,
where the continents in high latitudes have a **broad east–west spread**. ✅ On its **poleward side** it
merges into the **Arctic tundra** of Canada and Eurasia (~Arctic Circle); ✅ **southwards** it becomes
less severe and fades into the **temperate Steppe** climate.

> 🔑 Trap: There is **no Siberian-type climate in the Southern Hemisphere** — the continents there are
> too narrow at those latitudes; only **mountain uplands** (S. Chile, NZ, Tasmania, SE Australia) carry
> similar coniferous forest.

## 2. Climate

| Element | Character |
|---|---|
| ✅ Winter | **Bitterly cold, long** ("sub-Arctic"); very large annual range |
| ✅ Summer | Short, cool; brief warm growing season |
| ✅ Rainfall | Low to moderate, generally with a summer maximum; frontal and convectional sources vary |

## 3. Natural vegetation — the Taiga

✅ The predominant vegetation is **evergreen coniferous forest**, in a **great continuous belt across
North America, Europe and Asia**. ✅ The greatest single band is the **taiga** (Russian for coniferous
forest) in **Siberia**; in Europe, **Sweden and Finland** share this climate/forest. ⚠️ Softwood conifers
(pine, spruce, fir, larch) — conical shape, needle leaves, shallow roots — are adapted to cold and snow.

## 4. Economic value

- ⚠️ The taiga is the world's greatest **softwood** source → timber, **pulp & paper**, matches, furniture.
- ⚠️ Also **fur trapping**, and mining/energy in Siberia and Canada.

## 5. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Siberian type occurs **only in the Northern Hemisphere** (broad continents; no S-hemisphere land).
- ✅ Poleward → **tundra**; equatorward → **Steppe**.
- ✅ Vegetation = **taiga / boreal coniferous (softwood)** forest; **taiga = Russian for coniferous forest**.
- ✅ **Bitterly cold long winter**, short cool summer, **light summer rain**.
- ✅ Shared in Europe by **Sweden & Finland**; belt runs across N. America, Europe, Asia.

## 6. UPSC Traps

- ❌ Siberian type occurs in both hemispheres → **Northern Hemisphere only**.
- ❌ Taiga is deciduous broadleaf forest → it is **evergreen coniferous (softwood)**.
- ❌ It has a summer-dry, winter-rain regime → **low rainfall, light summer maximum**.

## 7. 📰 Current link

📰 **Taiga wildfire and permafrost thaw:** warming lengthens fire seasons and thaws frozen ground,
altering carbon storage, hydrology and infrastructure. Avoid one regional warming, fire or carbon-stock
number unless tied to a named monitoring period.

## 8. Mains angles

- Why the **taiga is confined to the Northern Hemisphere** — the interplay of continentality and land
  distribution.
- Boreal forests and permafrost as a **carbon sink turning into a source** — implications for global
  climate targets.
