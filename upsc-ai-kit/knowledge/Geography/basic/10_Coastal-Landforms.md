## 1. Marine erosion and deposition: the basic logic

✅ Waves shape coasts through **erosion, transport and deposition**. ✅ Marine erosion uses hydraulic action/quarrying, abrasion/corrasion, attrition, solution, salt weathering and alternate wetting and drying. ✅ Sediment moved by waves and longshore drift builds beaches, spits, bars, lagoons and tombolos.

> 🔑 Trap: Coastal landforms are not random labels — first identify **erosional vs depositional**, then link the landform to wave energy, sediment supply and relative sea-level/land movement.

## 2. Erosional landforms

| Landform | Formation | Recognition point |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Sea cliff | Wave attack undercuts a steep coast | Cliff retreats landward |
| ✅ Wave-cut notch/platform | Notch cut at cliff foot; platform exposed as cliff retreats | Gently seaward-sloping rock bench |
| ✅ Cave | Weakness/joint in headland enlarged by wave attack | First stage in headland sequence |
| ✅ Arch | Caves enlarge and meet through a headland | Opening through headland |
| ✅ Stack | Arch roof collapses | Isolated pillar offshore |
| ✅ Stump | Stack reduced by further erosion | Low remnant visible near sea level |
| ✅ Geo / blowhole | Cave roof/cavity collapse or vertical opening | Cleft/vertical spray hole |

## 3. Depositional landforms

| Landform | Formation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Beach | Accumulation of sand/shingle along shore | Sloping depositional strip |
| ✅ Spit | Longshore drift extends a ridge from land into water | Narrow projecting ridge |
| ✅ Bar | Spit/ridge grows across bay or inlet | May enclose lagoon |
| ✅ Lagoon | Coastal water enclosed behind bar/spit | Brackish/coastal lake possible |
| ✅ Tombolo | Spit/bar connects island to mainland | Island tied to shore |

## 4. Coast types: relative sea-level change

| Coast type | Process | Landforms / examples in theory |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Submergent coast | Land sinks or sea level rises, drowning valleys | Ria, fjord, Dalmatian, estuarine coast |
| ✅ Ria | Drowned river valley | Branching inlet pattern |
| ✅ Fjord | Drowned glacial trough | Deep, steep-sided inlet |
| ✅ Dalmatian coast | Drowned ridges parallel to coast | Long islands/channels parallel to shore |
| ✅ Emergent coast | Land uplift or sea-level fall | Raised beaches, straight cliffed coasts, deeper offshore water |

## 5. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) + traps

- ✅ Erosional sequence: **cave → arch → stack → stump**.
- ✅ Wave-cut platform widens as the cliff retreats.
- ✅ Longshore drift builds **spits, bars and tombolos**.
- ✅ Tombolo ties an island to the mainland.
- ✅ Submergent: **ria = drowned river valley**, **fjord = drowned glacial trough**, **Dalmatian = drowned parallel ridges**.
- ✅ Emergent coast produces raised beaches and straight cliffed coasts.

> 🔑 Trap: A **fjord** is not a drowned river valley; it is a drowned **glacial trough**. A drowned river valley is a **ria**.

### Trap list

- ❌ Stack forms before arch → Stack forms **after arch collapse**.
- ❌ Tombolo separates an island → Tombolo **connects** an island to the mainland.
- ❌ Lagoon is always freshwater → Coastal lagoons may be **brackish** because of sea-water and river-water mixing.
- ❌ Bars and spits are erosional → They are **depositional** landforms.
- ❌ Emergent and submergent coasts are wave-energy classes → They are based on **relative land/sea-level change**.

## 6. 📰 Current link

### CA Anchor — India's Coastal Erosion (NCCR)

📰 **News trigger (NCCR assessment period 1990–2018):** about **33.6%** of India's mainland coastline
was classified as eroding. State percentages belong to that mapping period and should not be presented
as timeless current rates.

✅ Coastal erosion is wave attack outpacing sediment supply — accelerated by sea-level rise, dams cutting sediment, mangrove loss and hard structures that disrupt longshore drift.

- ✅ NCCR mapping: ~33.6% eroding, ~26.9% accreting, ~39.6% stable.
- ✅ Drivers: sea-level rise, cyclones, reduced sediment (river dams), mangrove loss, and coastal structures (ports/seawalls) that interrupt longshore drift.
- ✅ Responses: shoreline management plans, beach nourishment, mangrove/bio-shields.

### Current-link Must-Know Facts

- ✅ NCCR: ~33.6% of India's coast eroding.
- ✅ In the **1990–2018 NCCR assessment**, West Bengal and Puducherry had especially high eroding shares.
- ✅ Cause link: sea-level rise + sediment starvation + hard structures.

### Current-link Traps

- ❌ Seawalls always stop coastal erosion everywhere → Hard structures often shift erosion downdrift by disrupting longshore drift.
