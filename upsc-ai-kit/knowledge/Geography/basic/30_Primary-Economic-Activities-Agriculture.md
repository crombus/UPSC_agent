## 1. What agricultural geography studies

✅ Agriculture is the leading **primary economic activity** because it draws directly on land, water, climate, soil and labour. ✅ In geography, the key question is not only "what is grown?" but **where, why there, and with what seasonal logic?**

⚠️ UPSC usually tests agriculture through **location + climate + labour intensity + market linkages + crop seasonality**, not through agronomy alone.

## 2. Main controls of agriculture

| Control | Geographic role | High-yield example |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Climate | Temperature, rainfall and length of growing season decide crop choice | Rice in humid monsoon Asia; wheat in temperate grasslands |
| ✅ Relief | Slopes, plains and altitude affect mechanisation and terraces | Terrace rice on hill slopes; large mechanised farms on plains |
| ✅ Soil | Fertility, texture and moisture-holding matter | Alluvial/deltaic lands for rice; chernozem for wheat |
| ✅ Water supply | Irrigation reduces rainfall uncertainty | Canal and tube-well farming in dry or seasonal climates |
| ✅ Labour | Labour-intensive crops cluster where labour is abundant | Wet-rice and plantation economies |
| ✅ Market & transport | Perishability and freight costs shape land use | Dairy/vegetables near cities; plantation crops near ports |

> 🔑 Trap: Fertile soil alone does not create an agricultural region; climate, water, labour and market access must align.

## 3. Major agricultural systems

| System | Core characteristics | Typical world setting |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Shifting cultivation | Temporary clearing, short cultivation, long fallow | Tropical forest margins in humid low latitudes |
| ✅ Subsistence wet-rice farming | Small holdings, dense labour, high water need | Monsoon Asia river valleys and deltas |
| ✅ Nomadic herding / pastoralism | Mobility over sparse grass/shrub lands | Arid and semi-arid belts |
| ✅ Mixed farming | Crops + livestock integrated on same farm | Temperate Europe and North American farming belts |
| ✅ Mediterranean agriculture | Winter rain, summer drought; fruits, vines, olives | Mediterranean basin, California, Chile, SW Australia |
| ✅ Plantation agriculture | Estate scale, monoculture, export orientation | Tropical colonies/coastal humid zones |
| ✅ Commercial grain farming | Large mechanised farms, low labour density | Prairies, Pampas, Steppes, Downs |
| ✅ Market gardening / dairy | Perishable products for urban demand | Around metropolitan belts |

✅ G.C. Leong's human-geography passages strongly distinguish **wet-rice**, **plantation**, **Mediterranean**, **mixed farming** and **commercial grain farming** by labour use, climate and market orientation.

## 4. Von Thunen model: why distance matters

✅ Von Thunen explained how **transport cost and perishability** organise agricultural land use around a market when physical conditions are assumed equal.

    [Central Market]
         |
    Ring 1 -> Dairy / vegetables / flowers
    Ring 2 -> Forest / fuelwood (classical model)
    Ring 3 -> Field crops / grain
    Ring 4 -> Ranching / extensive grazing

⚠️ Modern relevance: refrigeration, highways and global trade weaken the exact rings, but the core logic still matters - **high-value perishables stay closer to markets unless cold-chain infrastructure compensates**.

| Model idea | Why it matters in exams |
|---|---|
| ✅ Distance from market raises freight cost | Explains dairy/vegetable belts near cities |
| ✅ Land rent falls outward from market | Inner rings favour intensive, high-value uses |
| ⚠️ Technology can stretch the rings | Air cargo, cold chain and expressways change old distance penalties |

> 🔑 Trap: Von Thunen is a **location model**, not a climate classification.

## 5. Major world agricultural regions

| Region | Dominant type | Core spatial logic |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Monsoon Asia | Wet-rice subsistence / intensive farming | High population density + summer rain + alluvial plains |
| ✅ Eurasian Steppe & North American Prairies | Commercial grain farming | Vast plains, mechanisation, temperate climate |
| ✅ Mediterranean margins | Horticulture, vines, olives, citrus | Winter rain and dry summer |
| ✅ Tropical plantation belts | Tea, coffee, cocoa, rubber, sugarcane | Humid tropics + colonial port orientation |
| ✅ Savanna and semi-arid belts | Livestock and drought-resistant crops | Low rainfall reliability |
| ✅ Urban-industrial belts | Market gardening and dairy | Near dense consumer markets |

⚠️ Good map habit: pair each agricultural region with one **climate type**, one **signature crop**, and one **settlement/labour pattern**.

## 6. Agricultural change: from subsistence to commercialisation

✅ D.R. Khullar's agriculture discussion highlights irrigation, improved seeds, fertilisers, roads and market access as key forces that alter cropping patterns and intensity. ⚠️ Globally, the broad transition is from **subsistence -> surplus -> commercial specialisation -> agro-processing linkages**.

| Driver | Geographic consequence |
|---|---|
| ✅ Irrigation | Expands cultivation into seasonal/dry belts |
| ✅ HYV / inputs | Raises yield, encourages double cropping |
| ✅ Roads / ports | Connects rural regions to export and urban demand |
| ⚠️ Contract farming / agribusiness | Strengthens value-chain clustering |
| ⚠️ Cold chain | Permits distant marketing of perishables |

## 7. Actor / institution lens

| Actor / institution | Geographic role |
|---|---|
| ⚠️ National agriculture ministries | Shape crop policy, irrigation support and food-security priorities |
| ⚠️ Irrigation and river-valley agencies | Expand or limit cultivation by controlling water |
| ⚠️ Cooperatives / mandis / commodity boards | Connect farmers to markets and procurement channels |
| ⚠️ FAO and global food-data systems | Help compare regions through production and food-security indicators |

## 8. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) and UPSC traps

- ✅ Shifting cultivation uses **temporary plots and fallow rotation**; it is not the same as plantation farming.
- ✅ Plantation agriculture is **large-scale, monocultural and export-oriented**.
- ✅ Mixed farming integrates **crop cultivation and livestock**.
- ✅ Mediterranean farming specialises in **fruits, vines, olives and winter crops**, not tropical wet rice.
- ✅ Commercial grain farming depends on **large fields and mechanisation**.
- ✅ Von Thunen links agricultural land use with **distance from market and transport cost**.
- ✅ Market gardening and dairy are usually **peri-urban** because products are perishable.

> 🔑 Trap: Intensive farming does not always mean mechanised farming; wet-rice farming is highly intensive but often labour-intensive rather than machine-intensive.

- ❌ Plantation farming is subsistence agriculture -> It is **commercial/export agriculture**.
- ❌ Rice everywhere requires identical water conditions -> Upland rice, irrigated rice and deltaic rice differ sharply by terrain and water control.
- ❌ All large farms are commercial grain farms -> Ranching, plantation and corporate horticulture are different systems.

## 9. PYQ / exam linkage

- ⚠️ Recurring Prelims theme: distinguish **shifting vs plantation vs mixed vs commercial grain**.
- ⚠️ Recurring Prelims theme: link **crop with climate region** - wheat/steppe, olives/Mediterranean, rice/monsoon Asia.
- ⚠️ Recurring GS-I theme: explain how **relief, irrigation, markets and technology** transform agriculture from subsistence to commercial systems.
- ⚠️ Recurring GS-III theme: connect crop geography with **food security, procurement and climate stress**.
