## 1. Islands: basic classification

✅ Islands are classed as **continental**, **oceanic/volcanic** or **coral**. ✅ Continental islands are detached from continental landmasses. ✅ Oceanic islands commonly rise from deep ocean floors through volcanism. ✅ Coral islands are low islands built from reef material by coral organisms.

| Island type | Origin | Examples from notes |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Continental | Detached from a landmass / continental shelf | British Isles, Newfoundland |
| ✅ Oceanic (volcanic) | Volcanic islands rising from deep ocean | Hawaii; Barren Island (India) |
| ✅ Coral | Built by coral reef growth and debris | Maldives, Laccadives/Lakshadweep, Marshall Islands |

## 2. Conditions for coral growth

✅ Coral reefs are built by tiny coral animals/polyps whose limestone skeletons accumulate into reefs. ✅ Corals host symbiotic algae called **zooxanthellae**. ✅ Coral growth needs **warm tropical water**, **shallow sunlit depth**, **clear sediment-free water** and **normal salinity**.

| Requirement | UPSC meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ Warm water | About **23–25 C** in the source notes |
| ✅ Shallow water | Sunlight must reach the algae/coral system |
| ✅ Clear water | Muddy/sediment-rich water blocks light and smothers coral |
| ✅ Saline water | Normal marine salinity; not fresh or heavily diluted water |
| ✅ Tropical belt | Reefs cluster broadly between about **30 N–30 S** |

> 🔑 Trap: Coral reefs are not deep-sea features; they are warm, shallow, clear-water tropical landforms.

## 3. Reef types and Darwin's sequence

| Reef type | Position/form | Recognition point |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Fringing reef | Attached to shore | No lagoon or only a narrow lagoon |
| ✅ Barrier reef | Offshore reef separated by lagoon | Wide lagoon between reef and land; Great Barrier Reef example in source table |
| ✅ Atoll | Ring-shaped reef enclosing lagoon | Common in Maldives-type coral islands |

✅ Darwin's subsidence theory explains reef evolution as **fringing reef → barrier reef → atoll** while a volcanic island slowly subsides and coral grows upward/outward to keep pace. ✅ Daly's theory stresses sea-level/glacial change; the source notes that borings showing basalt at depth support Darwin, while a combination explains reefs best.

## 4. Must-Know Facts (Prelims)

- ✅ Island types: continental, oceanic/volcanic and coral.
- ✅ Coral polyps build limestone skeletons; zooxanthellae are symbiotic algae.
- ✅ Coral needs warm (~23–25 C), shallow, clear, sediment-free saline water.
- ✅ Reef sequence/types: fringing, barrier and atoll.
- ✅ Darwin's **subsidence** theory: fringing → barrier → atoll as island sinks.
- ✅ Atoll = ring reef enclosing a lagoon.
- ✅ Daly's theory stresses sea-level/glacial change; borings at depth support Darwin's subsidence mechanism.

## 5. UPSC Traps

- ❌ Corals thrive in cold, deep, muddy water → Corals need **warm, shallow, clear, sediment-free saline water**.
- ❌ Atoll forms before barrier reef → Darwin sequence is **fringing → barrier → atoll**.
- ❌ Coral islands are high and rugged → Coral islands are generally **low**; volcanic islands are high.
- ❌ Zooxanthellae are coral skeletons → Zooxanthellae are **symbiotic algae** associated with coral polyps.
- ❌ All islands are continental fragments → Islands may be continental, oceanic/volcanic or coral.

## 6. 📰 Current link

### CA Anchor — Fourth Global Coral Bleaching Event (2024)

📰 **News trigger:** In 2024 NOAA declared the fourth global coral bleaching event — warm seas (global warming + El Nino) bleached reefs worldwide, including India's Lakshadweep and Gulf of Mannar.

✅ Bleaching is heat stress made visible: corals expel their zooxanthellae, turn white and, if stress persists, die — directly linking reef geomorphology to climate change.

- ✅ Bleaching = expulsion of symbiotic zooxanthellae under heat stress; the coral whitens and may die.
- ✅ Trigger 2024: record sea-surface temperatures (warming + El Nino); the 4th global event (after 1998, 2010, 2015–17).
- ✅ Indian hotspots hit: Lakshadweep (severe), Gulf of Mannar (India's first marine biosphere reserve, ~117 coral species).

### Current-link Must-Know Facts

- ✅ 2024 = 4th global bleaching event (NOAA); after 1998, 2010, 2015–17.
- ✅ Cause: high SST (warming + El Nino) → zooxanthellae expelled.
- ✅ India hit: Lakshadweep, Gulf of Mannar, Andaman & Nicobar.

### Current-link Traps

- ❌ Bleached corals are already dead → Bleaching is stress (algae expelled); corals can **recover** if temperatures fall soon enough.
