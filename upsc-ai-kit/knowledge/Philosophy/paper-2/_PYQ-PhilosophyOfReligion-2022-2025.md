**Takeaway:** Like Section A, EVERY syllabus item is asked ≥1/year (Religion-without-God the only near-miss, skipped 2022). **Soul (Immortality/Rebirth/Liberation)** is the single highest-yield item — 2 questions EVERY year. Proofs for God, Notions of God, and Religious Language are the next tier. Section B is the most predictable half of the entire optional.

### The 4 question archetypes ⚠️
1. **Statement/quote critique** [dominant] — "All evil is either sin or punishment for sin" (Augustine, 2023), "Immortality of Soul is a necessary postulate for rebirth" (2022), "God must have some physical manifestation" (2024), "unquestionable acceptance of one Absolute Truth → exclusivism" (2022).
2. **Named-thinker exposition + critique** — Aquinas' Five Ways (2022), Hume on Design argument (2025), Rāmānuja on God–Self (2022), Tillich on symbol (2023), Wittgenstein (2023), Braithwaite (2024), William James (2022), Radhakrishnan (2025), Vivekananda (2025), Nietzsche (2025).
3. **Indian–Western bridge** — the syllabus itself demands "Indian and Western"; questions pair them: Nyāya proofs vs Western proofs, Advaita jīvanmukti vs Western liberation, Cārvāka/Buddhism critique of God.
4. **Applied/conceptual-necessity** — "Is immortality necessary for rebirth?", "Is soul-immortality necessary for a robust conception of liberation?", "Can there be religion without morality?", "Must normative principles reference God?"

### Highest-yield clusters (must master first) ⚠️
- **Soul — Immortality / Rebirth / Liberation**: 2/year, every year. Angles: Plato's a priori proofs (2023), Buddhism's anātman challenge (2022), Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika apavarga (2024), Advaita jīvanmukti (2025), rebirth in Karma theory (2023), Gītā on immortality-rebirth link (2025), immortality+rebirth as precondition for mokṣa (2024). → build one master node linking Indian (Vedānta/Nyāya/Buddhist/Jain) + Western (Plato) views.
- **Proofs for the Existence of God (Indian + Western)**: Western — Aquinas' Five Ways (2022), Ontological (2024), Design/Hume (2025), a priori vs a posteriori distinction (2022). Indian — Nyāya proofs (2023 + 2025). → tabulate cosmological/teleological/ontological/moral + Nyāya's Īśvara-anumāna, with standard critiques (Hume, Kant).
- **Notions of God**: personalistic vs impersonalistic (2023), immanent–transcendent (2023), Spinoza's substance-God (2024), Rāmānuja's God–Self (2022), "God needs physical manifestation" (2024). → cover Saguṇa/Nirguṇa + Western theism/pantheism/deism.
- **Religious Language**: symbolic (2025), analogical (2024), cognitivist vs non-cognitivist (2022, 2024), Tillich (2023), Wittgenstein (2023), Advaitic anirvachanīyatā (2025). → master Aquinas (analogy), Tillich (symbol), Braithwaite/Wittgenstein/Ayer (non-cognitivism), plus the Indian anirvachanīya angle.
- **Problem of Evil**: exactly 1/year — Augustine (2023), human-agent responsibility (2022), God-concept dependence (2024), Process Theodicy (2025). → learn logical vs evidential problem + theodicies (Augustinian, Irenaean, Process, karma-based).

### Cross-paper & intra-paper bridges ⚠️
- **Section B ↔ Paper-I Indian**: Cārvāka (2025), Buddhism (2022, 2024), Jainism soul/bondage (2022), Advaita Brahman (2022), Nyāya proofs (2023, 2025) — the SAME doctrines recur; a Paper-I Indian note doubles as Section-B ammunition.
- **Section B ↔ Paper-I Western**: Spinoza's God (2024) ↔ Rationalism; Plato's soul-proofs (2023) ↔ Plato item; Nietzsche (2025) ↔ existentialist milieu.
- **Religion & Morality ↔ Section A Secularism/Ethics** and **Religion without God ↔ Humanism** (Section A item 6).
