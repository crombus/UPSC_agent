# SOCIAL & POLITICAL IDEALS — Equality, Justice, Liberty — Paper-II Sec-A, Item 1

> **Syllabus:** Equality, Justice, Liberty.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (O.P. Gauba, *Introduction to Political Theory*; Rawls, *A Theory of Justice*; Berlin; Mill) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis · 📰 = current example
> **Nature:** The **triad of foundational ideals** — every Paper-II Sec-A answer draws on them. **Highest-recurring cluster (6 Qs 2022–25):** J.S. Mill on equality (2025), Rawls' justice (2023), liberty–equality critique (2024), "equality & liberty inadequate without justice" (2025). Must know each ideal *individually* AND their *interrelation/tension*.

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
        THREE IDEALS — in permanent creative tension
   LIBERTY ◄──────── tension ────────► EQUALITY
   (freedom to act)                    (sameness of status/worth)
        \                                   /
         \        JUSTICE reconciles       /
          \      (gives each their due,   /
           ▼      balances the two)      ▼
                    JUSTICE
        = fairness (Rawls) / due proportion (Aristotle)
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — "L-E-J: Liberty & Equality quarrel, Justice referees."** Max liberty → inequality (the strong dominate); max equality → loss of liberty (levelling coercion). **Justice is the balancing ideal.**

---

## 1. LIBERTY ✅
- **Meaning:** absence of constraint + the positive capacity for self-realisation.
- **Negative vs Positive liberty (Isaiah Berlin, *Two Concepts of Liberty*):**
  - **Negative** — *freedom FROM* interference; "how large is the area within which I am left alone?" (Mill, Hobbes, Hayek). ✅
  - **Positive** — *freedom TO* be one's own master, self-realisation, rational self-direction (Rousseau, Hegel, T.H. Green). Risk: can be perverted into coercion ("forcing men to be free"). ✅
- **J.S. Mill (*On Liberty*):** the **Harm Principle** — the only ground for restraining an individual's liberty is to prevent **harm to others**; over his own body & mind the individual is sovereign. Defends liberty of thought, expression, tastes, association. **Self-regarding vs other-regarding acts.** ✅

---

## 2. EQUALITY ✅
- **Meaning:** all persons are of **equal worth** and entitled to equal consideration; *not* uniformity.
- **Kinds:** natural/moral, civil (rule of law), political (one-person-one-vote), social (no caste/class hierarchy), **economic** (the contested one).
- **Formal vs Substantive:** formal = equal treatment/equality before law; substantive = equality of *outcome/opportunity* requiring positive state action (affirmative action, reservation). ✅
- **J.S. Mill on equality (2025 PYQ):** progressive liberal — equal rights, women's equality (*The Subjection of Women*), utilitarian equal-consideration ("each to count for one"), yet wary of levelling that crushes individuality. ⚠️
- **Equality ≠ Sameness:** treating unequals equally can itself be unjust → grounds **equity** (differential treatment to correct disadvantage).

---

## 3. JUSTICE ✅
- **Aristotle:** justice = giving each their **due** — *distributive* (proportionate to merit) + *corrective/rectificatory* (restoring balance). Treat equals equally, unequals unequally *in proportion*. ✅
- **Rawls — Justice as Fairness (2023 PYQ):** principles chosen behind a **"veil of ignorance"** in the **original position** (no one knows their class, talents, gender). Two principles: ✅
  1. **Equal basic liberties** for all (liberty principle — lexically prior).
  2. **Difference Principle** — social/economic inequalities are just only if they (a) benefit the **least advantaged** and (b) attach to positions open under **fair equality of opportunity**.
- **Nozick's libertarian reply:** justice = **entitlement** (just acquisition + just transfer); redistribution violates rights. ⚠️
- **Amartya Sen (*Idea of Justice*):** shift from ideal institutions to **capabilities** & actual realisations (*nyaya* over *niti*). 📰 India-relevant.
- **Kinds:** social, economic, political, legal.

---

## 4. THE INTERRELATION — the exam's favourite angle ⚠️
| Pair | Tension | Reconciliation |
|---|---|---|
| Liberty ↔ Equality | unrestricted liberty breeds inequality; enforced equality curbs liberty | Rawls: equal liberty first, then regulated inequality (difference principle) |
| Equality ↔ Justice | equal treatment of unequals is unjust | justice as *proportion/equity* corrects it |
| Liberty ↔ Justice | absolute liberty (e.g. property) can produce injustice | just constraints (harm principle, fair opportunity) |

> 🔑 **"Equality & liberty are inadequate without justice" (2025 PYQ):** liberty alone → survival-of-fittest; equality alone → mechanical levelling; **only justice supplies the principle that decides *which* liberties and *which* equalities are legitimate.** Justice is the master-ideal that orders the other two.

## 7. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 7.1 — "How are equality & liberty inadequate without justice?" (2025, 20m)
```
Intro : the triad in tension; justice as the ordering master-ideal.
B1    : liberty alone → Tawney's pike/minnow inequality (self-regarding excess).
B2    : equality alone → mechanical levelling, loss of liberty/individuality (Mill's warning).
Link  : Rawls — equal liberties + difference principle shows justice ORDERS both.
Assess: Nozick's entitlement counter (justice ≠ patterned redistribution); Sen's capabilities.
Concl : justice supplies the principle deciding which liberties/equalities are legitimate.
```

### 7.2 — "Explain Rawls' theory of justice as fairness." (2023, 10m)
```
Intro : justice = fairness; principles chosen in the original position.
Body  : veil of ignorance → (1) equal basic liberties (prior); (2) difference principle
        (inequality just only if it benefits the least advantaged + fair equality of opportunity).
Crit  : Nozick (rights/entitlement), communitarians (abstract unencumbered self), Sen (capabilities).
Concl : a deontological liberal-egalitarian reconciliation of liberty and equality.
```
