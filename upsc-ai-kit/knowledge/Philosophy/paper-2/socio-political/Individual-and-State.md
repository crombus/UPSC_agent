# INDIVIDUAL & STATE — Rights, Duties, Accountability — Paper-II Sec-A, Item 3

> **Syllabus:** Rights; Duties and Accountability.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Gauba; Laski, *Grammar of Politics*; T.H. Green; Hohfeld) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** The relationship of the **individual to the state** — what the individual is *owed* (rights), what she *owes* (duties), and how power is held answerable (accountability). Steady yield (4 Qs 2022–25): "duties are obligation, rights are entitlement — no necessary connection?" (2025), "duty & accountability over rights?" (2023), "unconditional rights → anarchy?" (2022).

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
        INDIVIDUAL ⇄ STATE  (a reciprocal, correlative bond)
   RIGHTS ──────────── correlativity ──────────── DUTIES
   (entitlements/claims)                          (obligations)
        │  my right = your duty (Hohfeld)              │
        ▼                                              ▼
   the STATE guarantees rights ◄── ACCOUNTABILITY ──► the STATE
                                    (power must answer:
                                     rule of law, elections,
                                     judiciary, transparency)
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — "R-D-A: Rights & Duties are two sides of ONE coin; Accountability keeps the coin honest."** No right without a correlative duty; no legitimate power without accountability.

---

## 1. RIGHTS ✅
- **Definition:** rights = **claims/entitlements** recognised by society as necessary for the individual's development. Laski: *"rights are those conditions of social life without which no man can seek to be his best self."* ✅
- **Theories of rights:**
  - **Natural rights** (Locke) — life, liberty, property; pre-social, inalienable.
  - **Legal/positive rights** — created & enforced by the state (Bentham: natural rights = *"nonsense upon stilts"*).
  - **Moral rights** — grounded in ethics, may lack legal force.
  - **Human rights** — universal, by virtue of being human (UDHR). 📰
- **Hohfeld's analysis:** "right" = a family of **claim / liberty / power / immunity**, each with a correlative (duty / no-right / liability / disability). ⚠️
- **Negative vs positive rights:** freedom *from* interference vs entitlements *to* provision (welfare rights).

---

## 2. DUTIES ✅
- **Definition:** duties = **obligations** the individual owes to others and to the state; the counterpart of rights.
- **Legal duties** (enforceable) vs **moral duties** (conscience). In India: **Fundamental Duties** (Art 51A, 42nd Amendment). 📰
- **The correlativity thesis:** *every right entails a corresponding duty* — (a) my right imposes a duty of respect on others; (b) my enjoyment of rights entails my duty to discharge social obligations. ✅
- **The 2025 challenge** — "duties are obligation, rights are entitlement, so **no necessary connection**": the essay must *defend correlativity* — rights are meaningless unless others recognise the duty to honour them; a community of pure entitlement-claimants with no duties self-destructs. ⚠️

---

## 3. ACCOUNTABILITY ✅
- **Definition:** the principle that those who **exercise power must answer** for its use — legally, politically, and morally.
- **Mechanisms:** rule of law, free elections, an independent judiciary, legislative oversight, a free press, **RTI/transparency**, ombudsman/Lokpal. 📰
- **Why it matters:** rights are only as secure as the accountability of the power that guarantees them; unaccountable sovereignty → tyranny (links to Laski's critique). ✅
- **Individual accountability too:** citizens are accountable for discharging duties and for the health of democratic institutions.

---

## 4. THE INDIVIDUAL–STATE RELATIONSHIP — theories ⚠️
| View | Individual–State relation | Rights basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Liberal/individualist** (Locke, Mill) | state is a means; individual & rights primary | natural/negative rights, limited state |
| **Idealist** (Hegel, Green, Bosanquet) | state is an ethical whole; freedom *through* the state | rights = conditions of self-realisation |
| **Marxist** | state = instrument of class rule; "rights" mask exploitation | rights realised only in classless society |
| **Anarchist** | state itself illegitimate | rights without a coercive state |
| **Gandhian** | state authority secondary to conscience/duty (swaraj) | duty (*svadharma*)-centric; rights follow duties |

> 🔑 **Gandhi's inversion:** *"The true source of rights is duty. If we all discharge our duties, rights will not be far to seek."* A powerful line for any rights-vs-duties question.

## 7. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 7.1 — "'Duties are obligation, rights are entitlement; therefore no necessary connection.' Agree?" (2025, 15m)
```
Intro : state the claim; thesis — DISAGREE, rights & duties are correlative.
B1    : Hohfeld/Laski — my right = another's duty; rights presuppose recognition.
B2    : enjoyment of rights entails discharge of social duties; pure entitlement w/o duty self-destructs.
Link  : Gandhi — "true source of rights is duty"; Fundamental Rights ↔ Fundamental Duties (Art 51A).
Assess: concede a conceptual/analytic distinction, but deny practical/moral separability.
Concl : rights and duties are two faces of one social bond.
```

### 7.2 — "Does the idea of unconditional rights lead to anarchy?" (2022, 15m)
```
Intro : "unconditional/absolute right" — a right with no limit or correlative duty.
Body  : rights are SOCIAL & CONDITIONAL — bounded by others' equal rights + duties (Mill's harm principle);
        absolute rights would collide → chaos; hence rights are always regulated by the state.
Crit  : but over-conditioning rights → state overreach; balance via rule of law + accountability.
Concl : rights are conditioned, not unconditional — this prevents, not causes, anarchy.
```
