## 1. ANARCHISM ✅
- **Core:** all forms of **coercive authority — especially the state — are illegitimate, unnecessary and harmful**; social order can arise from **voluntary cooperation**. ✅
- **"All States, always & everywhere, are illegitimate and unjust" (2023 PYQ):** the state monopolises violence, coerces, and serves the powerful; even democratic states rest on domination. ⚠️
- **Strands:** collectivist/communist (**Bakunin, Kropotkin** — mutual aid), individualist (Stirner), **mutualism** (Proudhon — *"property is theft"*).
- **Critique:** utopian about human nature; a stateless order risks the strong dominating; no mechanism for large-scale coordination/justice. ⚠️
- **Gandhian link:** Gandhi's ideal of **"enlightened anarchy"** — the state that governs least, self-rule (swaraj), non-violence. ✅

---

## 2. SOCIALISM ✅
- **Core:** **social/collective ownership** of the means of production + distribution according to need/contribution; subordinates private profit to **social welfare & equality**. ✅
- **Varieties:** utopian (Owen, Fourier, Saint-Simon), **democratic/Fabian** (gradual, parliamentary — Britain), state socialism, guild socialism, market socialism.
- **Socialism vs Communism (2024 PYQ):** ⚠️
  - **Socialism** — transitional; workers own production; distribution *"to each according to his contribution/work"*; may retain a state & markets; often gradual/democratic.
  - **Communism** — the *final* stateless, classless stage; *"from each according to his ability, to each according to his needs"*; no private property, no state. ✅

---

## 3. MARXISM ✅ (master from 4 angles)

### (a) Dialectical & Historical Materialism (2023 PYQ)
- **Dialectical materialism:** reality is **matter** in dialectical motion (Hegel's dialectic *inverted* — material, not ideal). Thesis–antithesis–synthesis via **contradiction**. ✅
- **Historical materialism:** the **mode of production (economic base)** determines the **superstructure** (law, religion, ideology). History = a succession of modes (primitive→slave→feudal→capitalist→socialist→communist) driven by **class struggle**. *"The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles."* ✅

### (b) Alienation (2024 PYQ)
- Under capitalism the worker is **alienated** (Marx, *1844 Manuscripts*) in **4 ways**: from the **product** (owned by another), from the **process/labour** (mechanical, coerced), from his **species-being** (creative essence), and from **fellow workers** (competition). ✅

### (c) Class struggle → Revolution → Communism
- Capitalism's contradictions (falling profit, immiseration, over-production) sharpen the proletariat–bourgeoisie conflict → **revolution** → **dictatorship of the proletariat** (transitional) → **the state withers away** → **classless, stateless communism**. ✅

### (d) Equity vs Equality (2022 PYQ)
- Marx rejects *bourgeois formal* equality (equal legal rights masking economic exploitation). True aim = **equity**: distribution by **need** — *"from each according to his ability, to each according to his needs."* Substantive over formal. ⚠️

> ⚠️ **Standard critiques of Marx:** economic determinism/reductionism; revolutions occurred in agrarian (not advanced-capitalist) states; the "withering" state instead grew totalitarian; neglect of nationalism, religion, individual liberty; failed predictions.

## 7. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 7.1 — "Explain Historical Materialism & its relevance." (2023, 20m)
```
Intro : Marx inverts Hegel — material base, not Idea, drives history.
B1    : base (mode of production) determines superstructure; class struggle is the engine.
B2    : stages primitive→…→capitalist→communist; capitalism's contradictions → revolution.
Relev : explains inequality, labour movements, welfare state, dependency/global capital. 📰
Crit  : economic determinism; wrong predictions; agrarian revolutions; neglect of culture/agency.
Concl : a powerful analytic of class & economy, if not a deterministic law of history.
```

### 7.2 — "Evaluate Marxism as a political ideology." (2025, 15m)
```
Intro : Marxism = dialectical+historical materialism → class struggle → communism.
Body  : strengths — critique of exploitation/alienation, substantive equality, praxis;
        weaknesses — determinism, authoritarian outcomes, neglect of liberty/nationalism/religion.
Assess: as analysis (inequality, capital) enduring; as prophecy (inevitable communism) falsified.
Concl : a diagnostic tool of lasting value, a blueprint of doubtful validity.
```
