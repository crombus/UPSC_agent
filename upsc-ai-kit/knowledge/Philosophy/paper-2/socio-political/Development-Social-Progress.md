## 1. DEVELOPMENT — beyond growth ✅
- **Old (economistic) view:** development = **economic growth** (rising GDP/per-capita income, industrialisation). ✅
- **Critique:** growth can coexist with poverty, inequality, unemployment, ecological ruin (**"growth without development"**, jobless/immiserating growth). Money is a *means*, not the end. ⚠️
- **Human Development (UNDP, Mahbub ul Haq):** development = enlarging people's **choices** — measured by the **HDI** (income + health/longevity + education). 📰
- **Amartya Sen — Development as Freedom:** development = the **expansion of real freedoms/capabilities** people have reason to value; removes "unfreedoms" (poverty, tyranny, poor facilities). **Capabilities** (what a person can actually do/be), not mere resources/utility. ✅
- **Nussbaum:** a list of **central human capabilities** (life, health, bodily integrity, thought, affiliation, play…) as a universal threshold of dignity. ✅

---

## 2. SOCIAL PROGRESS — the normative layer ✅
- **Definition:** progress = **directional, value-laden improvement** of society toward justice, equality, freedom, and dignity — not just more output. ✅
- **Development ≠ Progress:** development (better capabilities) becomes *progress* only if it is **just, inclusive, and sustainable** — otherwise "development" can deepen inequality or harm marginalised groups/ecology. ⚠️
- **Indicators of progress:** falling inequality, social justice, empowerment of the marginalised, environmental sustainability, human rights, quality of democratic life.

---

## 3. THE KEY DEBATES ⚠️

### (a) Is economic development necessary / sufficient for progress? (2022, 2024)
- **Necessary?** Largely **yes** — extreme poverty blocks freedom/dignity; some material base is required.
- **Sufficient?** **No** — growth without redistribution, rights, or ecological care does *not* yield human development or social progress. → **necessary but not sufficient.** ✅
- Sen: economic and social progress are **interdependent** but not identical.

### (b) Skill education & development (2023)
- Skill education **enhances** development by building **human capital & employability** (capability expansion, dignity of work), but *only within* an enabling ecosystem (jobs, quality, equity); risks — narrow employability without critical/holistic education. ⚠️ 📰 (Skill India).

### (c) Development vs tribal/indigenous values (2025)
- **Tension:** mainstream development (displacement, resource extraction, market integration) can **erode tribal culture, land, autonomy**. ⚠️
- **Reconciliation:** *inclusive & participatory* development — recognise tribal values (community, ecological harmony, self-governance — PESA, Forest Rights Act), development *with* consent (FPIC), pluralist notion of progress. **Development need not mean assimilation.** 📰

```
Intro : distinguish growth ⊂ development ⊂ progress.
Body  : NECESSARY — material base removes poverty-unfreedoms (Sen);
        NOT SUFFICIENT — growth w/o equity/rights/ecology ≠ human development or progress.
Assess: verdict — necessary but not sufficient; Sen's capabilities as the bridge.
Concl : progress needs growth PLUS justice, freedom and sustainability.
```

```
Intro : mainstream development often clashes with tribal land, culture, autonomy.
Body  : tension (displacement, extraction) vs reconciliation (participatory development,
        FPIC, PESA/FRA, ecological wisdom, pluralist progress).
Assess: development ≠ assimilation; genuine progress recognises diverse ways of life.
Concl : inclusive, consent-based development can serve both progress and tribal dignity.
```
