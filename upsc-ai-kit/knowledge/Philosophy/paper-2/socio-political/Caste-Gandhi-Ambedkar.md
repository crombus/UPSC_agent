## 1. GANDHI ON CASTE ✅
- **Target — untouchability, not (initially) varna:** Gandhi called untouchability a **sin & a blot on Hinduism**, campaigned to abolish it; coined **"Harijan"** (people of God). ✅
- **Idealised varna:** early Gandhi defended **varṇāśrama** as a **division of labour/duty (not hierarchy or birth-superiority)** — all varnas equal in dignity; no high/low. He later grew more radical (endorsing inter-caste marriage). ⚠️
- **Method:** **change of heart**, self-purification, moral/spiritual reform, **trusteeship**, *sarvodaya*, working *within* Hindu tradition; **gradual** and non-coercive. ✅
- **Critique (Ambedkar's):** reforming hearts leaves the *structure* intact; defending varna (even idealised) legitimises the system that breeds caste.

---

## 2. AMBEDKAR ON CASTE ✅
- **Target — caste itself:** untouchability is merely a symptom; the disease is **caste**, a **"system of graded inequality"** that divides and ranks society and kills fraternity. ✅
- **Religious sanction:** caste is not just custom but has **scriptural/religious authority** (the Shastras) → mere social reform fails; one must **destroy the belief in the sanctity of the Shastras**. ✅
- **Annihilation of Caste (1936, 2023 PYQ):** true abolition requires **inter-caste dining and especially inter-marriage** (fusion of blood), a **religion based on reason, liberty, equality, fraternity** — not on rules. ✅
- **Method:** **law, constitutional safeguards, political power, education, and — ultimately — conversion** (Ambedkar embraced **Buddhism**, 1956, rejecting Hinduism). *"Educate, Agitate, Organise."* ✅
- **Liberty–Equality–Fraternity:** Ambedkar's ideal (he said he took it from the Buddha, not the French Revolution); **fraternity** = "essence of democracy" and the real antidote to caste. ✅

---

## 3. THE DEBATE — structured comparison (exam gold) ⚠️
| Axis | Gandhi | Ambedkar |
|---|---|---|
| The evil | untouchability (a sin within Hinduism) | **caste itself** (graded inequality) |
| Varna | retain as ideal division of labour | **reject** entirely |
| Root cause | moral degradation / practice | **religious/scriptural sanction** |
| Method | change of heart, self-reform, gradual | law, political power, structural, radical |
| Religion | reform Hinduism from within | leave it → **conversion to Buddhism** |
| Key text | *Harijan* | *Annihilation of Caste* |
| Voice | reformer (savarna, for Harijans) | **the oppressed speaking for themselves** |

- **Poona Pact (1932):** the historic clash — Ambedkar's demand for **separate electorates** for Depressed Classes vs Gandhi's **fast-unto-death**; compromise = **reserved seats** within joint electorates. ✅ 📰
- **Assessment:** ⚠️ Gandhi mobilised mass Hindu conscience & dignity but under-estimated structure; Ambedkar rightly located caste in structure/religion & delivered **constitutional** remedies (reservation, Art 17 abolishing untouchability). Modern consensus leans Ambedkarite on structure, while valuing Gandhi's moral mobilisation.

## 6. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 6.1 — "The Gandhi–Ambedkar debate on caste discrimination." (2025, 20m — flagship)
```
Intro : two towering responses — reform-within (Gandhi) vs annihilation (Ambedkar).
B1    : Gandhi — attack untouchability, Harijan, idealised varna, change of heart, gradual.
B2    : Ambedkar — caste itself is the evil (graded inequality); religious sanction →
        destroy Shastra-authority; inter-marriage, law, political power, conversion (Buddhism).
Clash : Poona Pact (separate electorates vs fast); structure vs sentiment.
Assess: Gandhi mobilised conscience but spared structure; Ambedkar's structural-legal remedy prevailed
        (Art 17, reservation); fraternity as the antidote.
Concl : the debate frames India's continuing project of social justice.
```

### 6.2 — "Significance of Ambedkar's annihilation of caste." (2023, 15m)
```
Intro : caste = graded inequality with scriptural sanction.
Body  : annihilation (not reform) — end endogamy via inter-marriage; a rational religion of
        liberty-equality-fraternity; law + political power + education; conversion.
Signif: constitutional abolition of untouchability, reservation, Dalit assertion, fraternity as democracy's essence. 📰
Crit  : challenges of implementation; caste persists socially.
Concl : the foundational charter of India's anti-caste, social-justice constitutionalism.
```
