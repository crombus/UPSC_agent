## 1. HUMANISM ✅
- **Core:** places **human beings — their reason, dignity, worth and this-worldly flourishing — at the centre**; often (not always) sceptical of supernatural authority. ✅
- **Enlightenment origin (2022, 2024 PYQ):** the **Renaissance → 18th-c Enlightenment** shifted authority from Church/scripture to **reason, science, and the autonomous individual** — Kant's *"Sapere aude! (Dare to know)"*. This paved the way for humanism by making **man, not God, the measure**. ✅
- **Types:** Renaissance humanism, secular/scientific humanism, **Marxist humanism** (species-being), religious humanism, **Indian humanism** (Vivekananda's *daridra-nārāyaṇa*; Tagore; Gandhi's service of *Daridra Narayan*).
- **Tagore's humanism (2025 PYQ) — "reconciliation of opposites":** a *universal* humanism harmonising East–West, spiritual–material, individual–universal ("the religion of man"); the human is realised in creative unity with the infinite. ⚠️

---

## 2. SECULARISM ✅
- **Core:** governs the **relation of state to religion**. Two models: ✅
  - **Western (esp. French *laïcité*, US "wall of separation"):** strict **separation** — the state stays out of religion, religion out of the state.
  - **Indian secularism (*sarva dharma sama bhāva*):** **principled distance / equal respect** — the state is not anti-religious but treats **all religions equally**, may engage them (reform, minority rights). Bhargava: *"principled distance."* ✅
- **"Secularism = acceptance of all religions, not rejection of religion" (2023 PYQ):** the *Indian* conception — not irreligion but **equal respect (sarva dharma sama bhāva)**. ⚠️
- **Gandhi's secularism (2024):** rooted in *sarva dharma samabhāva* — **equal reverence for all religions**, religion as ethical-spiritual force in public life (differs from Western privatisation). ✅
- **Secularism & pluralism (2025):** secularism *presupposes* religious pluralism — only where many faiths coexist does equal-respect become necessary; but secularism adds the **normative** demand of state neutrality. (Bridge to Sec-B item 9.) ⚠️
- **Challenges (India):** communalism, majoritarianism, uniform civil code debate, politicisation of religion. 📰

---

## 3. MULTICULTURALISM ✅
- **Core:** the **recognition and accommodation of cultural diversity** within a single polity; rejects forced assimilation. ✅
- **Descriptive vs Normative (2022 PYQ):** ⚠️
  - **Descriptive** — the *fact* of cultural diversity in a society.
  - **Normative** — the *value/policy* of positively recognising & protecting group identities (group rights, minority rights).
- **Politics of Recognition (Charles Taylor):** identity is dialogically formed; **misrecognition harms** — hence a demand for public recognition of cultures. **Kymlicka:** liberal defence of **group-differentiated (minority) rights**. ✅
- **Tolerance & coexistence (2024):** ethical foundations of a multicultural society — mutual respect, dialogue, accommodation. ✅
- **India (2023 PYQ):** the classic multicultural society ("**unity in diversity**") — linguistic, religious, caste diversity; challenges: identity politics, communal tension, majority–minority balance, secular-vs-group-rights tension (personal laws). 📰
- **Critiques:** may entrench group boundaries, ossify cultures, subordinate individual rights (esp. women) to community ("multiculturalism vs feminism" — Susan Okin), risk of relativism. ⚠️

## 7. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 7.1 — "Is secularism necessarily related to religious pluralism?" (2025, 10m)
```
Intro : secularism = state's relation to religion; pluralism = fact/value of many faiths.
Body  : pluralism is the CONDITION that makes secularism necessary; Indian secularism =
        sarva dharma sama bhāva (equal respect), not mere separation.
        BUT secularism adds a normative demand (state neutrality/principled distance) beyond mere pluralism.
Assess: related but not identical — pluralism describes, secularism prescribes.
Concl : secularism is pluralism institutionalised as state policy.
```

### 7.2 — "Challenges of a multicultural society with reference to India." (2023, 10m)
```
Intro : multiculturalism = recognition/accommodation of diversity; India = "unity in diversity".
Body  : challenges — communal tension, identity politics, majority-minority balance,
        group rights vs individual rights (personal laws, women), risk of ossifying cultures.
Remedy: principled distance, tolerance/coexistence, constitutional safeguards, dialogue.
Concl : India's model shows accommodation is possible but demands constant secular-democratic vigilance.
```
