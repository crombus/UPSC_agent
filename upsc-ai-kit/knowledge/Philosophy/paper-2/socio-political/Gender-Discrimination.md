## 1. SEX vs GENDER — the foundational distinction ✅
- **Sex** = biological/anatomical difference (male/female). **Gender** = the **socially & culturally constructed** roles, norms, and expectations attached to sex. ✅
- **Simone de Beauvoir (*The Second Sex*):** *"One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman."* Woman is constructed as the **"Other"** against the male norm. ✅
- **Gender as cultural category (2022 PYQ):** because gender is *constructed*, patriarchal hierarchy is **contingent, not natural** — and therefore **reformable**. ⚠️
- **"Gender affects opportunities, rights, access to resources" (2025):** the construct translates into unequal access to education, work, property, health, and voice. ✅

---

## 2. FEMALE FOETICIDE ✅
- **Definition:** sex-selective abortion of female foetuses, driven by **son-preference** (dowry, patrilineal property, old-age security, ritual roles). 📰
- **Amartya Sen — "Missing Women":** millions of women demographically "missing" due to sex-selective neglect/abortion → skewed **sex ratio** and social imbalance. ✅
- **"Foeticide → social imbalance" (2023):** distorted sex ratio → bride shortage, trafficking, violence against women, further devaluation. ⚠️ 📰 (India: PCPNDT Act; *Beti Bachao Beti Padhao*).
- **Philosophical framing:** violates the **right to life & equal dignity**; the ultimate expression of gender devaluation; reveals how the *social construct* becomes lethal.

---

## 3. LAND & PROPERTY RIGHTS ✅
- **"Have property rights empowered women?" (2023):** ⚠️
  - Legal reform (**Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act 2005** — equal coparcenary rights for daughters) is a **necessary** step. 📰
  - But **law ≠ reality**: social norms, lack of awareness, family pressure, and enforcement gaps limit actual control. Property rights **empower** by giving economic security, bargaining power, and reduced vulnerability — *when* accompanied by social change.
- **Economic independence** is central to empowerment (Marxist-feminist insight: women's oppression is tied to economic dependence).

---

## 4. EMPOWERMENT ✅
- **Definition:** expanding women's **agency, voice, capabilities, and control** over their own lives — the antidote to discrimination. ✅
- **Sen/Nussbaum (capability approach):** empowerment = enlarging real **freedoms/capabilities** (education, health, work, political participation, bodily integrity). ✅
- **Dimensions:** economic (income, property), social (education, health), political (representation — 33% reservation, *Nari Shakti Vandan* Act 📰), psychological (self-worth, agency).
- **"Gender equality → empowerment → curbing foeticide" (2024):** empowerment is both an *end* (justice) and a *means* (empowered, educated, earning women are less subject to son-preference) → breaks the discrimination cycle. ⚠️
- **Feminist frameworks:** liberal (equal rights/opportunity), Marxist (economic roots), radical (patriarchy as primary), intersectional (caste+class+gender — esp. India, Dalit women). ⚠️
