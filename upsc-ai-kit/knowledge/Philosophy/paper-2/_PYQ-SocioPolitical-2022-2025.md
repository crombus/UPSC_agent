## 2025 (SLPM-B-PHL) ✅

**Q1 (10×5=50, ~150 words each)**
- (a) "Corrupt practices reveal an inherent tension between particularistic and universalistic normative standards." Do you agree with this statement? Give reasons and justification for your answer. **[10]**
- (b) How does gender as a social construct affect individuals' opportunities, rights, and access to resources? Critically discuss. **[10]**
- (c) Is the idea of secularism necessarily related to the idea of religious pluralism? Discuss. **[10]**
- (d) Comment on Plato's critique of Democracy. **[10]**
- (e) Discuss the salient features of equality according to J.S. Mill. **[10]**

**Q2**
- (a) Present a detailed account of the debate between Gandhi and Ambedkar on the issue of caste discrimination. **[20]**
- (b) Evaluate Marxism as a Political Ideology. **[15]**
- (c) 'There is no permanent friend or permanent enemy.' Discuss this statement in the light of Kautilya's view on Sovereignty. **[15]**

**Q3**
- (a) Can one's right to life be absolute? Answer with reference to the idea of Capital Punishment. **[20]**
- (b) How does the reconciliation of opposites take place in the Humanism of Tagore? Evaluate. **[15]**
- (c) Is it possible to reconcile the concept of development with tribal values to bring social and economic progress? Discuss. **[15]**

**Q4**
- (a) How are both equality and liberty inadequate as social and political ideals without justice? Discuss. **[20]**
- (b) Can Theocracy be accepted as a valid form of Government? Give reasons and justification in support of your answer. **[15]**
- (c) "Duties are of the nature of obligation while Rights are of the nature of entitlement. Therefore there is no necessary connection between the two." Do you agree with this statement? Give reasons and justification for your answer. **[15]**

**Takeaway:** EVERY one of the 10 syllabus items appears ≥1/year — Paper-II Section A is fully predictable at the item level; the challenge is the *angle*. Cluster 6 (Humanism/Secularism/Multiculturalism) is the single highest-yield block, followed by Forms of Government and the Ideals triad.

### The 4 question archetypes (same as Paper-I) ⚠️
1. **Statement/quote critique** [dominant] — "Corrupt practices reveal a tension…", "Complete liberty may lead to inequality…", "Duties are obligation, Rights are entitlement…", "Severity of punishment should be proportionate…" → agree/disagree + justify.
2. **Cross-thinker comparison/debate** — Gandhi vs Ambedkar (caste, asked 2022+2025), Plato vs Aristotle (statism/individualism), socialism vs communism, equity vs equality, gender vs sex.
3. **Applied/contemporary scenario** — female foeticide, tribal values vs development, skill education, propaganda, juvenile punishment, multiculturalism in India.
4. **Critically-examine doctrine** — Bodin/Laski/Kautilya on sovereignty, Rawls' justice, Marx's alienation/historical materialism, Plato's democracy.

### Highest-yield clusters (must master first) ⚠️
- **Caste — Gandhi vs Ambedkar debate**: asked as a 20-marker in 2025, 15-marker in 2022, plus factor-analysis 10-markers (2024) and Ambedkar's annihilation-of-caste (2023). The single most repeated named-thinker clash. → build a dedicated comparison grid.
- **Sovereignty — thinker-specific**: Bodin (2022, absolute/perpetual/undivided), Laski (2023, rejection of absolutism), Kautilya/Arthaśāstra (2024 + 2025). Austin not yet asked directly but implied. → master each thinker's distinctive thesis.
- **Ideals triad (Equality–Justice–Liberty)**: recurs as J.S. Mill on equality (2025), Rawls' justice-as-fairness (2023), liberty–equality critique (2024), "equality & liberty inadequate without justice" (2025). → learn each ideal individually AND their interrelation.
- **Marxism**: as political ideology (2025), alienation (2024), historical materialism (2023), equity vs equality (2022) — cover Marx from 4 angles.
- **Secularism**: linked to religious pluralism (2025), Gandhi's secularism (2024), "acceptance of all religions" (2023) — recurring, always with an applied/Indian twist.

### Cross-paper bridges ⚠️
- **Secularism ↔ Religious Pluralism** (Paper-II Sec B item 9): the 2025 Q1(c) explicitly links them — a Section-A answer can borrow from Section-B.
- **Capital Punishment ↔ Problem of Evil / value of life** — ethical framing overlaps.
- **Humanism ↔ Religion without God** (Sec B item 7).
