## 1. WESTERN PROOFS ✅

### (a) Ontological (a priori — Anselm, Descartes)
- **Anselm:** God = *"that than which nothing greater can be conceived."* A being that exists *in reality* is greater than one existing only *in the mind* → God must exist in reality (else not the greatest). **Descartes:** existence is a perfection; a supremely perfect being cannot lack it. ✅
- **Critique:** **Gaunilo** ("perfect island"); **Kant** — *"existence is not a real predicate"* — it adds nothing to a concept; you cannot define a thing into existence. ✅

### (b) Cosmological (a posteriori — Aquinas' Ways 1–3, Kalām)
- Everything is contingent/caused/moved → to avoid infinite regress there must be a **First Cause / Unmoved Mover / Necessary Being** = God. ✅
- **Critique:** **Hume** — why not an infinite series? why must the cause be God/personal? **Kant** — it secretly relies on the ontological argument (leaps from "necessary being" to "God"). ✅

### (c) Teleological / Design (a posteriori — Aquinas' 5th Way, Paley)
- The order, purpose, adaptation in nature implies an intelligent **Designer** (Paley's **watch → watchmaker**). ✅
- **Critique (Hume — 2025 PYQ):** ✅ (a) weak **analogy** (universe unlike a machine); (b) an imperfect world implies at best an imperfect/finite designer; (c) why one God, not many? why not a "botched" first attempt?; (d) **evil & disorder** count against a good designer; (e) **Darwin** — order can arise from natural selection, no design needed.

### (d) Moral (Kant)
- Kant demolishes the theoretical proofs but reinstates God as a **postulate of practical reason** — morality (the *summum bonum*, uniting virtue & happiness) requires God + immortality as postulates. ✅

## 2. INDIAN PROOFS — Nyāya (2023, 2025 PYQ) ✅
- **Nyāya's Īśvara-anumāna (inference to God):** the world (earth, mountains, etc.) is an **effect (kārya)** — a composite arrangement of atoms; every effect needs an **intelligent efficient cause** who knows and combines the atoms → **Īśvara (God)**. ✅ (A cosmological-teleological hybrid.)
- **Further Nyāya grounds:** the authority of the **Vedas** needs an author (God); the law of **karma** needs an intelligent dispenser (God as *karmādhyakṣa*) to allot fruits. ✅
- **God in Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika:** eternal, omniscient, the **efficient (not material)** cause of creation; combines eternal atoms per adṛṣṭa/karma. ✅
- **Critique of Nyāya proofs:** ✅
  - **Cārvāka** — no perception of God; inference unreliable; only this world.
  - **Buddhism/Jainism** — the world runs by karma/law without a creator God.
  - **Mīmāṃsā** — the Vedas are **eternal & authorless** (apauruṣeya) → no need of God; karma is self-operating.
  - **Śaṃkara (Advaita)** — Nyāya's God is a *saguṇa* Īśvara at the empirical level, not the absolute Nirguṇa Brahman; the atomistic proof is logically weak.

## 3. A PRIORI vs A POSTERIORI (2022 PYQ) ⚠️
| | A priori | A posteriori |
|---|---|---|
| Basis | concept of God alone | facts of the world |
| Example | Ontological | Cosmological, Teleological, Nyāya |
| Strength | if valid, gives a necessary God | connects to observable reality |
| Weakness | Kant: existence not a predicate | at best a probable/finite designer |
> ⚠️ **Which to prefer?** A posteriori arguments are more persuasive (rooted in experience) but yield only a *probable, finite* God; the ontological, if it worked, would give a *necessary* God but commits the existence-predicate fallacy. Most philosophers find **none demonstrative** — hence the turn to faith/experience.

## 6. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 6.1 — "Design argument + Hume's criticism." (2025, 20m)
```
Intro : teleological proof — order/purpose → intelligent designer (Paley's watch, Aquinas 5th).
Body  : state the argument (analogy from artefacts to universe).
Crit  : Hume — weak analogy; imperfect world → imperfect/finite/plural designer; evil; 
        Darwinian natural selection dissolves the need for design.
Assess: at most probable, never demonstrative; retains intuitive/cumulative force.
Concl : design points beyond itself but cannot compel — faith supplements reason.
```

### 6.2 — "Nyāya's arguments for the existence of God; evaluate." (2023, 20m)
```
Intro : Nyāya offers a rational, inferential theism (Īśvara-anumāna).
Body  : world = effect → intelligent efficient cause; Veda needs author; karma needs dispenser.
Crit  : Cārvāka (no perception), Buddhism/Jainism (karma without God),
        Mīmāṃsā (Vedas authorless), Advaita (only saguṇa, not absolute).
Assess: a genuine cosmological-teleological proof; shares the a-posteriori limits (Hume-type).
Concl : rationally suggestive, not conclusive — mirrors the Western proofs' fate.
```
