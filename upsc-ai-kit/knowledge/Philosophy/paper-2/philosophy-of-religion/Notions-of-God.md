## 1. WESTERN NOTIONS ✅
- **Classical theism:** God = a **personal, transcendent Creator**, distinct from the world, with the "omni" attributes: **omnipotent, omniscient, omnibenevolent, eternal, immutable, simple, necessary**. Immanent (present/active in world) *and* transcendent (beyond it). ✅
- **Deism:** God creates the world + natural laws, then **withdraws** — no miracles/revelation (the "cosmic clockmaker"). ✅
- **Pantheism (Spinoza — 2022, 2024 PYQ):** **God = Nature (Deus sive Natura)** — the one infinite substance; Thought & Extension are attributes; things are modes. **Impersonal, immanent, necessary**; no transcendent creator. ✅
- **Panentheism:** the world is **in** God but God exceeds the world (Process theism — God dipolar).
- **Immanent & Transcendent (2023 PYQ):** theism holds **both** — God is *transcendent* (beyond, self-sufficient, creator) yet *immanent* (sustaining, present in creation). ⚠️

---

## 2. INDIAN NOTIONS ✅
- **Saguṇa vs Nirguṇa Brahman:** ✅
  - **Nirguṇa** — Brahman **without qualities**, beyond all predication, impersonal, indescribable (*anirvachanīya*); the highest reality for **Advaita (Śaṃkara)**.
  - **Saguṇa (Īśvara)** — Brahman **with qualities**, personal God, object of devotion (**Rāmānuja, bhakti** traditions).
  - Advaita: Saguṇa Īśvara is Brahman-as-related-to-māyā, a lower (*vyāvahārika*) standpoint; Nirguṇa is the absolute (*pāramārthika*).
- **Attributes:** **Sat-Cit-Ānanda** (Being-Consciousness-Bliss); **antaryāmin** (inner controller — immanent) yet transcendent.
- **Rāmānuja's God (Viśiṣṭādvaita — 2022 PYQ):** a **personal, saguṇa God (Brahman)** whose **body** is the world (cit + acit); souls (*jīvas*) are real **modes/attributes** of God — related as **aprthak-siddhi** (inseparable). God is both the material & efficient cause (real transformation — pariṇāma). ✅
- **Personalistic vs impersonalistic (2023 PYQ):** the Saguṇa/Nirguṇa distinction *is* the Indian form of this debate. ⚠️

---

## 3. RELATION OF GOD TO WORLD & MAN ⚠️
| System | God–World relation | God–Man relation |
|---|---|---|
| Classical theism | creator, transcendent+immanent | personal, covenantal; man in God's image |
| Deism | creator then absent | remote, no intervention |
| Spinoza (pantheism) | God = Nature (identity) | man is a mode of God |
| Advaita (Nirguṇa) | world = vivarta (appearance) of Brahman | ātman **IS** Brahman (identity) |
| Rāmānuja (Saguṇa) | world = God's real body (pariṇāma) | jīva = real mode of God (aprthak-siddhi), devotion |

> 🔑 **"Must God have physical manifestation to be ultimate cause?" (2024):** classical theism & Advaita say **NO** (God/Brahman is non-physical, spiritual); pantheism identifies God with nature; Rāmānuja gives God a "body" (the world) but not a *physical form* in the anthropomorphic sense. Argue: ultimacy requires **non-dependence**, which a physical form would compromise. ⚠️

## 4. STATEMENT / QUOTE BANK ⚠️
1. ✅ *"Deus sive Natura"* (God, or Nature) — Spinoza.
2. ✅ *"Sat-Cit-Ānanda"* — Brahman as Being-Consciousness-Bliss.
3. ✅ *"Tat tvam asi"* (That thou art) — ātman–Brahman identity (Advaita).
4. ✅ Rāmānuja: the world & souls are the **body** of God (śarīra–śarīrī relation).
5. ⚠️ Nirguṇa = "not this, not this" (*neti neti*) — God beyond all attributes.

## 6. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 6.1 — "Elucidate the personalistic & impersonalistic aspects of God." (2023, 10m)
```
Intro : the deepest divide in God-concepts — personal (with qualities) vs impersonal (beyond).
Body  : Western — theism (personal creator) vs Spinoza's impersonal pantheism;
        Indian — Saguṇa Īśvara (Rāmānuja, bhakti) vs Nirguṇa Brahman (Advaita, neti neti).
Assess: Advaita reconciles — Saguṇa (vyāvahārika) & Nirguṇa (pāramārthika) as two levels of ONE.
Concl : the two aspects answer different needs — devotion vs metaphysical ultimacy.
```

### 6.2 — "Relation between God and Self according to Rāmānuja." (2022, 15m)
```
Intro : Viśiṣṭādvaita — qualified non-dualism; a personal saguṇa Brahman.
Body  : jīva (self) is a real mode/attribute of God; world+souls = God's body (śarīra–śarīrī);
        aprthak-siddhi (inseparable, not identical); real transformation (pariṇāma); bhakti + prapatti.
Contrast: vs Advaita's ātman=Brahman identity (self is not a mode but IS Brahman).
Concl : self is really distinct-yet-inseparable from a personal God — grounds devotion.
```
