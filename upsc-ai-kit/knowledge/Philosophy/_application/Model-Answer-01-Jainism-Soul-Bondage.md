# WORKED MODEL ANSWER #01 — Knowledge → Marks Pipeline

> **Purpose:** Show *exactly* how the content in a topic note becomes a scoring answer. This is the bridge between "I know Jainism" and "I scored 12/15 on the Jainism question."
> **Question chosen:** 2022 Paper-II, Q8(b) — *"Discuss the nature of Soul and Bondage according to Jainism."* **[15 marks · ~250 words]**
> **Source note:** `paper-1/indian/Jainism.md` (§3 Bondage/Liberation, §1 Reality, §7.1 skeleton).
> How to read: **the boxed text = the actual answer you write. The ⟵ lines = why that move scores.**

## STEP 0 — Decode the question (30 seconds, in your head) ⚠️

| Question element | What it demands |
|---|---|
| Directive: **"Discuss"** | Explain + give a balanced, reasoned account (not just list) |
| Scope: **"Soul AND Bondage"** | TWO parts — must cover both, roughly equal weight |
| **"according to Jainism"** | Stay inside Jaina doctrine; contrast is a *bonus*, not the core |
| 15 marks / 250 words | ~4–5 short paragraphs; 1 diagram allowed; intro + conclusion mandatory |

> 🔑 **Rule:** underline the directive word + list the sub-parts *before* writing. Half of lost marks come from ignoring a sub-part ("bondage" here).

## STEP 2 — The full written answer (with scoring annotations)

**▣ INTRODUCTION**
> In Jaina metaphysics, reality falls into two eternal categories — **jīva** (conscious soul) and **ajīva** (non-soul). The soul is intrinsically perfect; bondage is its entanglement with a specific form of *ajīva*, namely karmic matter.

⟵ *Scores: opens with the jīva/ajīva framework (note §1) → shows you place the question in the school's system, not in a vacuum. Examiner sees command of structure in line 1.*

**▣ NATURE OF THE SOUL (jīva)**
> The jīva is a real, eternal, conscious substance, **distinct in each body** (Jaina pluralism). By its intrinsic nature it possesses **infinite knowledge, perception, bliss and power (ananta-catuṣṭaya)**. Souls are graded — from one-sensed beings (nigoda) to fully-sensed humans — the gradation reflecting how far innate consciousness is obscured. Crucially, the soul is **co-extensive with the body it occupies** (like light filling a room), neither atomic (Nyāya) nor all-pervading (Sāṃkhya).

⟵ *Scores: (1) the technical term ananta-catuṣṭaya = depth; (2) the "graded souls" point = completeness; (3) the room-light simile = examiner-friendly clarity; (4) the two contrasts (Nyāya/Sāṃkhya) in one clause = synthesis without derailing.*

**▣ BONDAGE (bandha)**
> Bondage is the soul's beginningless association with **karma, which Jainism uniquely conceives as subtle material particles (pudgala)**, not a mere mental disposition. Driven by passions (**kaṣāyas** — anger, pride, deceit, greed) and wrong belief (**mithyā-darśana**), the soul's activities attract karmic matter that **flows in (āsrava)** and **binds (bandha)**, clouding its innate omniscience — as dust clings to a wet cloth.

⟵ *Scores: the ONE fact that defines this question — karma = matter — stated explicitly as "unique." Naming the mechanism (āsrava→bandha) and the causes (kaṣāya, mithyā-darśana) with a simile = full sub-part coverage.*

```
   jīva (innately omniscient)
        │  passions (kaṣāya) + wrong faith
        ▼
   ĀSRAVA (inflow of karmic matter) → BANDHA (bondage)
        │  (reverse via) saṃvara → nirjarā
        ▼
   MOKṢA (kaivalya — infinite jñāna restored)
```
⟵ *A 20-second diagram (note §0 map). Examiners reward a clean flow-diagram — it signals mastery and breaks the text wall.*

**▣ CONCLUSION**
> Thus for Jainism the soul is not *made* imperfect but *veiled*: bondage is a reversible, quasi-physical accretion. This is why liberation (mokṣa) is possible by **stopping inflow (saṃvara)** and **shedding accumulated karma (nirjarā)** through the Three Jewels — right faith, knowledge and conduct. Bondage and freedom are two states of the *same* ever-perfect soul.

⟵ *Scores: a conclusion that ANSWERS (bondage is reversible/veiling, not corruption) rather than merely summarising. Links back to liberation = closes the loop the question opened.*

## STEP 3 — Why this would score ~12–13 / 15 ⚠️

| Scoring lever | Where it appears |
|---|---|
| **Both sub-parts covered** (soul + bondage) | separate headed paragraphs |
| **Technical Sanskrit terms** used correctly | jīva, ajīva, pudgala, kaṣāya, āsrava, bandha, ananta-catuṣṭaya |
| **The distinctive thesis foregrounded** | "karma as *matter*" flagged as unique |
| **Synthesis (light contrast)** | vs Nyāya (atomic self), Sāṃkhya (all-pervading) |
| **Visual** | the āsrava→mokṣa flow diagram |
| **Answering conclusion** | "bondage = reversible veiling," not a recap |
| **Word discipline** | ~240 words, 4 paras + diagram |

**What would push it to 14+:** one critical line, e.g. *"the materiality of karma invites an objection — how does a non-physical soul interact with physical pudgala? Jainism answers by their beginningless conjunction, but the dualism-interaction problem lingers."*

## STEP 4 — The transferable template (use for ANY "Discuss X according to school Y")

```
1. FRAME  : place X inside the school's larger system (1–2 lines).
2. CORE   : X's positive doctrine, with 2–3 technical terms + 1 simile.
3. PARTS  : cover EVERY sub-part named in the question.
4. SYNTH  : one contrast clause with a rival school (don't over-do it).
5. VISUAL : a 20-sec flow/table if the concept is a process or classification.
6. CLOSE  : an answering conclusion (take a stance / state the upshot), not a summary.
```

> 🔑 Every `indian/*.md §7 skeleton` is the raw material; this template is the *mould* you pour it into. The note gives **content**; the mould gives **marks**.

## HOW THE 4 ARCHETYPES MAP TO MOULDS ⚠️
(from the PYQ banks — every question is one of these four)

| Archetype | Signature mould |
|---|---|
| **Discuss/expound doctrine** | the STEP-4 template above |
| **Quote-critique** ("'…' — do you agree?") | Restate quote → unpack its claim → evidence FOR → evidence AGAINST → **your reasoned stand** → close |
| **Compare/contrast** | 1-line frame of the axis → **grid/table** → 2–3 debate points → which is stronger + why |
| **Critically examine** | State doctrine fairly → strengths → objections (name critics) → **assess** → balanced verdict |
