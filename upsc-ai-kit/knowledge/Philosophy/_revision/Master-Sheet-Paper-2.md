## A. SOCIO-POLITICAL PHILOSOPHY (items 1–10)

| # | Item | ONE-LINE THESIS | Key thinkers/terms | Exam fault-line |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ideals: Equality, Justice, Liberty** | Liberty & equality **tension**; justice referees (Rawls: justice as fairness) | Rawls, Berlin (2 liberties), Sen | negative vs positive liberty |
| 2 | **Sovereignty** | Supreme authority; **Austin** (legal, command) vs **Laski** (pluralist) | Austin, Bodin, Laski, Kautilya | monistic vs pluralist |
| 3 | **Individual & State** | Rights, duties, accountability; individual vs collective | Locke (natural rights), Hohfeld | rights vs duties balance |
| 4 | **Forms of Government** | Monarchy / theocracy / **democracy** (procedural vs substantive) | Aristotle's typology | majoritarianism critique |
| 5 | **Political Ideologies** | **Anarchism** (no state), **Marxism** (class/dialectical materialism), **Socialism** | Marx, Bakunin | revolution vs reform |
| 6 | **Humanism, Secularism, Multiculturalism** | Human-centred; state-religion separation; recognition of diversity | Taylor, Kymlicka, Bhargava | Indian "principled distance" |
| 7 | **Crime & Punishment** | Retributive vs reformative; corruption, genocide, **capital punishment** | Beccaria, retributivism | death-penalty debate |
| 8 | **Development & Social Progress** | Growth vs **capability**/human development | Sen (capability approach) | GDP vs well-being |
| 9 | **Gender Discrimination** | Foeticide, property rights, **empowerment** | Beauvoir, feminism | equality vs difference |
| 10 | **Caste: Gandhi vs Ambedkar** | Gandhi: **reform** varna, abolish untouchability; Ambedkar: **annihilate** caste | Gandhi, Ambedkar | reform vs annihilation |

> 🔑 **Sec-A memory hooks:** ideals **"L-E-J"** (Liberty & Equality quarrel, Justice referees) · Caste = **Gandhi "HEALS" Hinduism vs Ambedkar "ANNIHILATES" caste** (single most-repeated named clash).

## B. PHILOSOPHY OF RELIGION (items 1–10)

| # | Item | ONE-LINE THESIS | Key thinkers/terms | Exam fault-line |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Notions of God** | Attributes (omni-3, personal/impersonal); relation to world | saguṇa/nirguṇa; theism/pantheism | personal vs impersonal |
| 2 | **Proofs for God** | Ontological, cosmological, teleological, moral (+ Indian anumāna) | Anselm, Aquinas 5 ways, Kant | each proof's critique |
| 3 | **Problem of Evil** | Evil vs omnipotent-omnibenevolent God | Augustine, Hick's soul-making, karma | free-will defence |
| 4 | **Soul: Immortality, Rebirth, Liberation** | Survival of death; **I→R→L**; Buddhism = rebirth w/o soul | ātman, karma, mokṣa/nirvāṇa | soul vs no-soul |
| 5 | **Reason, Revelation, Faith** | Relation of rational proof, scripture, trust | Aquinas, Kierkegaard (leap) | fideism vs rationalism |
| 6 | **Religious Experience** | Numinous (Otto), mystical (Stace); ineffable | Otto, Stace, James | veridical? |
| 7 | **Religion without God** | Buddhism, Jainism, ethical religion, Braithwaite | non-theistic religion | is it "religion"? |
| 8 | **Religion & Morality** | Is good good *because* God wills it? **Euthyphro dilemma** | Euthyphro, divine command | autonomy of ethics |
| 9 | **Religious Pluralism** | Many paths true? **E-I-P** (Exclusivism/Inclusivism/Pluralism) | Hick (pluralism), Rāmānuja | absolute truth problem |
| 10 | **Religious Language** | Analogy (Aquinas), symbol (Tillich); cognitive vs non-cognitive | Aquinas, Tillich, Braithwaite, Ayer | meaningful? factual? |

> 🔑 **Sec-B highest yield:** **Soul/Immortality/Rebirth/Liberation** asked **2×/year every year.** Euthyphro keys item 8; **E-I-P** keys item 9; anirvachanīya bridges to Advaita (Paper-I).
