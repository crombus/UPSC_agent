# MASTER SHEET — PAPER-I (History & Problems of Philosophy) — one-line thesis per thinker/school

> **Use:** final-week sprint. One thinker/school per row: **core thesis + the ONE key term + the exam fault-line.** Read top-to-bottom in 15 min. ✅ canonical · ⚠️ exam-synthesis.

---

## A. WESTERN (items 1–11)

| # | Thinker/School | ONE-LINE THESIS | Key term | Exam fault-line |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Plato** | Reality = eternal **Forms**; particulars merely *participate* | Form / Idea | Third Man regress |
| 1 | **Aristotle** | Form is **in** matter; substance = form+matter compound; 4 causes; potentiality→actuality | Hylomorphism, substance | vs Plato's separate Forms |
| 2 | **Descartes** | Method of **doubt** → *cogito*; mind & body two substances | Cogito, dualism | interaction problem |
| 2 | **Spinoza** | **One** substance = God/Nature; mind & body two attributes | Monism, parallelism | determinism vs freedom |
| 2 | **Leibniz** | Infinite **monads**, windowless, pre-established harmony | Monad | no real interaction |
| 3 | **Locke** | Mind a *tabula rasa*; ideas from experience; primary/secondary qualities | Tabula rasa | substance = "something I know not what" |
| 3 | **Berkeley** | *Esse est percipi* — no material substance, only minds & ideas | Immaterialism | God perceives all |
| 3 | **Hume** | No impression → no idea; self = **bundle**; causation = habit | Bundle, constant conjunction | scepticism, induction |
| 4 | **Kant** | **Synthetic a priori** possible; mind imposes space/time & categories | Copernican turn, categories | phenomena vs noumena |
| 5 | **Hegel** | Reality = Absolute Spirit unfolding by **dialectic** (thesis-antithesis-synthesis) | Dialectic, Absolute | idealist monism |
| 6 | **Moore** | **Common sense** defence; refutation of idealism | Common sense | vs Berkeley/Hegel |
| 6 | **Russell** | **Logical atomism**; logical constructions; incomplete symbols (theory of descriptions) | Definite description | reduce to atomic facts |
| 6 | **Early Wittgenstein** | **Picture theory**: proposition pictures fact; saying vs showing | Picture theory | mystical = shown not said |
| 7 | **Logical Positivism** | Meaning = **verifiability**; metaphysics = nonsense; necessary truths = analytic | Verification principle | principle self-refuting |
| 8 | **Later Wittgenstein** | Meaning = **use**; language-games; no private language | Language-game | anti-private-language argument |
| 9 | **Husserl** | **Phenomenology**: bracket (epoché) & describe essences; anti-psychologism | Epoché, intentionality | pure consciousness |
| 10 | **Kierkegaard** | Truth is **subjectivity**; leap of faith; the individual | Leap of faith | vs Hegel's system |
| 10 | **Sartre** | **Existence precedes essence**; radical freedom; bad faith | Radical freedom | condemned to be free |
| 10 | **Heidegger** | **Dasein** = being-in-the-world; being-towards-death; temporality | Dasein | fundamental ontology |
| 11 | **Quine** | Attacks **two dogmas** (analytic/synthetic; reductionism); holism | Two dogmas, web of belief | no a priori |
| 11 | **Strawson** | **Descriptive metaphysics**; basic particulars = bodies; person is primitive | Basic particulars, person | vs Cartesian ego |

## B. INDIAN (items 12–20)

| # | School | ONE-LINE THESIS | Key term | Exam fault-line |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 12 | **Cārvāka** | Only **perception** valid; body-consciousness; no soul/afterlife/God | Pratyakṣa only | rejects inference |
| 13 | **Jainism** | Reality has infinite aspects; **anekāntavāda / syādvāda** (saptabhaṅgī) | Syādvāda | 7-fold predication |
| 14 | **Buddhism** | Dependent origination; momentariness; **no-self** | Pratītyasamutpāda, kṣaṇikavāda, anātma | 4 schools |
| 15 | **Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika** | Realist pluralism; **7 padārthas**; 4 pramāṇas; atomism; God as inference | Pramāṇa, padārtha | proofs for God |
| 16 | **Sāṃkhya** | Dualism of **Puruṣa** (many, inactive) & **Prakṛti** (one, 3 guṇas); satkāryavāda | Puruṣa-Prakṛti, guṇa | atheistic; evolution |
| 17 | **Yoga** | Sāṃkhya + method: still **citta-vṛtti**; 8 limbs → kaivalya | Cittavṛtti-nirodha, samādhi | theistic (Īśvara) |
| 18 | **Mīmāṃsā** | Dharma via Veda; knowledge **intrinsically valid** (svataḥ prāmāṇya); 6 pramāṇas | Svataḥ prāmāṇya | eternal Veda |
| 19 | **Vedānta** | Brahman = reality. Śaṅkara: non-dual, world=māyā; Rāmānuja: qualified; Madhva: dualist | Brahman, māyā, adhyāsa | 3 schools grid |
| 20 | **Aurobindo** | **Evolution & involution**; Supermind; integral yoga | Supermind, integral yoga | spiritual evolution |

> 🔑 **Paper-I spine:** Western = *substance & the crisis of knowledge* (Aristotle→Hume→**Kant the hinge**→analysts dissolve metaphysics). Indian = *pramāṇa (how we know) + causation (satkārya vs asatkārya) + self/liberation (ātma vs anātma)*.
