# Paper-I · Section A — Western Philosophy · PYQ Bank (2022–2025)

> Source: UPSC CSM Philosophy Paper-I question papers (2022, 2023, 2024, 2025), OCR-read from scans.

## 2025
- "Ideas are timeless and spaceless." Elucidate with reference to **Plato**. [10]
- "In the empirical world, everything is a compound of *Matter* and *Form*." Evaluate w.r.t. **Aristotle**. [10]
- Explain the difference between **being-for-itself and being-in-itself** as presented by **Sartre**. [10]
- "The golden mountain is very high." Discuss in context of **Russell's theory of descriptions**. [10]
- How does **Hegel** challenge **Kant's** distinction between *Phenomena* and *Noumena*? Discuss. [10]
- What are the basic tenets of **Rationalism**? How does **Descartes** build a system of philosophy in consonance with them? [20]
- "All determination is negation." Comment w.r.t. **Spinoza**. [15]
- Examine **Hume's** refutation of *Causal relation* and **Kant's** response to it. [15]
- "We should look not to an ideal language… but empirically to the ways in which languages are actually used." Explain the transition from **early to later Wittgenstein**. [20]
- Present an exposition of the **verification theory of meaning** (Logical Positivists); differentiate the **"strong" and "weak" sense of "verifiable"**. [15]
- "Blue is one object of sensation and green is another, and consciousness… is different from either." Present **Moore's refutation of idealism** w.r.t. this statement. [15]
- How is **Husserl's** account of "I think" different from **Descartes**? Critically discuss. [20]
- "We can affirm the truth of any sentence in our total system… so long as we are prepared to make adjustments elsewhere." Discuss in light of **Quine's *Two Dogmas of Empiricism***. [15]
- Explain **Berkeley's** doctrine of **nominalism** and his refutation of **abstract ideas**. [15]

## 2024
- Differentiate between **Plato's and Aristotle's** conceptions of **form**. [10]
- How does **Kant** respond to **Hume's scepticism** with regard to a priori judgments? Discuss. [10]
- What arguments are offered by **Moore** to prove that there are certain **truisms**, knowledge of which is a matter of common sense? Critically discuss. [10]
- Why does **later Wittgenstein** think there cannot be a **private language** (that only one person can speak)? Discuss. [10]
- How does **Kierkegaard** define **truth in terms of subjectivity**? Critically discuss. [10]
- Is rejection of **Locke's primary qualities** instrumental in **Berkeley's** leaning towards idealism? How is Berkeley's **subjective idealism** different from **Hegel's absolute idealism**? [20]
- How does **Spinoza** establish that God alone is absolutely real ("Whatever is, is in God")? Critically discuss. [15]
- Critically examine **Kant's objections against the ontological argument** for the existence of God. [15]
- Explain **Russell's** notion of **incomplete symbols**. How does it lead to the doctrine of **logical atomism**? [20]
- Is "All objects are either red or not red" meaningful in the same way as "This page is white", according to **Logical Positivists**? [15]
- Among the **rationalists**, whose account of the **mind-body problem** is compatible with human freedom and free will? Critically discuss. [15]
- What do existentialists mean by **"existence precedes essence"**? How is human existence related to human freedom? [10+10]
- Why does **Husserl** think **essences** exhibit a kind of continuity between consciousness and being? Discuss. [15]
- Explain the nature of the **two dogmas** that **Quine** refers to in *Two Dogmas of Empiricism*. [15]

## 2023
- "Precepts without concepts are blind and concepts without precepts are empty." How does **Kant reconcile rationalism with empiricism**? [10]
- "History is a process of dialectical change." Discuss **Hegel's** approach to understanding history. [10]
- "That thing is said to be free which exists solely from the necessity of its own nature…" Discuss **Spinoza's** views on **freedom and determinism**. [10]
- How does **Kierkegaard** argue against **Hegel's idea of universal spirit** in favour of the individual as the essence of spirit? Critically discuss. [10]
- What are the main arguments offered by **Kant** to prove that **a priori synthetic judgments** are possible? Discuss with examples. [10]
- Critically analyse **Hume's** argument that **causality is a matter of habit/custom** involving the psychological principle of association. [20]
- Present an exposition of **Aristotle's** distinction between **actuality and potentiality**. Does it solve the problem of **being and becoming**? [15]
- Discuss **Descartes' theory of innate ideas** and the grounds on which **Locke** refutes it. [15]
- Does the **rejection of metaphysics** by Logical Positivists relate to a problem of meaning, knowledge, nature of things, or all together? [20]
- Elucidate the significance of **bracketing and reduction** in **Husserl's phenomenological method**. [15]
- "Consciousness is what it is not and is not what it is." Bring out the chief features of **Sartre's conception of consciousness**. [15]
- Why does **Strawson** consider **person** to be a **primitive concept**? What implication does it have for **mind-body dualism**? [20]
- Why, according to **Russell**, is "The present king of France is bald" problematic? Critically discuss. [15]
- What were the main reasons that led **Wittgenstein** to shift from **picture-theory to use-theory of meaning**? Critically discuss. [15]

## 2022
- How does **Plato** use the **theory of forms** to establish the relation between **epistemology and metaphysics**? Discuss. [10]
- What is **Russell's method of logical analysis**? How does it end in the **atomic theory of meaning**? Discuss. [10]
- Establish the tenability of **later Wittgenstein's** notion of **language as form of life**. [10]
- What is **psychologism**? How does **Husserl** avoid it in transcendental phenomenology? Critically discuss. [10]
- What is **apperception** according to **Kant**? Discuss w.r.t. his transcendental exposition of **space and time**. [10]
- Provide a critical account of **Heidegger's Being-in-the-world** and the problem of **'authenticity'** in the context of **Dasein**. [20]
- Is **Aristotle's** view of the nature of **identity** in consonance with his metaphysical view of **causes as processes**? [15]
- Discuss the concept of **substance** according to **Spinoza**. Does it lead to **pantheism**? Substantiate. [15]
- How does **Kant** construct **antinomies** to illustrate the illusory tendencies of pure reason? Explain and examine them. [20]
- What is the **dialectical method** in **Hegel**? How does it help in realizing the **Absolute**? Discuss. [15]
- Is there any difference between **pictorial form and logical form** in **Wittgenstein's picture theory**? How does logical form define the relation between language and reality? [15]
- How does **Kierkegaard** define **subjectivity**? Explain with reference to the **three stages of existence**. [20]
- How does **Descartes** explain **certainty** w.r.t. knowledge of the **self**? How does it differ from knowledge of the world? Critically discuss. [15]
- Why and how does **Locke** refute **innate ideas**? Elucidate the nature and source of knowledge in Locke's epistemology. [15]

**Guaranteed every year:** Rationalism (esp. **Descartes + Spinoza-substance**), Empiricism, Later Wittgenstein, Husserl, Existentialism, Quine/Strawson each appear ≥ once/year. Build these to full depth.

**Question archetypes (same 4 as Indian):**
1. **Statement/quote critique** — dominant. Recurring quotes to master:
   - "Precepts without concepts are blind…" (Kant) · "existence precedes essence" (Sartre) · "All determination is negation" (Spinoza) · "Whatever is, is in God" (Spinoza) · "Consciousness is what it is not…" (Sartre) · "The golden mountain / present king of France" (Russell descriptions) · "Blue is one object of sensation…" (Moore).
2. **Cross-thinker comparison/debate** — Plato vs Aristotle (form); Berkeley vs Hegel (idealism); Descartes vs Husserl ("I think"); Hume vs Kant (causation/scepticism); rationalists compared (mind-body & freedom).
3. **Applied / does-it-solve** — "Does Spinoza's substance lead to pantheism?"; "Does actuality-potentiality solve being-becoming?"; "Is X meaningful the same way as Y?" (verification).
4. **Critically examine** — Kant's antinomies; Kant vs ontological argument; Strawson's person as primitive.

**Highest-yield clusters to build first (Western):**
- 🔴 **Descartes** (method, cogito, certainty, innate ideas, mind-body) + **Spinoza substance** (determination-negation, pantheism, freedom-determinism) — asked *every single year*.
- 🔴 **Hume causation** ↔ **Kant response** (a priori synthetic, apperception, antinomies) — a linked pair; prepare together.
- 🔴 **Russell** (descriptions, incomplete symbols, logical atomism, logical analysis) + **Moore** (refutation of idealism, common-sense truisms).
- 🔴 **Plato ↔ Aristotle** grid (Form vs form-in-matter, act-potency, causation).
- 🟠 **Wittgenstein early→later transition** (picture theory → meaning-as-use → private-language argument) — asked as a *transition* narrative, not two separate blocks.
- 🟠 **Husserl** (psychologism, bracketing/reduction, essences, "I think" vs Descartes).
- 🟠 **Existentialism** trio distinguished (Kierkegaard subjectivity/3 stages · Sartre for-itself/consciousness/freedom · Heidegger Dasein/authenticity).
