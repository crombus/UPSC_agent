## 1. THEORY OF CATEGORIES (Vaiśeṣika padārthas) ✅

Reality = **7 categories (padārtha = "the knowable/nameable")**:
| # | Padārtha | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Dravya** (substance) | 9 substances: earth, water, fire, air, ākāśa, time, space, soul, mind |
| 2 | **Guṇa** (quality) | 24 qualities inhering in substances |
| 3 | **Karma** (action/motion) | movement |
| 4 | **Sāmānya** (universal) | the common, real universal (against Buddhist nominalism) |
| 5 | **Viśeṣa** (particularity) | ultimate differentiator of eternal atoms (→ school's name) |
| 6 | **Samavāya** (inherence) | permanent, inseparable relation (whole-part, quality-substance) |
| 7 | **Abhāva** (non-existence) | added later; four kinds of absence |

- **Realism + pluralism:** universals, particulars, relations are all *real, mind-independent* entities.

## 2. THEORY OF PRAMĀṆA (Nyāya epistemology) ✅

- **4 valid sources:** pratyakṣa, anumāna, upamāna, śabda (see `_themes/Pramana-across-schools.md`).
- **Perception (pratyakṣa):** two stages — **nirvikalpaka** (indeterminate, bare) → **savikalpaka** (determinate, qualified: "this is a pot"). Also *extraordinary* (alaukika) perception.
- **Inference (anumāna) — the famous 5-membered syllogism (pañcāvayava):** ✅
  1. **Pratijñā** (thesis): "The hill has fire."
  2. **Hetu** (reason): "because it has smoke."
  3. **Udāharaṇa** (example + vyāpti): "wherever smoke, there fire, as in the kitchen."
  4. **Upanaya** (application): "the hill has such smoke."
  5. **Nigamana** (conclusion): "therefore the hill has fire."
  - Rests on **vyāpti** (invariable concomitance) + **pakṣadharmatā** (mark present in subject).
- **Theory of validity — parataḥ-prāmāṇya:** knowledge's validity is established **externally** (by correspondence/success), NOT intrinsically. (Contrast Mīmāṃsā's *svataḥ*.)
- **Theory of error / Theory of Appearance — anyathākhyāti ("misapprehension"):** in illusion ("this is silver" seeing shell), a *real* silver (seen elsewhere) is *wrongly presented as* the "this." The object is real, only the *relation/place* is wrong → realist theory of error. ✅

## 3. SELF (ātman) & LIBERATION (apavarga/mokṣa) ✅

- **Ātman:** a real, eternal, infinite **substance**; distinct in each body; the substrate of consciousness, cognition, desire, volition — but consciousness is an **adventitious** (āgantuka) quality, arising only when soul-mind-body conjoin, NOT the soul's essence. ✅
- **Manas (mind):** atomic internal sense, mediates soul and senses.
- **Liberation (apavarga):** *absolute cessation of all pain* — and, strikingly, of **consciousness too**. The liberated soul exists as pure substance **without knowledge, bliss, or pain** — like a stone. ✅
- ⚠️ **The famous objection:** critics (esp. Advaita) call this a "stone-like" mokṣa — who would seek a liberation devoid of bliss/consciousness? *Nyāya reply:* freedom from pain is the rational goal; bliss is not guaranteed and may bring new pain.

## 4. GOD & PROOFS FOR THE EXISTENCE OF GOD ✅ (highest-yield sub-topic)

- **Īśvara** = the eternal, omniscient, omnipotent **efficient cause** of creation (not material cause — atoms are the material cause). God is a special ātman, never bound. Orders the atoms, dispenses karma-fruits.
- **Proofs (all via anumāna):**
  1. **Kāryāt (Causal/Cosmological):** the world (effect, made of parts) must have an intelligent maker; atoms are unintelligent → God directs them.
  2. **Āyojanāt / Adṛṣṭa argument:** the first motion of atoms (to combine) needs an intelligent guide; karma (adṛṣṭa) is unconscious → needs God to allot fruits.
  3. **Śabda / Āptavākya:** the authority of the Vedas presupposes an omniscient author = God.
  4. (Later Naiyāyikas add) argument from **support (dhṛti), destruction, saṃkhyā-viśeṣa**, etc.
- ⚠️ **Critiques to attach:** Cārvāka (no imperceptible cause), Sāṃkhya (unconscious prakṛti suffices, no God needed), Mīmāṃsā (Vedas are authorless/eternal — no divine author), plus Western parallels (Hume/Kant on cosmological & design — ⚠️ cross-paper bridge).

---

## 5. THEORY OF CAUSATION & ATOMISTIC CREATION ✅

- **Asatkāryavāda (ārambhavāda):** the effect is a **new beginning**, NOT pre-existent in the cause. The cloth is a *fresh* whole, distinct from the threads. (Opposite of Sāṃkhya's satkāryavāda.) ✅
- **Atomism (paramāṇu-vāda):** matter is composed of eternal, indivisible, partless **atoms** (of earth, water, fire, air). Creation = atoms combine (dyad = dvyaṇuka → triad = tryaṇuka → gross bodies); dissolution = they separate.
- **God + adṛṣṭa initiate the first combination**; hence atomism needs a theistic controller (unlike Cārvāka's svabhāva).

## 6. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika | Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Causation | asatkāryavāda (new effect) | Sāṃkhya/Vedānta: satkāryavāda |
| Validity of knowledge | parataḥ (extrinsic) | Mīmāṃsā/Advaita: svataḥ |
| God | efficient cause, proved by inference | Sāṃkhya/Mīmāṃsā: no God · Advaita: Brahman (not a maker) |
| Liberation | painless but consciousness-less | Advaita: blissful (ānanda); Sāṃkhya: kaivalya |
| Universals | real (sāmānya) | Buddhism: nominal (apoha) |
| Self | substance, consciousness adventitious | Advaita: self = consciousness itself |

3. ✅ *"Consciousness is an adventitious quality of the self, not its essence."* — Nyāya psychology.
4. ⚠️ *"Atoms combine at creation under the guidance of God and adṛṣṭa."*

## 9. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 9.1 — "Critically examine the arguments of Nyāya for the existence of God." (2023 P-II Q7a, 20m; also 2025 Q7b)
```
Intro : God = efficient cause; all proofs are inferences (anumāna).
B1    : Causal (kāryāt) — world-as-effect needs intelligent maker.
B2    : Adṛṣṭa/āyojanāt — atoms & karma unconscious → need divine director.
B3    : Śabda — Vedic authority presupposes omniscient author.
Crit  : Sāṃkhya (prakṛti suffices), Mīmāṃsā (authorless Veda), Cārvāka; + Hume/Kant parallels.
Concl : Rational theism, but each proof rests on contestable premises (world-as-designed, karma-needs-controller).
```

```

```
