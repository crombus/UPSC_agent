# MĪMĀṂSĀ (Pūrva-Mīmāṃsā) — Syllabus Item 18

> **Syllabus:** Theory of Knowledge.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Chatterjee & Datta; C.D. Sharma) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** Āstika, realist, ritualist school founded by Jaimini. Exists to **defend the authority and self-validity of the Vedas** and the science of dharma. Two sub-schools: **Bhāṭṭa** (Kumārila) & **Prābhākara** (Prabhākara).

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
MĪMĀṂSĀ = defence of Vedic authority via a theory of knowledge
   │
   ├─ SVATAḤ-PRĀMĀṆYA : knowledge is valid BY ITSELF (only error is external)
   ├─ PRAMĀṆAS        : Prābhākara = 5 ; Bhāṭṭa = 6 (+ anupalabdhi)
   ├─ ŚABDA (Veda)    : eternal, authorless (apauruṣeya) → infallible
   └─ THEORY OF ERROR : Prābhākara = akhyāti ; Bhāṭṭa = viparīta-khyāti
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — svataḥ = "Self-valid; only Sourness (error) is extraneous."**

---

## 1. SVATAḤ-PRĀMĀṆYA — intrinsic validity (THE core doctrine) ✅
- **Knowledge is valid by itself (svataḥ prāmāṇya).** Every cognition, as it arises, is *presumed true*; its validity is **not** dependent on any external verification. ✅
- Both the **origin (utpatti)** and the **ascertainment (jñapti)** of validity are intrinsic — a cognition carries its own truth-guarantee.
- **Only invalidity is extrinsic (parataḥ):** error needs a *positive external cause* (defective sense-organ, contradicting cognition). We doubt a cognition only when later experience contradicts it.
- ⚠️ **Purpose:** this is the *master move* to make the Veda self-authoritative — if all knowledge is intrinsically valid and the Veda has no (fallible) author, its statements about dharma are automatically true.

> **Contrast (must cite):** Nyāya holds **parataḥ-prāmāṇya** — BOTH validity and invalidity are established externally (by correspondence/fruitful activity). Mīmāṃsā reverses this for validity. Buddhism: both extrinsic. Sāṃkhya: both intrinsic.

---

## 2. THE PRAMĀṆAS ✅
- **Prābhākara accepts 5:** pratyakṣa, anumāna, upamāna, śabda, **arthāpatti** (postulation).
- **Bhāṭṭa accepts 6:** the above + **anupalabdhi** (non-apprehension, to cognise absence/abhāva).
- **Distinctive two:**
  - **Arthāpatti (postulation):** assuming an unseen fact to reconcile an apparent contradiction. *"Devadatta is fat yet never eats by day → (postulate) he eats at night."* Irreducible to inference (Mīmāṃsā argues). ✅
  - **Anupalabdhi (non-apprehension):** knowledge of the *absence* of a thing from its very non-perception — "there is no pot here" is known directly, not inferred. (Prābhākara denies this, treating abhāva differently.)
- **Śabda (verbal testimony)** is the *supreme* pramāṇa for supersensible matters (dharma, heaven), esp. Vedic injunctions.

---

## 3. AUTHORITY OF THE VEDA (apauruṣeyatva) ✅
- The Vedas are **eternal and authorless (apauruṣeya)** — not composed by any person (nor by God). Being authorless, they are free of the defects that afflict human authors → infallible. ✅
- **Words and their meanings are eternal**; the word–meaning relation is natural, not conventional (against Nyāya).
- **The core of the Veda is injunction (vidhi/codanā):** the Veda's purpose is to prescribe *action* (yāga, ritual) for attaining desired ends (heaven), not to describe existing facts. "Dharma is that which is enjoined." (codanā-lakṣaṇo'rtho dharmaḥ).
- **Mīmāṃsā is originally atheistic/ritualist:** it needs no creator-God — the eternal Veda + the principle of **apūrva** (an unseen potency generated by ritual that yields future fruit) explains reward without divine agency. ✅

---

## 4. THEORY OF ERROR (khyāti-vāda) ✅
| Sub-school | Theory | Explanation of "this is silver" (seeing shell) |
|---|---|---|
| **Prābhākara** | **Akhyāti** (non-discrimination) | Two *separate valid* cognitions — "this" (perceived) + "silver" (remembered) — whose *difference is not grasped*; no positive error, only failure to discriminate |
| **Bhāṭṭa** | **Viparīta-khyāti** (misapprehension) | The shell is positively *mis*cognised *as* silver — a real but wrong identification |

- ⚠️ Contrast with Nyāya's **anyathākhyāti** (real silver-elsewhere presented in wrong place) and Advaita's **anirvacanīya-khyāti** (the illusory silver is *indescribable*, neither real nor unreal).

## 5. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Mīmāṃsā | Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Validity of knowledge | svataḥ (intrinsic) | Nyāya: parataḥ |
| Number of pramāṇas | 5 (Prābhākara) / 6 (Bhāṭṭa) | Nyāya: 4 · Sāṃkhya: 3 |
| Veda | eternal, authorless | Nyāya: composed by God |
| God | not required (apūrva) | Nyāya/Yoga/Vedānta: Īśvara/Brahman |
| Theory of error | akhyāti / viparīta-khyāti | Nyāya anyathā · Advaita anirvacanīya |
