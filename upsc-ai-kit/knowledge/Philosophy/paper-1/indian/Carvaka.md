# CĀRVĀKA (Lokāyata) — Syllabus Item 12

> **Syllabus:** Theory of Knowledge; Rejection of Transcendent Entities.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Chatterjee & Datta, *Intro to Indian Philosophy*; C.D. Sharma, *Critical Survey*) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis · 📰 = web-verified
> **Nature:** The lone *materialist, atheist, empiricist* (nāstika) school. Its whole identity = what it *rejects*.

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
CĀRVĀKA = materialism (bhūtavāda) + empiricism (only perception) + hedonism
   │
   ├─ EPISTEMOLOGY: only PRATYAKṢA is pramāṇa  → rejects anumāna & śabda
   ├─ METAPHYSICS : only 4 bhūtas (earth,water,fire,air)  → NO ākāśa, NO soul, NO God
   ├─ MIND        : consciousness = by-product of matter (bhūta-caitanya-vāda)
   └─ ETHICS/GOAL : kāma (pleasure) is the only puruṣārtha; no dharma, no mokṣa
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — what Cārvāka DENIES: "God, Soul, Karma, Veda, Afterlife, Inference"** (G-S-K-V-A-I).

---

## 1. EPISTEMOLOGY — "PERCEPTION ALONE" ✅

- **Only pratyakṣa (perception) is a valid pramāṇa.** Anumāna (inference) and śabda (testimony) are rejected as independent sources.
- **Why inference fails:** inference depends on **vyāpti** (universal invariable concomitance, e.g. "wherever smoke, there fire"). But vyāpti cannot be established:
  - not by perception — we cannot perceive *all* smoke-fire cases (past, future, distant);
  - not by another inference — that would need its own vyāpti → **infinite regress**;
  - not by testimony — testimony is itself invalid for the Cārvāka.
  - Hence vyāpti is never certain → inference is only probable, not a pramāṇa. ✅
  - 📖 *Source-verified* (C.D. Sharma, *Critical Survey*): *"Vyāpti therefore is the nerve of all inference. But the Cārvāka challenges this universal and invariable relationship of concomitance and regards it a mere guess-work."*
- **Why testimony fails:** words of others (incl. Vedas) are unreliable, authored by cunning priests; verbal knowledge reduces to (uncertain) inference anyway.

> ⚠️ **The self-refutation charge (memorise):** The claim "inference is invalid" is *itself* a universal generalisation — reached by inference. So the Cārvāka either (a) refutes himself, or (b) tacitly uses inference. This is the standard Nyāya/Jaina rebuttal and the pivot of most critique answers.

---

## 2. METAPHYSICS — REJECTION OF TRANSCENDENT ENTITIES ✅

Because only the perceptible is real:
| Rejected entity | Cārvāka reasoning |
|---|---|
| **Ākāśa (ether)** | imperceptible → only 4 elements (earth, water, fire, air) admitted |
| **Soul (ātman)** | not perceived apart from the living body → soul = the conscious *body* |
| **God (Īśvara)** | imperceptible, and world explained by *svabhāva* (nature) — no creator needed |
| **Adṛṣṭa / Karma** | unseen moral force → unverifiable |
| **Rebirth / afterlife / heaven / hell** | no perceptual evidence |
| **Vedic authority / dharma / mokṣa** | testimony rejected; priest-craft for livelihood |

- **Bhūta-caitanya-vāda:** consciousness *emerges* from the combination of the four elements. ✅ Mind is not a separate substance.
  - 📖 *Source-verified* (C.D. Sharma, *Critical Survey*): *"Just as the combination of betel, areca nut and lime produces the red colour, or just as fermented yeast produces the intoxicating quality in the wine — though the ingredients separately do not possess either — similarly a particular combination of the elements produces consciousness, though the elements separately do not possess it."* (Two analogies: **betel+areca+lime → red**; **yeast → intoxicant**.)
- **Svabhāva-vāda (naturalism):** things behave by their own inherent nature; no divine governance, no purpose.

---

## 3. ETHICS & PURUṢĀRTHA ✅
- Rejects dharma & mokṣa; of the four aims accepts only **kāma (pleasure)** and **artha (wealth, as its means)**.
- Ethical hedonism: *"While life is yours, live joyfully; none can escape death's searching eye."* (Cārvāka verse). Enjoy now — the body will not return.
- ⚠️ **Refinement to note:** UPSC-level answers distinguish *crude* hedonism (eat-drink-be-merry) from a more *refined* reading (maximise pleasure, minimise pain intelligently) — do not caricature.

---

## 4. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES ⚠️

| Axis | Cārvāka | Opponents |
|---|---|---|
| Validity of inference | invalid (vyāpti unprovable) | Nyāya: valid; self-refutation charge |
| Nature of self | conscious body | all others: distinct ātman/jīva |
| Source of consciousness | emergent from matter | Sāṃkhya/Vedānta: puruṣa/Brahman |
| God | none needed (svabhāva) | Nyāya/Yoga/Vedānta: Īśvara |
| Highest good | pleasure (kāma) | all others: mokṣa/kaivalya |

> 🔑 Cārvāka is the **permanent foil**: every āstika school defines itself partly *against* Cārvāka materialism. Cite it to sharpen contrasts (e.g., "unlike Cārvāka, Sāṃkhya posits an imperceptible puruṣa known through inference").

### 7.1 — "Discuss Cārvāka's critique of the belief in the existence of suprasensible entities." (2025 P-II Q5a, 10m)
```
Intro : Cārvāka = radical empiricism → only pratyakṣa is pramāṇa.
B1    : Epistemic ground — reject anumāna (vyāpti regress) & śabda.
B2    : Hence NO valid access to suprasensibles → soul, God, adṛṣṭa, heaven, rebirth, Veda all denied.
B3    : Positive metaphysics — 4 bhūtas, bhūta-caitanya-vāda, svabhāva-vāda.
Crit  : Self-refutation (rejection is itself a generalisation); pragmatic use of inference.
Concl : A salutary empiricist check on dogma, but epistemically self-limiting.
```
