> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Chatterjee & Datta; C.D. Sharma) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** Realist, pluralist, relativist (nāstika — rejects Vedic authority) school of *anekāntavāda*.

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
JAINISM
 ├─ REALITY (sat) = dravya, guṇa, paryāya ; sat = origination + decay + permanence
 │     └─ 2 categories: JĪVA (soul, conscious) + AJĪVA (non-soul)
 ├─ EPISTEMOLOGY of RELATIVITY:
 │     ANEKĀNTAVĀDA (many-sidedness of reality)
 │        ├─ NAYAVĀDA (standpoints)

```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — Three Jewels (ratnatraya):** *"Right F-K-C"* = Right **F**aith (samyak darśana) · Right **K**nowledge (jñāna) · Right **C**onduct (cāritra).

---

## 1. THEORY OF REALITY (Anekāntavāda metaphysics) ✅

- **Sat (real) = that which has origination (utpāda), decay (vyaya), and permanence (dhrauvya) simultaneously.** Substance is permanent; its modes constantly arise and perish. → reconciles change and permanence (against Buddhist flux AND Advaita changeless being). ✅
- **Dravya (substance)** has **guṇa (essential qualities)** and **paryāya (changing modes)**.
- **Two fundamental categories:**
  - **JĪVA** — conscious soul; infinite, each distinct; intrinsically has infinite knowledge/bliss, obscured by karma. Jīvas graded (from one-sense beings to humans).
  - **AJĪVA** — non-soul: **pudgala** (matter), **dharma** (medium of motion), **adharma** (medium of rest), **ākāśa** (space), **kāla** (time). ✅
- **Realism + pluralism:** many real substances; reality is genuinely many-sided (anekānta), not reducible to one principle.

---

## 2. ANEKĀNTAVĀDA → SYĀDVĀDA → SAPTABHAṄGĪNAYA (the star topic) ✅

**Logical ladder:**
1. **Anekāntavāda** (non-absolutism): reality has infinite aspects; no single view captures the whole. *Metaphysical* thesis.
2. **Nayavāda**: every judgement is from a **standpoint (naya)** — partial, not false, but incomplete.
3. **Syādvāda**: therefore every predication must be qualified by **"syāt"** ("in some respect / from a standpoint / conditionally"). *Epistemic-linguistic* thesis.

### Saptabhaṅgī — the 7-fold conditional predication ✅
For any object and any predicate, seven truth-qualified assertions are possible:
| # | Formula | Meaning (about "the pot exists") |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | syād asti | in some respect, it IS |
| 2 | syād nāsti | in some respect, it IS NOT |
| 3 | syād asti nāsti | in some respect it is AND is not (successively) |
| 4 | syād avaktavyam | in some respect it is INDESCRIBABLE (is + is-not simultaneously) |
| 5 | syād asti avaktavyam | is + indescribable |
| 6 | syād nāsti avaktavyam | is-not + indescribable |
| 7 | syād asti nāsti avaktavyam | is + is-not + indescribable |

- Built from 3 basics (**is / is-not / indescribable**) combined. Guards against dogmatic one-sidedness (ekānta).
- 📚 **Illustration:** the **blind men and the elephant** — each grasps a part, each right *from his standpoint*, all wrong in claiming the whole.

> ⚠️ **Standard critique (memorise):** (a) **Self-contradiction charge** — Rāmānuja/Śaṃkara: attributing opposite predicates to one thing violates non-contradiction. *Jaina reply:* the "syāt" qualifier removes contradiction — predicates hold in *different* respects, not the same. (b) **Sthūlabhaṅga/regress** — if every claim needs "syāt," so does "syādvāda" itself. (c) *Charge of scepticism* — reply: it is not scepticism but *conditional realism*.

## 3. BONDAGE AND LIBERATION ✅

- **Karma is subtle MATTER (pudgala)** — uniquely Jaina: karmic particles physically flow into (āsrava) and bind (bandha) the soul, weighing it down and obscuring its innate omniscience. ✅
- **Chain:** *āsrava* (inflow) → *bandha* (bondage) → checked by *saṃvara* (stoppage) → *nirjarā* (shedding of accumulated karma via austerity/tapas) → *mokṣa* (liberation).
- **Cause of inflow:** passions (kaṣāyas) and activities driven by **mithyā-darśana** (wrong faith).
- **Path to liberation — the Three Jewels (Ratnatraya):** Right Faith + Right Knowledge + Right Conduct, *together*. 📖 *Source-verified* (Chatterjee-Datta): *"Right faith, right knowledge and right conduct constitute the three gems (triratna) that shine in a good life"* (Tattvārtha-sūtra's opening sūtra). Right Conduct includes the **five vows (mahāvratas): ahiṃsā, satya, asteya, brahmacarya, aparigraha.**
- **Mokṣa/Kaivalya:** the soul, freed of all karmic matter, regains its intrinsic **infinite knowledge, bliss, power, and perception**; rises to the top of the universe (siddha-śilā). Liberation is of the *individual* soul (pluralist — many liberated souls). ✅

## 4. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Jaina position | Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Change vs permanence | BOTH (sat = origin+decay+permanence) | Buddhism: only flux · Advaita: only being |
| One truth or many | many-sided (anekānta) | Advaita/most: one absolute truth |
| Nature of karma | subtle *matter* | others: mental disposition/adṛṣṭa |
| Number of liberated souls | many (pluralism) | Advaita: one Brahman |
| God | no creator-God; liberated souls (Tīrthaṅkaras) as ideals | Nyāya/Vedānta: Īśvara |

## 5. STATEMENT / QUOTE BANK ⚠️
1. ✅ *"Reality is characterised by origination, decay and permanence."* (utpāda-vyaya-dhrauvya-yuktaṃ sat) — Umāsvāti, *Tattvārtha-sūtra*. 📖 *Source-verified* (Chatterjee-Datta): *"A substance is real (sat). Reality consists of three factors: permanence, origination, and decay."*
2. ✅ *"Ahiṃsā paramo dharmaḥ"* — non-violence is the highest duty.
3. ⚠️ *"Reality is many-sided; our judgements are one-sided; hence say 'syāt'."* — anekānta paraphrase.
