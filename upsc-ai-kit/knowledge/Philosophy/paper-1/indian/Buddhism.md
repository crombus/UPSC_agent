## 1. PRATĪTYASAMUTPĀDA — Dependent Origination ✅

- **Central insight:** *"asmin sati idaṃ bhavati"* — "this being, that arises." Everything arises **in dependence on conditions**; nothing exists independently or permanently. ✅
- It is the **middle path** between eternalism (śāśvatavāda) and annihilationism (ucchedavāda), and between absolute being and absolute non-being.
- Formalised as the **twelve-linked chain (dvādaśa-nidāna / bhava-cakra)**: avidyā → saṃskāra → vijñāna → nāma-rūpa → ṣaḍāyatana → sparśa → vedanā → tṛṣṇā → upādāna → bhava → jāti → jarā-maraṇa. ✅
  - Root cause = **avidyā (ignorance)**; key link that binds = **tṛṣṇā (craving)**. Break the chain (esp. at craving) → nirvāṇa.
- **Two implications flow directly from it:** if everything is conditioned & arising, then (a) everything is **momentary** (kṣaṇikavāda) and (b) there is **no permanent substance/self** (nairātmyavāda).

---

## 2. KṢAṆIKAVĀDA — Doctrine of Momentariness ✅

- **Whatever exists, exists only for a moment (kṣaṇa); the next moment a new entity arises.** Apparent persistence = a rapid *series* (santāna) of causally-linked momentary entities — like a **flame** or a **stream** that looks continuous but is ever-new. ✅
- **Argument from "sattva = arthakriyākāritva":** to be real is to be causally efficient; the causally efficient must produce its effect *now*; a permanent thing would either always or never act → contradiction. Hence the real is momentary. ✅ (This is the classic *inference to momentariness*.)
- **Argument from universal destruction (kṣaṇabhaṅga):** destruction is spontaneous, needs no external cause; things carry their perishing within → they cannot last beyond a moment.

> ⚠️ **Critique:** Nyāya/Jaina — momentariness cannot explain **memory, recognition ("this is the same pot"), moral responsibility, or bondage-liberation** across a discontinuous series. *Buddhist reply:* the santāna (causal series) + pratyabhijñā as re-cognition explains continuity without a permanent self.

---

## 3. NAIRĀTMYAVĀDA — No-Self / No-Substance ✅

- **There is no permanent, unchanging ātman.** What we call "self" is a bundle of **five skandhas**: rūpa (body), vedanā (feeling), saṃjñā (perception), saṃskāra (dispositions), vijñāna (consciousness) — all momentary, all conditioned. ✅
- 📚 **Chariot analogy (Milindapañha):** "chariot" is just a name for the assembled parts; no "chariot" over and above them. Likewise "self" is a label for the skandha-aggregate.
- **Extended to all things = dharma-nairātmya** (esp. in Mahāyāna): no thing has svabhāva (own-being).
- **Reconciles with rebirth & karma:** no transmigrating soul, yet the causal series carries karmic momentum — *"neither the same nor another"* (na ca so na ca añño). Rebirth = continuity of the stream, like one flame lighting the next.

> ⚠️ **The paradox to flag:** how can there be moral responsibility, bondage & liberation *without* a self? The Buddhist answer is always the **causal continuum (santāna)**, not a substance. This is the crux of most critique questions (and the 2022 P-II Q5c "immortality of soul as postulate for rebirth — examine w.r.t. Buddhism").

## 4. THE FOUR SCHOOLS (realism → śūnya) ✅

| School | Yāna | External world? | Core thesis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vaibhāṣika** | Hīnayāna | Real, **directly perceived** | Direct realism (bāhya-pratyakṣa-vāda) |
| **Sautrāntika** | Hīnayāna | Real, but only **inferred** | Representative realism (bāhyānumeya-vāda) |
| **Yogācāra / Vijñānavāda** | Mahāyāna | **Unreal**; only consciousness | Subjective idealism; ālaya-vijñāna (store-consciousness) |
| **Mādhyamika / Śūnyavāda** | Mahāyāna | Neither real nor unreal | **Śūnyatā** — all is empty of svabhāva; two-truths (saṃvṛti/paramārtha), Nāgārjuna |

> ⚠️ **Exam use:** the four schools show an internal logic — pushing dependent-origination to its limit lands in Nāgārjuna's śūnyavāda (everything empty of own-being). Cite the gradient to answer "critically examine Buddhist schools."
> 📖 *Source-verified* (C.D. Sharma / Śaṅkara's classification): the four are often grouped as **Sarvāstivāda** (= Vaibhāṣika + Sautrāntika), **Vijñānavāda** (Yogācāra), and **Śūnyavāda** (Mādhyamika) — worth citing to show mastery of the Hīnayāna/Mahāyāna split.

## 5. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Buddhism | Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Self | no permanent self (anātman) | Nyāya/Vedānta/Jaina: real ātman/jīva |
| Persistence | momentary series | Jaina: permanence-in-change · Advaita: changeless Brahman |
| Substance | none (dharmas only) | Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika: substances (dravya) |
| Ground of continuity | causal stream (santāna) | others: enduring soul |
| Metaphysical stance | middle path / anti-substantialism | eternalism & annihilationism both rejected |

## 8. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 8.1 — "'Immortality of Soul is a necessary postulate for rebirth.' Critically examine with reference to Buddhism." (2022 P-II Q5c, 10m)
```
Thesis  : Common view (Nyāya/Vedānta) — rebirth needs an enduring soul.
Buddhist: Rejects it — nairātmyavāda; rebirth explained by causal santāna, not a transmigrating ātman.
Analogy : One flame lighting the next; seed→sprout — continuity without identity.
Assess  : Buddhism DISPROVES the "necessary postulate" claim → shows rebirth is conceivable without soul-immortality.
Concl   : Karmic continuity ≠ soul-substance; the postulate is not necessary.
```

### 8.2 — "State and evaluate Buddhism as a religion without God." (2024 P-II Q7a, 20m)
```
Intro : Buddhism = non-theistic; no creator-God, no eternal soul.
B1    : Pratītyasamutpāda replaces divine creation — world is conditioned, not created.
B2    : Nairātmyavāda — salvation (nirvāṇa) by self-effort, not grace.
B3    : Ethical-psychological religion (Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path).
Eval  : "Religion without God" — yes in theistic sense; but nirvāṇa/dharma function as ultimate concern.
Concl : A soteriology without theology (⚠️ bridge to Phil-of-Religion 'Religion without God').
```
