# SCHOOLS OF VEDĀNTA — Syllabus Item 19

> **Syllabus:** Brahman; Īśvara; Ātman; Jīva; Jagat; Māyā; Avidyā; Adhyāsa; Mokṣa; Apṛthaksiddhi; Pañcavidhabheda.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Chatterjee & Datta; C.D. Sharma; Radhakrishnan) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** Uttara-Mīmāṃsā — interpretations of the *Prasthāna-trayī* (Upaniṣads, Brahma-Sūtra, Gītā). Three principal schools: **Advaita** (Śaṃkara), **Viśiṣṭādvaita** (Rāmānuja), **Dvaita** (Madhva).

## 1. ADVAITA VEDĀNTA (Śaṃkara) — non-dualism ✅

**Central thesis:** *Brahma satyaṃ jagat mithyā, jīvo brahmaiva nāparaḥ* — "Brahman is real, the world is false, the jīva is none other than Brahman."

| Concept | Advaita account |
|---|---|
| **Brahman** | The sole reality — **nirguṇa** (attributeless), pure Being-Consciousness-Bliss (sat-cit-ānanda), one-without-a-second. |
| **Īśvara** | Brahman *as associated with māyā* — the saguṇa, personal God (creator); real only at the empirical (vyāvahārika) level. |
| **Ātman** | The individual self, in reality *identical* with Brahman. "Tat tvam asi." |
| **Jīva** | Ātman *apparently* limited by avidyā/antaḥkaraṇa; not really different from Brahman. |
| **Jagat (world)** | **Vivarta** — an *unreal appearance* (not a real transformation) of Brahman, like the snake on the rope; empirically real, absolutely false. |
| **Māyā / Avidyā** | The cosmic power / individual ignorance that projects plurality; **anirvacanīya** (indescribable — neither real nor unreal). |
| **Adhyāsa** | **Superimposition** — mutual mis-attribution of the real (self) and non-real (not-self), root of bondage (opening of Śaṃkara's Bhāṣya). |
| **Mokṣa** | Removal of avidyā by **jñāna** → realisation of the ever-present identity Ātman = Brahman; **jīvanmukti** possible. Not something *produced*, only *revealed*. |

- **Three levels of reality:** pāramārthika (absolute — Brahman), vyāvahārika (empirical — world/Īśvara), prātibhāsika (illusory — snake-rope, dream). ✅
- **Causation = vivartavāda:** effect is an *unreal appearance* of the cause (contrast Sāṃkhya pariṇāma). ✅

> ⚠️ **The 2022 P-II Q8c question** ("Does Śaṃkara's Brahman leave room for theism?") turns on nirguṇa vs saguṇa: strictly, the personal God (Īśvara) belongs only to the vyāvahārika level → Advaita subordinates theism to the impersonal Absolute.

---

## 2. VIŚIṢṬĀDVAITA (Rāmānuja) — qualified non-dualism ✅

**Central thesis:** reality is ONE Brahman, but a Brahman *qualified by* (viśiṣṭa) real, inseparable modes — the conscious selves (cit) and matter (acit). Non-dual *whole*, but with real internal distinctions.

| Concept | Rāmānuja account |
|---|---|
| **Brahman** | **Saguṇa** — the personal God (Viṣṇu/Nārāyaṇa), with infinite auspicious qualities; NOT attributeless. Rejects nirguṇa Brahman. |
| **Jīva & Jagat** | **Real** modes / attributes forming God's "body" (śarīra-śarīrī-bhāva); God is the indwelling **soul (Antaryāmin)** of all. |
| **Māyā** | God's *real* creative power (not illusion); the world is a **real transformation**, not appearance. Rejects Advaita's māyā-as-illusion. |
| **Mokṣa** | Not merger/identity, but the freed jīva attains likeness to God and eternal loving communion (bhakti + prapatti/surrender); jīva remains a distinct real self. |

- **APṚTHAKSIDDHI (key term):** "inseparable existence" — cit and acit are inseparable *attributes* of Brahman, having no independent existence apart from it, yet **really distinct** from it. This is how Rāmānuja is *non-dual* (one substance) yet admits *real difference* (qualified). ✅
- Refutes Advaita's māyā by the **seven objections (sapta-vidhā anupapatti).**

---

## 3. DVAITA (Madhva) — dualism ✅
- **Brahman (Viṣṇu), jīvas, and jagat are all eternally REAL and distinct.** God is the independent (svatantra) reality; jīvas & world are dependent (paratantra) realities.
- **PAÑCAVIDHABHEDA (fivefold real difference)** — the signature doctrine: eternal, real distinctions between — ✅
  1. God ↔ jīva, 2. God ↔ matter, 3. jīva ↔ jīva, 4. jīva ↔ matter, 5. matter ↔ matter.
- Mokṣa = the jīva's eternal blissful service of God (with a *gradation* of released souls — unusual). Emphasises bhakti + God's grace.

## 4. COMPARISON GRID (the exam gold) ⚠️
| Issue | Advaita | Viśiṣṭādvaita | Dvaita |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brahman | nirguṇa | saguṇa (Viṣṇu) | saguṇa (Viṣṇu), supreme |
| Reality of world | false (vivarta) | real (pariṇāma) | real |
| Jīva–Brahman | identical | part/mode (apṛthaksiddhi) | wholly distinct (bheda) |
| Māyā | illusion-power | real creative power | — (no illusionism) |
| Causation | vivartavāda | pariṇāma (Brahman-pariṇāma) | — |
| Mokṣa | identity, jīvanmukti | communion, distinct self | eternal service, graded |
| Path | jñāna | bhakti/prapatti | bhakti + grace |

## 7. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 7.1 — "Critically examine Śaṃkara's idea of Brahman. Does it leave room for theism?" (2022 P-II Q8c, 15m)
```
Intro : Brahman = nirguṇa sat-cit-ānanda, one-without-a-second.
B1    : nirguṇa (pāramārthika) vs saguṇa Īśvara (vyāvahārika) — two levels.
B2    : Theism (personal God) is real only empirically; sublated in Brahman-realisation.
Assess: Advaita ACCOMMODATES theism at a lower level but SUBORDINATES it → not ultimately theistic.
Concl : Room for theism as vyavahāra, not as final truth.
```

### 7.2 — "Is liberation possible without a real agent? Advaita vs Viśiṣṭādvaita." (2022 P-II Q7b, 15m)
```
Advaita       : jīva is not ultimately a real distinct agent; mokṣa = realising identity (agent dissolves).
Viśiṣṭādvaita : jīva is a REAL, distinct self (apṛthaksiddhi); liberation = its communion with God — needs a real agent.
Assess        : the disagreement traces to vivarta (unreal world/jīva) vs pariṇāma (real modes).
```

### 7.3 — "Discuss jīvanmukti in Advaita Vedānta." (2025 P-II Q5d, 10m)
```
Def   : embodied liberation — freed while living, once avidyā is destroyed by jñāna.
Mech  : prārabdha karma sustains the body (potter's-wheel momentum); no new karma binds.
State : the jīvanmukta sees Brahman everywhere, acts without ego/attachment.
Concl : mokṣa is not post-mortem attainment but present realisation.
```
