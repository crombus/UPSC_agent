# SĀṂKHYA — Syllabus Item 16

> **Syllabus:** Prakṛti; Puruṣa; Causation; Liberation.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Chatterjee & Datta; C.D. Sharma) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** Āstika, **dualist** (puruṣa–prakṛti), **atheist**, evolutionist school. Founder: Kapila. Provides the metaphysics for Yoga.

---

## 1. PURUṢA (the conscious principle) ✅
- **Pure consciousness** — the eternal, self-luminous **witness (sākṣī)**; NOT the body, mind, or ego.
- Attributes: inactive (niṣkriya), attributeless, beyond the guṇas, mere spectator.
- **Plurality of puruṣas (proof):** since birth/death/faculties differ across beings, and some are liberated while others bound, there must be *many* puruṣas — not one universal self (against Advaita's single ātman). ✅
- **Proofs for puruṣa's existence:** aggregates (like prakṛti's evolutes) exist *for another's* use; there must be a controller/experiencer beyond the guṇas; the striving for liberation presupposes a self that seeks it.

---

## 2. PRAKṚTI & THE THREE GUṆAS ✅
- **Prakṛti** = the single, eternal, unconscious, uncaused **material cause** of the whole world (mind included). Known only by **inference** (from its effects) — it is imperceptible.
- Composed of **three guṇas** in equilibrium:
  | Guṇa | Nature | Function |
  |---|---|---|
  | **Sattva** | light, buoyant | manifestation, knowledge, pleasure |
  | **Rajas** | mobile, stimulating | activity, energy, pain |
  | **Tamas** | heavy, veiling | restraint, inertia, delusion |
- **Proof of prakṛti (satkāryavāda-based):** the finite, limited, correlated world must have an unlimited, unmanifest cause; effects imply a subtle homogeneous root. ✅
- In dissolution the guṇas rest in equilibrium (avyakta); creation begins when equilibrium is disturbed.

---

## 3. CAUSATION — SATKĀRYAVĀDA (the effect pre-exists) ✅ (star sub-topic)
- **The effect pre-exists in its material cause** *before* production (only *manifested*, not newly created). Curd is latent in milk; oil in the seed. ✅
- **Sub-type: pariṇāmavāda** — effect is a *real transformation* (pariṇāma) of the cause. (Distinguish from Advaita's *vivartavāda* = *unreal appearance*.)
- **Five arguments for satkāryavāda:** ✅
  1. **Asadakaraṇāt** — the non-existent cannot be produced (nothing comes from nothing).
  2. **Upādāna-grahaṇāt** — a specific material is needed for a specific effect (curd only from milk).
  3. **Sarvasambhavābhāvāt** — not everything can arise from everything (implies effect latent in specific cause).
  4. **Śaktasya śakyakaraṇāt** — only a *capable* cause produces its effect (capacity = effect already present potentially).
  5. **Kāraṇabhāvāt** — the effect is the essence of the cause (non-different from it).
- **Consequence:** the world is a real transformation of prakṛti → Sāṃkhya is a **realist evolutionism**.

---

## 4. EVOLUTION — the 23 tattvas ✅
From prakṛti, on contact with puruṣa, evolves (in order):
```
Prakṛti → Mahat/Buddhi (intellect) → Ahaṃkāra (ego)
   ├─(sāttvika) → Manas + 5 jñānendriyas + 5 karmendriyas  (11 organs)
   └─(tāmasa)  → 5 tanmātras (subtle essences) → 5 mahābhūtas (gross elements)
```
- Total **25 = Puruṣa + Prakṛti + Mahat + Ahaṃkāra + Manas + 10 senses + 5 tanmātras + 5 bhūtas.**
- Evolution is **teleological**: prakṛti evolves *unconsciously but purposively* — "for the liberation of puruṣa."

---

## 5. BONDAGE & LIBERATION (kaivalya) ✅
- **Bondage = non-discrimination (aviveka):** puruṣa wrongly identifies with buddhi/prakṛti's states, mistaking prakṛti's experiences for its own. It is a *reflection* problem, not real change in puruṣa.
- **Liberation (kaivalya = "aloneness") = viveka-jñāna:** the discriminative knowledge *"prakṛti is not my self; I am the pure witness."* Prakṛti, having accomplished puruṣa's purpose, ceases to evolve *for that puruṣa* — like a **dancer who retires once seen** (Sāṃkhya-Kārikā). ✅
- **Jīvanmukti:** liberation attainable while living; the body continues by momentum (like a potter's wheel spinning after the push) until prārabdha karma exhausts.
- **No God needed:** unconscious prakṛti + plurality of puruṣas explains everything → Sāṃkhya is **nirīśvara (atheistic)**. Its argument against God is a stock cross-reference for Nyāya God-proof critiques. ✅

## 6. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Sāṃkhya | Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Causation | satkāryavāda / pariṇāmavāda (real transformation) | Nyāya: asatkāryavāda · Advaita: vivartavāda (unreal) |
| Number of selves | many puruṣas | Advaita: one Brahman |
| God | none (atheist) | Nyāya/Yoga: Īśvara |
| Self & consciousness | puruṣa = pure consciousness, inactive | Nyāya: consciousness adventitious |
| World | real evolute of prakṛti | Advaita: māyā/appearance |
