# YOGA — Syllabus Item 17

> **Syllabus:** Citta; Cittavṛtti; Kleśas; Samādhi; Kaivalya.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Chatterjee & Datta; C.D. Sharma; Patañjali, *Yoga-Sūtra*) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** Āstika. Shares Sāṃkhya's metaphysics (dualism of puruṣa–prakṛti) but is **theistic (seśvara)** and adds a **practical discipline** for liberation. Founder: Patañjali.

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
YOGA = Sāṃkhya metaphysics + Īśvara + PRACTICE
   Definition: "Yogaḥ citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ" (Yoga = restraint of the modifications of citta)
        │
  CITTA (mind-stuff, an evolute of prakṛti) ── has ── CITTAVṚTTIS (5 modifications)
        │                                              hindered by ── KLEŚAS (5 afflictions)
   remove vṛttis via ── AṢṬĀṄGA YOGA (8 limbs) ──► SAMĀDHI ──► KAIVALYA
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — 8 limbs (aṣṭāṅga): "Yamas Never Approach Painful Persons During Deep Sleep"** = **Ya**ma · **Ni**yama · **Ā**sana · **Prā**ṇāyāma · **Pra**tyāhāra · **Dhā**raṇā · **Dhyā**na · **Samādhi**.

---

## 1. CITTA & CITTAVṚTTI ✅
- **Citta** = "mind-stuff": the first evolute of prakṛti (= Sāṃkhya's *mahat/buddhi + ahaṃkāra + manas* functioning together). Being of sattva, it is *translucent* and can reflect the light of puruṣa.
- **The reflection problem:** puruṣa (pure consciousness) is reflected in citta; the empirical self mistakes citta's modifications for its own experience → this is bondage.
- **Cittavṛtti = modifications/mental states** of citta. **Five kinds:** ✅
  1. **Pramāṇa** (valid cognition), 2. **Viparyaya** (error), 3. **Vikalpa** (verbal/imaginary), 4. **Nidrā** (sleep), 5. **Smṛti** (memory).
- **Yoga's aim = citta-vṛtti-nirodha** (cessation of ALL vṛttis). When the vṛttis cease, puruṣa "abides in its own nature" (*tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe'vasthānam*). ✅

**Five stages of citta (cittabhūmi):** kṣipta (restless) · mūḍha (torpid) · vikṣipta (distracted) · ekāgra (one-pointed) · niruddha (restrained) — only the last two are yogic.

---

## 2. KLEŚAS — the five afflictions ✅
The root causes of suffering and bondage that colour the citta: ✅
| # | Kleśa | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Avidyā** | ignorance (mistaking non-self for self) — root of the other four |
| 2 | **Asmitā** | egoism (identifying puruṣa with buddhi) |
| 3 | **Rāga** | attachment to pleasure |
| 4 | **Dveṣa** | aversion to pain |
| 5 | **Abhiniveśa** | clinging to life / fear of death |
- Kleśas produce karma → karmāśaya (karmic residue) → rebirth. Yoga uproots them (via discrimination + kriyā-yoga: tapas, svādhyāya, Īśvara-praṇidhāna).

---

## 3. AṢṬĀṄGA YOGA — the eight limbs ✅
| Limb | Meaning | Type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 **Yama** | restraints (ahiṃsā, satya, asteya, brahmacarya, aparigraha) | external/ethical |
| 2 **Niyama** | observances (śauca, santoṣa, tapas, svādhyāya, Īśvara-praṇidhāna) | external/ethical |
| 3 **Āsana** | steady posture | external/bodily |
| 4 **Prāṇāyāma** | breath-control | external/bodily |
| 5 **Pratyāhāra** | withdrawal of senses | bridge |
| 6 **Dhāraṇā** | concentration/fixation | internal (antaraṅga) |
| 7 **Dhyāna** | meditation (steady flow) | internal |
| 8 **Samādhi** | absorption | internal |
- Limbs 6–8 together = **saṃyama** (the yogic core).

---

## 4. SAMĀDHI ✅
The culminating state of absorption, of two grades:
- **Samprajñāta (conscious) samādhi** — concentration *on an object*; still has vṛttis of a subtle kind; stages: savitarka, savicāra, sānanda, sāsmita.
- **Asamprajñāta (supra-conscious) samādhi** — *objectless*; all vṛttis (even sāttvika) cease; only latent saṃskāras remain, then are burnt. This directly precedes liberation. ✅
- Powers (siddhis) may arise via saṃyama but are *obstacles* to be renounced.

---

## 5. KAIVALYA (liberation) & ĪŚVARA ✅
- **Kaivalya** = same as Sāṃkhya: the "aloneness" of puruṣa, freed from all identification with prakṛti/citta; the guṇas resolve back, citta dissolves, puruṣa shines in its own nature. ✅
- **Īśvara (the Yoga innovation):** a *special puruṣa* (puruṣa-viśeṣa) — eternally free, untouched by kleśas, karma, and their fruits; omniscient; the first teacher (guru of even the ancients); denoted by **praṇava (OM)**. ✅
  - **Role:** NOT a creator (prakṛti is the material cause) and NOT strictly needed metaphysically — Īśvara is chiefly an **object of devotion (Īśvara-praṇidhāna)** that removes obstacles and accelerates samādhi.
  - ⚠️ **Exam point:** Yoga's God is a *practical/devotional* aid, a "perfect model," not a cosmic cause — contrast Nyāya's creator-God and Advaita's Brahman.

## 7. STATEMENT / QUOTE BANK ⚠️
1. ✅ *"Yogaḥ citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ"* — Yoga is the restraint of the modifications of the mind (Yoga-Sūtra I.2).
2. ✅ *"Then the seer abides in his own nature"* (tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe'vasthānam, I.3).
3. ✅ *"Īśvara is a special puruṣa, untouched by afflictions, actions, and their fruits."* (kleśa-karma-vipākāśayair aparāmṛṣṭaḥ puruṣa-viśeṣa Īśvaraḥ, I.24).

## 9. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 9.1 — "Citta, Cittavṛtti, Kleśas, Samādhi, Kaivalya." (Syllabus item 17)
```
Citta      : mind-stuff (prakṛti-evolute) reflecting puruṣa.
Cittavṛtti : 5 modifications; yoga = their nirodha.
Kleśas     : avidyā, asmitā, rāga, dveṣa, abhiniveśa (root = avidyā).
Samādhi    : samprajñāta → asamprajñāta.
Kaivalya   : aloneness of puruṣa; role of Īśvara-praṇidhāna.
```

### 9.2 — "Role of Īśvara in Yoga." (Syllabus item 17)
```
Frame : puruṣa-viśeṣa, ever-free, omniscient, OM.
Role  : devotional aid (Īśvara-praṇidhāna) removing obstacles — NOT creator.
Contr : vs Nyāya creator-God, vs Sāṃkhya atheism.
Concl : theism grafted onto Sāṃkhya for practical soteriology.
```
