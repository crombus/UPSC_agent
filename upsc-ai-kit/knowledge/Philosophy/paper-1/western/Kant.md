# KANT — Syllabus Item 4

> **Syllabus:** Possibility of Synthetic a priori Judgments; Space and Time; Categories; Ideas of Reason; Antinomies; Critique of Proofs for the Existence of God.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Y. Masih; Copleston; Kenny; Kant, *Critique of Pure Reason*) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** The great **synthesis** of Rationalism + Empiricism. "Awakened from dogmatic slumber by Hume." His **Copernican Revolution**: objects conform to mind, not mind to objects.

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
KANT'S PROBLEM: How is synthetic a priori knowledge possible? (i.e. how is science valid?)
   COPERNICAN REVOLUTION: the mind SHAPES experience (objects conform to knower)
        │
   Knowledge = SENSIBILITY (a priori forms: SPACE & TIME) + UNDERSTANDING (12 CATEGORIES)
        │  applies ONLY to PHENOMENA (appearances) — NOT to NOUMENA (things-in-themselves)
        ▼
   REASON over-reaches into noumena → ANTINOMIES + failed GOD-PROOFS
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — "Sensibility Supplies Space-Time; Understanding Uses Categories."** Beyond experience, Reason runs into contradictions.

---

## 1. THE CENTRAL PROBLEM — synthetic a priori judgments ✅
Two distinctions combine into four kinds of judgment:
| | **A priori** (independent of experience, necessary/universal) | **A posteriori** (from experience) |
|---|---|---|
| **Analytic** (predicate contained in subject) | "All bodies are extended" — trivial | (none) |
| **Synthetic** (predicate adds new info) | **⭐ "7+5=12"; "every event has a cause"** | "This body is heavy" |

- **The key category = SYNTHETIC A PRIORI:** informative (synthetic) yet necessary & universal (a priori). Maths, geometry, and the principles of physics are all of this kind. ✅
- **Rationalists** couldn't explain the *synthetic* (informative) side; **empiricists** (Hume) couldn't explain the *a priori* (necessary) side. Kant's whole *Critique* asks: **How are synthetic a priori judgments possible?** — because on them rests the validity of mathematics and science. ✅

---

## 2. THE COPERNICAN REVOLUTION ✅
- Instead of the mind conforming to objects (which never yields necessity), suppose **objects conform to the mind's structure**. The knowing subject actively *constitutes* experience by imposing its a priori forms. ✅
- Consequence: we can know things only *as they appear* to us (**phenomena**), shaped by our cognitive forms — never things *as they are in themselves* (**noumena**). ✅

---

## 3. SPACE & TIME — the forms of Sensibility (Transcendental Aesthetic) ✅
- **Space** = the a priori form of **outer** sense; **Time** = the a priori form of **inner** (and all) sense. ✅
- They are **not** things, nor properties of things-in-themselves, nor empirical concepts — they are the mind's **pure intuitions**, the subjective conditions under which anything can be sensed.
- This explains **geometry (synthetic a priori via space)** and **arithmetic (via time)**: they are necessarily true of all objects of experience because space & time are *our* forms of receiving them.

---

## 4. THE CATEGORIES — forms of Understanding (Transcendental Analytic) ✅
- Sensibility gives raw intuitions; the **understanding** organises them via **12 a priori concepts (categories)** in 4 groups (Quantity, Quality, Relation, Modality). ✅
- The star category is **causality** (under Relation): Hume said causation is mere habit; Kant answers that **causality is an a priori category the mind necessarily applies** to order phenomena → so "every event has a cause" is synthetic a priori, valid for all experience. This *rescues science from Hume*. ✅
- **"Thoughts without content are empty; intuitions without concepts are blind."** Knowledge = categories applied to sensory intuitions. Hence categories are valid **only within possible experience (phenomena)**.

---

## 5. IDEAS OF REASON & THE ANTINOMIES (Transcendental Dialectic) ✅
- **Reason** seeks the unconditioned totality, generating three **transcendental Ideas**: the **Soul** (totality of the mental), the **World** (totality of phenomena), **God** (totality of all being). These are *regulative* (useful guides), not *constitutive* (they don't give knowledge). ✅
- When reason mistakes these Ideas for objects of knowledge (applying categories beyond experience), it falls into **illusion**:
  - **Paralogisms** — fallacious proofs of a substantial soul.
  - **ANTINOMIES** — pairs of equally "provable" contradictory theses about the World: ✅
    | # | Thesis | Antithesis |
    |---|---|---|
    | 1 | world has a beginning in time & is limited in space | world is infinite |
    | 2 | there are simple (indivisible) substances | everything is infinitely divisible |
    | 3 | there is freedom (uncaused causality) | all is determined by natural causation |
    | 4 | a necessary being exists | no necessary being |
  - **Resolution:** the antinomies arise from wrongly treating phenomena as things-in-themselves. Once we confine categories to phenomena, thesis & antithesis can both be handled (1–2 both false; 3–4 both true in different domains — freedom in the noumenal, determinism in the phenomenal). ✅

---

## 6. CRITIQUE OF THE PROOFS FOR GOD'S EXISTENCE ✅ (high-yield, cross-paper)
God lies beyond possible experience → the categories cannot reach Him → all theoretical proofs fail:
1. **Ontological argument (refuted):** *existence is not a predicate.* Adding "exists" to the concept of God adds nothing to its content — "a hundred real dollars contain no more than a hundred possible dollars." You cannot define a thing into existence. ✅
2. **Cosmological argument:** depends on the ontological (to identify the necessary being with the most-perfect being) → falls with it; also misapplies causality beyond experience.
3. **Physico-theological (design) argument:** at best proves an *architect* (working on given matter), not an omnipotent *creator*; and still smuggles in the ontological to reach infinity.
- ✅ **Result:** God's existence can be neither proved nor disproved by theoretical reason. Kant instead grounds God as a **postulate of practical (moral) reason** — a necessary presupposition of morality. ("I had to deny knowledge to make room for faith.")

## 10. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 10.1 — "How are synthetic a priori judgments possible?" (item 4, core)
```
Intro : the two distinctions → the puzzle of synthetic a priori (maths, science).
Body  : Copernican revolution; space/time (sensibility) + categories (understanding) supply the a priori.
Point : they're valid because the mind constitutes phenomena — but ONLY for phenomena.
Concl : synthetic a priori possible ⇒ metaphysics-as-science of noumena impossible.
```

### 10.2 — "Kant's critique of the proofs for the existence of God." (2023/2022 P-II adjacents; item 4)
```
Frame : God = noumenon → categories (esp. causality/existence) cannot apply.
Body  : ontological (existence≠predicate) → cosmological (parasitic + misuses causality) → design (only an architect).
Turn  : theoretical reason fails BOTH ways; God as postulate of PRACTICAL reason.
Concl : "denied knowledge to make room for faith."
```
