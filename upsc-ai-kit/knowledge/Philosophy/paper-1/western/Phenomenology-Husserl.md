## 1. METHOD — INTENTIONALITY, EPOCHÉ, REDUCTION ✅
- **Intentionality (from Brentano):** the defining mark of consciousness — it is always **"consciousness *of* something."** Every mental act (perceiving, judging, imagining, willing) is directed at an **object (noema)** via an **act (noesis)**. Consciousness is not a sealed box but an *opening-onto*. ✅
- **The Epoché ("bracketing" / phenomenological reduction):** *suspend* the **"natural attitude"** — our unquestioned belief that the world exists independently. We don't *deny* the world; we put its existence-status "in brackets" (`[ ]`), **bracketing all assumptions, theories, and the natural-scientific worldview**, to attend purely to *how phenomena present themselves to consciousness*. ✅
  - Result: a **presuppositionless** starting point — description, not explanation or causal theory.
- **Transcendental reduction:** a further step back to the **transcendental ego** — pure consciousness as the field within which all meaning is constituted. (Husserl's later turn toward transcendental idealism.) ⚠️
- **Descriptive, not speculative:** phenomenology *describes* the structures of experience; it neither builds metaphysical systems nor offers empirical-causal explanations.

---

## 2. THEORY OF ESSENCES (EIDETIC REDUCTION) ✅
- **Aim:** move from the *particular fact* to its **essence (eidos)** — the invariant "whatness" without which the phenomenon could not be what it is.
- **Method — "free/imaginative variation":** take an instance (e.g. a perceived colour, or "a triangle"), vary it freely in imagination — change size, shade, angle — and note **what cannot be varied away without destroying the phenomenon**. That invariant residue **is** the essence. ✅
- **Wesensschau ("intuition of essences"):** essences are *directly intuited*, given to consciousness with self-evidence — not inferred, not abstracted empirically. A **categorial/eidetic intuition** parallels ordinary sense-intuition but grasps the universal. ✅
- **Essences are ideal, a priori, and objective** — not psychological contents and not Platonic entities in a separate heaven; they are the *meanings* present in and to consciousness.
> ⚠️ **Exam link:** the eidetic method is why phenomenology claims to be a **rigorous science of essences** (*strenge Wissenschaft*) rather than mere introspection.

---

## 3. AVOIDANCE OF PSYCHOLOGISM ✅
- **What psychologism is:** the doctrine (Mill, some 19th-c logicians) that **logic and mathematics are branches of empirical psychology** — that the "laws of thought" are generalisations about how the human mind *happens* to think.
- **Husserl's refutation (*Prolegomena to Pure Logic*, Vol 1 of *Logical Investigations*):**
  1. **Category confusion:** psychological laws are *empirical, vague, probable, contingent*; logical laws are *exact, necessary, a priori*. You cannot derive the necessary from the contingent. ✅
  2. **Self-refutation / relativism charge:** if logic depended on the human constitution, truth would be **species-relative** — "2+2=4" would hold only *for us*. This makes truth relative and is **self-defeating** (the very claim would only be "true for us"). ✅
  3. **Ideality of meaning:** the *content* "2+2=4" is one **ideal, timeless meaning**; the countless real acts of thinking it are datable mental events. Confusing the ideal content with the real act = psychologism's core error (**act vs content** distinction). ✅
- **Positive upshot:** logic and essences belong to a domain of **ideal objectivity**; phenomenology studies consciousness *without* reducing truth to psychology. This defends the **objectivity of knowledge** against relativism.

## 4. INTER-THINKER DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Husserl | Psychologism (Mill) | Descartes | Existentialists (heirs) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Logic/truth | ideal, a priori | empirical mental law | innate/clear ideas | (presupposed) |
| Method | epoché + eidetic reduction | empirical generalisation | methodic doubt | describe *existence* |
| Consciousness | intentional, world-directed | mental states | private substance | being-in-the-world |
| Starting point | presuppositionless phenomena | psychology | the doubting ego | thrown existence |

> ⚠️ **Husserl vs Descartes:** both seek a certain, presuppositionless foundation via a "reduction"; but Husserl's ego is *intentional* (always of-the-world), not a windowless res cogitans. **Heidegger's critique:** the epoché wrongly brackets *existence* — but existence (Dasein's being-in-the-world) is exactly what matters → the existentialist turn.

## 7. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 7.1 — "Explain Husserl's phenomenological method." (recurring)
```
Intro : "back to the things themselves" — presuppositionless descriptive science of consciousness.
B1    : intentionality — consciousness is always OF an object (noesis–noema).
B2    : epoché — bracket the natural attitude / world's existence → pure phenomena;
        eidetic reduction — free variation → intuition of essence (Wesensschau).
Assess: rigorous & anti-reductive; but transcendental ego risks a new idealism (Heidegger's charge).
Concl : founds a method that reorients philosophy toward lived experience.
```

### 7.2 — "How does Husserl avoid/refute psychologism?" (15m)
```
Intro : psychologism = logic is a branch of empirical psychology.
Body  : (i) necessary/exact logic ≠ contingent/vague psychology;
        (ii) relativism/self-refutation — truth would be species-relative;
        (iii) ideal CONTENT vs real ACT — the meaning is timeless, the thinking is datable.
Crit  : later "transcendental idealism" reintroduces a subject-relativity worry.
Concl : secures the objectivity/ideality of logic and meaning against relativism.
```
