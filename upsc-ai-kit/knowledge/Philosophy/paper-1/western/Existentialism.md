# EXISTENTIALISM (Kierkegaard, Sartre, Heidegger) — Syllabus Item 10

> **Syllabus:** Existence and Essence; Choice, Responsibility and Authentic Existence; Being-in-the-world and Temporality.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Sartre, *Existentialism is a Humanism*, *Being and Nothingness*; Heidegger, *Being and Time*; Kierkegaard; Y. Masih; Copleston) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** A revolt against system-philosophy and abstraction — **the concrete, existing, choosing individual comes first.** Three very different thinkers: **Kierkegaard** (theistic, faith), **Sartre** (atheistic, radical freedom), **Heidegger** (fundamental ontology of *Dasein*). **Exam angle: hold the three apart while showing the shared primacy of existence.**

## 1. EXISTENCE PRECEDES ESSENCE ✅
- **The core reversal:** for a manufactured thing (a paper-knife) the **essence** (its design/function) precedes its **existence** — the maker conceives it first. Traditional metaphysics (and theism) treated *man* the same way: a divine artisan makes us to a plan. **Sartre denies this:** *"man first exists, encounters himself, surges up in the world — and defines himself afterwards."* ✅
- **Consequence:** there is **no fixed human nature**, no blueprint. *"Man is nothing else but what he makes of himself."* We are **not** determined by essence, God, or biology; we are a **project**, self-created through action.
- **Kierkegaard's version:** truth is not an objective system (attack on **Hegel**) but **"subjectivity"** — what I passionately appropriate and *live*; existence is a task, not a spectacle.
- **Heidegger's version:** *"the essence of Dasein lies in its existence"* — human being is not a *what* (a fixed nature) but a *how*, a being for whom its own being is at issue. ✅

---

## 2. CHOICE, RESPONSIBILITY, AUTHENTIC EXISTENCE ✅
- **Radical freedom (Sartre):** since there is no essence and (atheistically) no God, we are **"condemned to be free"** — freedom is not a property we have but what we *are*; we cannot *not* choose (even refusing to choose is a choice). ✅
- **Total responsibility & anguish:** in choosing for myself I choose an image of **man as he ought to be** — hence *"in choosing myself I choose all men."* This universal responsibility brings **anguish (angoisse)**; **abandonment (délaissement)** — no God, no *a priori* values to lean on; **despair** — I can rely only on what is within my will. ✅
- **Bad faith (mauvaise foi):** the self-deception of **denying one's freedom** — pretending one is fixed by a role, nature, or circumstance ("I *am* a waiter", "I couldn't help it"). Fleeing the anguish of freedom. ✅
- **Authenticity:** to **own one's freedom and responsibility** without excuses, choosing lucidly in the face of anguish. (Kierkegaard's route to it: the **leap of faith** — the passionate, risk-laden commitment of the *religious* stage, "the knight of faith" Abraham.)
- **Kierkegaard's three stages of existence:** **aesthetic** (living for pleasure/the moment → despair/boredom) → **ethical** (living by universal moral duty) → **religious** (individual standing "before God", the leap of faith, suspension of the ethical). Movement between stages is by **choice/leap**, not logical deduction. ✅

---

## 3. BEING-IN-THE-WORLD & TEMPORALITY (Heidegger) ✅
- **Dasein ("being-there"):** Heidegger's term for the human being — the entity that *questions* Being and for whom Being is an issue. Not a subject facing objects, but always-already involved. ✅
- **Being-in-the-world (In-der-Welt-sein):** rejects the Cartesian split of an inner subject vs an external world. Dasein is **not first a mind that then meets a world**; it is *constitutively* engaged — practically absorbed, using tools ("ready-to-hand", *zuhanden*) before theorising ("present-at-hand", *vorhanden*). ✅
- **Thrownness (Geworfenheit):** I find myself *already* thrown into a world, a situation, a history I did not choose. **Facticity.**
- **Care (Sorge)** is the being of Dasein; and Dasein exists as **being-with (Mitsein)** others — but risks losing itself in **"the They" (das Man)** — anonymous conformism, **inauthenticity**. ✅
- **Being-toward-death (Sein-zum-Tode):** death is my **ownmost, non-relational, certain** possibility. Authentically facing my finitude — *anticipatory resoluteness* — individualises me and lets me live authentically; fleeing it (idle talk, curiosity) is inauthentic. ✅
- **Temporality (Zeitlichkeit):** the **meaning of the being of Dasein is time.** Dasein is not *in* clock-time; it *is* temporal — stretched across **having-been (past/thrownness), making-present, and coming-toward (future/projection)**. The **future** (being-toward-death, projecting possibilities) has primacy. Existence is **ecstatic temporality**. ✅

## 4. INTER-THINKER DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Kierkegaard | Sartre | Heidegger |
|---|---|---|---|
| God | theistic (leap of faith) | atheistic ("no God, no excuse") | brackets God (ontology, not theology) |
| Core term | subjectivity, stages, leap | freedom, bad faith, responsibility | Dasein, being-in-the-world, temporality |
| Path to authenticity | religious leap | owning radical freedom | anticipatory resoluteness (being-toward-death) |
| Foe | Hegel's system | determinism, essentialism, bad faith | Cartesian subject-object dualism |
| Mood disclosing truth | anxiety, despair | anguish, nausea | anxiety (Angst), care |

> ⚠️ **Key contrasts:** Sartre makes freedom **absolute** and value **self-created**; Heidegger disliked being called a humanist and put **Being (not the subject)** first — he thought Sartre stayed trapped in the metaphysics of subjectivity. Kierkegaard alone anchors the leap in **God**.

```
Intro : reversal of the artisan model — no fixed human nature; man is a self-making project.
B1    : no God/essence → radical freedom → "condemned to be free"; choosing self = choosing man.
B2    : anguish, abandonment, despair; bad faith vs authenticity.
Link  : Kierkegaard (leap of faith) & Heidegger (essence of Dasein = existence) share the primacy.
Assess: empowering ethics of responsibility; charge — is total freedom/valuelessness self-undermining?
Concl : places the concrete choosing individual at the centre of philosophy.
```

### 7.2 — "Explain Heidegger's being-in-the-world and temporality." (15m)
```
Intro : Dasein — the entity for whom Being is at issue; rejects Cartesian subject/object.
Body  : being-in-the-world (ready-to-hand vs present-at-hand); thrownness, care, being-with, das Man.
        being-toward-death individualises → authenticity; temporality (past-present-future,
        future-primacy) is the meaning of Dasein's being.
Crit  : obscurity; Sartre reads it more humanistically; is authenticity a moral norm?
Concl : recasts the self as temporal, engaged, finite existence — not a thinking substance.
```
