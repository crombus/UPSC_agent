# LATER WITTGENSTEIN — Syllabus Item 8

> **Syllabus:** Meaning and Use; Language-games; Critique of Private Language.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Wittgenstein, *Philosophical Investigations*; Y. Masih; Kenny, *Wittgenstein*) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** The *second* Wittgenstein (*Investigations*, 1953) **repudiates his own earlier *Tractatus***. Meaning is no longer *picturing*; it is **use in a form of life**. Philosophy's job is not to build theories but to *dissolve* confusions born of language "on holiday". **High-yield — asked frequently; always frame as a turn AGAINST the early self and against positivism.**

---

```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — "USE-GAME-FORM":** meaning is **USE**, uses are organised as **language-GAMES**, and games are grounded in a **FORM of life.** (U-G-F.)

---

## 1. MEANING IS USE ✅
- **The thesis:** *"For a large class of cases... the meaning of a word is its use in the language."* Don't ask what a word *stands for*; ask how it is *employed*.
- **Attack on the referential/"Augustinian" picture:** the idea that every word names an object (his own early view) is a primitive picture — fine for "table", useless for "the", "if", "five", "pain", "meaning". Words are **tools** in a box; their function, not a corresponding object, gives meaning. ✅
- **No hidden mental essence:** understanding a word is not an inner experience but a **mastery of a technique** — being able to *go on* using it correctly.
- **Family resemblance:** words like "game" have **no single defining essence** — board-games, ball-games, solitaire, war-games share "a complicated network of similarities overlapping and criss-crossing", like features running through a family. **Concepts have blurred edges, and that is fine.** ✅ (Kills the Socratic demand for one definition — and the Tractatus's one logical form.)

---

## 2. LANGUAGE-GAMES ✅
- **Definition:** a **language-game** = the whole of language *and the activity into which it is woven*. Language is not one uniform calculus but **countless different games** — describing, commanding, joking, praying, thanking, greeting, guessing riddles, reporting, speculating.
- **The builders' example:** "Slab!", "Block!" — a complete primitive language where words *are* moves in a cooperative activity.
- **Rules are like game-rules:** meaning is governed by rules of use, learned by training, not by grasping a Platonic essence. **New games arise and old ones die** — language is a living, evolving practice.
- **Form of life:** language-games rest on shared human practices — *"to imagine a language is to imagine a form of life."* Agreement in language is agreement not in opinions but **in form of life**. Justifications end here: *"This is simply what I do."* ✅
> ⚠️ **Exam pivot:** because meaning depends on the *game*, philosophical problems arise when a word is dragged out of its home game and misused ("language goes on holiday"). Philosophy = returning words to their everyday use = **therapy**, not theory. *"Philosophy leaves everything as it is."*

---

## 3. THE PRIVATE-LANGUAGE ARGUMENT ✅
- **What is meant:** a *private language* = one whose words refer to **inner sensations knowable only to the speaker**, in principle unteachable to anyone else (the Cartesian/empiricist dream of a purely private inner realm).
- **The argument (the "S/beetle" thought-experiments):**
  - Suppose I name a private sensation "S" and write "S" in a diary each time it recurs. To use "S" correctly I must **re-identify** the same sensation — but there is **no independent check**: *"whatever seems right will be right, and that only means we can't talk about 'right'."* Without a public criterion of correctness, the **distinction between correct and merely-seeming-correct collapses** — so "S" has no rule, no meaning. ✅
  - **Beetle-in-a-box:** everyone has a box no one else can see; each calls its content "beetle". The word "beetle" has a use in the common language *only if* the private object "drops out as irrelevant". → **meaning cannot be grounded in a private object.** ✅
- **Consequence:** language is **essentially public/rule-governed**; a rule cannot be *privately* followed (that would be merely *thinking* one follows it). This **demolishes Cartesian dualism's private mind, classical empiricism's private sense-data, and the referential theory of meaning** in one stroke.
> 🔑 The private-language argument is the *jewel* — it shows the inner/private is parasitic on the outer/public, inverting Descartes.

```

### 7.2 — "Reconstruct Wittgenstein's private-language argument." (15m)
```
Intro : target = language of sensations knowable only to the speaker (Cartesian/empiricist).
Body  : diary-"S": no independent criterion → no correct/seems-correct distinction → no rule → no meaning.
        beetle-in-box: the private object "drops out as irrelevant" to the shared word.
Implic: meaning is essentially public; inner is parasitic on outer → refutes dualism & sense-datum theory.
Crit  : some (Ayer) resist — a lone Crusoe could coin words; reply — even he needs re-identification criteria.
Concl : the inner life is real but its *language* is public — inverts Descartes.
```
