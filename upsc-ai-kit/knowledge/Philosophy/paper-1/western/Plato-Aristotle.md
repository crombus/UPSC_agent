# PLATO & ARISTOTLE — Syllabus Item 1

> **Syllabus:** Ideas; Substance; Form and Matter; Causation; Actuality and Potentiality.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (W.T. Stace, *Critical History of Greek Philosophy*; Y. Masih; Copleston) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis
> **Nature:** The two fountainheads of Western metaphysics. Plato = **transcendent** Forms (idealist); Aristotle = **immanent** forms (realist). The whole item is a *contrast*.

---

## 0. ONE-SCREEN MAP ⚠️

```
PLATO                                    ARISTOTLE (his pupil, his critic)
 Forms/Ideas exist SEPARATELY            Forms exist IN things (immanent)
 (transcendent, in a higher realm)       substance = this concrete individual
 Real = the universal Idea               Real = the primary substance (tode ti)
 World of sense = copy/shadow            matter + form (hylomorphism)
 Knowledge = recollection of Forms       change = potentiality → actuality
 Two worlds                              Four Causes (material/formal/efficient/final)
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — "Plato Points UP, Aristotle points HERE."** Plato's reality is *above* the world; Aristotle's is *in* it.

---

## 1. PLATO — THEORY OF IDEAS/FORMS ✅
- **The Forms (eidos/idea):** eternal, immutable, non-material universals existing in a **transcendent realm**, apart from sensible things. The Form of Beauty, Justice, the Good — perfect archetypes. ✅
- **Two worlds:** the intelligible world of Forms (real, known by reason) vs the sensible world of particulars (a changing, imperfect **copy/participation (methexis)**, known by opinion).
- **The Good** is the highest Form — "the sun" of the intelligible realm, source of being and knowability (Republic).
- **Knowledge = recollection (anamnesis):** learning is the soul recalling Forms it knew before birth.
- 📚 **Allegory of the Cave:** prisoners mistake shadows for reality; the philosopher ascends to the sunlight (the Good) — the ascent from illusion to knowledge of Forms. ✅
- **Substance for Plato = the Form** (the universal is the truly real).

---

## 2. ARISTOTLE'S CRITIQUE OF PLATO'S FORMS ✅
1. **"Third Man" regress:** if a man resembles the Form of Man, a further Form is needed to explain the resemblance → infinite regress.
2. **Separation problem:** Forms *separated* from things cannot explain the being or change of things — they are useless "duplications."
3. **Participation is a mere metaphor** — Plato never explains how particulars "participate" in Forms.
4. Forms cannot be causes of motion (they are static). ✅
> ⚠️ This critique IS the pivot of most Plato-Aristotle questions — Aristotle *immanentises* the Form to fix Plato's transcendence.

---

## 3. ARISTOTLE — SUBSTANCE, FORM & MATTER (hylomorphism) ✅
- **Substance (ousia)** is the fundamental reality. **Primary substance = the concrete individual** ("this man," "this horse" — *tode ti*); secondary substances = species/genus. Reverses Plato: the *individual*, not the universal, is most real. ✅
- **Hylomorphism:** every substance = **matter (hyle)** + **form (morphe)**. Matter = the potential substratum; Form = the essence/structure that makes it *what it is*. A bronze statue = bronze (matter) + shape (form).
- Form is **immanent** (in the thing), not transcendent. Pure form without matter = God (the Unmoved Mover); pure matter without form = prime matter (a limiting concept).

---

## 4. CAUSATION — THE FOUR CAUSES ✅
To fully explain anything, give its four "becauses" (aitia):
| Cause | Question it answers | Statue example |
|---|---|---|
| **Material** | what is it made of? | the bronze |
| **Formal** | what is its form/essence? | the shape of Hermes |
| **Efficient** | what brought it about? | the sculptor |
| **Final (telos)** | what is it for? | to honour the god |
- **Teleology:** the **final cause** is primary — nature does nothing in vain; everything aims at its end (telos). The efficient + formal + final often coincide. ✅

---

## 5. ACTUALITY & POTENTIALITY (Aristotle's theory of change) ✅
- **Change is the passage from potentiality (dynamis) to actuality (energeia/entelecheia).** The acorn is *potentially* an oak; the oak is its actuality. Motion = "the actualisation of the potential *as* potential." ✅
- Solves the **Parmenidean puzzle** (how can being come from non-being?): the effect pre-exists *potentially* in the cause — a middle path between sheer being and sheer non-being.
- **Actuality is prior to potentiality** (in definition, time, and substance): the actual oak explains the acorn's potential, not vice versa.
- **The Unmoved Mover (God):** pure actuality (actus purus), no potentiality; moves the world as the **final cause** — "moves by being loved," the object of all desire and motion. ✅

---

## 6. INTER-THINKER DEBATES ⚠️
| Axis | Plato | Aristotle |
|---|---|---|
| Where are Forms? | transcendent (separate realm) | immanent (in things) |
| What is most real? | the universal Idea | the individual substance |
| Knowledge | recollection of Forms | abstraction from sense-experience |
| Change | devalued (sensible world) | central (potency→act) |
| Causation | Forms as paradigms | four causes, teleology |
| God | the Form of the Good | the Unmoved Mover (pure act) |

## 9. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 9.1 — "Explain Aristotle's theory of causation (Four Causes) and its link to potentiality-actuality." (item 1)
```
Intro : to explain X = state its 4 causes; change = potency→act.
Body  : four-causes table; teleology (final cause primary).
Link  : potency→act solves Parmenides; Unmoved Mover = pure actuality/final cause.
Contr : vs Plato's transcendent Forms as paradigmatic cause.
Concl : Aristotle grounds causation in immanent form + telos.
```

### 9.2 — "Comment on Plato's critique of Democracy." (2025 P-II Q1d — cross-paper)
```
Frame : from the Republic — the soul/state analogy; rule by knowledge (philosopher-king).
Crit  : democracy = rule by the unqualified many; liberty degenerates into licence; demagoguery → tyranny.
Assess: prescient on populism/propaganda, but elitist; ignores participation's value.
Concl : a caution, not a refutation, of democracy.
```
