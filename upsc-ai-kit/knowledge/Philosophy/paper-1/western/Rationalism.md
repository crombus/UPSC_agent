## 1. DESCARTES — METHOD & CERTAIN KNOWLEDGE ✅
- **Goal:** a foundation of *indubitable* knowledge, as certain as mathematics.
- **Method of doubt (methodic/hyperbolic doubt):** doubt everything that *can* be doubted — the senses (illusion), the world (dream argument), even mathematics (the **evil demon/genius** could deceive). ✅
- **The Cogito:** but I cannot doubt that *I* am doubting — *"Cogito, ergo sum"* ("I think, therefore I am"). The thinking self is the first indubitable certainty — the **Archimedean point**. ✅
- **Criterion of truth:** whatever is perceived **"clearly and distinctly"** is true (the cogito is the model).
- **Rebuilding knowledge:** from the cogito → prove God (see §3) → God is no deceiver → therefore clear-and-distinct ideas (incl. the external world, mathematics) are trustworthy.
> ⚠️ **The Cartesian Circle (stock objection):** he uses clear-and-distinct ideas to prove God, then uses God to guarantee clear-and-distinct ideas — circular.

## 2. SUBSTANCE — the trio compared (STAR sub-topic, asked repeatedly) ✅

**Definition all three inherit:** substance = *that which exists independently, needing nothing else in order to exist.*

### Descartes — THREE substances (dualist)
- Strictly, only **God** fits the definition (needs nothing); but *created* substances are of two kinds:
  - **Mind (res cogitans)** — essence = **thought**, unextended.
  - **Matter (res extensa)** — essence = **extension**, unthinking.
- ⚠️ Tension: by his own definition only God is a substance; calling mind & matter "substances" is a concession.

### Spinoza — ONE substance (monist/pantheist)
- Applies the definition rigorously: if substance needs nothing else, there can be **only ONE** — infinite, self-caused (**causa sui**) = **God = Nature (Deus sive Natura)**. ✅
- Mind and matter are not substances but two of God's infinite **attributes** (Thought & Extension) that we can know.
- Individual things (incl. us) = **modes** (modifications) of the one substance.
- **This directly answers 2024 P-II Q6b** ("God embedded in Spinoza's metaphysics of substance & attributes").

### Leibniz — INFINITE substances (pluralist)
- Reality = infinitely many **monads** — simple, unextended, **windowless** ("no windows" = they don't causally interact), soul-like centres of force/perception. ✅
- Each monad mirrors the whole universe from its viewpoint; they form a graded hierarchy up to God (the supreme Monad).

> 🔑 **The logic is one argument, three destinations:** *apply the "independent existence" definition strictly* → Descartes stops at 3 (inconsistently), Spinoza collapses to 1, Leibniz explodes to ∞. Show this and you own the substance question.

## 3. GOD ✅
- **Descartes' proofs:** (a) **Trademark/causal** — my idea of a perfect God must have a cause as perfect as the idea → God; (b) **Ontological** — perfection entails existence (a perfect being lacking existence is a contradiction).
- **Spinoza:** God = the one infinite substance = Nature. Not a personal, transcendent creator but the immanent, necessary order of all that is. Impersonal pantheism.
- **Leibniz:** God is the necessary being who, from infinite possible worlds, chose to actualise the **"best of all possible worlds"** (principle of sufficient reason + optimism). Sets up the pre-established harmony.

---

## 4. MIND–BODY PROBLEM ✅
- **Descartes' interactionism:** mind (thought) & body (extension) are really distinct substances that *causally interact* — supposedly via the **pineal gland**. ✅
- **The interaction problem:** how can an unextended mind move an extended body (and vice versa)? Causation between utterly different substances is unintelligible. → the central objection to dualism.
- **Spinoza's solution — parallelism (double-aspect):** mind & body are not two things interacting but **one thing under two attributes**; "the order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things." No interaction needed. ✅
- **Leibniz's solution — pre-established harmony:** windowless monads don't interact; God synchronised them from the start, like **two clocks** keeping perfect time independently. Mind and body only *seem* to interact. ✅

---

## 5. DETERMINISM & FREEDOM ✅
- **Descartes:** the will is **free** (indeed infinite); error arises when the (infinite) will affirms beyond what the (finite) intellect clearly perceives. Libertarian on the mind.
- **Spinoza:** **strict determinism** — everything follows necessarily from God's nature; "free will" is an illusion born of ignorance of causes. **Freedom = understanding necessity** (acting from adequate ideas, not being externally compelled). Freedom ≠ contingency but *self-determination through reason*. ✅
- **Leibniz:** **soft determinism/compatibilism** — the monad's every state unfolds from its own nature (determined), yet acts are "inclined without being necessitated"; freedom = spontaneity + intelligence + contingency (this world is *chosen*, not logically necessary).

## 9. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 9.1 — "Compare the conceptions of substance in Descartes, Spinoza and Leibniz." (recurring, asked ~every year)
```
Frame : shared definition (independent existence) → three destinations.
Body  : Descartes 3 (dualist, tension) → Spinoza 1 (strict → pantheism) → Leibniz ∞ (monads).
Link  : each substance-view dictates the mind-body solution (interaction/parallelism/harmony).
Assess: Descartes inconsistent; Spinoza logically tightest but impersonal; Leibniz saves plurality at cost of ad-hoc harmony.
Concl : one definition, rigorously applied, drives the whole of Continental Rationalism.
```

### 9.2 — "How is God embedded in Spinoza's metaphysics of substance and attributes?" (2024 P-II Q6b, 15m)
```
Intro : Spinoza defines substance as self-caused, independent → only ONE possible = God.
Body  : God = the one infinite substance = Nature; Thought & Extension = 2 knowable attributes; things = modes.
Implic: pantheism, determinism, freedom = understanding necessity, impersonal God.
Crit  : loss of a personal/transcendent creator; acosmism charge.
Concl : God is not IN the system — God IS the system.
```
