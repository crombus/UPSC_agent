## 0. THE MASTER STORYLINE ⚠️

> **"Substance" = that which exists in itself, needing nothing else — the ultimate bearer of properties.** The history of Western metaphysics is the *rise and fall* of this concept.

## 1. THE POSITIONS ✅

### A. Aristotle — substance as concrete individual
- **Primary substance** = the individual thing (*this* man, *this* horse) — the ultimate subject that is *never predicated of anything else*. Secondary substances = species/genera. Substance = **form + matter** compound (hylomorphism). ✅ (Corrects Plato's separate Forms — the universal is *in* the particular.)

### B. Descartes — three substances (rationalist dualism)
- Substance = "what needs nothing else to exist." Strictly only **God**; created substances = **mind (res cogitans, essence = thought)** and **matter (res extensa, essence = extension)**. → **Dualism** + the interaction problem. ⚠️ Inconsistency: by his own definition only God qualifies.

### C. Spinoza — one substance (monism/pantheism)
- Apply the definition *rigorously* → only **ONE** self-caused substance = **God = Nature (Deus sive Natura)**; Thought & Extension are its **attributes**; things are **modes**. Monism. ✅

### D. Leibniz — infinite substances (pluralism)
- Reality = infinitely many **monads** — simple, unextended, **windowless**, soul-like centres of force. Pluralism + pre-established harmony. ✅

### E. Locke — the empiricist doubt begins
- We have no clear idea of substance itself; it is a **"something, I know not what"** — an unknown **substratum** we *suppose* to support the observed qualities. Substance is inferred, not perceived. ✅ (Empiricism starts eroding the concept.)

### F. Berkeley — abolishes *material* substance
- *Esse est percipi.* There is no unperceiving material substratum; only **spirits (minds)** and their ideas. Keeps **spiritual** substance, kills **material** substance. ✅

### G. Hume — eliminates substance entirely
- No impression of any substance/substratum → no legitimate idea of it. **Material** substance is a fiction; the **self/mind** too is not a substance but a **"bundle of perceptions"** in perpetual flux. Substance = a habit-born fiction. ✅ (The concept's low point.)

### H. Kant — relocates substance to the mind
- Substance is neither perceived (Hume right) nor a thing-in-itself we know; it is an **a priori category of the understanding** that the mind *imposes* to make experience of an objective, enduring world possible. "Substance" is a **condition of experience**, not a discovered entity. ✅

### I. Strawson — substance as basic particular (linguistic revival)
- In *descriptive metaphysics*, the modern heir of "substance" is the **basic particular** — the re-identifiable **material body** in one spatio-temporal framework, presupposed by all reference. Persons are the other basic particular. ✅

## 3. THE FOUR MOVES — how the concept transforms ⚠️
1. **Ground it (Aristotle):** substance = the concrete individual (against Plato's Forms).
2. **Multiply/purify it (Rationalists):** count the substances by applying the "independent existence" definition — 3, 1, ∞.
3. **Erode & eliminate it (Empiricists):** Locke (unknown) → Berkeley (no matter) → **Hume (no substance at all, self = bundle).**
4. **Relocate/reinterpret it (Kant & Strawson):** from a *thing out there* to a *category of mind* (Kant) or a *basic particular of our conceptual scheme* (Strawson).

> 🔑 The empiricist "slide to the bundle-self" is the pivot: **Hume's dissolution of substance forces Kant's Copernican rescue.** Show this causal chain and you own the theme.

---

## 4. INTER-THINKER DEBATE POINTS ⚠️
- **Spinoza vs Descartes:** if substance needs *nothing* else, there cannot be *three* — Spinoza's monism is Descartes made consistent.
- **Hume vs Descartes on the self:** cogito's thinking *substance* vs bundle of perceptions — no impression of a persisting "I".
- **Kant vs Hume:** Hume is right that substance is not perceived, wrong to call it fiction — it is the *a priori form* that makes objective experience possible.
- **Cross-paper bridge:** **Hume's bundle-self ↔ Buddhist nairātmya / anattā** (no substantial self, only fleeting states). A high-value comparison. → `indian/Buddhism.md`.
