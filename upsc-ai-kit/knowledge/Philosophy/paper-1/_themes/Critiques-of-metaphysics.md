## 1. THE FOUR CRITIQUES ✅

### A. HUME — the empiricist bonfire
- **Principle:** every genuine idea must derive from an **impression**. Metaphysical notions — **substance, the self, causal necessity, God** — have **no corresponding impression** → they are illegitimate or fictions. ✅
- **The famous fork test:** any book (metaphysics/divinity) that contains *neither* abstract reasoning about quantity/number *nor* experimental reasoning about matters of fact — *"commit it then to the flames, for it can contain nothing but sophistry and illusion."* ✅
- **Character:** metaphysics fails a **content** test — its terms lack empirical meaning.

### B. KANT — the critical limitation
- **Not rejection but boundary-drawing:** the categories (substance, cause) are valid **only within possible experience** (phenomena). When reason applies them **beyond experience** to the unconditioned (soul, cosmos, God), it generates **illusion**. ✅
- **Symptoms:** the **Antinomies** (equally valid proofs of contradictory theses, e.g. world is/ isn't finite in time) show reason contradicting itself when it oversteps; the **Paralogisms** (fallacies about the soul-substance); and the **demolition of the proofs for God's existence** (ontological, cosmological, teleological). ✅
- **Character:** metaphysics-as-science of the transcendent is impossible, but the *illusion is explained* and a "metaphysics of experience" survives. A **critical**, not dismissive, verdict.

### C. LOGICAL POSITIVISM — the verificationist execution
- **Verification principle:** a statement is cognitively meaningful only if analytic or empirically verifiable. Metaphysical statements are neither → **literally meaningless / pseudo-propositions** (not false — *nonsense*). ✅
- **Diagnosis:** metaphysicians are deceived by **surface grammar** ("the Absolute", "Nothing noughts"). Ethics & theology fall too (emotivism; "God exists" = meaningless).
- **Character:** a **meaning** test, sharper than Hume — metaphysics doesn't lose the game, it isn't *playing*. ⚠️ (Weakness: the principle is itself unverifiable → self-refuting.)

### D. LATER WITTGENSTEIN — the therapeutic dissolution
- **Meaning = use in a form of life.** Philosophical/metaphysical problems arise when words are wrenched from their ordinary use — *"language goes on holiday"*; philosophy is *"a battle against the bewitchment of our intelligence by means of language."* ✅
- **Method:** not to *refute* metaphysics with a counter-theory but to **dissolve** the confusion by returning words to everyday use — *therapy*. *"Philosophy leaves everything as it is."*
- **Character:** metaphysics is neither false nor meaningless-by-a-criterion but a **muddle** to be cured. Gentler, diagnostic — rejects the positivists' blunt criterion too.

### E. QUINE (reinforcement) — no privileged a priori
- By demolishing the **analytic/synthetic distinction** and adopting **holism/naturalism**, Quine denies metaphysics (or logic) any special a priori standing — but, more moderately, **allows a naturalised ontology** ("to be is to be the value of a bound variable"). ⚠️

## 3. HOW THEY DIFFER — the crucial discriminators ⚠️
- **Hume vs Positivists:** both empiricist, but Hume attacks **content** (no impression), positivists attack **meaning** (unverifiable → *nonsense*). Positivism is Hume "logicised".
- **Kant vs the rest:** Kant alone **saves** a chastened metaphysics (of experience) and *explains why* the illusion arises — he limits, he doesn't behead.
- **Positivists vs Later Wittgenstein:** both target language, but positivists wield a **single sharp criterion** (verification); Witt rejects any such criterion — dissolves case-by-case via **use**. Witt even turns against the *Tractatus* (his own earlier attempt to draw limits, item 6).
- **The trajectory:** *content-attack (Hume) → limit-drawing (Kant) → meaning-criterion (Positivism) → therapeutic dissolution (Witt).* Increasingly **linguistic**.
