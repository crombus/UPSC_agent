## 0. THE MASTER QUESTION ⚠️

> **Does the effect (kārya) already exist in its material cause (upādāna) before production?**

```
                 CAUSATION THEORIES
     ┌───────────────────────┴───────────────────────┐
 ASATKĀRYAVĀDA                                    SATKĀRYAVĀDA
 (effect is NEW, did not pre-exist)               (effect PRE-EXISTS in cause)
     │                                                  │
 Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika = ĀRAMBHAVĀDA              ┌────────────┴────────────┐
 (new whole from parts)                 PARIṆĀMAVĀDA              VIVARTAVĀDA
 Buddhism (in a sense: momentary        (REAL transformation)    (UNREAL appearance)
   arising, pratītyasamutpāda)          Sāṃkhya, Rāmānuja        Advaita (Śaṃkara)
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic:** *"**A**sat = **A**rambha (new); **Sat** splits into **P**ariṇāma (real change) vs **V**ivarta (mere appearance)."*

---

## 1. THE THREE POSITIONS ✅

### A. ASATKĀRYAVĀDA — "the effect is non-existent (before production)"
- **Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika (Ārambhavāda / "the theory of the new beginning"):** the effect is a **fresh entity**, numerically distinct from its cause. Threads (cause) and cloth (effect) are two different things; the cloth is *begun* (ārambha) anew by the conjunction of parts. ✅
- **Grounds:** effect and cause are perceived as distinct; the effect has properties the cause lacks; we say the effect is "produced."
- **Buddhism** is sometimes grouped here (asat-kārya in spirit): under pratītyasamutpāda a *new* momentary entity arises dependent on the prior — nothing persists to "contain" the effect.

### B. SATKĀRYAVĀDA — "the effect pre-exists (in latent form)"
Effect is already present in the cause and is only *manifested*. Splits by **whether the transformation is real or only apparent**:

- **B1. PARIṆĀMAVĀDA (real transformation):**
  - **Sāṃkhya:** the effect is a **real modification** of prakṛti (curd really from milk; the world really evolves from prakṛti). 5 arguments (asadakaraṇāt, upādāna-grahaṇāt, sarvasambhavābhāvāt, śaktasya-śakyakaraṇāt, kāraṇabhāvāt). ✅
  - **Rāmānuja (Viśiṣṭādvaita):** **Brahma-pariṇāma-vāda** — the world is a *real* transformation of Brahman (Brahman with cit-acit as real modes). Māyā = God's real creative power.

- **B2. VIVARTAVĀDA (apparent/unreal transformation):**
  - **Advaita (Śaṃkara):** the effect is only an **apparent, unreal appearance** of the cause — Brahman does not really change; the world is *vivarta*, like the **snake superimposed on the rope** or silver on shell. The cause alone is real; the "effect" is a name-and-form (nāma-rūpa) illusion. ✅
  - Special sub-cases: *ajātivāda* (Gauḍapāda — "no real origination at all") and *dṛṣṭi-sṛṣṭi-vāda*.

## 3. WHY IT MATTERS — causation determines the whole system ⚠️

The causation stance is the *root* from which each metaphysics grows:
- **Asatkāryavāda → pluralistic realism** (Nyāya): many really-new things, atoms, a God to combine them.
- **Pariṇāmavāda → evolutionary realism** (Sāṃkhya): one real prakṛti unfolding; (Rāmānuja): one real Brahman with real parts.
- **Vivartavāda → non-dual idealism** (Advaita): only Brahman real, world illusory → māyā, avidyā, jīvanmukti all follow.

> 🔑 In any "compare/critically examine" answer, **trace the school's larger doctrine back to its causation theory** — this shows synthesis and scores.

---

## 4. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATE POINTS ⚠️
- **Sāṃkhya vs Nyāya:** "nothing comes from nothing" (satkārya) vs "the effect is a genuinely new whole" (asatkārya). Nyāya replies: if effect = cause, why the effort to produce it, and why the perceived difference?
- **Advaita vs Sāṃkhya (both satkārya):** the crucial split — is the transformation **real (pariṇāma)** or **apparent (vivarta)**? Advaita: prakṛti-pariṇāma still leaves you in an unexplained real-plurality; only vivarta preserves non-duality.
- **Advaita vs Rāmānuja:** vivarta (world unreal) vs pariṇāma (world real, God's body). The 2022/2025 liberation questions trace to this.

2. ✅ *"The effect is a new beginning, distinct from its cause."* — Nyāya ārambhavāda.
3. ✅ *"Brahman does not really change; the world is a vivarta (apparent modification)."* — Advaita.
