## 1. NATURE OF THE SELF — school by school ✅
| School | Self is… | Consciousness |
|---|---|---|
| **Cārvāka** | the living **body** | emergent by-product of matter (bhūta-caitanya) |
| **Buddhism** | **no permanent self**; a stream of 5 skandhas | momentary vijñāna, no owner |
| **Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika** | an eternal **substance** (dravya), one per body | *adventitious* quality (arises only with body-mind) |
| **Jaina** | **jīva**, intrinsically omniscient & blissful, size of the body | essential; obscured by karmic matter |
| **Sāṃkhya / Yoga** | **puruṣa** — pure consciousness, inactive witness, many | *is* consciousness itself (but inactive) |
| **Mīmāṃsā** | eternal substance, real agent & enjoyer | Bhāṭṭa: potential consciousness; Prābhākara: self revealed in cognition |
| **Advaita** | **Ātman = Brahman** — non-dual sat-cit-ānanda | self *is* pure consciousness (svayaṃ-prakāśa) |
| **Viśiṣṭādvaita** | real, atomic, distinct **jīva**, a mode of God | essential attribute |
| **Dvaita** | real, eternally distinct jīva (graded) | essential |
| **Aurobindo** | the psychic being — a portion of the Divine, **evolving** | Spirit unfolding through matter |

**The great fault-line:** Buddhism (**anātman**, no self) vs everyone else (some real self). The "self as pure consciousness" (Sāṃkhya, Advaita) vs "self as substance with adventitious consciousness" (Nyāya) is the second key split.

## 2. BONDAGE — what binds, and by what? ✅
| School | Bondage is… | Root cause |
|---|---|---|
| Buddhism | the wheel of duḥkha / rebirth | avidyā → tṛṣṇā (craving) |
| Jaina | karmic **matter** clogging the jīva | mithyā-darśana + passions (kaṣāya) |
| Nyāya | the stream of pain-laden births | mithyā-jñāna (false knowledge) |
| Sāṃkhya/Yoga | puruṣa's mis-identification with prakṛti/citta | **aviveka** / avidyā (kleśas) |
| Advaita | the jīva's apparent limitation | **avidyā / adhyāsa** (superimposition) |
| Viśiṣṭādvaita/Dvaita | separation from God, karma | karma + ignorance of dependence on God |

> 🔑 Note the recurring root: **avidyā/ignorance** (Buddhism, Nyāya, Sāṃkhya, Advaita) — bondage is overwhelmingly *epistemic*, so liberation is by *knowledge*.

## 3. LIBERATION — nature, means, and "with or without body?" ✅
| School | Mokṣa is… | Means | Jīvanmukti? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cārvāka** | — (rejected) | — (enjoy life) | — |
| **Buddhism** | **nirvāṇa** — cessation of craving/duḥkha ("blowing out") | Eightfold Path (prajñā-śīla-samādhi) | arhat, yes |
| **Jaina** | **kaivalya** — jīva regains infinite jñāna/bliss, rises to siddha-śilā | Three Jewels + 5 vows + tapas | yes (kevalin) |
| **Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika** | **apavarga** — absolute cessation of pain, **no consciousness/bliss** ("stone-like") | tattva-jñāna (right knowledge of categories) | debated (mostly videha) |
| **Sāṃkhya** | **kaivalya** — puruṣa's aloneness, freed from prakṛti | **viveka-jñāna** (discrimination) | **yes** (potter's-wheel) |
| **Yoga** | kaivalya | aṣṭāṅga-yoga + Īśvara-praṇidhāna | yes |
| **Mīmāṃsā** | (early) heaven via ritual; (later) release | karma + jñāna | — |
| **Advaita** | **identity with Brahman** — sat-cit-**ānanda** (blissful) | **jñāna** (Brahma-jñāna, "tat tvam asi") | **yes** (prārabdha sustains body) |
| **Viśiṣṭādvaita** | eternal **loving communion** with God, jīva stays distinct | **bhakti + prapatti** (surrender) + grace | limited |
| **Dvaita** | eternal blissful **service** of God (graded) | bhakti + grace | no |
| **Aurobindo** | **divine life on earth** — supramental transformation | **Integral Yoga** (aspiration+surrender→descent) | transformation, not escape |

## 4. KEY DEBATES (examiner's favourites) ⚠️
1. **Is liberation blissful or merely painless?** Advaita (ānanda) & Jaina (infinite bliss) **vs** Nyāya ("stone-like" apavarga, no consciousness). → the stock critique of Nyāya mokṣa.
2. **Liberation *with* a self or *without*?** Buddhism dissolves the self in nirvāṇa; Advaita realises the self *is* Brahman; Viśiṣṭādvaita/Dvaita keep the self **distinct** — hence "is liberation possible without a real agent?" (2022/2025 PYQ) = Advaita vs Viśiṣṭādvaita.
3. **Escape vs transformation:** all classical schools = *release from* saṃsāra; **Aurobindo** = *divinise* the world. The modern reversal.
4. **Rebirth without a soul?** Buddhism's nairātmyavāda vs the "immortality-of-soul-necessary-for-rebirth" thesis (2022 PYQ). Continuity via **santāna**, not ātman.
5. **Means — jñāna vs bhakti:** knowledge-schools (Sāṃkhya, Advaita, Nyāya) vs devotion-schools (Viśiṣṭādvaita, Dvaita).

## 5. STATEMENT / QUOTE BANK ⚠️
1. ✅ *"Tat tvam asi" / "Ahaṃ brahmāsmi"* — Advaita: the self is Brahman.
2. ✅ *"That which is without self (anattā) — all dharmas."* — Buddhist nairātmya.
3. ✅ *"Liberation is the absolute cessation of pain."* — Nyāya apavarga.
4. ✅ *"As a dancer retires having been seen, prakṛti ceases for the liberated puruṣa."* — Sāṃkhya kaivalya.
5. ⚠️ *"For Aurobindo, liberation is the beginning, not the end — a divine life on earth."*
