# THEME NOTE — A Priori / A Posteriori & Theories of Knowledge (Western)

> **Type:** Cross-thinker theme file (Paper-I, Section A). Spans items 2–4, 7, 11.
> **Why this file exists:** The whole of modern epistemology is a duel over **how much of knowledge is a priori (from reason, independent of experience) vs a posteriori (from experience)** — and whether **synthetic a priori** knowledge exists. Rationalism, Empiricism, Kant, Positivism, Quine are *moves in this one game*. UPSC asks it directly ("synthetic a priori", "critique of empiricism") and it underlies every Western answer.
> **Grounding:** ✅ = canonical (Y. Masih; Copleston; Kenny; Kant, *Critique of Pure Reason*) · ⚠️ = exam synthesis

## 0. THE TWO DISTINCTIONS + THE FAMOUS BOX ⚠️

```
 EPISTEMIC axis:   A PRIORI (independent of experience) │ A POSTERIORI (from experience)
 SEMANTIC  axis:   ANALYTIC (predicate in subject,      │ SYNTHETIC (predicate adds new
                    true by meaning, uninformative)      │  info, informative)

              │  ANALYTIC              │  SYNTHETIC
   ───────────┼───────────────────────┼──────────────────────────
   A PRIORI   │  "bachelors unmarried" │  ★ SYNTHETIC A PRIORI ★
              │  (all agree exists)    │  (7+5=12; "every event has a cause")
              │                        │   KANT: YES · EMPIRICISTS/POSITIVISTS: NO
   ───────────┼───────────────────────┼──────────────────────────
   A POSTERI. │  (empty)               │  "the table is brown" (all agree exists)
```
> 🔑 **Mnemonic — the whole fight is ONE box:** *Does the top-right cell (**synthetic a priori**) exist?* Rationalists ~yes(loosely), Empiricists **no**, **Kant YES (his whole system)**, Positivists **no**, Quine **erases the grid**.

## 1. THE POSITIONS ✅

### A. Rationalism (Descartes, Spinoza, Leibniz) — reason is primary
- **Certain knowledge is a priori**, modelled on mathematics; the senses deceive. **Innate ideas** (God, self, axioms). Leibniz: *"truths of reason"* (necessary, a priori) vs *"truths of fact"* (contingent, a posteriori). Reality is intelligible by pure reason. ✅

### B. Empiricism (Locke, Berkeley, Hume) — experience is primary
- **No innate ideas** (Locke's *tabula rasa*); all ideas trace to **impressions/experience**. Hume's **fork**: every meaningful statement is either a *"relation of ideas"* (a priori, analytic, necessary — maths/logic, but *empty of fact*) or a *"matter of fact"* (a posteriori, synthetic, contingent). **Nothing is both synthetic *and* a priori.** ✅
- **Hume's bombshell:** **causation** ("every event has a cause") is *not* a priori (no contradiction in denying it) and *not* observed (we see only constant conjunction, not necessary connection) → causal necessity is a **habit of the mind**. Threatens all science. ✅

### C. Kant — the synthetic a priori rescue
- **The problem Hume set:** if causality isn't a priori and isn't given in experience, science collapses. **Kant's answer: there IS synthetic a priori knowledge** — judgements that are *informative* (synthetic) yet *necessary & universal* (a priori). Examples: **maths (7+5=12), and the principles of pure natural science (every event has a cause)**. ✅
- **How it's possible — the Copernican Revolution:** knowledge conforms to the mind, not the mind to objects. The mind *imposes* **forms of intuition (space & time)** and **categories (incl. causality, substance)** on the raw manifold. So these truths are a priori (mind-contributed) yet apply to all experience (synthetic). ✅
- **Price:** we know only **phenomena** (things-as-they-appear), never **noumena** (things-in-themselves). A priori knowledge is bought by confining it to possible experience.

### D. Logical Positivism — abolish the synthetic a priori
- Revives Hume's fork. **Two exhaustive kinds only:** analytic (a priori, tautological, empty — logic & maths) + synthetic (a posteriori, verifiable — empirical). **Synthetic a priori is a myth**; Kant's category is dissolved. Necessary truths are **verbal/linguistic** (tautologies), not insight into reality. Metaphysics (which pretends to synthetic a priori knowledge of reality) = meaningless. ✅

### E. Quine — erase the grid itself
- Attacks the **analytic/synthetic distinction** as undefinable (the synonymy circle). With **confirmation holism**, *no* statement is confirmed or a priori in isolation; even logic/maths are revisable "come what may". → the sharp **a priori/a posteriori line dissolves**; epistemology is naturalised, continuous with science. ✅

## 3. THE STORYLINE — one continuous argument ⚠️
```
Rationalism (a priori reason) ⇄ Empiricism (a posteriori sense)
        │  Hume pushes empiricism to SCEPTICISM (causation, self, induction)
        ▼
KANT synthesises both: "percepts without concepts are blind;
        concepts without percepts are empty" → SYNTHETIC A PRIORI
        │  but confines knowledge to phenomena
        ▼
POSITIVISM rejects synthetic a priori → analytic/synthetic + verification
        ▼
QUINE rejects the analytic/synthetic distinction itself → no sharp a priori line
```
> 🔑 **Kant is the hinge.** Everything before him builds toward the Hume-crisis; everything after reacts to Kant's synthetic-a-priori solution (accepting/rejecting it). Frame any Western epistemology answer on this arc.

## 4. INTER-THINKER DEBATE POINTS ⚠️
- **Kant vs Hume:** Hume — causal necessity is subjective habit; Kant — it is an **objective a priori category**, the condition of any experience of nature. Kant *concedes* Hume's point (not from sense) but *inverts* the conclusion (so it must come from mind).
- **Positivists vs Kant:** the synthetic a priori is illusory; "necessary" truths are merely **analytic/verbal**.
- **Quine vs Positivists:** you cannot even draw the analytic/synthetic line cleanly → the empiricist framework self-destructs.
- **Cross-paper bridge:** Kant's **antinomies + critique of the proofs for God** flow from this same phenomena/noumena limit → `Kant.md`, Paper-II `Proofs-for-God.md`.
