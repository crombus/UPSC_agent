## 1. THE SIX PRAMĀṆAS — WHAT EACH IS ✅

| # | Pramāṇa | Definition | Classic example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pratyakṣa** (perception) | Direct cognition from sense-object contact (sannikarṣa) | "This is a pot" (seen) |
| 2 | **Anumāna** (inference) | Knowledge of the unperceived via a mark (liṅga) + invariable concomitance (vyāpti) | "Hill has fire, because smoke" |
| 3 | **Upamāna** (comparison) | Knowledge of a thing's name via similarity to a known thing | "A gavaya is like a cow" |
| 4 | **Śabda** (verbal testimony) | Knowledge from the reliable word of a trustworthy authority (āpta) | Knowing heaven from Vedas |
| 5 | **Arthāpatti** (postulation) | Assuming an unseen fact to explain an otherwise inexplicable one | "Fat Devadatta doesn't eat by day → he eats at night" |
| 6 | **Anupalabdhi** (non-apprehension) | Cognition of absence (abhāva) via non-perception | "There is no pot on the floor" |

## 2. SCHOOL-BY-SCHOOL: THE DISTINCTIVE THESIS ✅

Each school's *epistemology* is really its stand on (a) how many pramāṇas, and (b) *why it rejects* the ones it drops.

### Cārvāka — radical empiricism (1: perception only)
- Accepts **only pratyakṣa**; **rejects anumāna** as a valid independent source because inference rests on **vyāpti** (universal concomitance), which itself cannot be established without either (i) another inference → infinite regress, or (ii) perception → but we can't perceive *all* cases. Hence vyāpti is uncertain. ✅
- Consequence: rejects all suprasensible entities — God, soul, afterlife, adṛṣṭa, the Vedas' authority. (*Directly the 2025 Q5(a) question.*)
- ⚠️ **Counter (Nyāya):** if inference is invalid, the Cārvāka's *own* general claim "inference is invalid" is itself a general (inferential) claim → self-refuting. This is the standard rebuttal to memorise.

### Buddhism — (2: perception + inference)
- Accepts **pratyakṣa + anumāna** only. Perception (nirvikalpaka, non-conceptual) alone grasps the **unique particular (svalakṣaṇa)**; inference grasps only mentally-constructed universals (sāmānyalakṣaṇa). ✅
- Dignāga/Dharmakīrti: the two pramāṇas correspond to two kinds of object. All else (testimony, comparison) reduces to inference.

### Vaiśeṣika — (2: perception + inference)
- Same two as Buddhism, but **subsumes śabda under anumāna** (verbal knowledge = inferential). ✅

### Sāṃkhya / Yoga — (3: + testimony)
- Adds **śabda**. Rejects upamāna, arthāpatti, anupalabdhi as reducible to the three. Testimony needed because Sāṃkhya's 25 categories (incl. imperceptible prakṛti, puruṣa) are known through authoritative scripture + inference. ✅

### Nyāya — (4: + comparison) — THE GOLD STANDARD
- The **fullest realist epistemology**: pratyakṣa, anumāna, upamāna, śabda. ✅
- Reduces arthāpatti → inference, anupalabdhi → a form of perception (of absence). This is the most-tested school; its 4-pramāṇa scheme is the benchmark against which others are compared.
- Anumāna elaborated as **five-membered syllogism (pañcāvayava)** — see the dedicated Nyāya note.

### Mīmāṃsā — (5 Prābhākara / 6 Bhāṭṭa)
- Whole system exists to **defend the self-validity + eternal authority of the Vedas (śabda)**. ✅
- **Prābhākara (5):** + arthāpatti. **Bhāṭṭa (6):** + anupalabdhi (to cognise abhāva).
- Doctrine of **svataḥ-prāmāṇya** (intrinsic validity of knowledge) — knowledge is valid by itself; only error needs external explanation. (Contrast Nyāya's *parataḥ-prāmāṇya* — validity is externally certified.)

### Advaita Vedānta — (6)
- Accepts all six (following Bhāṭṭa). But all six are **vyāvahārika** (empirically valid only); at the pāramārthika level, pramāṇa itself is transcended in Brahman-realisation. ✅
- Anupalabdhi lets Advaita cognise the "absence" that figures in its analysis; śabda (Upaniṣads) is the decisive pramāṇa for Brahman-knowledge.

## 3. INTER-SCHOOL DEBATES (the examiner's favourite battleground) ⚠️

| Debate axis | Position A | Position B | Where UPSC uses it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Is inference valid?** | Cārvāka: NO (vyāpti unprovable) | Nyāya/all others: YES | Cārvāka Q; "reject transcendent entities" |
| **Intrinsic vs extrinsic validity** | Mīmāṃsā/Advaita: *svataḥ* (self-valid) | Nyāya: *parataḥ* (externally validated) | Mīmāṃsā theory-of-knowledge Q |
| **How many pramāṇas are irreducible?** | Nyāya: 4 | Mīmāṃsā: 5–6 · Buddhism: 2 | Cross-school comparison Q |
| **Object of perception** | Buddhism: bare particular (svalakṣaṇa) | Nyāya: qualified real object (savikalpaka) | Buddhist Kṣaṇikavāda linkage |
| **Status of testimony (śabda)** | Vaiśeṣika/Buddhism: reduces to inference | Nyāya/Mīmāṃsā: independent | Any śabda question |
| **Can absence be known?** | Bhāṭṭa/Advaita: yes, via anupalabdhi | Nyāya: yes, but via perception | Mīmāṃsā vs Nyāya |

> 🔑 **The master fault-line:** *empiricist minimisers* (Cārvāka 1, Buddhism 2) vs *scripture-defending maximisers* (Mīmāṃsā 5–6, Advaita 6), with **Nyāya (4) as the realist middle** that every answer can use as a reference point.

## 4. STATEMENT / QUOTE BANK (for quote-critique archetype) ⚠️

Deploy these as opening hooks or counterpoints:

1. ✅ *"Perception is the only pramāṇa; inference and testimony are not authoritative."* — Cārvāka (Bṛhaspati-sūtra tradition). → use in Cārvāka/empiricism answers.
2. ⚠️ *"Vyāpti cannot be established by perception (limited) nor by inference (circular) — hence inference is not self-certain."* — Cārvāka argument, paraphrased. → the crux of "reject inference."
3. ✅ *"Knowledge is valid intrinsically (svataḥ-prāmāṇya); invalidity alone is extrinsic."* — Mīmāṃsā (Kumārila/Prabhākara). → Mīmāṃsā theory-of-knowledge.
4. ✅ *"Nyāya admits four and only four independent sources of valid knowledge."* — Nyāya (Gautama). → benchmark line for cross-school questions.
5. ⚠️ *"For the Advaitin every pramāṇa is valid only within vyavahāra; the knower of Brahman goes beyond all pramāṇa."* → Advaita epistemology-to-metaphysics bridge.

## 5. APPLIED-QUESTION DRILLS ⚠️

Practise reframing the *same* pramāṇa content into different archetypes:

1. **[Doctrine]** "Discuss the Nyāya theory of pramāṇa." → straightforward 4-fold exposition + reductions.
2. **[Critique]** "Cārvāka's rejection of inference is self-refuting. Critically examine." → present Cārvāka argument → Nyāya's self-refutation charge → assess.
3. **[Comparison]** "Compare the Nyāya and Mīmāṃsā theories of the validity of knowledge." → *parataḥ* vs *svataḥ* + 4 vs 5/6 pramāṇas.
4. **[Applied]** "Can there be knowledge of non-existence? Discuss with reference to anupalabdhi." → Bhāṭṭa/Advaita yes vs Nyāya's perception-of-absence.
5. **[Bridge]** "How does a school's epistemology determine its metaphysics?" → Cārvāka (1 pramāṇa → materialism), Advaita (śabda → Brahman), Mīmāṃsā (śabda → dharma/Veda-authority).

## 6. PYQ-MAPPED MODEL-ANSWER SKELETONS ⚠️

### 6.1 — "Discuss Cārvāka's critique of the belief in the existence of suprasensible entities." (2025 P-II Q5a, 10m)
```
Intro   : Cārvāka = radical empiricism; only pratyakṣa is pramāṇa.
Body-1  : Rejection of anumāna — vyāpti problem (regress/circularity), no perception of universal concomitance.
Body-2  : Therefore no valid route to suprasensibles → God, soul, afterlife, adṛṣṭa, heaven, Vedic authority all rejected.
Body-3  : Consequences — materialism (bhūtacaitanyavāda), hedonism, denial of mokṣa.
Critique: Nyāya's self-refutation charge (the rejection is itself a generalisation); pragmatic indispensability of inference.
Concl.  : Powerful empiricist check, but epistemically self-limiting.
```

### 6.2 — "What proofs do Nyāya philosophers offer for the existence of God?" (2025 P-II Q7b, 15m) — *epistemology feeds theology*
```
Frame   : Nyāya uses anumāna (inference) — one of its 4 pramāṇas — to prove Īśvara.
Body    : Kāryāt (causal/cosmological), Āyojanāt (design/adṛṣṭa-governance), Śruti/śabda (testimony) arguments.
Link    : Ground each proof in the pramāṇa it rests on → shows why a 4-pramāṇa realist CAN reach God whereas 1-pramāṇa Cārvāka cannot.
Critique: Advaita/Buddhist objections; Kant-style parallels (⚠️ Western bridge).
```

### 6.3 — "Mīmāṃsā theory of knowledge." (recurring, Syllabus item 18)
```
Core    : Purpose = defend Veda's self-validity → svataḥ-prāmāṇya.
Body-1  : 5 (Prābhākara) / 6 (Bhāṭṭa) pramāṇas; explain arthāpatti + anupalabdhi as the distinctive additions.
Body-2  : Intrinsic validity — knowledge true by itself; error = external (khyāti theories).
Contrast: vs Nyāya parataḥ-prāmāṇya.
Concl.  : Epistemology entirely in service of ritual/Vedic authority (dharma).
```
