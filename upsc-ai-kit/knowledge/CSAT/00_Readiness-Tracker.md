# CSAT - Readiness Tracker

> **Purpose:** a **reusable** self-audit for CSAT. It holds a skill checklist, a timed-set log
> template, accuracy/attempt/risk metrics, an error log, a spaced-revision plan, readiness gates, and
> a topic matrix. **Templates are intentionally blank** - fill your own numbers each cycle; do not
> treat any sample row as a real score.
> **Pairs with:** `00_Master-Framework.md` (Section 8 error codes, Section 7 risk logic).

---

## 1. Skill checklist (tick when you can do it cold, timed)

Mark each `[ ]` -> `[x]` only when you can do it **without notes** and **within the time ceiling**.

**Topic 01 - Reading Comprehension**
- [ ] Identify question type (central idea / inference / assumption / tone/scope).
- [ ] Answer strictly from the passage; reject a true-but-unstated option.
- [ ] Eliminate "too broad / too narrow / distortion / unstated" options.
- [ ] Handle a two-passage compare-and-contrast set.

**Topic 02 - Number Systems**
- [ ] Divisibility rules (2,3,4,5,6,8,9,11) and unit-digit cycles.
- [ ] Remainders, HCF/LCM word problems, factor counting, trailing zeros.
- [ ] Number/letter series and cumulative-pattern items.

**Topic 03 - Arithmetic (commercial math)**
- [ ] Ratio/proportion scaling; percentage and successive percentage change.
- [ ] Averages, mixtures/alligation, profit-loss/discount, interest, partnership, ages.

**Topic 04 - Rates, Motion, Time & Geometry**
- [ ] Time-work and pipes/cisterns via one-day/unit-rate work.
- [ ] Speed-distance, trains/boats, races and circular tracks.
- [ ] Clocks (angle, coincidence) and calendars (odd days).
- [ ] Perimeter/area/volume and cube-cutting counts.

**Topic 05 - Algebra, Inequalities & Data Sufficiency**
- [ ] Linear/simple equations and word-to-equation translation.
- [ ] Inequalities and quantitative comparison.
- [ ] The two-statement data-sufficiency decision (four standard verdicts).

**Topic 06 - Logical Reasoning, Coding, Counting & DI**
- [ ] Linear/circular arrangements, blood relations, directions.
- [ ] Coding-decoding, syllogism/deduction, decision-making scenarios.
- [ ] Counting/probability basics and reading DI tables/graphs.

- **Net marks** = `2.5 x correct - 0.833 x wrong` (blank contributes 0).
- Keep **time cap** realistic (e.g., 30 items in 45 min for a topic set).

## 3. Accuracy / attempt / risk metrics (compute each set)

| Metric | Formula | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Attempt rate** | `attempted / items` | Are you reaching enough of the paper? |
| **Accuracy** | `correct / attempted` | Quality of the attempts you make. |
| **Net-mark efficiency** | `net marks / items` | Marks actually banked per item faced. |
| **Guess quality** | `correct guesses / guesses made` | Was elimination real (>0.25) or blind? |
| **Blank discipline** | `blanks that were negative-EV / total blanks` | Did you skip the right items? |

> 🔑 **Diagnostic pairing:** high attempt rate + low accuracy = you are **over-attempting**
> (tighten selection). Low attempt rate + high accuracy = you are **under-attempting** (speed up
> Pass 1 and take more positive-EV guesses).

## 6. Readiness gates (do not skip a gate)

Advance only when the gate condition holds on **at least two** recent timed sets.

| Gate | Condition | Cleared? |
|---|---|---|
| **G1 - Foundation** | All Section 1 checkboxes ticked for Topics 01, 05, 06 (the three biggest families). | [ ] |
| **G2 - Accuracy** | Accuracy on attempted items is consistently high on topic sets. | [ ] |
| **G3 - Selection** | Attempt rate high **and** guess quality above the 1-in-4 blind line. | [ ] |
| **G4 - Time** | Full 80-item mock finished within two hours with a Pass-3 buffer. | [ ] |
| **G5 - Margin** | Net marks on full mocks sit **comfortably above** the qualifying bar (verify the bar against the current notification). | [ ] |

> 🔑 **G5 is the real target.** CSAT is qualifying: once G5 holds repeatedly, shift effort back to
> Paper I. Do **not** chase a perfect CSAT score at the cost of GS-I.
