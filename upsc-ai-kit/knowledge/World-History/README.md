> **Spine:** Norman Lowe, *Mastering Modern World History* (5th edition) is the mandatory,

> Archives material where relevant, and standard reference historiography, with inference
> clearly separated from sourced fact.

## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` gives the standard global narrative (what happened, why,
  core actors and concepts); `advanced/NN` adds historiographical debate, India/world linkages,
  consequences and a fuller Mains-answer architecture.
- **Same number = same topic** across both tiers (e.g., `basic/13_...md` pairs with
  `advanced/13_...md`).
- **Tagging:** ✅ = directly grounded in a cited source (Lowe's text, NCERT/NIOS, Britannica,
  archival material); ⚠️ = analytical inference, historiographical synthesis, or an
  interpretive verdict (including where a source author's own argued opinion is being
  reported — it is still marked ⚠️ as an interpretation rather than a bare fact); 📰 = a
  verified, dated current-affairs anchor (used sparingly here, since most topics are historical;
  present mainly in Topics 16, 18, 20, 21 where live institutional/geopolitical continuity
  exists).
- **Answer discipline:** no invented dates, statistics, quotes, or PYQ question numbers appear
  in this folder. Where a specific UPSC PYQ could not be verified, the file cites the general,
  real UPSC GS-I/GS-II syllabus line and flags the theme as "⚠️ recurring UPSC theme" instead of
  fabricating a question number.
- **Cross-links, not duplication:** Indian-side depth (colonial economic drain and
  deindustrialisation, the Indian freedom movement's own periodisation, Non-Aligned Movement
  positioning, post-1991 liberalisation) is already covered by the `Modern-Indian-History` and
  `Economy` folders; this folder cross-links to them rather than re-explaining that material.

## Cross-subject links

- **Modern-Indian-History:** the Indian freedom movement's own periodisation, colonial
  deindustrialisation, WWI/WWII's impact on India, and the Non-Aligned Movement are covered in
  depth there; this folder cross-links from Topics 04, 07, 10, 14-15, 18-19 rather than
  duplicating India-specific material.
- **Economy:** India's post-1991 liberalisation and its relationship to the broader globalising
  world economy (Topic 20) is covered in depth in `Economy`; this folder supplies the
  world-economic-history framing.
- **Polity:** UN Security Council structure/reform (Topic 16) and international-institution
  design touch GS-II territory; this folder keeps the historical/structural narrative and
  flags live reform debates only where independently, currently verifiable.

  causation chains, especially for Topics 01-08.
- Library of Congress / US National Archives material where directly relevant (e.g., American
  Revolution primary-source grounding, Topic 02).
- Audited UPSC GS-I/GS-II syllabus lines and recurring-theme patterns (no invented PYQ question
  numbers).
