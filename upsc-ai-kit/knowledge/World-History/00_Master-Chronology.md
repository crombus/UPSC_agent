# World History — MASTER CHRONOLOGY (Full Timeline, 18th century to present)

> **Subject:** History (World History) · **GS Paper:** GS-I (World History) · **Also Prelims.**
> **Grounded in:** Norman Lowe, *Mastering Modern World History* (5th ed.) for 1914 onward +
> old NCERT/NIOS world-history material and Encyclopaedia Britannica for pre-1914 dates and
> standard chronology cross-checks.
> ✅ = standard, cross-verifiable historical fact (book/NCERT/Britannica) · ⚠️ = inference /
> historiographical framing / periodisation choice · 📰 = current affairs.
> *This is the spine. Every topic file (`basic/NN`, `advanced/NN`) carries its own mini-timeline
> and source-grounding; this file stitches them into one continuous sequence.*
> *Dating convention: all dates below are CE unless stated. Where Lowe or NCERT differ
> marginally on a date (e.g., exact day of an armistice), the notes flag it rather than
> silently pick one.*

## 1. Age of Enlightenment and Revolutions (mid-1700s – early 1800s)

| Era/event | Approx date | Defining feature | Key marker |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ Enlightenment | c. 1650s-1780s | Reason, natural rights, social-contract thought (Locke, Montesquieu, Rousseau, Voltaire) | Intellectual seedbed for revolutions |
| ✅ American Revolution | 1775-1783 | Thirteen colonies' war of independence from Britain | Declaration of Independence, 4 July 1776; Treaty of Paris, 1783 |
| ✅ French Revolution | 1789-1799 | Overthrow of Bourbon absolutism; Declaration of the Rights of Man | Storming of the Bastille, 14 July 1789; Reign of Terror, 1793-94 |
| ✅ Napoleonic era | 1799-1815 | Napoleon Bonaparte's rise, empire, and defeat | Coronation 1804; defeat at Waterloo, 1815 |
| ✅ Industrial Revolution | c. 1760s-1840s (Britain first) | Mechanised production, factory system, urbanisation | Steam engine, textile mills; spreads to Europe/USA through 19th c. |

> 🔑 Trap: the American (1775-83) and French (1789-99) Revolutions are separate events roughly a
> decade apart; French revolutionaries were influenced by, but did not directly cause, American
> independence — the causal arrow (Enlightenment ideas -> both revolutions) runs in parallel,
> not sequentially from one revolution to the other.

## 2. Congress of Vienna to the "Age of Nationalism" (1814 – 1900)

| Era/event | Approx date | Defining feature | Key marker |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ Congress of Vienna | 1814-1815 | Post-Napoleonic settlement; balance-of-power, conservative restoration | Concert of Europe established |
| ✅ Unification of Italy | 1859-1871 (core phase 1859-61, completed 1870-71) | Cavour's diplomacy + Garibaldi's expedition unify Italian states | Kingdom of Italy proclaimed, 1861; Rome annexed, 1870 |
| ✅ Unification of Germany | 1864-1871 | Bismarck's three wars (Danish, Austro-Prussian, Franco-Prussian) | German Empire proclaimed at Versailles, 1871 |
| ✅ New Imperialism / Scramble for Africa | c. 1870s-1914 | European powers partition Africa and expand Asian colonies | Berlin Conference, 1884-85 |
| ✅ Latin American Independence | c. 1808-1826 | Spanish/Portuguese colonies win independence | Bolivar, San Martin campaigns; most mainland Spanish colonies independent by mid-1820s |

> 🔑 Trap: Latin American independence (early 1800s) chronologically overlaps the Congress of
> Vienna/early-1800s European settlement, not the later Scramble for Africa (1880s) — keep the
> two "colonisation"-related topics (Latin America gaining independence vs Africa being
> colonised) on separate timelines; they run in different directions in the same century.

## 3. World in 1914 to the End of the Second World War (1900 – 1945)

| Era/event | Approx date | Defining feature | Key marker (Norman Lowe) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ World in 1914 / outbreak of WWI | 1914 | Alliance system, militarism, nationalism, imperial rivalry | Assassination at Sarajevo, 28 June 1914; war begins August 1914 |
| ✅ First World War | 1914-1918 | Trench warfare, total war, new military technology | Armistice, 11 November 1918 |
| ✅ Paris Peace Settlement | 1919-1920 | Treaty of Versailles and related treaties redraw boundaries | Treaty of Versailles signed, 28 June 1919 |
| ✅ League of Nations era / interwar diplomacy | 1920-1939 | Collective-security experiment; its successes and failures | League founded 1920; fails to stop Manchuria (1931), Abyssinia (1935) |
| ✅ Russian Revolutions and Lenin's USSR | 1917-1924 | Fall of Tsar, Bolshevik seizure of power, civil war, NEP | February/March and October/November 1917 revolutions |
| ✅ Stalin's USSR | 1924-1953 | Power consolidation, five-year plans, collectivisation, Purges | Stalin supreme by c. 1929 |
| ✅ Rise of Fascism/Nazism/militarism | 1918-1939 | Mussolini (1922), Hitler (1933), Japanese militarism | March on Rome, 1922; Hitler Chancellor, January 1933 |
| ✅ Second World War | 1939-1945 | Global war, Holocaust, atomic bombs | Germany invades Poland, 1 Sept 1939; Japan surrenders, Aug/Sept 1945 |

> 🔑 Trap: the Russian Revolution (1917) happens **during** the First World War (1914-18), not
> after it — Russia's withdrawal (Treaty of Brest-Litovsk, 1918) is a direct consequence of the
> Bolshevik seizure of power, and this sequencing is a common Prelims/Mains confusion point.

## 4. Cold War and Decolonization (1945 – 1991)

| Era/event | Approx date | Defining feature | Key marker (Norman Lowe) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ United Nations founded | 1945 | Successor to the League, stronger collective-security design | UN Charter signed, San Francisco, 1945 |
| ✅ Cold War origins and early phase | 1945-1953 | US-USSR bipolar rivalry, containment, division of Europe | Berlin Blockade, 1948-49; NATO, 1949 |
| ✅ Chinese Communist victory | 1949 | Mao Zedong's CCP defeats the Kuomintang | People's Republic of China proclaimed, 1 Oct 1949 |
| ✅ Cold War crises and detente | 1953-1979 | Cuban Missile Crisis, Vietnam War, detente | Cuban Missile Crisis, 1962 |
| ✅ Middle East wars and diplomacy | 1948-1991 | Arab-Israeli wars, Palestinian question, superpower involvement and oil-security politics | 1948-49 war; Suez, 1956; Six-Day War, 1967; Yom Kippur War, 1973; Camp David, 1978-79; Gulf War, 1990-91 |
| ✅ Western European integration | 1951-1991 | ECSC and EEC deepen cooperation while western and eastern Europe remain divided | ECSC, 1951; Treaty of Rome, 1957; Maastricht agreed, 1991 |
| ✅ Decolonization of Asia and Africa | 1945-1975 (bulk 1945-1965) | End of European empires | India independence, 1947; most African states independent by mid-1960s |
| ✅ Latin America under US influence | 1945-1980s | US interventions, debt crisis | Cuban Revolution, 1959; 1980s debt crisis |
| ✅ Renewed Cold War tension and end | 1979-1991 | Afghanistan invasion, Gorbachev reforms, collapse of communism | Fall of Berlin Wall, 1989; USSR dissolved, Dec 1991 |

> 🔑 Trap: decolonization (mostly 1945-1965) and the Cold War (1947-1991) run concurrently and
> interact — many newly independent states became Cold War proxy-conflict arenas (Korea,
> Vietnam, Congo, Angola); do not treat decolonization as a separate, unconnected timeline from
> the Cold War.

## 6. Memory ladder (one line per era — revise in 60 seconds)

- ⚠️ **Ideas to revolutions:** Enlightenment reason/rights (1650s-1780s) -> **American (1775-83)**
  and **French (1789-99)** Revolutions -> Napoleon (1799-1815).
- ⚠️ **Vienna to nationalism:** Conservative Vienna settlement (1814-15) -> Italian (1859-71) and
  German (1864-71) unification -> New Imperialism/Scramble for Africa (1870s-1914) alongside
  earlier Latin American independence (1808-26).
- ⚠️ **World wars bracket:** WWI (1914-18) -> interwar League/appeasement failure (1919-39) ->
  WWII (1939-45), with the Russian Revolution (1917) and rise of fascism (1918-39) unfolding
  inside/around this bracket.
- ⚠️ **Bipolar age:** UN founded (1945) -> Cold War (1947-91), Middle East crises and western
  European integration unfold alongside decolonization (1945-65) -> Cold War ends with USSR's
  collapse (1991).
- ⚠️ **Post-1991:** Unipolar "new world order" -> rise of terrorism/9/11 (2001) -> Arab Spring
  (2010-12) -> ongoing multipolarity debate (post-2013, treat as dated current affairs only).

> 🔑 Trap: do not compress "decolonization" and "end of the Cold War" into one event — European
> empires substantially ended by the mid-1960s, while the Cold War itself continued for another
> 25+ years until 1989-91.
