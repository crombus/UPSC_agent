## 3. Crisis of the Old Regime

| Estate / force | ✅ Source-grounded fact | Historical role |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ First Estate | Clergy enjoyed privilege and tax exemptions | Linked Church to old order |
| ✅ Second Estate | Nobility held privilege, office and much land | Beneficiary of feudal hierarchy |
| ✅ Third Estate | Common people formed about 95% of population in old NCERT | Carried tax burden and political exclusion |
| ✅ Peasantry | Rent, dues, taxes and bad harvests made life insecure | Anti-feudal anger |
| ✅ Bourgeoisie / middle class | Wealth without matching status or power | Leadership in institutional rupture |
| ✅ Urban workers / artisans | Long hours, poverty, secret societies and unrest | Mass pressure from below |

- ✅ Old NCERT calls the French monarchy financially troubled and ultimately pushed toward bankruptcy.
- ✅ Both NCERT and old NCERT link the crisis to **social inequality + fiscal disorder + intellectual challenge**.

## 4. Ideas and phases of the Revolution

| Phase | What happened | What to remember |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ 1789–91 | National Assembly, Bastille, Rights of Man, constitutional monarchy | End of legal privilege begins |
| ✅ 1792–94 | Republic, war, Jacobins, Terror | Revolution radicalises under pressure |
| ✅ 1795–99 | Directory | Instability and fatigue weaken civilian rule |
| ✅ 1799–1815 | Napoleon | Revolutionary gains mixed with authoritarian empire |

```text
fiscal crisis + unequal estates + bread stress
                   +
         Enlightenment attack on privilege
                   ->
         1789 political rupture
                   ->
    war + counter-revolutionary fear + radicalisation
                   ->
         Jacobin republic -> Directory -> Napoleon
```

## 5. Napoleon: continuity and contradiction

- ✅ Old NCERT says feudal laws were annulled and many revolutionary gains survived even after Napoleon seized power.
- ✅ Old NCERT and Britannica both connect Napoleon with the **Napoleonic Code**, which preserved important elements of legal equality and modern civil order.
- ⚠️ Napoleon should be written as both **heir to the Revolution** (legal codification, merit, anti-feudal reform) and **its limiter** (empire, censorship, conquest, concentration of power).

## 6. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) and UPSC traps

- ✅ **14 July 1789** = storming of the Bastille.
- ✅ **Third Estate** represented the overwhelming majority but lacked privilege.
- ✅ **Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen** proclaimed equality before law and rights of citizenship.
- ✅ **Jacobins** are associated with radical republicanism and the Reign of Terror.
- ✅ **Napoleonic Code** is a major legal legacy of the revolutionary era.
- ✅ The Revolution gave the term **nation** a modern political meaning in old NCERT.

> 🔑 Trap: Do not present the French Revolution as a single-stage event of 1789 only; UPSC expects phases.

- ❌ Bastille was important because many prisoners were inside. → ✅ Its symbolic political value mattered more than prisoner count.
- ❌ Declaration of Rights immediately ended all inequality. → ✅ It transformed law and legitimacy, but practice remained conflict-ridden.
- ❌ Napoleon simply restored the old order. → ✅ He preserved important anti-feudal legal changes even while ending the republic.
