## 1. Why the UN was created

✅ Lowe presents the UN as the post-1945 successor to the League of Nations. Its founders wanted to remove some of the League's weaknesses and create an organization better equipped to preserve peace.

✅ Lowe lists three broad aims:
- ✅ preserve peace and eliminate war;
- ✅ remove causes of conflict by encouraging economic, social, educational, scientific and cultural progress;
- ✅ safeguard the rights of individuals, peoples and nations.

⚠️ For UPSC, this topic must be prepared in two layers: **historical origin after the Second World War** and **institutional structure/mandate for GS-II**.

## 2. Structure: what to remember for exam use

✅ Lowe's chapter discusses the General Assembly, Security Council, Secretariat, International Court of Justice, Trusteeship Council and ECOSOC, and also covers the International Criminal Court in the wider UN system.

✅ The official UN "main bodies" page today lists **six principal bodies** under the Charter:

| Principal UN body today | Core function |
|---|---|
| ✅ General Assembly | Deliberation, debate, budget, elections and broad resolutions |
| ✅ Security Council | Primary responsibility for peace and security |
| ✅ ECOSOC | Coordination on economic, social and development matters |
| ✅ Trusteeship Council | Historical trust-territory oversight; now inactive |
| ✅ International Court of Justice | Principal judicial organ |
| ✅ Secretariat | Administrative machinery led by the Secretary-General |

⚠️ Exam caution: the **ICJ** is the UN's principal judicial organ; the **ICC** is important in global governance, but it is not one of the UN's six principal Charter bodies.

## 3. UN versus League of Nations

| Point of comparison | Lowe-grounded judgment |
|---|---|
| Scope | ✅ UN spent far more effort on economic and social work |
| Human rights | ✅ UN committed itself to protecting individual human rights |
| Decision-making | ✅ Assembly and Security Council procedures allowed more decisive action than the League managed |
| Membership | ✅ UN became a far wider world organization than the League |
| Persisting weakness | ✅ Security Council veto still blocks action |
| Persisting weakness | ✅ UN still lacks a permanent army of its own |

✅ Lowe's central verdict is balanced: the UN has been more successful than the League, but some structural weaknesses remained.

## 4. Peacekeeping: where the UN succeeded and failed

### Relative successes in Lowe's account

- ✅ West New Guinea: helped negotiations and supervised transfer.
- ✅ Korea: acted decisively, though only because the Soviet delegation was absent.
- ✅ Suez Crisis: one of the UN's strongest moments; ceasefire and peacekeeping force followed.
- ✅ Congo: complex and costly, but the UN did restore some order.
- ✅ Gulf War of 1991: collective action was forceful and effective.
- ✅ Cambodia and Mozambique: the post-Cold-War UN helped stabilize transitions.

### Clear limitations

- ✅ Hungary 1956 and Czechoslovakia 1968: Soviet veto and Soviet force made the UN ineffective.
- ✅ Cyprus and Kashmir: ceasefires and supervision were possible, but not final settlement.
- ✅ Somalia and Bosnia: too little force, too complex a civil-war environment, and weak political backing.
- ✅ Iraq 2003: the US-UK invasion without clear Security Council authorization exposed UN limits sharply.

⚠️ Lowe's broader lesson: the UN works best where great-power interests do not directly collide or where major powers choose to support enforcement.

## 5. What the UN does beyond peacekeeping

✅ Lowe repeatedly insists that the UN's non-military work is enormous and often underrated.

| Area | Lowe-grounded examples |
|---|---|
| ✅ Human rights | Universal Declaration of Human Rights; rights publicity and pressure |
| ✅ Labour | ILO and workplace standards |
| ✅ Health | WHO, epidemic control and disease campaigns |
| ✅ Food | FAO support for agriculture and emergency food needs |
| ✅ Education/culture | UNESCO literacy, schools and heritage work |
| ✅ Children/refugees | UNICEF and UNRWA |
| ✅ Finance/trade | IMF, World Bank, GATT/WTO, UNCTAD |
| ✅ Humanitarian relief | OCHA and disaster response |

✅ This is the section that often converts a one-sided "UN fails because wars continue" answer into a balanced one.

## 6. Lowe's verdict on the UN

✅ Lowe is neither celebratory nor dismissive.

### Main weaknesses
- ✅ no permanent UN army;
- ✅ delays over when and how to intervene;
- ✅ veto politics;
- ✅ shortage of funds;
- ✅ overlap and friction among agencies;
- ✅ tension between UN authority and powerful states, especially after the Cold War.

### Main strengths
- ✅ a world forum where even small states can speak;
- ✅ some wars shortened or contained;
- ✅ valuable human-rights pressure;
- ✅ immense contribution to health, welfare, refugees, literacy and development.

⚠️ A safe UPSC verdict is: **the UN is strongest as a forum, norm-setter and humanitarian coordinator; it is weakest when hard enforcement collides with great-power politics**.

## 7. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) and UPSC traps

- ✅ UNO officially came into existence in October 1945.
- ✅ Security Council has 15 members: 5 permanent and 10 non-permanent.
- ✅ The veto is the core institutional brake on decisive Security Council action.
- ✅ "Uniting for Peace" emerged from the Korean War context.
- ✅ Suez is a classic UN success; Hungary a classic UN failure.
- ✅ The Trusteeship Council helped supervise territories towards independence.
- ✅ WHO, FAO, UNESCO, UNICEF and UNRWA are high-yield associated bodies/agencies in Lowe's treatment.
- ✅ The official UN today recognizes six principal bodies under the Charter.

> 🔑 Trap: Do not reduce the UN to the Security Council alone. Lowe stresses that most of the UN's actual work lies in social, humanitarian and developmental fields.

- ❌ The UN completely solved Palestine, Kashmir or Cyprus. -> ✅ It managed crises and ceasefires better than final settlements.
- ❌ Veto means the General Assembly is irrelevant. -> ✅ Assembly resolutions, including "Uniting for Peace", can still matter politically.
- ❌ ICC and ICJ are the same thing. -> ✅ ICJ is the UN's principal court; ICC is separate in institutional origin.

## 8. GS-I/GS-II Mains exam linkage, answer frame and probable questions

✅ Topic 16 is one of the clearest GS-I to GS-II bridges in the World-History folder.

⚠️ A strong answer frame:
1. Begin with the Second World War background and the failure of the League.
2. Explain structure: General Assembly, Security Council, Secretariat, ICJ, ECOSOC, Trusteeship Council.
3. Use 2-3 cases to show mixed peacekeeping performance.
4. Add non-peacekeeping work.
5. End with a balanced verdict on veto, funding and great-power politics.

**Probable Mains questions**
- ⚠️ "How far has the United Nations been more successful than the League of Nations?"
- ⚠️ "Assess the UN as a peacekeeping organization with suitable examples."
- ⚠️ "The greatest strength of the UN lies outside the battlefield." Discuss.
- ⚠️ "Explain the structure and mandate of the United Nations with reference to contemporary global governance."
