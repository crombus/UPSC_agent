## 1. Mini-timeline

| Date | Event | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ 1791 | Haitian Revolution begins | First major anti-slavery revolutionary break in the region |
| ✅ 1804 | Haiti becomes independent | First free Black republic in the modern era |
| ✅ 1808 | Napoleon invades Spain; Portuguese court shifts to Brazil | Iberian imperial crisis opens the door to colonial autonomy |
| ✅ 1810 | Revolts begin in Spanish America; Hidalgo issues the Grito de Dolores in Mexico | Independence phase turns mass and regional |
| ✅ 1817 | San Martín crosses the Andes into Chile | Southern liberation takes military shape |
| ✅ 1819 | Bolívar secures New Granada and builds Gran Colombia | Northern liberation gains momentum |
| ✅ 1821 | Mexico becomes independent | Spanish rule weakens decisively in North America |
| ✅ 1822 | Brazil becomes independent under Pedro I | Portuguese America follows a different path |
| ✅ 1824 | Ayacucho breaks major royalist power in South America | Spanish military position collapses |
| ✅ 1825 | Bolivia emerges as an independent state | Bolívar's northern campaign reaches symbolic completion |

## 2. Social background and grievances

| Group / issue | ✅ What to remember | Why it mattered |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Peninsulares | Europeans born in Spain/Portugal held top colonial offices | Resented by creoles |
| ✅ Creoles | American-born whites were wealthy but politically restricted | Became leading independence elites |
| ✅ Mestizos, Indigenous peoples, enslaved Africans | Socially and politically subordinate | Mass base existed, but benefits of independence were uneven |
| ✅ Trade restrictions | Colonial monopoly and regulation bred resentment | Economic grievance mattered |
| ✅ Ideas and examples | Enlightenment, American Revolution, French Revolution and Haiti influenced the region | Intellectual and political inspiration |

## 3. The trigger: the Iberian crisis

- ✅ Britannica links Latin American independence directly to the shock created by Napoleon's intervention in Spain.
- ✅ Bolívar's biography says the colonies began to form **juntas** and challenge old authority after the Spanish monarchy was destabilized.
- ✅ San Martín's career also turns decisively after Napoleon's occupation of Spain in **1808**.
- ⚠️ The imperial centre weakened, and colonial elites used the crisis to move from autonomy claims toward independence.

## 4. The main regional leaders and paths

| Region / path | ✅ Leader(s) | Key point |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Northern South America | Simón Bolívar | Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru and Bolivia are tied to his campaigns |
| ✅ Southern South America | José de San Martín | Argentina, Chile and Peru are central to his role |
| ✅ Mexico | Hidalgo, Morelos, then Iturbide | A longer sequence from popular revolt to conservative settlement |
| ✅ Brazil | Pedro I | Independence came through a monarchical rather than republican path |
| ✅ Haiti | Toussaint Louverture, Dessalines and others | Most radical social revolution in the region |

```text
colonial grievance + Enlightenment + Iberian crisis (1808)
                             ->
juntas and wars of liberation
                             ->
Bolivar in the north / San Martin in the south / Mexico / Brazil
                             ->
independent states, but unequal societies remain
```

## 5. Outcomes and limitations

- ✅ By the mid-1820s, most of Spanish Latin America had become independent.
- ✅ Brazil became independent from Portugal in **1822** under **Pedro I**.
- ✅ Haiti showed that anti-colonial and anti-slavery revolution could be more socially radical than creole-led movements elsewhere.
- ⚠️ Independence ended imperial rule, but power often remained with creole elites.
- ⚠️ Social hierarchy, land inequality and regional fragmentation did not disappear with independence.

## 6. Must-Know Facts (Prelims) and UPSC traps

- ✅ **Simón Bolívar** is the leading figure of northern South American independence.
- ✅ **José de San Martín** is the key southern liberator linked with Argentina, Chile and Peru.
- ✅ **Hidalgo** issued the **Grito de Dolores** in **1810**.
- ✅ **Brazil** became independent in **1822** and followed a monarchical path.
- ✅ **Haiti** became independent in **1804** after a slave revolution.

> 🔑 Trap: Do not treat all Latin American independence movements as identical republican-national revolts. Haiti, Mexico, Spanish America and Brazil followed different social and political paths.

- ❌ Independence automatically created equality. -> ✅ Social and racial hierarchies often survived.
- ❌ Bolívar and San Martín worked in the same region in the same way. -> ✅ Their theatres overlapped only partly and their political roles differed.
- ❌ Brazil followed the same route as Spanish America. -> ✅ Brazil's path was more monarchical and less conventionally revolutionary.
- ❌ Haiti was a minor side-story. -> ✅ It was historically central as the first successful slave revolution.

## 7. Exam linkage and Mains angles

- ✅ Official UPSC GS-I syllabus includes **colonization**, **decolonization** and their effects on society; Latin American independence fits that frame directly.
- ⚠️ Good answers should balance **external crisis, elite grievance, military campaigns and post-independence limits**.
