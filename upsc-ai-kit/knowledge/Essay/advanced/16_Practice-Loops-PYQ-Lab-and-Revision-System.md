## 1. Advanced proposition and boundary

**Proposition:** ⚠️ a practice system is only as strong as its feedback
and spacing discipline — random, unlogged repetition of the same recent 16
prompts produces familiarity without necessarily producing skill
transfer to a genuinely new prompt. **Boundary:** ❌ this topic does not
reintroduce the full routing table (`basic/16` owns it) — it deepens
selected outlines, owns the full quotation ledger jointly with `09`, and
supplies the spaced-repetition method.

## 2. Concept definition and taxonomy

- **Deep outline:** an interpretive plan that includes thesis,
  qualification, 2–3 dimensions with mechanism, one counter-view, and a
  synthesis — richer than `basic/16`'s one-line outlines, still not a
  full essay.
- **Quotation ledger entry:** per-prompt record of attribution status
  and permissible use, mirroring `09`'s method, applied to the full corpus.
- **Spaced reattempt:** a deliberately delayed re-practice of a prompt or
  skill, timed to test retention rather than immediate recall.

## 3. Tension pairs and hidden assumptions

- **Coverage vs. depth:** the hidden assumption that practising all recent 16
  prompts once each is sufficient; in fact, revisiting a smaller subset
  multiple times with genuine spacing typically produces more transfer
  than a single pass through all recent 16.
- **Familiarity vs. transfer:** the assumption that being able to recall
  an outline for a specific prompt means the underlying skill (e.g.
  dialectical synthesis, `08`) transfers to an unfamiliar prompt; this
  should be explicitly tested (Section 6).

## 4. Levels of analysis and temporal-spatial scale

⚠️ Track practice at three levels: per-prompt (can I outline this
specific prompt well?), per-skill (across several prompts, is my
counter-view construction consistently strong or consistently weak?),
and per-cycle (is my overall practice-loop discipline — planning,
logging, spacing — actually being followed?).

## 7. Necessary/sufficient conditions and exceptions

⚠️ Logging an error (`15`) is necessary for a practice loop to improve
performance but not sufficient — the logged weakness must also be
revisited via a spaced reattempt (Section 8) to confirm improvement,
otherwise the log becomes a passive record rather than an active
feedback loop. **Exception:** a glaring one-off error (e.g. an invented
statistic caught immediately) can be corrected on the spot without
waiting for a full spaced-reattempt cycle.

## 8. Paradox, dialectic and counterfactual test

**Paradox:** re-attempting the *same* prompt after a gap is less
informative about genuine skill transfer than attempting a *different*
prompt that exercises the same logged weakness — e.g. if your logged
weakness is "weak counter-view construction" (`08`), a stronger test is
applying `08`'s method to a new prompt than re-writing the same essay.
**Counterfactual test:** "If I applied my logged-weakness fix to a
prompt I have never outlined before, would it hold up?" This is the real
test of whether a skill has actually improved, versus whether a specific
essay was merely patched.

## 11. Advanced structure, paragraph sequencing and style — deep-outline worked examples

**2024-A2 ("Empires of the mind") — deep outline:** Thesis: knowledge
and institutional capacity, not territorial control, are becoming the
decisive form of power, though this power must be made inclusive to be
legitimate. Dimensions: (a) individual/education — access to quality
learning as the base unit of this power; (b) institutional — research
and innovation capacity as its accumulation mechanism; (c) global/
digital — platform and data power as its newest, most concentrated
form. Counter-view: physical/military and resource power still matter
decisively in many conflicts and crises. Synthesis: inclusive,
accountable knowledge-power complements rather than fully replaces
material power.

**2025-B5 ("Muddy water… leaving it alone") — deep outline:** Thesis:
restraint is often the wiser response to a self-correcting or
inflammatory situation, but this is a bounded, not universal, wisdom.
Dimensions: (a) interpersonal/psychological — anger and conflict often
settle with time and space; (b) informational — misinformation/outrage
cycles sometimes fade faster without amplification; (c) policy —
complex systems sometimes self-correct without heavy-handed
intervention. Counter-view: some emergencies (public health, safety,
active injustice) worsen without timely action. Synthesis: diagnostic
wisdom — restraint where disturbance would worsen an already-settling
situation; timely action where inaction itself causes escalating harm.

## 12. ❌ Failure modes and revision protocol

- ❌ **Practising the same few "comfortable" prompts repeatedly.** →
  Protocol: deliberately rotate across both years and all four
  Sections (Section 5).
- ❌ **Logging errors without spaced reattempt.** → Protocol: schedule a
  reattempt of the logged skill (not necessarily the same prompt) after
  a genuine gap (Section 8).
- ❌ **Attaching an author to any of the 16 recent prompts, or to a remembered
  aphorism without verification.** → Protocol: apply Section 6's ledger
  before every use.

## 13. 📰 Dated anchor / practice lab / transfer task

📰 **Dated anchor:** not applicable — the practice-system method is
stable; any current illustration used during practice should carry its
own source and date per `09`/`12`.

**Practice lab:** Write a deep outline (Section 11 format) for one
prompt not already deep-outlined above — e.g. 2025-A3 ("thought finds a
world and creates one also") — within 15 minutes.

**Transfer task:** After a genuine gap (a different day), apply your
most recently logged weakness (from `15`) to a prompt you have not yet
practised at all, and assess via Section 8's counterfactual test whether
the underlying skill, not just the specific essay, has improved; record
the outcome as the next entry in your error log.
