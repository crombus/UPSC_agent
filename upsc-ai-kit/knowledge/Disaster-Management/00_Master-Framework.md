## 1. The risk-to-resilience results chain

```text
RISK = f(HAZARD, EXPOSURE, VULNERABILITY) / CAPACITY

UNDERSTAND RISK  (hazard + vulnerability + exposure assessment)
       |
       v
GOVERN RISK  (legal/institutional mandate, policy, planning)
       |
       v
INVEST IN RESILIENCE  (structural, non-structural, ecosystem-based, technological)
       |
       v
WARN --> ACT EARLY --> RESPOND --> RECOVER / BUILD BACK BETTER
       |
       v
risk-informed development feeds back into understanding and governing risk
```

**Core proposition:** ✅ VisionIAS defines disaster risk as arising "when
hazards interact with physical, social, economic and environmental
vulnerabilities" and states the working relationship "Risk = Probability
of Hazard x Degree of Vulnerability" (PDF p. 5). ⚠️ The four-term
hazard-exposure-vulnerability-capacity formulation used across this
folder is the internationally standard elaboration of the same relation
and is the correct frame for a Mains answer that must show *how* risk is
reduced, not merely define it.

## 2. Disaster management cycle (used throughout)

✅ VisionIAS structures the cycle as a pre-disaster **Risk Management
Phase** (prevention, mitigation, preparedness) and a post-disaster
**Crisis Management Phase** (relief, response, rehabilitation,
reconstruction, recovery) (PDF p. 6). The traditional "3 Rs" (Rescue,
Relief, Restoration) are described as being replaced by "3 Ps"
(Prevention, Preparedness, Proofing) (PDF p. 6).

| Phase | Core activities | Folder topics that operationalise it |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Prevention/Mitigation | Structural and non-structural risk reduction, land-use control, codes | 05-12, 14 |
| ✅ Preparedness | Early warning, plans, drills, community training | 03, 04, 17 |
| ✅ Response | Search and rescue, relief, Incident Command System | 17 |
| ✅ Recovery/Rehabilitation/Reconstruction | Restoring services, Build Back Better | 16, 17 |

## 3. India's operating architecture

```text
NDMA (PM-headed) / MHA / NEC / NIDM / NDRF
                    |
        SDMA (CM-headed) / SEC / SDRF
                    |
    DDMA (District Collector-headed) + PRIs + ULBs + communities
                    |
     last-mile warning, evacuation, response, recovery
```

✅ NDMA is the apex body under the DM Act, 2005, headed by the Prime
Minister; NDRF's "general superintendence, direction and control" vests
in the NDMA; NIDM works within NDMA's policy framework (PDF p. 13). ✅
SDMA is chaired by the Chief Minister; the DDMA is headed by the
District Collector/Magistrate with the elected local-authority
representative as Co-Chairperson (PDF pp. 14-15). Full detail in topic
`02`.

## 4. Mains answer engine

1. **Define the specific risk/problem** in one sentence — name the
   hazard, not disasters in general.
2. **Identify hazard + exposure + vulnerability + capacity** for the
   specific case asked about.
3. **Use the disaster management cycle** (Section 2) rather than a
   generic list of "steps."
4. **Assign institutions and scale** — Union → State → District/ULB/PRI
   → community (Section 3) — naming the correct authority, not a vague
   "the government."
5. **Combine structural, non-structural, ecosystem-based and
   technological measures** — an answer using only one category is
   incomplete.
6. **Add inclusive protection, finance and monitoring**, and close with
   **Build Back Better**.
7. **Conclude on risk-informed development / Sendai coherence** — show
   the answer feeds back into Section 1's cycle rather than ending on
   relief alone.

## 6. Sendai Framework quick reference

✅ VisionIAS names the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction
2015-2030's **four priorities**: understanding disaster risk;
strengthening disaster risk governance to manage disaster risk;
investing in disaster risk reduction for resilience; and enhancing
disaster preparedness for effective response and to "Build Back Better"
in recovery, rehabilitation and reconstruction (PDF p. 62). It is the
successor to the **Hyogo Framework for Action (2005-2015)**, whose five
priorities were: making DRR a priority; improving risk information and
early warning; building a culture of safety and resilience; reducing
risk in key sectors; and strengthening preparedness for response (PDF p.
61). ⚠️ Sendai adds **seven global targets** (quantitative, e.g. reducing
mortality, affected people, economic loss and damage to critical
infrastructure, while increasing national/local strategies, international
cooperation and access to early warning) — VisionIAS depicts these in a
figure without reproducing precise wording (PDF p. 62); do not invent
numeric targets not confirmed by a dated UNDRR source. ❌ **Sendai is a
voluntary, non-binding framework**, not a treaty — a frequent Prelims
trap. Full detail and the 2023 Midterm Review in topic `01`.

## 8. Recurring analytical gaps

| Gap | Failure | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| ⚠️ Document-period-as-current gap | Citing the VisionIAS document's NDRF battalion count or the 2019 Standing Committee funding figures as today's status; or citing NDMP 2016 (India's first edition) instead of the revised NDMP 2019 (the latest published plan) | Label as document-period evidence; verify current status against a dated NDMA/MHA source; cite NDMP 2019 as the operative plan |
| ⚠️ Relief-only gap | Treating disaster management as ending at relief | Carry every answer through rehabilitation, reconstruction and Build Back Better (topics 16-17) |
| ❌ Prediction-forecast conflation | Claiming earthquakes can be predicted | Distinguish prediction (not possible), forecast (probabilistic, e.g. weather) and early warning (post-detection alert, e.g. tsunami/cyclone) — topics 04-06 |
| ⚠️ Deadline-achievement gap | Treating an announced target/deadline as accomplished merely because the date has arrived | State only what a dated official release confirms |
| ⚠️ Single-hazard silo gap | Treating hazards as independent when cascading/compound risk is common (earthquake→landslide→dam breach; cyclone→flood→epidemic) | Note cascading linkages explicitly (topics 09, 10, 12, 14) |

## 9. Boundary routing

- Use **Environment** for climate science, UNFCCC/NDC/COP negotiation
  detail, ecology and pollution law.
- Use **Geography** for the physical-process mechanics of each hazard
  (plate tectonics, monsoon dynamics, glacial mechanics, terrain).
- Use **Science & Technology** for the technical engineering of sensors,
  satellites and forecasting models themselves.
- Use **Internal Security** for security-operational and armed-forces
  deployment questions.
- Use **Polity** for detailed constitutional/federal doctrine beyond the
  DM Act's own legal basis.
- Use **Social Justice** for general welfare-entitlement architecture.
- Use **Economy** for general fiscal-federalism and Finance Commission
  mechanics beyond disaster-specific funds.
- Use this folder for hazard definition, risk mechanism, institutional/
  legal architecture, current official status, and the Mains answer
  chain linking risk, response and resilience.
