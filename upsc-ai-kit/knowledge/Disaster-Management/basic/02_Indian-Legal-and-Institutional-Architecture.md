## 1. Visual foundation

```text
DISASTER MANAGEMENT ACT, 2005
              |
              v
NDMA (apex, PM-headed) --- NEC (Union Home Secretary-chaired)
              |
              v
SDMA (State, CM-headed) --- SEC (Chief Secretary-chaired)
              |
              v
DDMA (District, Collector-headed, elected local-body rep as Co-Chair)
              |
              v
Local Authorities (PRIs, Municipalities, Cantonment/Town Planning Boards)
              |
              v
NDRF / NIDM (specialist response and capacity-building arms of NDMA)
```

**Core proposition:** ✅ VisionIAS records that disaster management in
India "evolved from an activity-based reactive setup to a proactive
institutionalized structure," beginning with a 1990s "Disaster
Management Cell" under the Ministry of Agriculture (during the UN's
International Decade for Natural Disaster Reduction), and culminating in
the **Disaster Management Act, 2005**, which created the three-tier
NDMA-SDMA-DDMA structure (PDF p. 11).

## 2. Essential definitions

| Institution | Exam-ready mandate |
|---|---|
| ✅ **NDMA** | Apex body under the DM Act, 2005; headed by the Prime Minister; lays down policies, plans and guidelines; approves the National DM Plan and Central Ministry plans; exercises "general superintendence, direction and control" of NDRF (PDF p. 13). |
| ✅ **National Executive Committee (NEC)** | Constituted under the DM Act, 2005; chaired by the Union Home Secretary; the coordinating/monitoring body, with Secretary-level members from key ministries; may direct Ministries/States on measures for a specific disaster (PDF p. 13). |
| ✅ **NIDM** | Nodal agency for human-resource development, capacity building, training, research, documentation and policy advocacy; works within NDMA's policy framework; supports States via Disaster Management Centres (PDF p. 13). |
| ✅ **NDRF** | Specialist response force; general superintendence vested in NDMA; command/supervision with the Director General, NDRF; document-period strength recorded as "12 Battalions... each Battalion consisting of 1149 personnel" drawn from ITBP, BSF, CRPF, CISF (PDF p. 13) — ⚠️ treat this count as document-period; verify current strength officially. |
| ✅ **SDMA / SEC** | SDMA chaired by the Chief Minister, lays down State DM policy and approves the State DM Plan; SEC chaired by the Chief Secretary, coordinates and monitors DM activities in the State (PDF p. 14). |
| ✅ **DDMA** | Headed by the District Collector/Magistrate with the elected local-authority representative as Co-Chairperson; prepares the District DM Plan and ensures NDMA/SDMA guidelines are followed at district level (PDF p. 14). |
| ✅ **NPDRR** | Multi-stakeholder body chaired by the Union Home Minister; reviews DRR policy implementation progress and advises on Centre-State coordination (PDF p. 12). |

## 3. Risk/problem mechanism

1. **Legal basis:** ✅ though the Constitution is silent on "disaster,"
   the DM Act's legal basis draws on Concurrent List Entry 23 ("Social
   security and social insurance") and Entry 29 ("Prevention of the
   extension from one State to another of infectious or contagious
   diseases... affecting men, animals or plants"), as used during the
   COVID-19 lockdown (PDF p. 52).
2. **Three-tier cascade:** the Act's structure ensures a policy/guideline
   flow from NDMA → SDMA → DDMA → local authorities, with the DDMA as
   the operational nodal point closest to the affected population (PDF
   pp. 12-15).
3. **Response force activation:** NDRF's "proactive availability" and
   "pre-positioning" ahead of threatening disaster situations is
   highlighted as having "immensely helped minimise damage" (PDF p. 13).
4. **Special crisis mechanisms:** ✅ the Cabinet Committee on Security
   (CCS) is engaged if a disaster has serious security implications; the
   National Crisis Management Committee (NCMC), chaired by the Cabinet
   Secretary, is the nodal body for major disasters having serious or
   national ramifications and coordinates preparedness and response
   (PDF p. 13). 📰 The Disaster Management (Amendment) Act, 2025's
   amended definition of "disaster" explicitly excludes law-and-order
   situations from the DM Act's scope, confirming NCMC's role is
   disaster-coordination, not a security/law-and-order body.

## 4. Disaster-management cycle application

- ✅ **National Disaster Management Plan, 2016** (India's first edition)
  aligns with Sendai, the SDGs (2015-2030) and the Paris Agreement
  (COP-21); it organises action into five Thematic Areas across the DM
  cycle: Understanding Risk, Inter-Agency Coordination, Investing in DRR
  (structural), Investing in DRR (non-structural), and Capacity
  Development (PDF p. 14). 📰 The **revised NDMP, 2019** is the latest
  published National Disaster Management Plan, superseding the 2016
  edition; any claim about the Plan's current content/structure should
  cite the 2019 revision, not the 2016 edition, as the operative plan.
- ✅ The Plan's Response section identifies **eighteen broad activities**
  arranged in a role-responsibility matrix down to the Panchayat/ULB
  level, designed to be implemented "in a scalable manner in all phases
  of disaster management" (PDF p. 14).

## 5. Institutions and policy tools

- ✅ **National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF-the-fund, distinct from the
  NDRF-the-force)**, managed by the Centre for emergency response,
  relief and rehabilitation expenses beyond State Disaster Response Fund
  (SDRF) capacity; the National Calamity Contingency Fund was merged
  into it (PDF p. 15).
- ✅ **Standing Committee on Finance report (2019, Chair: Dr. M.
  Veerappa Moily)** on "Central Assistance for Disaster Management and
  Relief" recommended: enhanced SDRF/NDRF assistance scales; a separate
  Disaster Mitigation Fund; earmarking 10% of centrally sponsored scheme
  allocations for permanent restoration; and a 15% annual SDRF corpus
  increase for 2020-25 (PDF p. 15) — ⚠️ document-period recommendations;
  verify current fund rates/corpus officially before citing as present
  fact.
- ✅ **National Policy on Disaster Management (NPDM), 2009** — vision "to
  build a safe and disaster resilient India... through a culture of
  prevention, mitigation, preparedness and response" (PDF p. 15).

## 6. India applications and examples

- ✅ VisionIAS records the NDMP 2016's own acknowledged shortcomings: too
  generic in activity identification; no precise time frame; no
  projection of fund requirements or mobilisation; and — unlike Sendai
  or the SDGs — no goals or targets of its own (PDF p. 18). 📰 The
  **revised NDMP, 2019** is the latest published edition; verify whether
  these 2016-edition shortcomings have been addressed in the 2019
  revision before citing them as the current state of India's plan.
- ⚠️ **PYQ mapping:** no GS-III Mains question in the audited 2024-2025
  papers directly tests the NDMA/SDMA/DDMA institutional map by name;
  this topic instead supplies the institutional foundation every other
  topic's "measures taken" section cites (e.g. 2024 Q17's resilience
  framework, 2024 Q18's urban-flood policy architecture).

## 7. Must-Know Facts for Prelims

- ✅ The DM Act, 2005 created a three-tier structure: NDMA, SDMA, DDMA.
- ✅ NDMA is headed by the Prime Minister; SDMA by the Chief Minister;
  DDMA by the District Collector/Magistrate with an elected local-body
  representative as Co-Chairperson.
- ✅ NEC is chaired by the Union Home Secretary; SEC by the State Chief
  Secretary.
- ✅ NIDM is the nodal capacity-building/training/research agency,
  working within NDMA's policy framework.
- ✅ NDMP 2016 was India's first National Disaster Management Plan,
  aligned with Sendai, the SDGs and the Paris Agreement; the **revised
  NDMP, 2019** is the latest published edition.

## 8. ❌ Traps and stale-claim cautions

- ❌ NDRF's document-period strength ("12 Battalions," 1149 personnel
  each) is the current strength. -> This is VisionIAS's 2024-update
  figure (PDF p. 13); treat as document-period and verify current
  strength against a dated official NDMA/MHA source before citing.
- ❌ NDMP 2016 is still "the" current National Disaster Management Plan.
  -> The **revised NDMP, 2019** is the latest published National Disaster
  Management Plan; NDMP 2016 was only the first edition.
- ❌ The DM Act, 2005 is the only, unamended, current legal framework.
  -> The **Disaster Management (Amendment) Act, 2025 (Act 10 of 2025)**
  amends it (see Section 9); do not present the 2005 Act's original text
  as necessarily reflecting today's statutory position without checking
  amendment status.
- ❌ The Constitution explicitly lists "disaster management" as a
  subject. -> The Constitution is silent on "disaster"; the Act's legal
  basis draws on Concurrent List Entries 23 and 29 (PDF p. 52).

## 9. 📰 Current official anchor

- 📰 **Disaster Management (Amendment) Act, 2025 (Act 10 of 2025)** — the
  MHA/National Disaster Management Division's gazette notification is
  dated **8 April 2025**, and the Act came into force on **9 April
  2025**. Sections 8A, 8B, 9B and 41A are operative law. ⚠️
  Implementation actions that require a further step — e.g. a State
  actually constituting an Urban Disaster Management Authority under the
  Act, or operationalising the national disaster database — depend on
  the relevant State/Central notification under the Act, and that
  implementation status (not the Act's own commencement) is what must be
  separately verified before being cited as complete.

## 11. Mains framework

1. **Name the correct tier** (NDMA/SDMA/DDMA/local authority) for the
   specific action being discussed — never say "the government" alone.
2. **Cite the specific institution's precise mandate** (Section 2),
   distinguishing NDMA (policy) from NEC (coordination) from NDRF
   (operational response).
3. **Route financial questions** through the correct fund (SDRF vs.
   NDRF-the-fund) and note the 2019 Standing Committee's still-relevant
   critique (Section 5) as document-period evidence of a known funding
   gap.
4. **Acknowledge the NDMP 2016's self-stated limitations** (Section 6),
   and note that the **revised NDMP, 2019** is the latest published plan,
   when asked to evaluate India's planning framework.
5. **Flag the 2025 Amendment Act** (Section 9) as the current legal
   anchor, noting it is in force from 9 April 2025, with the caution
   that any *State/Central implementation action* under it (e.g. a
   constituted Urban DM Authority) needs its own notification check.
