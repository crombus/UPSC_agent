## 1. Visual foundation

```text
RISK ACCEPTANCE | RISK AVOIDANCE | RISK REDUCTION | RISK TRANSFER
                                                    (e.g. insurance)
        |
        v
SDRF (State-level, immediate relief) + NDRF-the-fund (Centre,
beyond-SDRF-capacity relief/rehabilitation)
        |
        v
MITIGATION INVESTMENT (Disaster Mitigation Fund - recommended)
        |
        v
BUILD BACK BETTER (Sendai Priority 4: recovery, rehabilitation,
reconstruction that reduces future risk)
```

**Core proposition:** ✅ VisionIAS names risk transfer — "shifting of the
burden of risk to another party," with insurance as "one of the most
common forms" — as one of four distinct risk-management strategies
alongside acceptance, avoidance and reduction (PDF p. 5).

## 2. Essential definitions

| Concept | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **National Disaster Response Fund (the fund, not the force)** | Centrally managed fund for emergency response/relief/rehabilitation expenses; used when State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) capacity is insufficient; the National Calamity Contingency Fund (NCCF, 11th Finance Commission) was merged into it (PDF p. 15). |
| ✅ **State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF)** | Used only for immediate relief to disaster victims at the State level (PDF p. 15). |
| ✅ **National Calamity Contingency Duty (NCCD)** | Funds the NDRF via excise/customs duty on specified goods; VisionIAS records its revenue falling from ₹5,690 crore (2015-16) to ₹2,500 crore (2018-19) as GST implementation shrank its scope (PDF p. 15) — ⚠️ document-period figures. |
| ✅ **"Build Back Better" (Sendai Priority 4)** | Enhancing preparedness for effective response, "and to 'Building Back Better' in recovery, rehabilitation and reconstruction" (PDF p. 62) — reconstruction that reduces, not recreates, future risk. |
| ✅ **CDRI's financing thematic area** | Names catastrophe bonds and budget reserve funds as disaster-risk-financing strategies for resilient infrastructure (PDF p. 64). |

## 3. Risk/problem mechanism

1. **Fund-escalation logic**: relief needs first draw on the State's own
   SDRF; only when State-level funds are insufficient does Central
   assistance flow via the National Disaster Response Fund (PDF p. 15) —
   a two-tier, need-triggered escalation, not automatic Central funding.
2. **2019 Standing Committee on Finance findings** (Chair: Dr. M.
   Veerappa Moily, "Central Assistance for Disaster Management and
   Relief"): recommended enhancing SDRF/NDRF assistance scales to cover
   major expenditure heads (government buildings, transmission
   stations); operationalising a separate Disaster Mitigation Fund;
   earmarking 10% of centrally sponsored scheme allocations for
   permanent restoration; and a 15% annual SDRF corpus increase for
   2020-25 given the funding gap between State demand and Central
   release (PDF p. 15) — ⚠️ document-period recommendations requiring
   current-status verification.
3. **CDRI's financing logic**: standardised risk data and design
   standards are prerequisites for both budget reserve funds and
   catastrophe bonds to be priced/structured correctly — financing
   depends on the risk-assessment thematic area functioning first (PDF
   p. 64).

## 4. Disaster-management cycle application

- ✅ **Pre-disaster**: mitigation investment (recommended Disaster
  Mitigation Fund) and risk-transfer instruments (insurance, catastrophe
  bonds) reduce or shift future financial exposure before an event
  occurs.
- ✅ **Response**: SDRF/NDRF finance immediate relief.
- ✅ **Recovery**: Build Back Better principle applied "not only for the
  structural design of the infrastructure but also in terms of
  management systems around it" (PDF p. 64) — financing reconstruction
  that reduces future risk, not merely restores the prior state.

## 5. Institutions and policy tools

- ✅ **Standing Committee on Finance (2019)** — the source's own
  detailed, named finance-reform review (Section 3).
- ✅ **CDRI** — international coalition linking risk data, design
  standards and financing mechanisms (catastrophe bonds, reserve funds)
  to multilateral-bank investment decisions (PDF p. 64).
- ✅ **NCCD** — the specific tax instrument funding NDRF-the-fund,
  whose scope is explicitly narrowing post-GST (PDF p. 15).

## 7. Must-Know Facts for Prelims

- ✅ Four risk-management strategies: acceptance, avoidance, reduction,
  transfer (insurance being the most common transfer form).
- ✅ SDRF funds immediate State-level relief; the National Disaster
  Response Fund provides Centre-level assistance beyond SDRF capacity.
- ✅ The National Calamity Contingency Fund (11th Finance Commission) was
  merged into the National Disaster Response Fund.
- ✅ NCCD funds the National Disaster Response Fund via excise/customs
  duty on specified goods.
- ✅ CDRI's financing tools include catastrophe bonds and budget reserve
  funds.

## 8. ❌ Traps and stale-claim cautions

- ❌ SDRF and the National Disaster Response Fund are interchangeable
  terms for the same fund. -> SDRF operates at the State level for
  immediate relief; the National Disaster Response Fund is the Central
  fund for needs beyond SDRF capacity (PDF p. 15) — distinct funds.
- ❌ The 2019 Standing Committee's recommendations (Disaster Mitigation
  Fund, 15% SDRF corpus increase) are confirmed implemented. -> These
  are document-period recommendations (PDF p. 15); verify current
  adoption status against a dated MHA/Finance Commission source.
- ❌ The ₹5,690 crore/₹2,500 crore NCCD figures are current. -> Document-
  period (2015-16/2018-19) figures; cite a current source for any
  present-day NCCD-revenue claim.
- ❌ "Risk reduction" and "risk transfer" are the same strategy. -> They
  are explicitly distinct: reduction lowers the likelihood/consequence
  of a hazard; transfer shifts the financial burden to another party
  (PDF p. 5).

## 9. 📰 Current official anchor

- 📰 The **Disaster Management (Amendment) Act, 2025 (Act 10 of 2025)**
  is in force from 9 April 2025 (gazette-notified 8 April 2025); its
  disaster-database provisions (Sections 8A/8B/9B) are operative law,
  checked as of the 18 July 2026 research cutoff, and are the correct
  current anchor for verifying current disaster-finance transparency
  and reporting architecture — VisionIAS's 2019 Standing Committee
  recommendations and NCCD figures must be checked against this and any
  subsequent Finance Commission award before being cited as current
  fact. ⚠️ Actual operational rollout of specific database features
  still requires verification against current notifications, but the
  Act's own commencement is settled.
