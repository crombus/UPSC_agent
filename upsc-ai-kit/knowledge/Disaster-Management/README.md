## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the must-do definitional,
  institutional and hazard-specific map for each area; `advanced/NN` adds
  risk-system analysis, governance/implementation gaps, vulnerability and
  equity, financing/accountability, and management trade-offs. Advanced
  does not repeat basic — it analyses what basic establishes.
- **Tagging:** ✅ = directly grounded in the retrieved VisionIAS passage, a
  verified PYQ or a dated official document; ⚠️ = analytical inference,
  a contested/unsettled policy question, an OCR defect carried from
  source, or a mandatory refresh warning; 📰 = a dated official current
  anchor; ❌ = a boundary/trap statement marking a stale claim, a common
  misconception, or what this folder does **not** cover.
- **Date discipline — VisionIAS-period vs current:** the VisionIAS
  material is a "2024 update" but still carries several **pre-2025**
  institutional descriptions (e.g. NDRF at "12 Battalions" — PDF p. 13;
  the National Disaster Management Plan of **2016** — India's first
  edition — described as the operative plan, superseded by the
  **revised NDMP, 2019** — PDF p. 14; NDMA's National Policy on Disaster
  Management of **2009**; the 2019 Standing Committee on Finance report
  on SDRF/NDRF funding — PDF p. 15). Every such fact is labelled
  **document-period evidence** and is never presented as the current
  state of affairs. Current facts — especially the **Disaster Management
  (Amendment) Act, 2025 (Act 10 of 2025)** (gazette-notified 8 April
  2025, in force from 9 April 2025; Sections 8A, 8B, 9B and 41A are
  operative law) and any State/Central *implementation* action taken
  under it — are cited only to a dated official source; the Act's own
  commencement is settled and is not treated as uncertain.
- **No stale-danger claims:** this folder does not claim precise
  earthquake prediction, does not treat an announced target as achieved
  merely because its date has arrived, and does not carry forward
  battalion counts, hazard-zone percentages, mortality/loss figures,
  fund corpora, coastline lengths or monitoring-station counts as current
  without a dated official citation.

## Boundaries with other subjects

- **Environment owns** climate science, UNFCCC/NDC/COP negotiations,
  ecology, biodiversity and pollution law. Disaster Management owns the
  **application layer** — how a hazard (including a climate-linked one)
  is assessed, warned, responded to and recovered from. See
  `15_Climate-Risk-Adaptation-and-Loss-and-Damage`.
- **Geography owns** the physical-process detail of hazards — plate
  tectonics, glacial/geomorphic mechanics, monsoon dynamics, terrain and
  drainage-basin description. Disaster Management owns risk governance,
  institutions and the management cycle built on that physical base.
- **Science & Technology owns** the technical engineering/science
  internals of sensors, satellites and forecasting models. Disaster
  Management owns their **application** to warning, response and
  resilience — see `04_Multi-Hazard-Early-Warning-and-Disaster-Technology`.
- **Internal Security owns** security operations, counter-terror and
  armed-forces/CAPF deployment. Disaster Management owns **civilian**
  emergency management; it references NDRF/Home Guards/Civil Defence
  only in their disaster-response mandate, not their security mandate.
- **Polity owns** detailed constitutional/federal doctrine (Lists,
  Article interpretation). Disaster Management uses the Concurrent List
  entries only as the DM Act's legal basis, not as a Polity topic in
  itself — see `02_Indian-Legal-and-Institutional-Architecture`.
- **Social Justice owns** welfare entitlements for vulnerable groups in
  general. Disaster Management owns inclusive protection **during and
  around a disaster event** — see
  `03_Community-Based-DRR-and-Inclusive-Protection`.
- **Economy owns** general fiscal policy and Finance Commission
  mechanics. Disaster Management owns disaster-specific finance and risk
  transfer — see
  `16_Disaster-Finance-Risk-Transfer-and-Build-Back-Better`.

## Study sequence

1. **Foundation:** `01` — the risk equation, DM cycle and Sendai
   framework every later topic reuses.
2. **Institutional spine:** `02-04` — legal/institutional architecture,
   inclusive community protection and the technology/early-warning layer
   that every hazard-specific topic below routes through.
3. **Hazard-specific topics in the VisionIAS document's own order:**
   `05-11` — geological (05, 06, 10), hydro-meteorological (07, 08, 09)
   and ecological (11) hazards.
4. **Anthropogenic and public-health hazards:** `12-13`.
5. **Cross-cutting resilience layer:** `14-16` — urban/infrastructure
   resilience, the climate interface, and finance/risk transfer.
6. **Operational and governance capstone:** `17-18` — the full relief-
   to-recovery cycle and the governance/international-cooperation
   architecture every earlier topic's "measures taken" section routes
   through.

## Dynamic-source rule

Current claims are anchored **only** to: NDMA/MHA (National Disaster
Management Division), NIDM, NDRF, IMD, CWC, INCOIS, GSI, ISRO, FSI,
MoEFCC, NCDC/MoHFW, AERB/DAE, and — for global framework status —
UNDRR, WMO, WHO. If a live fetch of one of these fails, another official
endpoint for the same institution is used; nothing dynamic is guessed.

## Citation rule

Every current-status number or claim carries a source and a date. Every
VisionIAS-sourced (non-current) fact cites the original document —
*VisionIAS Value Added Material Disaster Management* — and its PDF page
number(s), e.g. "PDF pp. 12-13".

## PYQ rule

Local 2024/2025 UPSC wording is quoted **verbatim**, including OCR
defects such as "targests" in 2024 Q17, which is explicitly labelled as
reproduced verbatim from the local source rather than corrected.
