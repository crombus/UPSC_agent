## 2. Essential definitions

| Concept | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Current-affairs anchor** | A specific, dated, source-attributed fact (e.g., a new protected-area notification, a species status change) used to update or exemplify a static concept. |
| ✅ **Source attribution** | Citing the exact originating document (PIB press release ID, MoEFCC notification, official convention Secretariat announcement) and its date for any dated claim. |

## 3. Topic mechanism

1. Static conceptual knowledge (Topics 01-27) explains *what* a Ramsar site, Tiger Reserve,
   IUCN category or COP mechanism *is* and *how it works* — this foundational knowledge does
   not change year to year.
2. Current affairs supply the *latest specific instance* of that concept (e.g., which
   wetland became India's 100th Ramsar site, which reserve became the 58th Tiger Reserve,
   which COP made which finance decision) — this changes frequently and must be tracked
   separately with precise dates.
3. A complete, exam-ready answer combines both layers: the static concept explains the
   mechanism and significance; the current-affairs anchor supplies the specific, dated,
   verifiable fact that demonstrates active, up-to-date knowledge.
4. This tracker file should be periodically updated (whenever a genuinely new, verified
   milestone or status change occurs) rather than treated as a one-time, static document —
   its value depends on continued, disciplined updating with accurate source attribution.
5. When a specific figure or claim cannot be verified against an official primary source at
   the time of updating, it should be omitted entirely rather than estimated or
   approximated, preserving this tracker's reliability as a fact-checked reference.

## 4. Institutions and policy tools

- ✅ **PIB (Press Information Bureau):** primary official source for Government of India
  announcements, including environment-related milestones (each release carries a citable
  PRID number).
- ✅ **MoEFCC:** source for notifications on protected areas, Schedule changes, and policy
  updates.
- ✅ **NTCA:** source for Tiger Reserve count and tiger-census updates.
- ✅ **Ramsar Convention Secretariat:** source for the official global Ramsar site count and
  designations.
- ✅ **IUCN:** source for Red List category updates.
- ⚠️ Secondary sources (news reports, coaching-material summaries) should be cross-checked
  against these primary sources before being treated as verified facts in this tracker.

## 7. UPSC traps

- ❌ Current-affairs figures (Ramsar count, Tiger Reserve count, forest-cover percentage) are
  fixed, unchanging numbers to memorise once and reuse indefinitely. -> They are dated
  snapshots that change as new notifications/reports are issued — always verify and cite
  the source/date.
- ❌ A number seen in a coaching-material summary can be cited without independent
  verification. -> Cross-check against the primary official source (PIB/MoEFCC/NTCA/
  Ramsar/IUCN) before treating it as reliable.
- ❌ Static concept knowledge and current-affairs facts are interchangeable substitutes for
  each other. -> A complete answer needs both: the concept explains significance/mechanism,
  the current fact demonstrates up-to-date awareness.
- ❌ Every environment-related news item is equally exam-relevant. -> Prioritise items with
  clear GS-paper linkage, PYQ precedent, or genuine policy/legal significance over
  decorative or purely descriptive news items.
