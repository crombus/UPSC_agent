## 2. Essential vocabulary

| Term | Exam-ready meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ **World Heritage Site (WHS)** | A property inscribed under the 1972 World Heritage Convention for outstanding cultural, natural or mixed universal value, selected by the 21-member World Heritage Committee (aided by IUCN, ICOMOS, ICCROM) (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF p. 723). |
| ✅ **Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH)** | Living "practices, representations, expressions, knowledge, skills" recognised under UNESCO's 2003 Convention through the Representative List, the List of ICH in Need of Urgent Safeguarding, and the Register of Good Safeguarding Practices (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF pp. 732-733; UNESCO 2003 Convention mechanisms). |
| ✅ **Memory of the World Register** | UNESCO's documentary-heritage register for manuscripts/archives (see topic 11). |
| ✅ **Geographical Indication (GI)** | Origin-linked intellectual property under domestic law (see topic 12) — **not** a UNESCO category at all. |
| ✅ **AMASR Act** | The Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1958, under which ASI functions, alongside the Antiquities and Art Treasure Act, 1972 (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF p. 956). |
| ✅ **Adarsh Smarak Scheme** | ASI's scheme identifying 100 monuments (e.g. Bhimbetka, Hampi, Konark, Elephanta, Mahabalipuram's Shore Temple, Taj Mahal, Humayun's Tomb) for enhanced visitor amenities (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF p. 956). |

## 3. Core classification

✅ Indian cultural heritage recognition operates across **four distinct,
non-interchangeable categories**: World Heritage Sites (tangible/mixed/
natural, 1972 Convention), Intangible Cultural Heritage (living practice,
2003 Convention), Memory of the World Register (documentary heritage),
and Geographical Indications (domestic IP law, unrelated to UNESCO). ✅
India's cultural institutions split by domain: **ASI** (monuments/
archaeology), **Sahitya Akademi** (literature), **Sangeet Natak Akademi**
(music/dance/drama), **Lalit Kala Akademi** (visual/fine arts),
**Anthropological Survey of India** (anthropological research), **National
Archives of India** (documentary records), **Asiatic Society** (oriental
studies), alongside NMMA, ICCR, CCRT and others (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF pp.
955-971).

## 4. Foundation narrative

📰 **UNESCO WHS in India:** as verified 21 July 2026, India has **44 World
Heritage properties** — 36 cultural, 7 natural,
1 mixed (Khangchendzonga National Park, the only Indian mixed-category
site) (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF pp. 723, 727). ✅ No Indian site currently appears
on UNESCO's List of World Heritage in Danger (as of July 2025); Manas
Wildlife Sanctuary and the Group of Monuments at Hampi were **previously**
listed as endangered (ethnic unrest and cable-bridge construction
respectively) but have since been removed following conservation
improvements (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF pp. 728-729). 📰 **UNESCO ICH in India:** India has **16 elements** on the Representative
List after Deepavali's 2025 inscription; none is currently on the Urgent
Safeguarding List (UNESCO ICH India page, verified 21 July 2026).
Network membership counts (such as Creative Cities) change independently
and should not be mixed with WHS/ICH totals.

✅ **ASI:** established 1861 under Alexander Cunningham ("father of Indian
archaeology" and first Director-General); functions under the AMASR Act,
1958, and the Antiquities and Art Treasure Act, 1972; maintains an
Underwater Archaeology Wing and the Adarsh Smarak scheme (*Nitin…pdf*,
PDF pp. 956-957). ✅ **Sahitya Akademi** (est. 1954): promotes literature
in 24 languages (22 Eighth Schedule languages plus English and
Rajasthani); awards include the Sahitya Akademi Award, Bhasha Samman, and
the Dr. Ananda Coomaraswamy and Premchand Fellowships (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF
p. 964). ✅ **Sangeet Natak Akademi** (est. 1952): India's first national
performing-arts academy; constituent units include the Jawaharlal Nehru
Manipur Dance Academy and Kathak Kendra; parented the National School of
Drama (independent since 1975) (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF pp. 965-967). ✅ **Lalit
Kala Akademi** (est. 1954): national fine-arts academy; runs the National
Exhibition of Art and the Triennale-India (since 1968) (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF
p. 967-968). ✅ **Constitutional provisions:** Article 29 protects
minority communities' right to conserve their own language/script/
culture; Article 49 obliges the State to protect monuments/objects of
national importance from "spoliation, disfigurement, destruction,
removal, disposal or export"; Article 51A(f) is a **Fundamental Duty**
requiring every citizen "to value and preserve the rich heritage of our
composite culture" (*Nitin…pdf*, PDF pp. 984-985).

conflated with them.

  Dynasty** at Charaideo on **26 July 2024** (India's 43rd WHS). The

## 9. ❌ Traps and corrections

- ❌ World Heritage Site, Intangible Cultural Heritage, Memory of the
  World, and Geographical Indication are interchangeable "heritage tags."
  -> Each is governed by a distinct instrument (1972 Convention; 2003
  Convention; UNESCO's documentary register; India's domestic GI Act,
  1999) with different criteria and a different granting body.
- ❌ A monument's inclusion in the UNESCO Tentative List means it is
  already a World Heritage Site. -> Tentative-list status (e.g.
  Bishnupur, Mattancherry Palace, Mandu, Sarnath, Sri Harimandir Sahib)
  is a nomination step, not an inscription.
- ❌ Any Indian site currently sits on the List of World Heritage in
  Danger. -> As of July 2025 (per the book), no Indian site is on this
  list; Manas and Hampi were **previously** listed but have since been
  removed.
- ❌ Moidams are ordinary earthen tumuli without a built or ritual system.
  -> UNESCO describes a mound over a hollow vault, a shrine and octagonal
  wall within a continuing Tai-Ahom funerary and ancestor-worship
  landscape.
- ❌ UNESCO inscription transfers ownership of a site or object to
  UNESCO. -> Inscription confers international recognition and
  Convention-based protections; ownership, management and legal
  jurisdiction remain with the State Party (India) and its own
  institutions (ASI/AMASR).
- ❌ UNESCO World Heritage buffer zones and AMASR prohibited/regulated
  areas are identical. -> They arise from different legal instruments and
  may have different mapped boundaries.
- ❌ Natyashastra's 2025 Memory of the World inscription is unverified. ->
  UNESCO/UN India announced both Bhagavadgita and Natyashastra additions;
  distinguish the manuscripts' inscription year from the texts'
  composition histories.
- ✅ **Local PYQ trap:** CSE 2024's "latest inclusion" question was
  time-bound (Garba then). Deepavali's 2025 inscription changes a live
  answer; always read the question's date.
- ❌ A repatriation of an artefact can be assumed or inferred without a
  dated official announcement. -> Never state that a specific object has
  been repatriated unless a dated official source names the object, the
  receiving institution and its provenance.

## 10. Mains framework

1. Name the precise heritage category (WHS/ICH/Memory of the World/GI)
   before making any claim about "UNESCO recognition."
2. Cite the exact, dated figure (with its access/verification date) for
   any count — never an undated round number.
3. Distinguish Tentative List status from actual inscription.
4. Name the specific Indian institution (ASI, SNA, LKA, Sahitya Akademi,
   etc.) with its precise mandate rather than a generic "cultural body."
5. Close with the constitutional frame (Articles 29, 49, 51A(f)) where a
   question invites a legal/rights dimension.
