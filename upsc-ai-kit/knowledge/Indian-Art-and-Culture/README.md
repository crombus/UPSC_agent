## How this folder works

- **Two tiers per topic:** `basic/NN` builds the essential vocabulary,
  classification and source-grounded narrative for each area of art and
  culture; `advanced/NN` adds comparative morphology, patronage analysis,
  evidence interpretation, contested attribution, conservation/safeguarding
  trade-offs and a Mains-ready answer architecture. Advanced does not repeat
  basic — it analyses what basic establishes. **Read `basic/NN` and
  `advanced/NN` for the same number as a matched pair.**
- **Tagging:** ✅ = directly grounded in the retrieved book passage, an
  official document or a verified PYQ; ⚠️ = analytical inference, a
  contested attribution, an OCR/spelling artefact carried from source, or a
  mandatory refresh warning; 📰 = a dated official current anchor (UNESCO,
  ASI, SNA, GI Registry, etc. — always carries an access/verification date);
  ❌ = a boundary statement or a common misconception/trap this file
  corrects.
- **Current-data discipline:** no monument count, GI total, UNESCO
  inscription list, awardee list or repatriation claim is presented as
  today's status merely because the book states it. Every changing number
  carries its own dated source; where no dated official update was
  verified, the file says so honestly rather than guessing.
- **This is a culture-first module, not a duplicate political chronology.**
  Political and dynastic sequence appears only as the minimum context
  needed to explain patronage of a form, material or practice — the
  detailed chronology belongs to the History folders (see Boundaries,
  below).

## Study sequence

1. **Architecture spine first:** `01-05` in numeric order — vocabulary and
   Harappan urbanism, then Mauryan/Buddhist/Jain rock-cut heritage, temple
   architecture (with the mandatory Chandella dossier in `03`), Indo-Islamic
   architecture, and colonial/post-Independence architecture.
2. **Visual and performing arts:** `06-10` — sculpture/pottery/iconography
   as the material-culture counterpart of architecture, then painting,
   music, dance and theatre/puppetry as the four core performance/visual
   traditions.
3. **Textual and material culture:** `11-12` — languages/scripts/literature/
   manuscripts, then crafts/textiles/folk/tribal traditions.
4. **Integrative closing pair:** `13-14` — religion/philosophy/cultural
   synthesis as the meaning-layer running through every earlier topic, and
   heritage conservation/institutions/UNESCO as the capstone through which
   every topic's "safeguarding" question routes.

## Chandella priority note

Topic 03 carries a **non-negotiable, dedicated Chandella/Khajuraho dossier**
in both tiers (a complete case dossier in
`advanced/03_Temple-Architecture-and-Chandella-Khajuraho.md`), because it is the
only 2025 direct Art & Culture question and because Khajuraho is the
clearest available case for mature Nagara morphology, plural Hindu-Jain
patronage and UNESCO World Heritage management together. Cross-linked from
topics 06 (sculpture), 09 (dance-sculpture links via mithuna/abhinaya
postures) and 14 (1986 UNESCO inscription, ASI/AMASR management,
conservation and tourism).

## Boundaries with other subjects

- **Ancient/Medieval/Modern History own political and dynastic
  chronology.** This folder uses only the minimum dynastic/dating context
  needed to explain patronage of a form; detailed reign-by-reign political
  narrative belongs to the History folders.
- **Philosophy owns doctrinal depth** of the six darshanas, Buddhist and
  Jain philosophy, and comparative metaphysics. This folder owns religion
  and philosophy **as they shape cultural form, patronage and expression**
  — see topic 13.
- **Indian Society owns marginality, living-community outcomes, gender and
  caste dynamics as social-science questions.** This folder notes them only
  where a source directly ties them to a craft, performance or heritage
  practice — see topics 09, 12.
- **Geography owns physical/spatial setting** (river courses, terrain,
  climate zones as physical geography). This folder owns how place and
  ecology shape material, form and circulation — see topics 01, 12.
- **Economy owns tourism and craft-market economics** as an economic
  sector. This folder owns craft ecology, GI as intellectual property and
  conservation funding logic — see topics 12, 14.
- **Current Affairs owns day-to-day event chronology.** This folder anchors
  only dated, source-verified current facts (UNESCO inscriptions, award
  years, GI totals) needed to correct book-period statements.
- **Art & Culture owns form, material, technique, patronage, meaning,
  performance, circulation and conservation** across every topic in this
  folder.
