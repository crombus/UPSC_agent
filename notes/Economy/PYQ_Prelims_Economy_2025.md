## Q1. Financial Markets — Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs)

**Question:** With reference to investments, consider the following:
- I. Bonds  II. Hedge Funds  III. Stocks  IV. Venture Capital

How many of the above are treated as Alternative Investment Funds?

- (a) Only one
- (b) Only two
- (c) Only three
- (d) All the four

**Answer: (b) Only two**

**Explanation:** Under SEBI's AIF Regulations, 2012, AIFs are pooled investment vehicles in three categories — Category I (venture capital, infrastructure, SME), Category II (PE/debt funds), and Category III (hedge funds). **Hedge Funds** (Category III) and **Venture Capital Funds** (Category I) are AIFs. Bonds are direct debt instruments and Stocks are direct equity instruments — neither is an AIF.

**Concept for revision:** SEBI AIF Regulations 2012 — three categories; distinction between AIF and direct market instruments.

## Q2. Banking — Sources of Income for RBI

**Question:** Which of the following are the sources of income for the Reserve Bank of India?
- I. Buying and selling Government bonds
- II. Buying and selling foreign currency
- III. Pension fund management
- IV. Lending to private companies
- V. Printing and distributing currency notes

Select the correct answer using the code given below.

- (a) I and II only
- (b) II, III and IV
- (c) I, III, IV and V
- (d) I, II and V

**Answer: (a) I and II only**

**Explanation:** RBI earns income from (I) open market operations in government securities and (II) forex reserve management (interest + trading gains). Pension fund management is handled by PFRDA — not RBI. RBI does not lend to private companies (it lends to banks/financial institutions). Currency notes are a **liability** on RBI's balance sheet (seigniorage is indirect, not a direct income line per UPSC's interpretation). Official UPSC key: A.

**Concept for revision:** RBI balance sheet — currency notes are liabilities; income sources = interest on G-secs + forex assets.

## Q3. Government Organizations — Enforcement Agencies

**Question:** With reference to the Government of India, consider the following information:

| Organization | Some of its functions | It works under |
|---|---|---|
| I. Directorate of Enforcement | Enforcement of the Fugitive Economic Offenders Act, 2018 | Internal Security Division-I, Ministry of Home Affairs |
| II. Directorate of Revenue Intelligence | Enforces the provisions of the Customs Act, 1962 | Department of Revenue, Ministry of Finance |
| III. Directorate General of Systems and Data Management | Carrying out big data analytics to assist tax officers for better policy and nabbing tax evaders | Department of Revenue, Ministry of Finance |

In how many of the above rows is the information correctly matched?

- (a) Only one
- (b) Only two
- (c) All the three
- (d) None

**Answer: (a) Only one**

**Explanation:**
- **Row I — INCORRECT**: Function correct (ED does enforce FEOA 2018 + PMLA + FEMA), but **ministry wrong** — ED is under **Department of Revenue, Ministry of Finance**, NOT Ministry of Home Affairs.
- **Row II — CORRECT ✓**: DRI enforces the Customs Act, 1962 and allied laws (NDPS, Arms Act, etc.) under CBIC, Department of Revenue, MoF.
- **Row III — INCORRECT**: The function "big data analytics to catch tax evaders" belongs to the **Directorate General of Analytics and Risk Management (DGARM)**, not DG Systems and Data Management (which handles IT infrastructure/data management for CBIC/CBDT).

Only Row II is correctly matched → **Only one**.

**Concept for revision:** ED (FEO/PMLA/FEMA) → Dept of Revenue, MoF. DGARM (analytics) ≠ DG Systems & Data Management.

## Q4. Corporate Governance — BRSR

**Question:** Consider the following statements:
- I. The Reserve Bank of India mandates all the listed companies in India to submit a Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR).
- II. In India, a company submitting a BRSR makes disclosures in the report that are largely non-financial in nature.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) I only
- (b) II only
- (c) Both I and II
- (d) Neither I nor II

**Answer: (b) II only**

**Explanation:** Statement I is **false** — BRSR is mandated by **SEBI** (not RBI), applicable to top 1,000 listed companies by market cap from FY 2022-23. Statement II is **true** — BRSR disclosures cover environmental, social, and governance (ESG) metrics which are predominantly non-financial (emissions, water usage, employee well-being, supply chain practices, etc.).

**Concept for revision:** BRSR = SEBI mandate (2021); replaces BRR; non-financial ESG disclosures; top 1,000 listed companies.

## Q5. Taxation — Agricultural Income & Capital Assets

**Question:** Consider the following statements:

**Statement I:** In India, income from allied agricultural activities like poultry farming and wool rearing in rural areas is exempted from any tax.

**Statement II:** In India, rural agricultural land is not considered a capital asset under the provisions of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

- (a) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct and Statement II explains Statement I
- (b) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct but Statement II does not explain Statement I
- (c) Statement I is correct but Statement II is not correct
- (d) Statement I is not correct but Statement II is correct

**Answer: (d) Statement I is not correct but Statement II is correct**

**Explanation:** Statement I is **false** — Allied agricultural activities like **poultry farming, dairy, wool rearing** are NOT considered agricultural income under the Income Tax Act, 1961, and are therefore taxable (they are separate business/professional income). Only income from land cultivation is agricultural income. Statement II is **true** — Rural agricultural land is specifically excluded from the definition of "capital asset" under Section 2(14) of the IT Act, 1961, and hence capital gains arising from its transfer are not taxable.

**Concept for revision:** Agricultural income (Sec 2(1) IT Act) = only land + cultivation-linked; poultry/dairy taxable. Rural agri land ≠ capital asset.

## Q6. Industry — Critical Minerals Policy

**Question:** Consider the following statements:
- I. India has joined the Minerals Security Partnership as a member.
- II. India is a resource-rich country in all the 30 critical minerals that it has identified.
- III. The Parliament in 2023 has amended the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 empowering the Central Government to exclusively auction mining lease and composite license for certain critical minerals.

Which of the statements given above are correct?

- (a) I and II only
- (b) II and III only
- (c) I and III only
- (d) I, II and III

**Answer: (c) I and III only**

**Explanation:** Statement I — **TRUE**: India joined the US-led Minerals Security Partnership (MSP) in June 2023. Statement II — **FALSE**: India is resource-deficient in many critical minerals (lithium, cobalt, nickel, graphite) — it cannot claim richness in all 30. Statement III — **TRUE**: The Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2023 empowered the Central Government to exclusively conduct auctions for 24 specified critical and strategic minerals like lithium, tungsten, etc.

**Concept for revision:** MSP (2022, US-led); India joins 2023; MMDR Amendment Act 2023 — Central Govt exclusive auction for 24 critical minerals.

## Q7. Financial Markets — Bonds vs Stocks

**Question:** Consider the following statements:

**Statement I:** As regards returns from an investment in a company, generally, bondholders are considered to be relatively at lower risk than stockholders.

**Statement II:** Bondholders are lenders to a company whereas stockholders are its owners.

**Statement III:** For repayment purpose, bondholders are prioritized over stockholders by a company.

Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

- (a) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I
- (b) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct and Statement I explains Statement II
- (c) Only one of the Statements II and III is correct and that explains Statement I
- (d) Neither Statement II nor Statement III is correct

**Answer: (a) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I**

**Explanation:** Statement II — **TRUE**: Bondholders are creditors (lenders), stockholders are equity owners. Statement III — **TRUE**: In liquidation, bondholders (secured/unsecured creditors) are paid before equity shareholders. Both II and III **explain** why bondholders are at lower risk (Statement I) — as creditors, they have contractual claim on interest + principal and priority in repayment over equity holders.

**Concept for revision:** Bond = debt instrument (creditor priority); Stock = equity (residual claim); risk hierarchy: secured creditor > unsecured > preference shareholder > equity shareholder.

## Q8. Financial Markets — India's Equity Derivatives Market

**Question:** Consider the following statements:
- I. India accounts for a very large portion of all equity option contracts traded globally thus exhibiting a great boom.
- II. India's stock market has grown rapidly in the recent past even overtaking Hong Kong's at some point of time.
- III. There is no regulatory body either to warn the small investors about the risks of options trading or to act on unregistered financial advisors in this regard.

Which of the statements given above are correct?

- (a) I and II only
- (b) II and III only
- (c) I and III only
- (d) I, II and III

**Answer: (a) I and II only**

**Explanation:** Statement I — **TRUE**: India became the world's largest derivatives market by volume (NSE ranks #1 globally by number of contracts as of 2023-24). Statement II — **TRUE**: India's market capitalization surpassed Hong Kong's in early 2024, making it the 4th largest globally. Statement III — **FALSE**: SEBI actively warns retail investors about F&O risks (issued circulars), registers/monitors investment advisors, and acts against unregistered entities.

**Concept for revision:** India = world's largest derivatives market (NSE); India's market cap > Hong Kong (2024); SEBI regulates investment advisors.

## Q9. Circular Economy

**Question:** Consider the following statements:

**Statement I:** Circular economy reduces the emissions of greenhouse gases.

**Statement II:** Circular economy reduces the use of raw materials as inputs.

**Statement III:** Circular economy reduces wastage in the production process.

Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

- (a) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I
- (b) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct but only one of them explains Statement I
- (c) Only one of the Statements II and III is correct and that explains Statement I
- (d) Neither Statement II nor Statement III is correct

**Answer: (a) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I**

**Explanation:** Circular economy is a regenerative economic model that keeps resources in use as long as possible. Statement II — **TRUE**: By reusing materials, it reduces energy-intensive raw material extraction (less mining, less forestry = less GHGs). Statement III — **TRUE**: Reducing production wastage reduces energy use in reprocessing and disposal. Both II and III directly **explain** reduced GHG emissions (Statement I) — they address both input extraction and production efficiency.

**Concept for revision:** Circular economy (vs linear "take-make-dispose") → reduces raw material extraction, waste, and GHG emissions simultaneously.

## Q10. Fiscal Policy — Capital Receipts Classification

**Question:** Consider the following statements:
- I. Capital receipts create a liability or cause a reduction in the assets of the Government.
- II. Borrowings and disinvestment are capital receipts.
- III. Interest received on loans creates a liability of the Government.

Which of the statements given above are correct?

- (a) I and II only
- (b) II and III only
- (c) I and III only
- (d) I, II and III

**Answer: (a) I and II only**

**Explanation:** Statement I — **TRUE**: Capital receipts either create future liability (borrowings = government must repay) or reduce assets (disinvestment = government sells assets). Statement II — **TRUE**: Borrowings (market loans, external debt) and disinvestment proceeds are classic capital receipts. Statement III — **FALSE**: Interest received on loans given by government is a **revenue receipt** (recurring income), not a capital receipt, and does NOT create a government liability.

**Concept for revision:** Capital receipts = create liability (borrowings) OR reduce assets (disinvestment + recovery of loans). Interest received = revenue receipt.

## Q35. Development Finance — ADB Nature Finance Hub

**Question:** Which one of the following launched the 'Nature Solutions Finance Hub for Asia and the Pacific'?

- (a) The Asian Development Bank (ADB)
- (b) The Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB)
- (c) The New Development Bank (NDB)
- (d) The International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD)

**Answer: (a) The Asian Development Bank (ADB)**

**Explanation:** The ADB launched the Nature Solutions Finance Hub for Asia and the Pacific to scale up nature-based solutions (NbS) financing for the region. ADB focuses on sustainable development, biodiversity finance, and climate resilience in Asia-Pacific, making it the natural fit for this hub. AIIB and NDB are newer institutions; IBRD (World Bank) operates globally but did not launch this specific hub.

**Concept for revision:** ADB = Manila-based; nature-based solutions finance; distinct from AIIB (infrastructure focus) and NDB (BRICS bank).

## Q60. Energy Regulation — PNGRB Scope

**Question:** Consider the following activities:
- I. Production of crude oil
- II. Refining, storage and distribution of petroleum
- III. Marketing and sale of petroleum products
- IV. Production of natural gas

How many of the above activities are regulated by the Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board in our country?

- (a) Only one
- (b) Only two
- (c) Only three
- (d) All the four

**Answer: (b) Only two**

**Explanation:** PNGRB was established under the PNGRB Act, 2006 to regulate **downstream and midstream** petroleum sector activities. It regulates refining, processing, storage, transportation, distribution, marketing and retailing of petroleum products and natural gas.
- **Activity I (Production of crude oil)** — upstream, regulated by **DGH/MoPNG** — NOT PNGRB ✗
- **Activity II (Refining, storage and distribution)** — YES, regulated by PNGRB ✓
- **Activity III (Marketing and sale)** — YES, regulated by PNGRB ✓
- **Activity IV (Production of natural gas)** — upstream, NOT PNGRB ✗

**Concept for revision:** PNGRB = downstream regulator (refining, storage, marketing); upstream (E&P) = DGH/MoPNG. PNGRB Act 2006.

## Q61. Fiscal Policy — Deficit Calculations

**Question:** Suppose the revenue expenditure is ₹80,000 crores and the revenue receipts of the Government are ₹60,000 crores. The Government budget also shows borrowings of ₹10,000 crores and interest payments of ₹6,000 crores. Which of the following statements are correct?
- I. Revenue deficit is ₹20,000 crores.
- II. Fiscal deficit is ₹10,000 crores.
- III. Primary deficit is ₹4,000 crores.

Select the correct answer using the code given below.

- (a) I and II only
- (b) II and III only
- (c) I and III only
- (d) I, II and III

**Answer: (d) I, II and III**

**Explanation:**
- **Revenue Deficit** = Revenue Expenditure − Revenue Receipts = 80,000 − 60,000 = **₹20,000 crores** ✓ (Statement I correct)
- **Fiscal Deficit** = Total Expenditure − Total Non-Debt Receipts = equals total borrowings = **₹10,000 crores** ✓ (Statement II correct)
- **Primary Deficit** = Fiscal Deficit − Interest Payments = 10,000 − 6,000 = **₹4,000 crores** ✓ (Statement III correct)

All three statements are correct. This is a straightforward application of deficit formulae.

**Concept for revision:** Revenue deficit = Rev Exp − Rev Receipts; Fiscal deficit = borrowings; Primary deficit = Fiscal deficit − Interest payments.

## Q65. Fiscal Policy — Gross Primary Deficit

**Question:** A country's fiscal deficit stands at ₹50,000 crores. It is receiving ₹10,000 crores through non-debt creating capital receipts. The country's interest liabilities are ₹1,500 crores. What is the gross primary deficit?

- (a) ₹48,500 crores
- (b) ₹51,500 crores
- (c) ₹58,500 crores
- (d) None of the above

**Answer: (a) ₹48,500 crores**

**Explanation:** **Primary Deficit = Fiscal Deficit − Interest Payments**. Non-debt creating capital receipts (e.g., disinvestment) are already accounted for in fiscal deficit calculations — they reduce the deficit but the question gives the net fiscal deficit figure of ₹50,000 crores. Primary deficit = 50,000 − 1,500 = **₹48,500 crores**. The ₹10,000 crores of non-debt receipts is a distractor — primary deficit is calculated from the given fiscal deficit figure.

**Concept for revision:** Primary deficit = Fiscal deficit − Interest payments; measures government's current borrowing need excluding interest burden.

## Q66. Finance Commission — 15th Finance Commission Recommendations

**Question:** Which of the following statements with regard to recommendations of the 15th Finance Commission of India are correct?
- I. It has recommended grants of ₹4,800 crores from the year 2022-23 to the year 2025-26 for incentivizing States to enhance educational outcomes.
- II. 45% of the net proceeds of Union taxes are to be shared with States.
- III. ₹45,000 crores are to be kept as performance-based incentive for all States for carrying out agricultural reforms.
- IV. It reintroduced tax effort criteria to reward fiscal performance.

Select the correct answer using the code given below.

- (a) I, II and III
- (b) I, II and IV
- (c) I, III and IV
- (d) II, III and IV

**Answer: (c) I, III and IV**

**Explanation:** Statement I — **TRUE**: 15th FC recommended ₹4,800 crores in education sector grants (2022-26) linked to performance in educational outcomes. Statement II — **FALSE**: The actual devolution recommended is **41%** of net Union tax proceeds to States (not 45%). Statement III — **TRUE**: ₹45,000 crores recommended as performance-based grants for States undertaking farm-sector reforms. Statement IV — **TRUE**: 15th FC reintroduced the tax effort criterion to reward States with better own-tax revenue mobilisation.

**Concept for revision:** 15th FC: 41% devolution (not 45%); education grants ₹4,800 Cr; agri reform incentive ₹45,000 Cr; tax effort criterion reinstated.

## Q67. International Finance — IBRD (World Bank)

**Question:** With reference to the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD), consider the following statements:
- I. It provides loans and guarantees to middle-income countries.
- II. It works single-handedly to help developing countries to reduce poverty.
- III. It was established to help Europe rebuild after World War II.

Which of the statements given above are correct?

- (a) I and II only
- (b) II and III only
- (c) I and III only
- (d) I, II and III

**Answer: (c) I and III only**

**Explanation:** Statement I — **TRUE**: IBRD primarily lends to middle-income and creditworthy low-income countries (not the poorest — that's IDA's role). Statement II — **FALSE**: IBRD does NOT work single-handedly; it operates as part of the World Bank Group (with IFC, IDA, MIGA, ICSID) and collaborates with governments, NGOs, and bilateral organizations. Statement III — **TRUE**: IBRD was established at the Bretton Woods Conference (1944) with its original mandate to finance reconstruction of war-devastated Europe; it later expanded to global development.

**Concept for revision:** World Bank Group = IBRD + IDA + IFC + MIGA + ICSID; IBRD = middle-income countries; IDA = poorest countries; founded 1944 (Bretton Woods).

## Q68. Banking — RTGS vs NEFT

**Question:** Consider the following statements in respect of RTGS and NEFT:
- I. In RTGS, the settlement time is instantaneous while in case of NEFT, it takes some time to settle payments.
- II. In RTGS, the customer is charged for inward transactions while that is not the case for NEFT.
- III. Operating hours for RTGS are restricted on certain days while this is not true for NEFT.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) I only
- (b) I and II
- (c) I and III
- (d) III only

**Answer: (a) I only**

**Explanation:** Statement I — **TRUE**: RTGS settles on a real-time (immediate) gross settlement basis; NEFT operates in half-hourly batches (net settlement), so each NEFT transfer takes up to 30 minutes. Statement II — **FALSE**: In RTGS, **inward transactions are free** for the beneficiary (charges apply only on outward transfers for senders); same applies to NEFT — no inward charges. Statement III — **FALSE**: Since December 2019, **both RTGS and NEFT operate 24×7, 365 days** — there are no restricted hours for either system.

**Concept for revision:** RTGS = real-time gross settlement; NEFT = half-hourly batch (net); both 24×7 since Dec 2019; inward transactions free in both.

## Q69. Banking / Digital Payments — UPI International Merchant Payments

**Question:** Consider the following countries:
- I. United Arab Emirates  II. France  III. Germany  IV. Singapore  V. Bangladesh

How many countries amongst the above are there other than India where international merchant payments are accepted under UPI?

- (a) Only two
- (b) Only three
- (c) Only four
- (d) All the five

**Answer: (b) Only three**

**Explanation:** As of the context of this paper (2025), UPI-based international merchant payment acceptance was operational in:
- **UAE** ✓ (since 2023, via NPCI International partnerships with Mashreq, etc.)
- **France** ✓ (since 2023, including at Paris landmark merchants like Eiffel Tower)
- **Singapore** ✓ (via PayNow-UPI interlinkage for cross-border payments)
- **Germany** ✗ (not confirmed for merchant payments)
- **Bangladesh** ✗ (UPI remittance corridor exists but merchant-level payments not operational)

Only three countries (UAE, France, Singapore) qualify.

**Concept for revision:** UPI international expansion led by NPCI International; merchant payments ≠ remittance linkage; Singapore (PayNow), UAE, France confirmed.
