## 2024 — GS Paper III (Economy core)

### Q1. Public expenditure on social services & inclusive growth · *10 marks*
**Examine the pattern and trend of public expenditure on social services in the post-reforms period in India. To what extent has this been in consonance with achieving the objective of inclusive growth?**
- **Directive:** Examine (pattern + trend + link to inclusive growth).
- **Approach:** Trend since 1991 — social-sector spend as % of GDP/total expenditure rose gradually (education, health, social security); cite Economic Survey social-sector chapter data. Gaps: health spend still ~ under 2% GDP target (NHP 2017 aims 2.5%); inter-state disparity. Link to inclusive growth: MGNREGA, PMAY, PDS, Ayushman Bharat. Conclude — rising but not yet commensurate with inclusive-growth needs.
- **Static hook:** Inclusive growth = broad-based, reduces inequality + poverty; social-sector expenditure is a key enabler.

### Q2. Persistent food inflation & RBI monetary policy · *10 marks*
**What are the causes of persistent high food inflation in India? Comment on the effectiveness of the monetary policy of the RBI to control this type of inflation.**
- **Directive:** Causes + comment on effectiveness.
- **Approach:** Causes — supply-side (erratic monsoon, crop damage, supply-chain gaps, hoarding, MSP/cost push, global prices). Why monetary policy is limited: food inflation is largely supply-driven; repo-rate hikes compress demand but can't fix supply; RBI targets headline CPI (food ~46% weight) under flexible inflation targeting (4%±2%). Need fiscal/administrative measures (buffer stocks, imports, PDS).
- **Static hook:** Flexible Inflation Targeting; MPC; CPI food weight ~45.9%; core vs headline inflation.

### Q3. Land reforms — factors of success · *10 marks*
**What were the factors responsible for the successful implementation of land reforms in some parts of the country? Elaborate.**
- **Approach:** Strong political will & mobilisation (Kerala, West Bengal — Operation Barga), effective tenancy records, peasant organisation, land ceiling enforcement, cooperative movement. Contrast with failure elsewhere (poor records, litigation, evasion).
- **Static hook:** Land reforms = abolition of intermediaries, tenancy reform, ceiling, consolidation.

### Q4. Millets for health & nutritional security · *10 marks*
**Explain the role of millets for ensuring health and nutritional security in India.**
- **Approach:** Nutri-cereals — rich in fibre, protein, micronutrients (iron, calcium); climate-resilient, low water, grow on marginal land; 2023 International Year of Millets; Shree Anna; PDS inclusion. Aids diabetes/anaemia mitigation.
- **Static hook:** Millets = coarse grains/nutri-cereals; IYM 2023; food + nutritional + climate security.

### Q5. IPR & commercialisation of patents · *10 marks*
**What is the present world scenario of intellectual property rights with respect to life materials? Although India is second in the world in filing patents, only a few have been commercialised. Explain the reasons behind this less commercialisation.**
- **Approach:** IPR on life materials — TRIPS, patents on biotech/pharma, biopiracy concerns, India's sui generis (PPV&FR Act). Low commercialisation: weak industry-academia linkage, funding gaps, poor tech-transfer ecosystem, regulatory delays.
- **Static hook:** TRIPS, product patents (post-2005), Section 3(d), National IPR Policy 2016.

### Q6. Electronic Toll Collection technology · *10 marks*
**What is the technology being employed for electronic toll collection on highways? What are its advantages and limitations? What are the proposed changes that will make this process seamless?**
- **Approach:** FASTag (RFID) on NHAI toll plazas; advantages — reduced congestion, fuel/time saving, digital record, less leakage. Limitations — tag reading errors, connectivity. Proposed: GNSS/GPS-based (satellite) barrier-free tolling, ANPR cameras.
- **Static hook:** FASTag → RFID; GNSS-based tolling reform.

### Q11. Four Labour Codes · *15 marks*
**Discuss the merits and demerits of the four 'Labour Codes' in the context of labour market reforms in India. What has been the progress so far?**
- **Approach:** The 4 Codes — (1) Code on Wages 2019, (2) Industrial Relations Code 2020, (3) Code on Social Security 2020, (4) OSH&WC Code 2020. Merits: consolidation of 29 laws, universal floor-wage framework, social-security architecture for gig/platform workers, ease of doing business and fixed-term employment. Concerns: trade-union and strike restrictions, the 300-worker threshold for prior permission under the Industrial Relations Code, implementation capacity and informal-sector coverage. **Current progress:** all four Codes were brought into force from **21 November 2025**; final Central Rules were notified in **May 2026**, while state-rule and enforcement readiness may vary.
- **Static hook:** 29 central labour laws → 4 Codes; gig & platform worker definition (Social Security Code).

### Q12. UDAN — regional air connectivity · *15 marks*
**What is the need for expanding regional air connectivity in India? Discuss the government's UDAN Scheme and its achievements.**
- **Approach:** Need — balanced regional development, tier-2/3 growth, tourism, last-mile connectivity. UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Naagrik) under RCS 2016 — VGF (viability gap funding), capped fares, revived airstrips; achievements — hundreds of routes/airports operationalised.
- **Static hook:** RCS-UDAN 2016; VGF; regional connectivity scheme.

### Q13. Indian irrigation — challenges · *15 marks*
**What are the major challenges faced by the Indian irrigation system in recent times? State the measures taken by the government for efficient irrigation management.**
- **Approach:** Challenges — over-dependence on monsoon, groundwater depletion, canal inefficiency, water-intensive cropping, inequity. Measures — PMKSY (Per Drop More Crop), micro-irrigation, command-area development, AIBP, watershed programmes.
- **Static hook:** PMKSY components; micro-irrigation (drip/sprinkler); water-use efficiency.

### Q14. Buffer stocks & price stabilisation · *15 marks*
**Elucidate the importance of buffer stocks for stabilising agricultural prices in India. What are the challenges associated with storage of buffer stock? Discuss.**
- **Approach:** Buffer stock (FCI) — food security, PDS supply, price stabilisation, emergencies. Challenges — storage/warehousing shortfall, wastage, carrying cost, buffer norms overshoot, MSP-open market distortion. Solutions — modern silos, PEG scheme, decentralised procurement.
- **Static hook:** FCI, MSP, buffer norms, PDS, decentralised procurement.

## 2025 — GS Paper III (Economy core)

### Q1. HDI vs IHDI · *10 marks*
**Distinguish between the Human Development Index (HDI) and the Inequality-adjusted Human Development Index (IHDI) with special reference to India. Why is the IHDI considered a better indicator of inclusive growth?**
- **Approach:** HDI (UNDP) = health (life expectancy) + education (schooling) + income (GNI per capita PPP). IHDI discounts HDI for inequality across the three dimensions — "actual" HDI. India's HDI–IHDI gap is large → high inequality; hence IHDI better captures inclusiveness.
- **Static hook:** HDI three dimensions; IHDI = HDI adjusted for inequality (loss %).

### Q2. Protectionism & bilateralism — challenges to Indian economy · *10 marks*
**What are the challenges before the Indian economy when the world is moving away from free trade and multilateralism to protectionism and bilateralism? How can these challenges be met?**
- **Approach:** Challenges — export uncertainty, tariff wars, supply-chain disruption, WTO paralysis, tech/data protectionism. Responses — FTAs (UAE CEPA, Australia ECTA), PLI/Atmanirbhar for resilience, export diversification, services strength, China+1.
- **Static hook:** WTO crisis, protectionism, FTAs/CEPA, supply-chain resilience.

### Q3. Farmers' choice of high-value crops · *10 marks*
**Explain the factors influencing the decision of farmers on the selection of high-value crops in India.**
- **Approach:** Price/profitability, market access & cold chains, irrigation, credit/insurance, contract farming, perishability & risk, MSP absence, agro-climatic suitability, FPOs.
- **Static hook:** High-value agriculture; diversification; value chains.

### Q4. Supply chain management of agri commodities · *10 marks*
**Elaborate the scope and significance of supply chain management of agricultural commodities in India.**
- **Approach:** Scope — procurement, storage, cold chain, logistics, processing, retail (e-NAM). Significance — reduces post-harvest losses, farmer income, price stability, food security, export competitiveness.
- **Static hook:** e-NAM, cold chain, post-harvest loss, PM Kisan SAMPADA.

### Q11. Fiscal Health Index · *15 marks*
**Explain how the Fiscal Health Index (FHI) can be used as a tool for assessing the fiscal performance of states in India. In what way would it encourage states to adopt prudent and sustainable fiscal policies?**
- **Approach:** FHI (NITI Aayog, launched Jan 2025) ranks states on quality of expenditure, revenue mobilisation, fiscal prudence, debt sustainability, debt index. Encourages competitive federalism, transparency, better own-tax effort, capex quality.
- **Static hook:** NITI Aayog FHI 2025; fiscal prudence; FRBM; cooperative/competitive federalism.

### Q12. Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme · *15 marks*
**Discuss the rationale of the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme. What are its achievements? In what way can the functioning and outcomes of the scheme be improved?**
- **Approach:** Rationale — boost domestic manufacturing, reduce imports, integrate into global value chains, jobs, Atmanirbhar Bharat. 14 sectors (electronics, pharma, semiconductors, etc.). Achievements — investment, mobile export surge, production. Improve — MSME inclusion, wider sectors, simpler disbursal, R&D linkage.
- **Static hook:** PLI 14 sectors; Atmanirbhar Bharat; GVC integration.

### Q14. Food processing industries & employment · *15 marks*
**Examine the scope of the food processing industries in India. Elaborate the measures taken by the government in the food processing industries for generating employment opportunities.**
- **Approach:** Scope — India large producer of fruits/veg/milk; low processing levels → huge potential; reduces wastage, doubles farm income. Measures — PM Kisan SAMPADA, Mega Food Parks, PLI for food processing, PMFME (micro enterprises), Operation Greens.
- **Static hook:** MoFPI; PMKSY (SAMPADA); PMFME; Mega Food Parks.

### Q16. India Semiconductor Mission · *15 marks*
**India aims to become a semiconductor manufacturing hub. What are the challenges faced by the semiconductor industry in India? Mention the salient features of the India Semiconductor Mission.**
- **Approach:** Challenges — capital intensity, water/power/ultra-clean fabs, tech & talent, import dependence, long gestation. ISM — 50% fiscal support fabs/ATMP, Semicon India Programme (~₹76,000 cr), design-linked incentive, partnerships (Micron Sanand).
- **Static hook:** India Semiconductor Mission; Semicon India; DLI; fabs/ATMP/OSAT.

### Q17. Mineral resources & mining as environmental hazard · *15 marks*
**Mineral resources are fundamental to the country's economy and these are exploited by mining. Why is mining considered an environmental hazard? Explain the remedial measures required to reduce the environmental hazard due to mining.**
- **Approach:** Hazards — land degradation, deforestation, air/water pollution, tailings, displacement, subsidence. Remedies — sustainable mining, reclamation, District Mineral Foundation, Star Rating, EIA, scientific closure.
- **Static hook:** MMDR Act, DMF, sustainable mining, EIA.

## Economy-adjacent — GS Paper I (Economic geography / economic history)

### 2024 GS1
- **Q13 (15):** *How far was the Industrial Revolution in England responsible for the decline of handicrafts and cottage industries in India?* — Deindustrialisation, drain of wealth, one-way free trade, tariff policy.
- **Q14 (15):** *The groundwater potential of the Gangetic valley is on serious decline. How may it affect the food security of India?* — Green Revolution belt, over-extraction, food-bowl risk.
- **Q17 (15):** *What is regional disparity? How does it differ from diversity? How serious is the issue of regional disparity in India?* — Per-capita income gaps, HDI divergence, balanced regional development.

### 2025 GS1
- **Q5 (10):** *What are non-farm primary activities? How are these related to physiographic features in India?* — Mining, fishing, forestry, quarrying.
- **Q6 (10):** *Explain briefly the ecological and economic benefits of solar energy generation in India.* — Energy security, jobs, import substitution, ISA.
- **Q14 (15):** *Geographical explanation of the distribution of off-shore oil reserves of the world; how do they differ from on-shore occurrences?* — Continental shelf, sedimentary basins (Mumbai High).
- **Q19 (15):** *Achieving sustainable growth with emphasis on environmental protection could conflict with poor people's needs in a country like India — comment.* — Growth vs environment vs equity trilemma.
