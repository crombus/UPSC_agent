## Q26. Agricultural Economics — 100 Million Farmers (WEF Platform)

**Question:** Which one of the following is the correct description of "100 Million Farmers"?

- (a) It is a platform for accelerating the transition towards food and water systems that are net-zero (carbon), nature-positive and that aims to increase farmer resilience.
- (b) It is an international alliance and a network of individuals and farming organisations interested in supporting and strengthening the development of the organic animal husbandry.
- (c) It is a digital platform fully integrated with service providers and built on blockchain that lets buyers, sellers and third parties trade fertilizers quickly and securely.
- (d) It is a platform with the mission of encouraging the farmers to form Farmer Product Organisations or Agribusiness Consortiums, thus facilitating the access to global open markets to sell their products.

**Answer: (a)**  
**Explanation:** "100 Million Farmers" is a World Economic Forum (WEF) initiative that aims to accelerate the transition of global food and water systems to be net-zero carbon, nature-positive, and climate-resilient. It targets reaching 100 million farmers by 2030 through adoption of climate-smart and regenerative agricultural practices. Options (b), (c), and (d) describe unrelated platforms.  
**Concept for revision:** WEF agricultural platforms — food system sustainability, net-zero, nature-positive agriculture.

## Q40. Banking & Monetary Policy — CBLO / Money Market

**Question:** With reference to the Indian economy, "Collateral Borrowing and Lending Obligations" are the instruments of:

- (a) Bond market
- (b) Forex market
- (c) Money market
- (d) Stock market

**Answer: (c)**  
**Explanation:** CBLO (Collateralized Borrowing and Lending Obligation) is a short-term money market instrument in India, developed by the Clearing Corporation of India Ltd (CCIL). It allows entities such as mutual funds, insurance companies, and NBFCs to borrow/lend overnight funds against government securities as collateral. It operates within the money market and is NOT a bond, forex, or stock market instrument. It has since been replaced by the Tri-party Repo (TREPS) mechanism.  
**Concept for revision:** Money market instruments — CBLO/TREPS, Call Money, Treasury Bills, Commercial Paper.

## Q41. Growth Indicators — Total Fertility Rate

**Question:** The total fertility rate in an economy is defined as:

- (a) the number of children born per 1000 people in the population in a year.
- (b) the number of children born to a couple in their lifetime in a given population.
- (c) the birth rate minus death rate.
- (d) the average number of live births a woman would have by the end of her child-bearing age.

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** Total Fertility Rate (TFR) is the average number of live births a woman would have during her reproductive lifetime (ages 15–49), assuming age-specific fertility rates remain constant. It is the standard demographic indicator for population replacement. India's TFR was ~2.0 as per NFHS-5, below the replacement level of 2.1. Option (a) describes Crude Birth Rate; option (c) is Natural Growth Rate.  
**Concept for revision:** Demographic indicators — TFR, CBR, NMR; replacement level fertility = 2.1.

## Q42. Banking & Financial Markets — NBFC / LAF / FII / G-Secs

**Question:** Consider the following statements:
1. In India, Non-Banking Financial Companies can access the Liquidity Adjustment Facility window of the Reserve Bank of India.
2. In India, Foreign Institutional Investors can hold the Government Securities (G-Secs).
3. In India, Stock Exchanges can offer separate trading platforms for debts.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 3 only
- (c) 1, 2 and 3
- (d) 2 and 3 only

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** Statement 1 is **incorrect** — LAF (Liquidity Adjustment Facility) is a monetary policy tool available only to scheduled commercial banks and Primary Dealers; NBFCs do not have direct access to the LAF window. Statement 2 is **correct** — FIIs/FPIs are permitted to hold G-Secs within overall RBI-prescribed limits (currently up to 6% of outstanding stock). Statement 3 is **correct** — both NSE and BSE operate dedicated debt segments (e.g., Corporate Bond market platform, G-Sec retail segment). *(Note: OCR of official key showed 'C' — web-verified correct answer is D.)*  
**Concept for revision:** RBI's LAF — only for scheduled commercial banks & PDs; FPI limits in G-Secs; NSE/BSE debt platforms.

## Q43. Financial Markets — Corporate Bonds & G-Secs: Eligible Traders

**Question:** In India, which of the following can trade in Corporate Bonds and Government Securities?
1. Insurance Companies
2. Pension Funds
3. Retail Investors

Select the correct answer using the code given below:

- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** All three entities can trade in Corporate Bonds and Government Securities. Insurance companies (regulated by IRDAI) are mandated to invest in G-Secs. Pension funds (regulated by PFRDA) also invest in these instruments. Retail investors can participate through the RBI Retail Direct Scheme (launched 2021) which provides direct access to G-Secs, and through SEBI's bond platforms for corporate bonds.  
**Concept for revision:** RBI Retail Direct — direct G-Sec access for individuals; SEBI's bond platform; institutional investors in debt markets.

## Q44. Financial Markets — Financial Instruments

**Question:** Consider the following:
1. Exchange-Traded Funds (ETF)
2. Motor vehicles
3. Currency swap

Which of the above is/are considered financial instruments?

- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1, 2 and 3
- (d) 1 and 3 only

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** A financial instrument is a contract that gives rise to a financial asset for one party and a financial liability or equity for another. **ETFs** are financial instruments traded on stock exchanges representing a basket of assets. **Currency swaps** are derivative financial instruments used to exchange principal and interest in different currencies. **Motor vehicles** are physical/tangible assets, NOT financial instruments. They may be used as collateral but are not instruments in the financial sense.  
**Concept for revision:** Financial instruments = stocks, bonds, ETFs, derivatives (futures, options, swaps), G-Secs. Physical goods ≠ financial instruments.

## Q45. Growth Indicators — Sectors of Indian Economy

**Question:** With reference to the sectors of the Indian economy, consider the following pairs:

| Economic Activity | Sector (as given in question) |
|---|---|
| 1. Storage of agricultural produce | Secondary |
| 2. Dairy farm | Primary |
| 3. Mineral exploration | Tertiary |
| 4. Weaving cloth | Secondary |

How many of the pairs given above are correctly matched?

- (a) Only one
- (b) Only two
- (c) Only three
- (d) All four

**Answer: (b)**  
**Explanation:** Pairs 2 and 4 are correctly matched. **Dairy farm = Primary** ✓ (direct use of natural resources — animal husbandry). **Weaving cloth = Secondary** ✓ (manufacturing — converts yarn into fabric). **Storage of agricultural produce** belongs to the **Tertiary** (service) sector, not Secondary. **Mineral exploration** belongs to the **Primary** sector per standard NCERT classification (preparatory activity within the mining chain), not Tertiary. Only 2 of 4 pairs are correct.  
**Concept for revision:** Sector classification — Primary (agriculture, mining, fishing), Secondary (manufacturing), Tertiary (services including storage, transport, banking).

## Q47. Microeconomics — Physical Capital: Fixed vs. Working

**Question:** With reference to physical capital in Indian economy, consider the following pairs:

| Items | Type (as given in question) |
|---|---|
| 1. Farmer's plough | Working capital |
| 2. Computer | Fixed capital |
| 3. Yarn used by the weaver | Fixed capital |
| 4. Petrol | Working capital |

How many of the above pairs are correctly matched?

- (a) Only one
- (b) Only two
- (c) Only three
- (d) All four

**Answer: (b)**  
**Explanation:** Pairs 2 and 4 are correctly matched. **Computer = Fixed capital** ✓ (durable asset used over multiple cycles). **Petrol = Working capital** ✓ (consumed in one production cycle). **Farmer's plough** is **Fixed capital** (durable tool reused across seasons), NOT working capital as given. **Yarn** is **Working capital** (raw material consumed when weaving), NOT fixed capital as given. Only 2 pairs are correctly matched.  
**Concept for revision:** Fixed capital = durable (machinery, tools, buildings); Working capital = inputs used in one cycle (raw materials, fuel, seeds).

## Q49. Banking & Monetary Policy — RBI Rules for Foreign Bank WOS

**Question:** With reference to the rule/rules imposed by the Reserve Bank of India while treating foreign banks, consider the following statements:
1. There is no minimum capital requirement for wholly owned banking subsidiaries in India.
2. For wholly owned banking subsidiaries in India, at least 50% of the board members should be Indian nationals.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** Both statements are incorrect. Statement 1 is wrong: RBI prescribes a **minimum initial paid-up voting equity capital of ₹500 crore** for a WOS of a foreign bank in India. Statement 2 is also wrong: RBI's framework states not less than 50% of directors must be **Indian nationals/NRIs/PIOs** (i.e., the broader category, not restricted to just Indian nationals as stated), AND at least one-third must be Indian nationals resident in India — the statement oversimplifies and misstates the rule.  
**Concept for revision:** RBI foreign bank WOS norms — minimum ₹500 crore capital; board composition; Priority Sector Lending norms apply.

## Q50. Industry / Corporate Governance — CSR Rules

**Question:** With reference to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) rules in India, consider the following statements:
1. CSR rules specify that expenditures that benefit the company directly or its employees will not be considered as CSR activities.
2. CSR rules do not specify minimum spending on CSR activities.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Answer: (a)**  
**Explanation:** Statement 1 is **correct**: Under Schedule VII and CSR rules of the Companies Act 2013, expenditures that primarily benefit the company itself or its employees are excluded from CSR activities. Statement 2 is **incorrect**: Section 135 of the Companies Act mandates that eligible companies (₹500 crore net worth, or ₹1,000 crore turnover, or ₹5 crore net profit) must spend **at least 2% of their average net profits** of the preceding three years on CSR — a clear minimum is specified.  
**Concept for revision:** CSR — Section 135, Companies Act 2013; 2% net profit rule; Schedule VII activities; excluded expenditures.

## Q51. External Sector / International Finance — US Treasury Bonds

**Question:** Consider the following statements:

**Statement-I:** If the United States of America (USA) were to default on its debt, holders of US Treasury Bonds will not be able to exercise their claims to receive payment.

**Statement-II:** The USA Government debt is not backed by any hard assets, but only by the faith of the Government.

Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

- (a) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II explains Statement-I
- (b) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct, but Statement-II does not explain Statement-I
- (c) Statement-I is correct, but Statement-II is incorrect
- (d) Statement-I is incorrect, but Statement-II is correct

**Answer: (a)**  
**Explanation:** Both statements are correct and Statement-II explains Statement-I. US Treasury securities are backed only by the "full faith and credit" of the US government (sovereign promise to pay) — they are NOT collateralised by any hard asset (no gold, land, or physical property pledged to bondholders). Because of this absence of hard-asset backing (Statement-II), if the US were to default, bondholders would have no asset to seize or enforce claims against (Statement-I). This is precisely why a US default would be catastrophic for global financial markets.  
**Concept for revision:** Sovereign debt — faith-based (fiat) system; no hard-asset backing for US Treasuries; global reserve currency implications.

## Q53. Banking & Monetary Policy — Digital Rupee (CBDC)

**Question:** Consider the following statements in respect of the digital rupee:
1. It is a sovereign currency issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) in alignment with its monetary policy.
2. It appears as a liability on the RBI's balance sheet.
3. It is insured against inflation by its very design.
4. It is freely convertible against commercial bank money and cash.

Which of the statements given above are correct?

- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 1 and 3 only
- (c) 2 and 4 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 4

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** Statements 1, 2, and 4 are correct. The Digital Rupee (e₹) is a **sovereign currency** issued by RBI under its monetary policy framework (Statement 1 ✓). Like physical currency, e₹ is a **direct liability of the RBI** appearing on its balance sheet (Statement 2 ✓). It is **freely convertible** with commercial bank money (deposits) and physical cash at par (Statement 4 ✓). Statement 3 is **incorrect**: no currency — digital or physical — is "insured against inflation by design." Inflation is a macroeconomic phenomenon; CBDC does not inherently protect against it.  
**Concept for revision:** CBDC/Digital Rupee — RBI liability, sovereign legal tender, retail vs. wholesale e₹, not inflation-proof.

## Q59. External Sector / Agriculture Economics — International Grains Council

**Question:** Consider the following statements:
1. India is a member of the International Grains Council.
2. A country needs to be a member of the International Grains Council for exporting or importing rice and wheat.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Answer: (a)**  
**Explanation:** Statement 1 is **correct**: India is a member of the International Grains Council (IGC), an intergovernmental organisation that promotes international cooperation and trade in grains. Statement 2 is **incorrect**: IGC membership is NOT a prerequisite for exporting or importing rice and wheat. Countries that are not IGC members freely engage in grain trade. The IGC primarily collects and disseminates grain market information; membership is not a trade licensing body.  
**Concept for revision:** IGC — London-based intergovernmental body; grain trade statistics; India as member; NOT a trade prerequisite.

## Q85. Fiscal Policy & Budget — Union Budget Constitutional Provisions

**Question:** With reference to Union Budget, consider the following statements:
1. The Union Finance Minister on behalf of the Prime Minister lays the Annual Financial Statement before both the Houses of Parliament.
2. At the Union level, no demand for a grant can be made except on the recommendation of the President of India.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Answer: (b)**  
**Explanation:** Statement 1 is **incorrect**: Per Article 112, the Annual Financial Statement (Union Budget) is presented to Parliament on behalf of the **President**, not the Prime Minister. In practice, the Finance Minister presents it, but on behalf of the President. Statement 2 is **correct**: Article 113(3) of the Constitution explicitly states that no demand for a grant shall be made except on the recommendation of the President. This is a constitutional safeguard ensuring executive control over spending proposals. *(Note: OCR of official key showed 'C' — web-verified correct answer is B.)*  
**Concept for revision:** Article 112 — Annual Financial Statement presented to Parliament (by Finance Minister on behalf of President); Article 113(3) — President's recommendation mandatory for demand for grants.

## Q92. External Sector / Trade Policy — India–US Apple Trade & GM Food

**Question:** Consider the following statements:

**Statement-I:** India does not import apples from the United States of America.

**Statement-II:** In India, the law prohibits the import of Genetically Modified food without the approval of the competent authority.

Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

- (a) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II explains Statement-I
- (b) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct, but Statement-II does not explain Statement-I
- (c) Statement-I is correct, but Statement-II is incorrect
- (d) Statement-I is incorrect, but Statement-II is correct

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** Statement-I is **incorrect**: India does import apples from the USA. In fact, the USA has been a significant supplier of apples to India. In 2023, India lifted additional retaliatory tariffs on US apples (part of WTO trade dispute resolution). Statement-II is **correct**: Import of GM food in India requires clearance from the Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee (GEAC) under MoEFCC, and FSSAI also regulates GM food standards. There is no blanket approval for GM food imports for human consumption.  
**Concept for revision:** GEAC — GM food/organisms regulator under MoEFCC; FSSAI — food safety standards including GM; India-US trade relations.

## Q97. International Economy — Venezuela Economic Crisis

**Question:** Consider the following statements:

**Statement-I:** Recently, Venezuela has achieved a rapid recovery from its economic crisis and succeeded in preventing its people from fleeing/emigrating to other countries.

**Statement-II:** Venezuela has the world's largest oil reserves.

Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

- (a) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II explains Statement-I
- (b) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct, but Statement-II does not explain Statement-I
- (c) Statement-I is correct, but Statement-II is incorrect
- (d) Statement-I is incorrect, but Statement-II is correct

**Answer: (d)**  
**Explanation:** Statement-I is **incorrect**: Venezuela has NOT achieved a rapid economic recovery. The country continues to suffer from hyperinflation, poverty, and severe shortages. It has one of the world's largest humanitarian/migration crises, with over 7 million people having emigrated. Statement-II is **correct**: Venezuela has the world's largest proven crude oil reserves (approximately 304 billion barrels per OPEC data), exceeding Saudi Arabia — much of it in the Orinoco Belt as heavy crude.  
**Concept for revision:** Venezuela — largest proven oil reserves; ongoing economic/humanitarian crisis; contrast with actual recovery narrative.

## Q100. Poverty & Employment — PM-SYM Yojana

**Question:** With reference to the Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Maan-dhan (PM-SYM) Yojana, consider the following statements:
1. The entry age group for enrolment in the scheme is 21 to 40 years.
2. Age specific contribution shall be made by the beneficiary.
3. Each subscriber under the scheme shall receive a minimum pension of ₹3,000 per month after attaining the age of 60 years.
4. Family pension is applicable to the spouse and unmarried daughters.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- (a) 1, 3 and 4
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 2 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 4

**Answer: (b)**  
**Explanation:** Statement 1 is **incorrect**: The actual entry age for PM-SYM is **18 to 40 years**, not 21 to 40. Statement 2 is **correct**: Beneficiaries make age-specific monthly contributions (₹55–₹200/month depending on entry age), matched equally by the Central Government. Statement 3 is **correct**: Each subscriber receives a minimum guaranteed pension of ₹3,000/month upon reaching 60 years. Statement 4 is **incorrect**: Family pension under PM-SYM is available only to the **spouse** (50% of pension amount), not unmarried daughters. Statements 2 and 3 alone are correct → option (b).  
**Concept for revision:** PM-SYM — voluntary pension scheme for unorganised sector workers; 18–40 entry age; ₹3000/month guaranteed pension; matching government contribution; spouse-only family pension.
