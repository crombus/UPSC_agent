# Economic Survey 2025-26 — Chapter-wise UPSC Reference

**Publication note:** Economic Survey 2025-26, Government of India, Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of Finance; published January 2026.  
**Survey thrust:** India enters FY27 with strong macro-stability, but the Survey’s deeper message is that the next growth frontier requires productivity reforms, state capacity, resilient supply chains, strategic indispensability, and citizen/private-sector partnership.  
**Accuracy note:** Headline figures below are included only where cross-checked with the official chapter/highlights PDFs or reputable summary sources. Figures from the local extracted text were treated as unreliable unless separately verified.  
**Primary official source:** [Economic Survey 2025-26 official chapter PDFs](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/) and [Highlights PDF](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/Infographics%20English.pdf).

## 1. State of the Economy: Pushing the Growth Frontier

**Theme / thrust:** India maintains strong FY26 growth despite global fragmentation; domestic demand, private consumption and investment drive expansion, while medium-term potential growth is assessed near 7%.

| Key data point | Verified figure |
|---|---|
| Real GDP growth, FY26 First Advance Estimate | ✅ **7.4%** — [Official Ch.1](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap01.pdf) |
| Real GVA growth, FY26 First Advance Estimate | ✅ **7.3%** — [Official Ch.1](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap01.pdf) |
| PFCE share in GDP, FY26 | ✅ **61.5%**, highest since FY12 — [Official Ch.1](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap01.pdf) |
| Services growth, FY26 FAE | ✅ **9.1%** — [Official Ch.1](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap01.pdf) |
| FY27 real GDP projection | ✅ **6.8–7.2%** — [Official Ch.1](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap01.pdf) |
| Medium-term potential growth | ✅ Around **7%** — [Official Ch.1](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap01.pdf) |

**Key initiatives / policy signals:** GST rationalisation; direct-tax relief; deregulation and compliance simplification; use of high-frequency indicators/nowcasting; reform momentum in manufacturing, logistics, labour codes and digital public infrastructure.

**UPSC relevance:**  
- **Prelims:** FAE = MoSPI; PFCE is the largest demand-side component and reached 61.5% of GDP.  
- **Mains angle:** Domestic demand-led growth amid global uncertainty; reforms must raise potential output, not only cyclical demand.

## 5. Inflation: Tamed and Anchored

**Theme / thrust:** India recorded unusually benign inflation in FY26 due mainly to food disinflation, while core inflation’s apparent stickiness was largely precious-metals driven.

| Oct 2025 CPI | ✅ **0.3%**, lowest monthly reading in current CPI series — [Official Ch.5](https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/economicsurvey/doc/eschapter/echap05.pdf) |

**UPSC relevance:**  
- **Prelims:** Current CPI base = 2012=100; headline CPI includes food/fuel, core excludes them.  
- **Mains angle:** Food-supply management, climate-resilient agriculture and monetary credibility jointly determine inflation stability.
