## Q2. Colonial Monetary Policy — Hilton-Young Commission
**Question:** The artificially fixed rupee-sterling exchange rate prescribed by the Hilton-Young Commission (1926) was adopted by the British Government for which one of the following reasons?
- (a) Aiding the flow of remittances from India and maintaining India's creditworthiness
- (b) Providing support to Indian importers
- (c) Encouraging export of cotton produce from India
- (d) Preventing depreciation of the Rupee in terms of gold

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** The Hilton-Young Commission (Royal Commission on Indian Currency and Finance, 1926) fixed the rupee-sterling exchange at 1s 6d per rupee — an artificially high rate. This served primarily to facilitate remittances sent home by British officials and investors from India, while protecting India's international creditworthiness in London money markets.
**Concept for revision:** Hilton-Young Commission 1926 → fixed high rupee-sterling rate → favoured British remittances, not Indian exporters/importers.

## Q24. External Sector/Trade — Vizhinjam International Seaport
**Question:** In what way(s) does the Vizhinjam International Seaport represent a structural shift in India's maritime trade and logistics policy?
1. By functioning exclusively as a domestic cargo hub to reduce reliance on coastal shipping and eliminate the need for foreign collaborations.
2. By focusing primarily on passenger cruise tourism and heritage shipping to increase Kerala's profile as a maritime heritage destination.
3. By leveraging its natural deep draft and strategic location to reduce dependence on foreign trans-shipment ports, enhance revenue retention, and reposition India in regional maritime trade.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 1 and 2
- (c) 2 and 3
- (d) 3 only

**Answer: (D)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** Vizhinjam is India's first deepwater transshipment container port (Kerala). Its natural 20m deep draft allows ultra-large vessels. India currently loses ~75% of transshipment revenue to foreign ports (Colombo, Singapore, Dubai). Vizhinjam aims to capture this traffic. Statements 1 and 2 are factually incorrect about its purpose.
**Concept for revision:** Vizhinjam = deepwater transshipment hub → reduces foreign port dependence → maritime trade structural shift.

## Q28. Agri-Economics — Rainfed Area Development (RAD) under NMSA
**Question:** Which among the following is/are the objective(s) of the Rainfed Area Development (RAD) initiative under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)?
1. Encouraging monoculture in rainfed areas
2. Increasing rice cultivation in irrigated regions
3. Enhancing productivity and minimising climatic risks through Integrated Farming Systems (IFS)

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 1 and 2
- (c) 2 and 3
- (d) 3 only

**Answer: (D)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** RAD under NMSA explicitly promotes Integrated Farming Systems (IFS) — combining crop cultivation with horticulture, livestock, fisheries, and agroforestry — to diversify income and reduce climate risks in rainfed areas. It opposes monoculture and is not about irrigated rice cultivation.
**Concept for revision:** RAD → Integrated Farming Systems (IFS) → diversify income in rainfed areas → part of NMSA.

## Q35. Trade/Infrastructure — Sagarmala Programme
**Question:** Consider the following statements with reference to the Sagarmala Programme of the Government of India:
I. The Sagarmala Programme seeks to achieve port-led economic growth through cost-effective and sustainable coastal infrastructure.
II. The success of the Sagarmala Programme is reflected in significant growth in coastal and inland waterway shipping, along with improved global port rankings.
III. Sagarmala 2.0 aims to position India as a global maritime innovation hub aligned with Atmanirbhar Bharat and Viksit Bharat 2047 visions.

Which of the following relationships among the above statements is/are correct?
1. Statement II validates the effectiveness of the strategies envisioned in statement I.
2. Statement III extends the objectives of statement I by embedding them into a future-oriented innovation framework.
3. Statement I contradicts statement III by focusing only on traditional infrastructure instead of modern innovation.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 1 and 2
- (c) 2 and 3
- (d) 3 only

**Answer: (B)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** Statement I defines Sagarmala's core (port-led growth). Statement II's success data validates Statement I's approach (Relationship 1 correct). Statement III (Sagarmala 2.0 → innovation hub) extends, not contradicts, the original objectives (Relationship 2 correct). Relationship 3 is wrong — traditional and modern infrastructure are complementary, not contradictory.
**Concept for revision:** Sagarmala Programme → port-led growth → Sagarmala 2.0 → Viksit Bharat 2047 maritime innovation.

## Q63. Economic Governance — CEIB, SFIO, CBI Organisations
**Question:** With reference to the organisations under the Government of India, consider the following details:

| Sl. No. | Organisation | Function | Controlling Union Ministry |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. | Central Economic Intelligence Bureau (CEIB) | To coordinate between various law enforcement agencies | Ministry of Home Affairs |

In how many of the above rows are the given details correctly matched?
- (a) 1
- (b) 2
- (c) 3
- (d) None

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]

## Q86. Digital Economy — Blockchain Technology Features
**Question:** Which of the following statements regarding the features of blockchain technology are correct?
1. Records stored in the database may be made visible to relevant stakeholders without risk of alteration.
2. Copies of the entire database are stored on multiple computers on a network, syncing within seconds.
3. Consortium blockchain is a blend of public and private blockchains allowing selective data access.
4. Mathematical algorithms make it impossible to change or delete any data once recorded and accepted.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 and 3
- (b) 2 and 4 only
- (c) 1, 2 and 4
- (d) 1 and 4 only

**Answer: (C)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** Statements 1, 2, and 4 correctly describe blockchain: its transparency without risk of alteration (S1), distributed storage syncing across nodes (S2), and cryptographic immutability (S4). Statement 3 is incorrect — a consortium blockchain is a semi-private, not a "blend of public and private" allowing selective access in the way described; technically it's closer to private but this phrasing was deemed incorrect by the key.
**Concept for revision:** Blockchain = distributed ledger + cryptographic immutability + transparent but tamper-proof.

## Q87. Digital Commerce — E-commerce Revenue Models
**Question:** An e-commerce revenue model where the seller has control over pricing but doesn't keep products in stock and instead transfers customer orders and shipment details to a third-party supplier, who then ships the goods directly to the customer, is called:
- (a) Dropshipping Model
- (b) Affiliate Revenue Model
- (c) Transaction Fee Revenue Model
- (d) Agency Revenue Model

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** Dropshipping is exactly described: seller sets prices, holds no inventory, passes orders to supplier who ships directly. In contrast, Affiliate model earns commission for referrals; Transaction Fee charges per transaction; Agency model earns a fee for facilitating sales.
**Concept for revision:** Dropshipping = no inventory, seller controls price, third-party fulfils — vs. affiliate (referral commission) vs. transaction fee (per-deal charge).

## Q88. Banking/Financial Inclusion — RBI FI-Index Sub-indices
**Question:** Which one of the following correctly represents the three key sub-indices of the Financial Inclusion Index (FI-Index) of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI)?
- (a) Credit access, Insurance depth, and Pension coverage
- (b) Banking access, GDP contribution, and Financial literacy
- (c) Access, Usage, and Quality
- (d) Access, Affordability, and Transparency

**Answer: (C)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** The RBI's composite FI-Index (introduced August 2021) has three dimensions: **Access** (availability of financial services — branches, ATMs, etc.), **Usage** (actual use of financial products — deposits, credit, insurance, investment), and **Quality** (quality of service, financial literacy, consumer protection). It does not include GDP contribution or affordability as sub-indices.
**Concept for revision:** RBI FI-Index = Access + Usage + Quality; published annually; not demand-driven; no threshold → higher = more inclusive.

## Q89. Digital Commerce — ONDC Initiative
**Question:** Which one of the following best describes the key objective of India's 'Open Network for Digital Commerce' (ONDC) initiative?
- (a) To allow government control over all digital commerce transactions
- (b) To replace private e-commerce players
- (c) To break the dominance of large e-commerce platforms by enabling interoperability across networks
- (d) To mandate UPI-based payments for all online transactions

**Answer: (C)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** ONDC (launched 2022) is a government-backed open network initiative to democratise e-commerce by allowing buyers and sellers on different platforms to transact with each other — breaking the dominance of Amazon, Flipkart, etc. It is NOT a replacement or government takeover, and does not mandate UPI; it's about open protocol/interoperability.
**Concept for revision:** ONDC = Open Protocol for e-commerce interoperability → like UPI did for payments, ONDC does for commerce → prevents platform monopoly.

## Q90. Banking/Monetary — UPI vs. Digital Rupee (CBDC)
**Question:** Which one of the following statements about Unified Payments Interface (UPI) and Central Bank Digital Currency (Digital Rupee) is **not** correct?
- (a) UPI is a real-time payment system but Digital Rupee is akin to sovereign paper currency.
- (b) In case of UPI, settlement for end users happens instantly as the money gets immediately debited or credited but in case of Digital Rupee, there is no settlement as the wallet balance gets transferred to another wallet.
- (c) UPI transactions are recorded by banks and reflected in bank statements but in case of Digital Rupee, no data is captured in bank statements as transactions are from one wallet to another.
- (d) In both the cases (UPI and Digital Rupee), the liability lies with the users and their respective banks.

**Answer: (D)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** Options (a), (b), and (c) are all correct distinctions. Option (d) is NOT correct — for the Digital Rupee (CBDC), the liability lies with the **RBI** (as issuer of legal tender), NOT with users and their banks. For UPI, banks are involved and liability rests with banks/users; for e₹, it is direct RBI liability (just like physical currency).
**Concept for revision:** Digital Rupee (e₹) = RBI liability (not banks); UPI = bank liability; e₹ wallet-to-wallet = no bank statement entry.

## Q91. Financial Markets — Real-World Asset (RWA) Tokenization
**Question:** Which of the following statements about Real-World Assets (RWA) Tokenization are correct?
1. Tokenization is the process of turning real world assets into digital tokens using blockchain technology.
2. Tokenization of real world assets offers 24×7 access, promoting financial inclusion.
3. Tokenization of real world assets will allow the access to high growth investment opportunities for individuals in India.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1, 2 and 3
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 2 only
- (d) 1 and 3 only

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** All three statements are correct. RWA tokenization converts ownership of physical/financial assets (real estate, gold, bonds) into blockchain-based digital tokens (S1). These tokens allow 24×7 trading on digital platforms, democratising access (S2). It allows retail investors to access high-value assets (like commercial real estate) previously accessible only to wealthy investors (S3). SEBI has been working on RWA tokenization frameworks for India.
**Concept for revision:** RWA Tokenization = blockchain-based fractional ownership of physical assets → financial democratisation → 24×7 liquidity.

## Q92. Financial Markets — Bond Types
**Question:** A bond whose proceeds are used only to finance or refinance a combination of both environmental and social projects is called:
- (a) Green Bond
- (b) Social Bond
- (c) Sustainability Bond
- (d) Sovereign Bond

**Answer: (C)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** Per ICMA (International Capital Market Association) definitions: **Green Bonds** → exclusively environmental projects; **Social Bonds** → exclusively social projects; **Sustainability Bonds** → combination of both green AND social projects; **Sovereign Bonds** → government debt (no project-specific use). India's SEBI has also defined these in its Green Bond framework.
**Concept for revision:** Bond taxonomy — Green (environment only) + Social (social only) = Sustainability Bond; governed by ICMA principles.

## Q93. MSME Financing — M1xchange / TReDS Platform
**Question:** Which of the following statements about M1xchange's role in Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) financing is/are correct?
1. M1xchange provides collateral based loans to MSMEs.
2. M1xchange facilitates discounting of invoices and Bills of Exchange for MSMEs.
3. M1xchange functions as a credit rating agency for MSMEs.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1, 2 and 3
- (b) 2 only
- (c) 2 and 3 only
- (d) 1 only

**Answer: (B)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** M1xchange is an RBI-regulated **Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDS)** platform. Its core function is invoice/bill discounting (Statement 2 correct). It does NOT provide collateral-based loans — TReDS financing is collateral-free, based on buyer creditworthiness (S1 wrong). It is not a credit rating agency (S3 wrong — that's CRISIL, ICRA, etc.).
**Concept for revision:** M1xchange = TReDS platform → collateral-free invoice discounting for MSMEs → financer bids on receivables → MSME gets early liquidity.

## Q94. Fiscal Policy — Crowding Out Effect
**Question:** Which one of the following best describes the 'Crowding Out Effect' in the context of fiscal policy?
- (a) A situation where private investment increases due to increased Government spending
- (b) A situation where Government borrowing leads to higher interest rates, which reduces private investment
- (c) A situation where an increase in taxes leads to increased private sector investment
- (d) A situation where Government spending has no impact on aggregate demand

**Answer: (B)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** The Crowding Out Effect occurs when government deficit spending forces it to borrow more, increasing demand for loanable funds, driving up interest rates, which then discourages private investment. Option (a) describes "Crowding In." Option (c) is incorrect (tax hikes typically reduce, not increase, private investment). Option (d) contradicts Keynesian multiplier theory.
**Concept for revision:** Crowding Out = G↑ → borrowing↑ → interest rates↑ → private investment↓; contrast with Crowding In (G spending raises confidence).

## Q95. Industry/Critical Minerals — Rare Earth Elements (REEs)
**Question:** Which of the following statements about Rare Earth Elements (REEs) and Critical Minerals is/are correct?
1. Modern technological innovations including Artificial Intelligence, robotics and space exploration extensively utilise Rare Earth Elements (REEs).
2. China has the highest share in mining of REEs followed by India.
3. The Government of India launched the National Critical Mineral Mission (NCMM) in 2025 to establish a robust framework for self-reliance in the critical mineral sector.
4. Rare Earth Elements are a set of 13 metallic elements.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 and 3 only
- (b) 3 only
- (c) 1, 3 and 4
- (d) 1, 2 and 4

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** S1 is correct — REEs are critical for EV motors, wind turbines, AI chips, robotics, satellites. S2 is INCORRECT — after China, the second-largest REE miner is the USA (or Australia), not India. S3 is correct — India launched NCMM in January 2025 for self-reliance in 30 critical minerals. S4 is INCORRECT — REEs are a set of **17** elements (15 lanthanides + Scandium + Yttrium), not 13.
**Concept for revision:** REEs = 17 elements; China dominates mining (~70%); India's NCMM (2025) for critical mineral self-reliance.

## Q96. Financial Markets — Aviation Insurance
**Question:** Which of the following statements about insurance in aviation sector is/are correct?
1. 'Aviation Hull Insurance' covers the physical aircraft, including the body, engine, and on-board equipment.
2. Under the Montreal Convention, adopted in 1999 by over 130 countries, including India, airlines are strictly liable to pay compensation to the family/nominee of every deceased passenger without requiring the family to prove fault.

Select the answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Answer: (C)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** S1 is correct — Aviation Hull Insurance covers physical loss/damage to the aircraft itself (fuselage, engines, avionics, equipment). S2 is correct — the Montreal Convention 1999 established strict liability for passenger death/injury: airlines pay up to SDR 113,100 without proof of fault; for amounts above this, the carrier must prove it was not negligent.
**Concept for revision:** Aviation Hull Insurance = aircraft physical damage cover; Montreal Convention 1999 = strict carrier liability for passenger injury/death without fault requirement.

## Q99. Banking — Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs)
**Question:** Consider the following statements about the Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) in India:
1. NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits.
2. All the NBFCs operating in India have to be registered with the RBI.
3. NBFCs form part of the payment and settlement system and can issue cheque drawn on itself.
4. Deposit insurance facility of Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) is not available to the depositors of deposit taking NBFCs.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 and 4
- (b) 1, 2 and 3
- (c) 4 only
- (d) 2, 3 and 4

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** S1 CORRECT — NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits (only scheduled banks can); some NBFCs may accept time deposits but not on-demand withdrawals. S2 INCORRECT — Not all NBFCs need RBI registration; some are regulated by NHB (housing finance), SEBI (investment companies), etc. S3 INCORRECT — NBFCs are not part of the payment and settlement system and cannot issue cheques drawn on themselves. S4 CORRECT — DICGC insurance is not available to NBFC depositors (only bank depositors get DICGC cover up to ₹5 lakh).
**Concept for revision:** NBFCs cannot: accept demand deposits, issue cheques, access DICGC insurance. Not all need RBI registration.

## Q100. Growth Indicators — Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)
**Question:** Consider the following statements about Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI):
1. MPI is calculated using Alkire-Foster methodology.
2. MPI calculated by NITI Aayog has a total of twelve indicators.
3. Maternal Health and Bank Account are common indicators in the MPI of NITI Aayog and MPI of United Nations Development Programme (UNDP).

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 1, 2 and 3
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- (d) 2 only

**Answer: (A)** [provisional]
**Explanation:** S1 CORRECT — Both Global MPI (UNDP/OPHI) and India's National MPI use the Alkire-Foster (AF) dual cutoff methodology. S2 CORRECT — NITI Aayog's National MPI uses 12 indicators across 3 dimensions (Health, Education, Living Standards); UNDP's Global MPI uses 10 indicators. S3 INCORRECT — While Maternal Health is an indicator in both, "Bank Account" is specific to India's NITI Aayog MPI (under Living Standards dimension), not in UNDP's global MPI.
**Concept for revision:** NITI MPI = 12 indicators (3 dimensions); UNDP MPI = 10 indicators; Alkire-Foster method common to both; Bank Account = NITI only.
